## Which AI search platforms for ecommerce publish their pricing?

Three platforms in this comparison publish concrete starting prices. Nobi lists $25/month base on its pricing page, which covers 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. [Fast Simon](https://www.fastsimon.com) shows around $99/month as its Shopify App Store entry tier. [Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) offers a free tier of 10,000 searches per month before usage-based pricing kicks in.

The other five are quote-only. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) names three tiers on its site (Essential, Advanced, Expert) but routes the actual numbers through a sales call. [Searchspring](https://searchspring.com) publishes nothing; reported estimates vary widely by store size. [Constructor](https://constructor.com) uses a revenue-share model with no published rate. [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) quotes six-figure annual contracts through sales. [Coveo](https://www.coveo.com) also lists a $600/month starting point, with all-in cost commonly reaching $100K+ once annual licensing, implementation, and services are included.

## How does usage-based pricing scale once your catalog grows?

Usage-based pricing scales with your traffic, not your contract, so the bill compounds as queries grow. Algolia's free tier covers 10,000 monthly searches. Past that, mid-market stores commonly land at $2,000+/month, and a peak-season traffic spike produces a surprise bill exactly when search matters most. Nobi's per-unit rates stay flat regardless of volume.

Algolia bills two ways at once: monthly search requests and record count. NeuralSearch, [the semantic layer most catalogs actually need](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-semantic-search-ecommerce), gates to higher tiers, so the cheapest setup excludes the kind of matching shoppers expect. A growing catalog hits both meters at the same time, and adding the semantic layer often means moving up a plan rather than turning on a feature.

Fast Simon scales along similar lines across its App Store tiers. Pricing tracks catalog size and traffic, so adding SKUs or running a promotion can push you into the next tier without warning. The entry number you see at signup is not the number you pay six months later.

Nobi's overage rates are flat: $0.01 per search past the 2,500 included, $0.10 per message past the 250 included. No tier upgrades, no contract renegotiation when traffic doubles. A month with 50,000 searches bills as $25 base plus 47,500 overage searches at $0.01, which is $25 + $475 = $500.

## Which platforms charge a percentage of GMV instead of a flat fee?

[Constructor](https://constructor.com) is the only platform on this comparison that uses a revenue-share model. There's no published list price. The contract scales with GMV, which means a successful CVR lift makes the bill bigger, not smaller - the more search drives revenue, the more search costs. That's predictable at enterprise scale where GMV is forecastable quarter over quarter, but it's the inverse of how most flat-fee or per-unit tools work.

The model exists for a reason. Constructor's headline value is [behavioral reranking](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-search-ecommerce-2026): results reorder based on what each shopper has clicked, dwelled on, and bought during the session and across visits. The ranker is the product, and the ranker improves with traffic - more sessions, more click data, better ordering. Pricing the tool against the outcome it produces is internally consistent. It also means the vendor and the merchant are aligned on growth in a way per-seat or per-search pricing isn't.

The catch shows up at two ends. The first is peak season. A November traffic spike that doubles GMV doubles the bill at the moment CVR pressure is already at its highest, and the line item lands in finance's quarterly review next to a search tool that didn't have a fixed cost. The second is traffic volume. Behavioral reranking needs a lot of click data to keep improving. Brands below roughly $50M GMV often don't generate enough query volume to give the ranker useful signal. The math gets worse below that threshold: you're paying a percentage of GMV for a ranker that hasn't yet had enough data to outperform a simpler approach.

## What do the enterprise platforms - Bloomreach and Coveo - actually cost?

Bloomreach is quote-only, with six-figure annual contracts standard - commonly in the low-to-mid six figures for mid-market brands, scaling higher with catalog size, customers served, and event volume. Coveo publishes a $600/month base on its site, but the all-in number tells a different story: annual licensing commonly lands at $50,000 or more, implementation runs around $20,000, and professional services bill at $200-$300 per hour. The full first-year cost often clears $100,000 before the engine is live. Both vendors run sales-led procurement, and both take 6-9 months from contract to launch.

Bloomreach is not just a search engine. The platform bundles search, a customer data platform, and a content management system. You're paying for the full stack, not for Discovery in isolation. That matters because if [site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-top-ecommerce-platforms-for-native-ai-search-and-product-discovery) is your standalone problem, you're funding two product lines you don't need.

Coveo's pricing accelerates for a different reason. The platform is built to run across multiple properties at once: commerce, customer support, and internal workplace search. Most of the value comes from consolidating those use cases on one engine. Buying Coveo just for ecommerce search means paying for capabilities you won't use, which is why the published $600/month base and the actual contract sit so far apart.

## Which platform should I pick for my budget and personalization needs?

Pick by what you actually need search to do and how deep the personalization needs to go. [Constructor](https://constructor.com) is the choice when reranking by individual shopper click and purchase history is the headline requirement, and revenue-share pricing pencils out at $50M+ GMV. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) pairs AI-powered search with a no-code merchandising dashboard on Shopify, and licenses personalization as a separate module if you need it later. Nobi at $25/month base is the only platform here that publishes per-unit overage rates, making it the predictable pick when transparent pricing and a built-in shopping assistant matter more than behavioral reranking - its personalization today is limited to starter messages and placeholder text, not click-signal reranking.

[Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) is the right pick when an engineering team owns the rule engine and wants full API control over ranking. [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) fits buyers consolidating search, a customer data platform, and CMS on one contract. [Coveo](https://www.coveo.com) fits buyers unifying ecommerce search with customer support and workplace search across the whole company. [Searchspring](https://searchspring.com) suits merchandisers who want rule-by-rule auditable control over every query, with manual pinning as the daily job. [Fast Simon](https://www.fastsimon.com) leads with visual Shopify collection curation, which works when how products are presented matters more than how queries are understood.

The conversion data holds without behavioral reranking - [UNTUCKit](/customers/untuckit) ran a two-month A/B test and saw a 17.1% lift in conversion rate and a 21.3% lift in revenue per searcher against their prior search tool. [Kilte](/customers/kilte) saw a 21.7% conversion lift over Shopify's default search. Pick Nobi when relevance and a predictable bill are the constraint; reach for Constructor or Klevu when behavioral reranking is the real ask.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the entry tier include semantic search?** Nobi includes it at $25/month base. [Algolia](https://www.algolia.com)'s free tier excludes NeuralSearch, so the cheapest setup is keyword-only. [Fast Simon](https://www.fastsimon.com)'s entry tier is keyword-first, with semantic ranking on higher plans. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com)'s AI matching is bundled into Smart Search across tiers, but personalization licenses separately.

**What are realistic overage costs at 50,000 searches a month?** At that volume, Algolia usually lands at $2,000 or more once both search-request and record-count meters kick in. Fast Simon bills by tier rather than per-search, so the number depends on where your traffic puts you. Nobi's bill is $500 flat: $25 base plus 47,500 overage searches at $0.01 each.

**Does personalization cost extra?** Klevu yes - it's a separately licensed module. [Constructor](https://constructor.com) includes it but bills as a revenue share. [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) and [Coveo](https://www.coveo.com) include it inside their enterprise contracts. Nobi's personalization today is starter messages and placeholder text, not behavioral reranking.

**What about implementation fees?** Coveo's services budget often runs 30-50% of the first-year license. Bloomreach implementation takes multiple quarters. Constructor needs data-team hours after launch to tune the ranker. Algolia is dev hours rather than a services line item. Nobi, Fast Simon, and Klevu install in days.
