## What are Rep AI, Conversica, and Nobi, and what problem does each one solve?

Rep AI is a proactive-chat tool built for Shopify that lets marketing ops teams design conversation flows without engineering help and handle post-purchase tasks like returns, cancellations, and order tracking inside the chat widget. Conversica builds AI agents that follow up with inbound leads by email and SMS after they leave your site. It does not operate during the visit itself. Nobi is a site-embedded assistant that routes each visitor query to either [instant semantic search](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-answer-engines-for-ecommerce-search) or a cited conversational answer, and captures leads in the same widget, without requiring you to maintain manual search rules.

[Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) and Nobi are both on-site tools. They engage visitors while the visit is happening. Rep AI's core value is scripted campaign logic. Its Flow Studio lets a marketing ops team build deterministic conversation paths for a Black Friday promo, a product launch, or an abandoned-cart rescue. Its live-agent drop-in lets a human join an AI session mid-conversation. The trade-off is that it is Shopify-only and built primarily around the chat flow, not search relevance or [open-ended product and policy Q&A](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-best-ai-assistants-for-pdps).

[Conversica](https://www.conversica.com) operates on a different channel entirely. Its defining motion is persistence: it keeps following up by email and SMS until a lead responds or opts out. That is genuinely useful when the problem is letting warm leads go cold after they leave, but Conversica has no role while the visitor is still on the page.

Nobi sits in a different place from both. It combines product search, automatic visitor Q&A grounded in your connected content, and lead capture in one widget. Every answer carries an inline citation back to the source document so visitors can verify what they're reading. Because it covers all three of those jobs in one product, teams with gaps in more than one place (search relevance, policy Q&A, and lead capture) don't have to stitch together separate tools. The honest gap: Nobi does not offer scripted conversation flows or a visual flow builder, so teams that need deterministic campaign-specific chat paths will want Rep AI for that job.

## How do Rep AI, Conversica, and Nobi compare on pricing?

Those different channels come with different pricing models, and the structural gaps between them matter as much as the dollar figures. Nobi is the only one of the three with a public self-serve entry price. Rep AI publishes a pricing calculator but scales its Pro tiers by session volume. Conversica shows you nothing until you've completed a formal sales process.

Nobi's base plan is $25/month, covering 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Beyond that, additional searches cost $0.01 each and additional messages cost $0.10 each. There's no revenue-share, no per-seat fee, and no tiered AI gating. You can work out what you'd pay before talking to anyone.

Rep AI publishes an interactive pricing calculator at hellorep.ai/pricing. The Pay As You Grow entry tier is $12 per 1,000 visitors, with unlimited sessions and a 500-product catalog cap. Pro tiers drop the catalog cap and scale by monthly sessions: at 125,000 sessions, the AI Support Agent is $280/month, the AI Sales Agent is $616/month, and the AI Concierge bundle (which combines both) is $740/month. A 30-day free trial is available at every tier.

Conversica publishes no pricing on its site. The model is per-conversation enterprise pricing, so cost scales with how many leads the system re-engages over a given period. There's no public starter tier and no calculator. Getting to an actual number means going through a multi-week sales evaluation before anything gets signed.

## Which tool is strongest for on-site visitor conversations and product discovery?

Those pricing structures reflect a deeper divide: where each tool actually shows up for a visitor. Rep AI and Nobi both run inside an on-site widget while the visit is happening; Conversica does not engage visitors on the page at all. Between Rep AI and Nobi, the difference comes down to whether you need scripted campaign logic or open-ended Q&A grounded in your catalog and policy content.

Rep AI has the clearest advantage here for marketing ops teams that want to design specific conversation paths. Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder: a non-engineer can map out a deterministic conversation for a Black Friday promo, a product launch, or an abandoned-cart rescue, then trigger it based on visitor behavior. No engineering ticket required. Rep AI also ships a Post-Order Tasks Agent that lets visitors cancel orders, check tracking, or start returns inside the chat without escalating to a helpdesk.

Nobi takes a different approach. Each input routes automatically: a short product query takes an instant semantic-search path with ranked typeahead results; a question gets a [grounded conversational answer](https://nobi.ai/blog/nobi-vs-chatgpt-site-search) with numbered citation pills linking back to the exact source document. There's no unsourced paraphrasing. Nobi does answer post-purchase policy questions (what a return window covers, what a warranty includes), but it does not execute transactions inside the chat. Teams whose visitors need to self-serve cancellations or tracking lookups in chat will find Rep AI more capable on that specific task.

Conversica has no on-site chat. Its motion is post-visit: persistent follow-up by email and SMS after a lead has already left. Evaluating it against Rep AI or Nobi for on-site conversations is the wrong comparison.

## How do Rep AI, Conversica, and Nobi handle lead capture and post-visit follow-up?

That channel difference (post-visit versus on-site) is exactly what separates these three tools on lead follow-up. Conversica is the only one of the three built for off-site lead nurture. Once a lead provides contact information, its AI agents follow up by email and SMS until the lead replies or explicitly opts out. Rep AI captures leads inside the chat widget during the visit but does not run post-visit email sequences. Nobi captures leads through the on-site assistant during the visit but has no email or SMS channel.

Conversica's defining strength is persistence. Its AI agents re-engage the same lead across multiple touchpoints without a human stepping in. The agent keeps going until the lead responds or opts out. That matters most for high-consideration products where decisions take weeks, not minutes. A lead that visits once and goes quiet is the specific problem Conversica is built to recover.

Rep AI captures contact details inside the chat flow and passes them to your CRM or email platform. What happens after the visit is your team's job; Rep AI doesn't run follow-up sequences on its own. If you already have a solid email nurture program in place, that handoff is all you need.

Nobi works the same way on the post-visit side. It captures leads through the on-site assistant and routes them to your CRM; follow-up from there runs through your existing email stack.

## What are the real gaps in each tool that vendor demos rarely surface?

Rep AI's chat widget runs independently of your site's browse experience. If visitors are struggling to find products on category pages or through typeahead search, adding Rep AI doesn't change that. The demo will show a smooth conversation flow, but the underlying catalog discoverability gap stays exactly where it was. Rep AI also doesn't reorder results or responses based on individual visitor behavior. What someone clicked last week or bought last month doesn't influence what they see in the chat today.

Conversica's demos focus on persistent follow-up sequences, and those sequences work. What they don't surface: there is no on-site chat component. If a lead hasn't provided contact information, Conversica has nothing to work with. The other gap is structural: enterprise pricing with a multi-week sales process before anyone gets to a number. Smaller teams that can't absorb a month-long evaluation before signing will effectively be priced out.

There are no [scripted conversation flows](https://nobi.ai/blog/rep-ai-alternatives-ecommerce). Marketing ops teams that need a deterministic path for a seasonal campaign (a Black Friday flow, a product-launch sequence) will need a separate tool for that job. The second gap: there is no hybrid live-agent mode. A human agent can't join an ongoing AI session mid-thread. The handoff is binary: AI or human, not both at once.

## When should you choose Rep AI, Conversica, or Nobi?

## 1. When is Rep AI the right pick?

Rep AI is the right pick when your marketing ops team needs scripted campaign flows live fast (a Black Friday promo, a product-launch sequence, an abandoned-cart rescue) without filing an engineering ticket. It's also the call when you want [a human agent able to join an active AI chat mid-thread](https://nobi.ai/blog/nobi-vs-tidio) without pausing the conversation, or when your visitors need to self-serve cancellations, returns, and order tracking inside the chat widget. Rep AI is Shopify-only today, so teams on other platforms should confirm availability before evaluating.

## 2. When should you choose Conversica?

Conversica fits when your problem is high-intent inbound leads going cold after the first contact. If a visitor hands over their details and then disappears for weeks, Conversica's persistent follow-up by email and SMS keeps re-engaging until they respond or opt out. The consideration window has to be long enough to justify it, and the enterprise pricing and sales cycle mean you'll need organizational budget and runway before anything gets signed.

## 3. When does Nobi fit best?

Nobi is the right pick when search quality or on-site Q&A is the conversion bottleneck. That means visitors not finding products because they describe what they want in plain language that a keyword index misses. Or visitors abandoning because they can't get a fast, cited answer to a policy or sizing question. It combines [semantic search](https://nobi.ai/blog/constructor-vs-algolia-vs-nobi) and conversational Q&A in one widget, so teams with gaps in both don't need two separate implementations.

A team with all three gaps (active seasonal campaigns, leads going cold, and poor on-site search) could legitimately evaluate all three for different parts of the funnel.

## Frequently asked questions about Rep AI, Conversica, and Nobi

### Does Rep AI work on platforms other than Shopify?

Rep AI is purpose-built for Shopify. Its catalog sync, post-purchase task execution, and behavior-based flow triggers are all Shopify-specific. Teams on other platforms should confirm what's available directly with the vendor before committing to an evaluation.

### Is Conversica designed for online retail or B2B?

Conversica launched in automotive in 2013 and expanded to B2B SaaS. It can follow up with any inbound lead by email and SMS, but it is not built around on-site catalog discovery or visitor engagement. Teams evaluating it for their specific lead-nurture use case should confirm it is a good fit before signing.

### Can Nobi replace an existing site search tool?

Nobi provides its own semantic search index that refreshes twice daily from your connected catalog and content, and routes short product queries to instant search results. For most teams it can replace a standalone search widget. It is not built for teams that need custom ranking logic or a search experience embedded in a fully bespoke frontend - those teams should look at [developer-centric alternatives](https://nobi.ai/blog/nobi-vs-algolia).

### Which of the three offers a free trial?

Rep AI offers a 30-day free trial. Nobi's pricing starts at $25/month with no sales call required to get started. Conversica requires a sales conversation before any trial access is available.
