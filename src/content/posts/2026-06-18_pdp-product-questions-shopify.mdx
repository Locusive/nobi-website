## How do you answer shopper questions directly on a Shopify product page?

Someone lands on your product page and has a question. Maybe they want to know if the sizing runs small, whether it ships in time, or if a part fits their setup. They look for an answer and don't find one. So they leave - and your analytics just call it a bounce.

Shoppers make purchase decisions based on specific answers, and most Shopify stores hand them a generic site-wide FAQ or a support form that takes hours. By the time a ticket gets answered, the shopper is somewhere else. Here's how to [set up an assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/adding-ai-chatbot-to-ecommerce-site) that reads your actual catalog, builds a crowd-sourced FAQ from real shopper questions, and outputs structured data that search engines can cite directly.

## Why do generic FAQs fail to answer shoppers on the product detail page?

A site-wide FAQ is written for the average shopper, not the person staring at a specific SKU wondering whether the fabric pills or whether the sizing runs narrow. By the time someone opens a support ticket, the moment is over - they have left or bought somewhere else.

The timing mismatch alone costs the sale. Tickets get answers in hours; a shopper's decision window on a product page closes in minutes. A store-wide FAQ can't scope its answer to the specific variant - color, size, material - the shopper is looking at right now. For apparel, that unanswered question has a real cost: fit uncertainty is one of the top drivers of apparel returns.

When a shopper leaves with a question unanswered, analytics records a bounce but not the question. The bottleneck looks like a traffic problem when it's a content gap.

Merchants adding AI to a product page have one real concern: fabricated specs, wrong inventory, policies that don't exist. The fix is grounding every answer to verified catalog content with citations shoppers can check.

## What is Nobi, and how does it answer product page questions without fabricating specs?

Nobi is a conversational website assistant that answers shopper questions from your own connected content - product pages, policy docs, FAQ routes, PDFs - and cites the exact source behind every answer. It doesn't pull from general internet knowledge or a static training set that can drift from your actual catalog. Every answer comes from content you control, not from what the model happened to learn at training time.

Every answer includes inline numbered citation pills. Hover one and you see the source document name, the date it was indexed, and the exact excerpt the answer came from. A sources sidebar lists every reference with direct links so shoppers can verify any claim against your official content without leaving the chat.

For high-stakes questions, query overrides give you full control. A return policy question, a warranty term, any prompt where paraphrasing creates risk - pin it to an exact, merchant-approved response. That text fires every time the question matches; everything else routes through the standard grounded pipeline.

Nobi also offers an optional second AI review before an answer goes out. A separate model reads the draft alongside the raw content from the cited sources and flags inaccuracies. Only answers that clear the review get sent. It's toggleable per merchant.

Connected knowledge sources refresh twice a day. A spec update or policy change lands in shopper answers within hours, not after a manual re-upload.

## How do I install a product page assistant on my Shopify store?

Getting that grounded, cited experience onto your product pages takes a small Shopify theme edit - most merchants are live the same day, no developer required.

In your Shopify admin, go to **Online Store > Themes > Edit code > Layout** and open `theme.liquid`. Paste in the Nobi snippet. That one addition activates the assistant across every page on your store. If you manage tags through Google Tag Manager instead, create a new Custom HTML tag, paste the Nobi code, set the trigger to All Pages, and publish - same result, no theme file changes needed.

Once the snippet is in place, connect your knowledge sources through the Nobi dashboard. Point it at URLs - product pages, FAQ routes, policy docs - or upload files directly, like PDFs for your return policy or size guide. Nobi reads what you already have; no manual re-entry required. A policy change or spec update reaches shopper answers within hours, not after a manual sync.

[Suggestion pills](https://nobi.ai/blog/suggestion-pills) appear automatically on each product page, generated from the page's content. A product with detailed sizing options surfaces fit-related prompts. A technical product with compatibility requirements surfaces those instead. Shoppers see relevant questions to tap before they type anything - a useful starting point on pages where a blank input would otherwise just sit there.

Pricing starts at $25/month, which includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Beyond those, it's $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. No revenue-share, no per-seat fees, no tiered AI gating - the overage math is simple and predictable before you sign anything.

## How does Nobi keep its answers scoped to the specific product a shopper is viewing?

The page-level suggestion pills hint at a broader principle: every answer Nobi gives is scoped to the exact product a shopper is looking at. It reads the current page URL and product metadata, so when a shopper asks a sizing question on a specific boot listing, the answer pulls from that product's description, variant notes, and spec content - not a sitewide disclaimer written to cover every item in the catalog.

That same context shapes the suggestion pills themselves. A shopper browsing a technical accessory sees compatibility prompts; a shopper on an apparel PDP sees fit and care questions. The pills are generated from the page's own content, not a fixed template, so what surfaces on one product page looks different from what surfaces on another.

When a shopper asks about a detail that isn't in the product content - a specific material, an edge case, something your page doesn't address - Nobi doesn't fill the gap by guessing. It tells the shopper the information isn't available. That might sound like a limitation, but it doubles as a signal: every unanswered question points to a specific product page that could use richer content. The gaps become an editorial queue.

## How does the crowd-sourced FAQ reduce support tickets and build shopper confidence?

Nobi's Reporting & Insights dashboard surfaces question patterns by product - what shoppers are asking, how often, and which questions go unanswered. The first 30 days typically surface the highest-frequency questions, giving merchants a prioritized list of which product pages need richer content.

That logged question data does two things. It shows which product pages have content gaps - every unanswered question points to a specific product or policy that could be more detailed. And it shows what shoppers are asking before they contact support, so you can address those topics proactively in your connected sources.

WISMO queries and sizing questions tend to top the question logs first. They're the two highest-volume categories in most ecommerce support inboxes, which means they also produce the sharpest drop in incoming messages per answered question. A shopper who reads "orders ship within 2 business days and tracking emails go out within 24 hours" doesn't need to send a message.

Compare inbound support volume on SKUs where the assistant is active against an equivalent set of SKUs where it isn't. The numbers separate quickly - a question the assistant answers on the page is a message that never reaches your inbox.

## How does a product page Q&A improve search rankings and appear in AI search results?

A question answered on the product page does more than keep a message out of your inbox - it becomes indexed content. On-page Q&A text is crawled alongside product descriptions, and a question answered directly on the page can rank for that query in organic search. [AI search engines](https://nobi.ai/blog/how-to-get-your-business-found-by-ai-agents) - Google's AI Overviews, Perplexity, and ChatGPT - all look for pages that directly answer questions. A product page with grounded, cited Q&A is structured exactly the way those systems prefer.

Each question the assistant answers is also a long-tail keyword the page now ranks for. "Does this jacket run small?" is a real query shoppers type before they buy. If your product page answers it in indexed, structured form, it captures that search. Without on-page Q&A, that query sends the shopper to a Reddit thread or a review site - and whoever ranks there gets the click.

The twice-daily refresh matters here too. When a spec changes or a price updates, the page content reflects it within hours, with no manual republish. AI search engines that cite product information pull from current indexed content, so stale content is a real citation risk.

The Q&A content that accumulates through everyday shopper questions ends up building the page's search authority at the same time - one body of content doing two jobs.

## How do I know if the product page assistant is working - and when is a different tool the right call?

The search authority builds in the background, but the on-page signal is faster: compare CVR and add-to-cart rate on assistant-enabled PDPs against a matched control group. Track sizing, material, and pre-purchase question volume in your support inbox week-over-week after the FAQ reaches meaningful coverage. Conversion movement confirms the assistant is closing pre-purchase gaps; ticket volume for question categories the FAQ now covers shows how much inbound work it's removing.

Nobi gives you the conversion side: what shoppers searched, clicked, and bought. It doesn't specifically analyze why shoppers abandon at a behavioral level. If understanding drop-off reasons is a primary need, pair Nobi with a dedicated analytics tool.

Two limitations are worth knowing before you commit. First, Nobi answers questions but doesn't execute post-purchase transactions inside the chat - returns, cancellations, and order modifications still need a helpdesk layer if you want shoppers to self-serve those actions. Second, Nobi answers via a web chat widget only. If email is your primary support channel, tools like [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) and [Re:amaze](https://www.reamaze.com) handle email and web chat from one inbox.

If the actual bottleneck is agent-side ticket workflows - macros, SLA tracking, routing rules - a helpdesk solves a different problem. Both can run at the same time.
