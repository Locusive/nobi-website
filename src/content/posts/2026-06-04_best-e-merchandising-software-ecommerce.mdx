## What does e-merchandising software actually do?

E-merchandising software controls which products appear to shoppers - and in what order - across search results, category pages, and recommendations. The job is closing the gap between what a shopper types and what converts. That gap comes from vocabulary mismatch between shoppers and catalog titles, collection pages surfacing the wrong products, and pre-purchase questions that go unanswered. Not every platform covers all three.

[Searchspring](https://searchspring.com), now a division of Athos Commerce, delivers AI-driven search and intent-based merchandising on Shopify. It automates relevance well but still expects manual tuning from your team.

[Fast Simon](https://fastsimon.com) covers search, merchandising, personalization, and AI shopping assistance on Shopify and Shopify Plus. It's a Shopify Plus Certified Partner; pick it when you want all of those capabilities under one Shopify contract.

## 2. Constructor

[Constructor](https://constructor.io) personalizes search results, category pages, browse pages, and recommendations from a single ranking model fed by what each shopper has clicked, viewed, and added during their visit. That breadth matters for a head of ecommerce who needs merchandising to move across the whole site at once, not just the search bar. A signal picked up on a collection page feeds what the shopper sees on search and browse too - the ranker adapts without a merchandiser stepping in. The model performs, but getting there takes work: revenue-share pricing with no published rate, weeks to months to implement, and a data team to keep the ranker tuned once you're live. Constructor is a behavioral personalization engine first; if grounded answers to pre-purchase questions are the gap you're filling, look elsewhere.

**Best for:** Large-volume retailers with an internal data team, where merchandising has to move across category, collection, browse, and search together and real-time behavioral personalization is the headline requirement.

**Pricing:** Revenue-share model. Costs scale with GMV.

**Pros:**
- Session-signal personalization reorders results in real time based on what each shopper clicks, views, and adds during a visit - the ranker keeps adapting without a merchandiser intervening
- Built-in A/B testing lets the team measure which change actually moved CVR, not just which change felt right
- One platform for search, browse, category, and recommendations means in-session signals from any page feed the shared ranking model

**Cons:**
- Revenue-share pricing model can create surprise costs
- Enterprise sales cycle

**Verdict:** Pick Constructor when full-site behavioral merchandising and real-time personalization are the headline requirements and the data team is there to feed the ranker; skip it when transparent per-unit pricing or a grounded answer engine for pre-purchase questions is what you actually need.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the difference between site search and e-merchandising?

[Site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ecommerce-search-save-merchandising-time) handles query matching - turning what a shopper types into a list of products. E-merchandising controls what comes next: which results rank first, which products get pinned or boosted, and what happens when a search returns nothing. Most platforms marketed as e-merchandising software bundle both layers.

### Does e-merchandising software replace the merchandising team?

No - it changes what the team works on. Rule-based tools shift time from manual data pulls to writing and auditing rules. AI tools like Constructor and Nobi shift time from rule maintenance to reading analytics and setting strategy.

### What is behavioral reranking and do I need it?

Behavioral reranking uses signals from what shoppers click, view, and add to cart to reorder results in real time. Constructor is the clearest example in this article. You need it when CVR gaps live across category and collection pages, not just the search bar.

### How long does implementation take?

Fast Simon ships via the Shopify App Store in days. Nobi typically goes live in hours to days. Searchspring's timeline depends on catalog complexity and any custom integrations. Constructor runs weeks to months and requires a data team in place before go-live.

### What is a zero-result rate and why does it matter?

Zero-result rate is the share of searches that return no products. Every null result is a dead end - the shopper either refines, abandons, or contacts support. A well-tuned e-merchandising platform drives this toward zero through synonym management, [semantic matching](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-semantic-search-ecommerce), and redirect rules.
