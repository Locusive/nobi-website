## Why are ecommerce brands looking for a Nosto alternative?

[Nosto](https://www.nosto.com) is a personalization platform built around behavioral product recommendations, dynamic content popups, and on-site A/B testing. Brands most often look for alternatives when its enterprise-leaning pricing moves above mid-market comfort, when the newer search add-on doesn't address the zero-result or vocabulary-mismatch problem they actually have, or when a more focused tool handles the specific job at lower cost and lower implementation overhead.

Nosto's core strength is recommendations - it reads shopper click and browse behavior, then surfaces personalized widgets across PDPs, category pages, and the cart. Its search product is a newer addition to the platform, not its primary focus. Pricing is quote-based on GMV, traffic volume, and module selection, with no published rate card and no self-serve trial. Pick Nosto when behavioral recommendations across the storefront are your primary goal. Skip it when the conversion gap lives in site search, pre-purchase shopper questions, or cart mechanics - a more focused tool usually closes those gaps faster.

Those focused alternatives were evaluated on four criteria. Job fit was the primary filter — how directly a tool closes the conversion gap. The other three: pricing transparency, implementation speed, and personalization depth. The primary filter was job fit: tools whose main function is email, live-agent chat, or quiz funnels don't compete with Nosto's on-site recommendation and discovery engine, so they didn't make the list. Every tool here discloses at least a pricing model or a credible anchor range. Nobi is our product and appears first; every other vendor is assessed on its own merits, including honest weaknesses.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi combines AI-powered site search and a shopping assistant on one platform. Where Nosto centers on behavioral recommendation widgets, Nobi's value sits in the search bar and the shopping assistant. The sources you connect — product pages, policy docs, FAQ routes, and PDFs — ground each answer with inline citation pills, so a shopper asking about a return policy or material spec gets a verifiable reply, not a paraphrase from a static training set. Search runs on your catalog by default - no hand-pinning required and no rule backlog to manage. [Kilte](/customers/kilte) runs Nobi on the search bar, collection filters, and product discovery - all drawing from the same catalog index.

**Best for:** Brands where search relevance and pre-purchase shopper questions are the main conversion gap, and where the team wants those handled without ongoing rule maintenance.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included). $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**
- Every answer grounded in your connected content - product pages, policy docs, PDFs - with inline numbered citation pills that link each response back to the exact source, date, and excerpt
- Query overrides let merchants lock exact responses to high-stakes questions - return policies, warranty terms - with no AI paraphrasing or variation

**Cons:**
- Limited personalization today.
- No site-wide merchandising.
- Not an API-first developer platform.

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when search relevance and pre-purchase shopper questions are the conversion gap. Skip it if you need full-site behavioral reranking based on individual purchase history, or in-chat post-purchase transaction handling.

## 2. Dynamic Yield

[Dynamic Yield](https://dynamicyield.com) is an enterprise personalization and A/B testing platform owned by Mastercard that covers every storefront surface under one contract. The AdaptML engine reads first-party behavioral data and adjusts homepage layouts, PLP rankings, PDP recommendations, email content, and push notifications per visitor. If your team runs structured A/B experiments across multiple surfaces and needs one platform to manage all of it, that's exactly what Dynamic Yield is built for. The tradeoffs are real: implementation takes months, requires engineering throughout, and there are no pre-built Shopify or BigCommerce connectors.

**Best for:** Enterprise retailers with a dedicated experimentation team who need behavioral personalization and A/B testing across every storefront surface - including email and push - managed under one contract.

**Pricing:** Enterprise pricing, custom quotes; not self-serve.

**Pros:**
- Server-side tests run at page load - no above-the-fold flicker, no layout shift, and page performance is unchanged for shoppers in the experiment
- One contract covers A/B testing across homepage, PLPs, email, and push; no stitching together separate tools per channel

**Cons:**
- No native shopping assistant or conversational search

**Verdict:** Pick Dynamic Yield when your team runs a structured A/B testing and personalization program across every storefront surface and has the budget for an enterprise rollout. Skip it when the gap is search relevance, conversational discovery, or pre-purchase shopper Q&A.

## 3. Constructor

[Constructor](https://constructor.io) runs one ranking model across every major surface on your site. Search results, category pages, collection pages, browse, and PDP recommendations all draw from the same behavioral data, adapting in real time as shoppers click and add to cart. A merchandiser managing the shoe category, ordering the jacket collection, and adjusting accessory PDP recommendations can do all of it from one platform. The trade-offs are real: pricing is a revenue-share contract tied to your GMV with no published rate, rollout takes weeks to months, and the behavioral ranker works best when someone on your team keeps it tuned after the initial setup.

**Best for:** Large-volume retailers with an internal data team, where the merchandising workload spans category, collection, browse, and search - and real-time behavioral personalization is the headline requirement.

**Pricing:** Revenue-share model. Costs scale with GMV.

**Pros:**
- One ranking model covers search, category pages, collection pages, browse, and PDP recommendations — no separate project per surface
- Real-time behavioral reranking adapts results as shoppers click and add to cart within the same session
- Built-in A/B testing lets the team measure which change actually moved CVR on a specific query or category page

**Cons:**
- Revenue-share pricing model can create surprise costs
- Enterprise sales cycle

**Verdict:** Pick Constructor when full-site behavioral personalization and real-time reranking are the headline requirements and a data team is available to keep the ranker tuned; skip it when your budget can't absorb revenue-share costs that scale with GMV, or when the rollout timeline and data-team requirement don't match what your team can sustain.

## 4. Rebuy

[Rebuy](https://rebuyengine.com) is a Smart Cart and product recommendation engine built specifically for Shopify. The core focus is AOV: dynamic product bundles, in-cart upsell flows, and post-purchase offers that fire after the sale closes. The ML engine trains on your catalog and order history, so recommendations adjust to what your shoppers actually buy together - no manual configuration required. Rebuy also includes Smart Flows, a no-code builder for promotions like Buy X Get Y and free-gift-with-purchase triggers, all from the same platform. One thing to model before signing: pricing scales with order volume, so costs rise as the store grows, and long-term contract terms have surfaced in merchant reviews.

**Best for:** Shopify brands where cart upsells, dynamic bundles, and post-purchase recommendation flows are the primary AOV lever and the conversion gap sits in the checkout flow rather than earlier in product discovery.

**Pricing:** Publicly listed tiers on the Shopify App Store; pricing scales with store GMV.

**Pros:**
- ML engine trains automatically on catalog and order history - recommendations improve without manual configuration
- Smart Flows ships Buy X Get Y and free-gift-with-purchase promotions without engineering work

**Cons:**
- Cart and post-purchase focus, not site-wide product discovery
- No search or conversational assistant

**Verdict:** Pick Rebuy when cart mechanics and post-purchase recommendation flows are the AOV lever on Shopify; skip it when the conversion gap is earlier in the funnel - in search relevance or pre-purchase shopper questions.

## 5. Klevu

[Klevu](https://klevu.com) is AI-powered site search built for Shopify. The problem it targets is the vocabulary gap between how shoppers describe products and how your catalog titles them. A shopper typing "wide-leg cropped trouser in navy" still lands on the right product even if the catalog entry says "cropped wide pant" - no synonym rule needed for every alternate phrasing a buyer might use. A no-code merchandising dashboard handles pinning, boosting, and zero-result redirects for seasonal pushes, and a Shopify install typically goes live in days. One thing worth flagging before you finalize your shortlist: Klevu is now a division of Athos Commerce, the same parent that owns Searchspring. If both are on your list, you're comparing two tools from the same company, not two independent vendors.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose zero-result rate comes from long, descriptive queries a keyword engine can't parse, and who need occasional pinning without filing an engineering ticket.

**Pricing:** Three tiers: Essential, Advanced, Expert. Quote-only - no published dollar figures on athoscommerce.com/pricing; all tiers show "Get a demo" CTA. Third-party sources cite roughly $499-$1,598/month range. Personalization features are included in Expert tier only, not lower tiers.

**Pros:**
- AI matching surfaces the right product for descriptive queries even when the catalog title doesn't match the shopper's phrasing - no manually maintained synonym list required

**Cons:**
- Expensive relative to brand size
- Merchandising UI is dated
- Personalization features are only in the Expert tier; Essential and Advanced tiers do not include them
- Consolidated under Athos Commerce (alongside Searchspring and Intelligent Reach) - shortlisting Klevu + Searchspring means picking between two products of the same parent company

**Verdict:** Pick Klevu when vocabulary mismatch is the main CVR leak on your Shopify store and you need occasional pinning on top; skip it if personalization is a priority - that feature is limited to the Expert tier.

## Which Nosto alternative fits your ecommerce stack?

The right pick depends on where in the funnel your conversion gap actually lives. If it's in the search bar and pre-purchase questions, Nobi or Klevu address that directly. If it's in behavioral personalization across the full site, Constructor or Dynamic Yield are purpose-built for that job. If it's in the cart and post-purchase flow on Shopify, Rebuy is the most focused option.

Nobi gives you AI search and a grounded shopping assistant on one platform, from $25/month with per-usage pricing you can model before signing. Klevu addresses the same search gap on Shopify, particularly for shoppers typing long, descriptive queries that don't match your catalog titles. Nobi adds the conversational layer on top of search; Klevu stays search-focused.

Constructor and Dynamic Yield both handle full-site behavioral personalization, but they require different commitments. Constructor prices on revenue-share tied to your GMV and works best when a data team can keep the ranker tuned after rollout. Dynamic Yield is the pick when you need enterprise A/B testing across every storefront surface - homepage, PLPs, email, and push - under one contract.

Rebuy is purpose-built for cart upsells and post-purchase recommendation flows on Shopify, without requiring a broader platform contract.

Nosto is still the right call when its existing recommendation widgets are already driving measurable lift and the implementation is stable. Switching costs are real - none of these alternatives replicate Nosto's recommendation-widget surface without a migration, and if it's working, that migration isn't worth the disruption.
