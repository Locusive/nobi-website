## What is an AI product finder, and what should it actually do for an ecommerce site?

An AI product finder is software that moves a shopper from vague intent ("something for sensitive skin," "a gift under $100") to a specific SKU using natural language, behavioral signals, or guided questions, instead of making them grind through category pages. In practice, product finders come in three shapes: search-led (semantic search that understands phrasing and context), chat-led (a conversational assistant that answers questions and recommends), and quiz-led (a structured multi-step flow). Most ecommerce stores end up needing two of the three, because a gift shopper, a brand-new visitor, and a spec-driven returning buyer don't want the same interaction.

Judge any tool by the numbers a Head of Ecommerce actually reports: conversion rate, AOV, and revenue per session. The common failure mode is a chat widget bolted on top of a catalog it doesn't really understand, so it answers in generic copy and recommends whatever's on the homepage. "AI product finder" is an umbrella term, not a single product category. Match the shape to the discovery problem you actually have.

## How does Nobi work as an AI product finder, and what does it cost?

Nobi is three things in one platform: a [site search engine](https://nobi.ai/why-nobi/better-search), a conversational shopping assistant, and a lead capture surface. A shopper can type "waterproof boots for hiking in Colorado" into the search bar or ask the same question in chat, and Nobi returns ranked products that adapt to real behavioral signals rather than merchandiser-tuned rules. Pricing is $25/month for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. No revenue share, no per-seat fees, no AI features locked behind a higher tier. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month pays roughly $975 ($25 base, plus $475 in search overage and $475 in message overage), not a percentage of attributed GMV.

Having all three surfaces in one tool matters for the finder use case because shopper intent doesn't stay in one place. Someone might start in search, get stuck on the results page, and switch to chat to ask "which of these is warmest?" Nobi handles that handoff inside the same product graph and behavioral model.

The clearest [deployment to point to](https://nobi.ai/customers) is Lucchese, the Shopify Plus luxury Western boot brand. They've attributed $1M+ in incremental revenue to Nobi in year one, $3.46M cumulative, and a 39x ROI, with the cart and PDP assistants driving meaningful incremental checkouts alongside search. Faherty's Shop with AI assistant runs at a 6.3% conversion rate and $315 AOV. UNTUCKit pushed for developer-controlled customization of how products render in results, which became Nobi's Hooks API, now available to every customer.

Where Nobi isn't the right answer: quiz-led product finders. If your hero discovery experience is a multi-step quiz ("answer 6 questions, get a curated bundle"), Octane AI is built for that and Nobi isn't. Start there instead.

## When is a quiz-led finder like Octane AI the right call instead of search or chat?

If your category requires structured elicitation before a recommendation makes sense, a quiz is a genuinely better finder than either search or chat. Think skincare sorted by skin type and concerns, supplements sorted by goals, fragrance sorted by notes and occasion. The shopper doesn't yet have the vocabulary to type a useful query, and they don't want to free-form chat their way through twelve clarifying questions. A short, well-structured quiz moves faster and feels more deliberate.

Octane AI is the most mature quiz builder on Shopify, and it's honest about who it serves. It positions itself as the #1 product quiz platform for Shopify and leans into beauty, skincare, supplements, and CPG, categories where guided questioning outperforms open-ended search. The product includes an AI quiz generator for fast setup, customizable quiz types (product finder, routine or bundle builder, personality, survey), and template logic tuned to those verticals. Pricing starts at $50/month on the Basic plan (400 credits), climbs to $200/month for Plus (2,200 credits), and goes to $500+/month at the Enterprise tier. If you run a beauty or CPG brand and your hero discovery surface is a quiz, Octane AI is likely a stronger pick than a search-first tool like Nobi.

Two tradeoffs worth naming. First, quizzes work less well outside attribute-heavy verticals; for apparel, home goods, or broad-catalog stores, shoppers usually want to browse or type, not answer eight questions. Second, a quiz doesn't fix your search relevance problem, it sits alongside it. Plenty of brands end up running both: Octane AI as the guided quiz on the landing page and a Nobi-style search and chat finder for shoppers who skip the quiz and go straight to the search bar.

## How do AI shopping chatbots like Rep AI and Alby compare for product discovery?

Rep AI and Alby are both chat-led finders, but they solve different parts of the funnel. Rep AI runs a proactive AI sales agent that watches on-site behavior and triggers a conversation before the shopper bounces. Alby, which Bluecore acquired in November 2024, embeds natural-language Q&A on product detail pages so a shopper can ask questions about the specific item in front of them. Neither one replaces the search bar, and that's the catch worth naming up front: if a shopper types a query into your search, you still need a real search engine behind it, and a chatbot sitting on top of weak search doesn't fix the weak search.

Rep AI's best fit is high-intent categories where browsers abandon without a nudge: skincare, supplements, specialty apparel, anything where the shopper has a half-formed question and won't stop to type it into a search box. The proactive trigger intercepts that hesitation. It's less useful on a site where shoppers already know what they want and just need to find it faster.

Alby reads more like a product Q&A layer than a finder. A shopper lands on a PDP, asks "is this waterproof," and gets an answer pulled from the product data. That's valuable on dense catalogs with technical specs, but it kicks in after the shopper has already found the page. Direction since the Bluecore acquisition is harder to read from outside, so if you're evaluating Alby today, ask for current roadmap specifics before committing.

For most ecommerce teams, the reasonable combo is a search-first finder (Nobi or equivalent) handling the typed query and results page, with Rep AI layered on top to catch the browsers who never touch the search bar. Budget permitting, that covers both intent modes without asking one tool to do both jobs badly.

## Can a live chat tool like Tidio or a helpdesk like Gorgias work as a product finder?

Partially, but know what you're buying. Tidio bundles live chat with a lightweight AI agent called Lyro that can answer simple product questions, which is enough for a smaller Shopify store but lighter on semantic understanding than a search-first tool. Lyro is available on Tidio's Plus plan or as an add-on, with a free trial of 50 conversations. If live chat coverage is your top pain, Tidio is a reasonable starting point. If shoppers are abandoning because they can't find the right product, a dedicated finder will move the number more.

Gorgias is a different animal. It is a support helpdesk first, built for ecommerce brands on Shopify and similar platforms, and its AI Agent resolves tickets at $0.90 per resolved conversation on top of ticket-based plans that run from $10/month (Starter, 50 tickets) to $750/month (Advanced, 5,000 tickets). That is priced for deflecting "where is my order" and return questions, not for guiding a confused shopper to the right SKU before checkout. Buying Gorgias to act as a product finder is using the wrong tool.

The common stack we see: Gorgias for post-purchase support, a discovery tool like Nobi, Rep AI, or Octane for pre-purchase. The two jobs have different data and different success metrics, and one tool rarely does both well.

Worth being explicit here: Nobi does not process returns, refunds, or order modifications inside the chat. If your shoppers need that, you'll keep Gorgias or Zendesk alongside Nobi. Nobi handles discovery and decision, the helpdesk handles the ticket queue, and the two coexist cleanly.

## How much do AI product finders actually cost, and how do the pricing models compare?

Published starting prices in this category swing from free to enterprise-only. Tidio offers a free plan and a free trial with 50 Lyro conversations. Gorgias Starter is $10/month. [Nobi starts at $25/month](https://nobi.ai/pricing). Tidio's paid tiers begin around $29/month. Octane AI's Basic plan is $50/month. Rep AI starts at $79/month. Alby, now inside Bluecore after the November 2024 acquisition, doesn't publish post-acquisition pricing and generally lands at enterprise levels.

The headline number hides the real cost. Nobi's $25/month base includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then charges $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A store doing 50,000 searches and 2,000 messages a month pays the $25 base plus roughly $475 in search overage and $175 in message overage. That math is visible before you sign. Octane AI works on credit tiers ($50/mo for 400 credits, $200/mo for 2,200, $500+ for enterprise), so cost scales with quiz volume rather than site traffic.

Gorgias needs a closer read. The $10 Starter plan caps you at 50 tickets a month, and AI Agent resolutions are billed separately at $0.90 per resolved conversation with $1.50 overages. If you're buying Gorgias for the AI, the real floor is higher than the advertised entry price, and third-party reviewers have flagged the double-charge structure as confusing.

The bigger trap at the top of the market is revenue share and opaque usage tiers. Some enterprise search vendors take a cut of attributed GMV or bundle "AI credits" that reprice at renewal. That's the pressure pushing mid-market Heads of Ecommerce to look at Nobi: a flat base, a published per-search and per-message rate, no percentage of revenue, no per-seat fees, no AI features locked behind a higher tier.

## How should a Head of Ecommerce actually choose between these AI product finders?

Start with the shape of your discovery problem, not the vendor list. If shoppers type into your search bar and leave unhappy, you need a search-led finder like Nobi. If they arrive knowing nothing and need structured elicitation to narrow the catalog, you need a quiz, and Octane AI is the category pick. If they browse passively and bounce, you need proactive chat like Rep AI. If your real bottleneck is ticket volume, you need a helpdesk like Gorgias, not a finder. The strongest shortlists pair one discovery tool with one support tool rather than asking a single vendor to do both.

Match the tool to your symptom: low on-site search conversion points at Nobi, category confusion at Octane AI, high bounce on product pages at Rep AI, support overflow at Gorgias.

Two honest boundaries on Nobi for this reader. Nobi does not run quiz-led funnels, so if a quiz is your hero discovery surface, Octane AI is the better pick. Nobi is web chat only, with no SMS, WhatsApp, or voice, so if messaging channels are core to your buyer, Nobi is not the right fit. Where Nobi does fit, the pitch is combined search, conversational AI, and email capture in one place starting at $25/month for 2,500 searches and 250 messages.

Run a 4-week A/B pilot on your single highest-traffic discovery surface before any rip-and-replace. And read the pricing model as hard as the feature list: a tool that looks cheap at 2,500 searches and expensive at 50,000 is a different tool at scale.
