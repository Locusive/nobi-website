## What is a conversational commerce assistant?

A conversational commerce assistant is an AI-powered chat experience embedded in an ecommerce site that helps shoppers find products, compare options, and decide what to buy using natural language. Unlike the rules-based chatbots of the last decade, these assistants understand product context, shopper intent, and session behavior. Ask one "which of these hiking boots works for wide feet?" and it should answer the way a good store associate would, pulling from the catalog rather than a scripted decision tree.

You'll see them show up in a few places: inside the search bar, on product detail pages, in the cart, or as a floating bubble in the corner. That surface area is what separates them from traditional helpdesk bots. A helpdesk bot exists to deflect tickets. A conversational commerce assistant exists to drive discovery and merchandising, guiding a shopper from "I need something for a winter trip" to a product page they actually convert on.

Heads of ecommerce are evaluating this category in 2026 for a simple reason: paid acquisition keeps getting more expensive, and site conversion rates have been flat or declining. The assistant is the lever most teams haven't pulled yet.

## What should a head of ecommerce look for in a conversational commerce assistant?

The evaluation criteria that actually matter are narrow: does it lift conversion rate and average order value on real A/B tests, does it work across search, PDP, cart, and chat or only one surface, how much engineering time does launch take, and is the pricing predictable at scale. Tools that demand months of rule-tuning, charge a percentage of attributed GMV, or only cover a single surface rarely pay back for ecommerce teams.

Start with lift. Ask every vendor for a holdout or A/B test from a customer roughly your size, in roughly your vertical, run for at least four weeks. Vendor-reported "average CVR uplift" across their whole book is close to meaningless. You want the raw test setup, the sample size, and whether the lift held past the novelty window.

Next, surface coverage. A chat widget that only fires when a shopper clicks the bubble will touch maybe 2% of sessions. The assistants worth buying cover site search, product detail page questions, cart recovery prompts, and conversational chat, because shoppers bounce between all four during a single visit. Single-surface tools leave most of the revenue on the table.

Time to launch is where buyers get burned. Enterprise platforms routinely quote six to twelve weeks of implementation before a single shopper sees anything. Modern tools should be live on the storefront in days via a tag or Shopify app, with catalog sync running in the background.

Pricing shape matters as much as pricing level. Revenue-share deals (a percentage of "attributed" GMV) get very expensive very quickly once the platform starts working, and attribution models are opaque. Flat or metered pricing is easier to forecast. Nobi, for example, starts at $25 a month for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message, so a brand running 50,000 searches can model the bill before signing.

Finally, merchandising workload. If the tool needs your team writing synonyms, boosting rules, and redirect maps every week to stay useful, the savings evaporate. Favor platforms that learn from shopper click and purchase behavior automatically and only ask for rules when you want to override the default ranking.

## Which conversational commerce assistants are worth evaluating in 2026?

Six tools cover the realistic shortlist for ecommerce teams: Nobi, Alby, Rep AI, Octane AI, Gorgias, and Tidio. Each solves a different primary job, so the right pick depends on whether your bottleneck is discovery, proactive engagement, quiz-led funnels, ticket deflection, or live chat.

**Nobi** combines [semantic search](https://nobi.ai/why-nobi/better-search), a conversational assistant, and lead capture in one platform. Pricing starts at $25/month for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A brand running 50,000 searches a month will pay well above the base, so model your real volume before comparing. Lucchese, a Shopify Plus boot brand, used Nobi for search plus a cart and PDP assistant and attributed $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one at a 39x ROI.

**Alby** is a Shopify-native AI shopping assistant that answers product Q&A and positions around outcome-based pricing tied to assisted conversions. Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024, and the post-acquisition roadmap has not been clarified publicly, so factor that uncertainty into any multi-year decision.

**Rep AI** is a proactive sales chatbot that engages browsers based on behavioral triggers, with a focus on re-engaging disengaged shoppers and feeding back shopper intelligence. It's a chat surface, not a search engine, so pair it with your existing search if discovery is also weak.

**Octane AI** is the quiz-led option, with plans at $50, $200, and $500+ per month. It's most popular with beauty and CPG brands whose funnels convert through guided product finders and routine builders.

**Gorgias** is helpdesk-first, with an AI Agent that answers order questions and resolves tickets. Pricing runs from $10 to $900/month with per-ticket overages. Pick it when deflection is the primary need, not discovery.

**Tidio** pairs affordable live chat with Lyro, its AI agent, and offers a free tier of 50 Lyro conversations. It's a reasonable starter for smaller Shopify stores that mostly need chat coverage.

## How does pricing compare across conversational commerce assistants?

Pricing across conversational commerce assistants falls into three patterns: flat metered usage, app-store or tiered SaaS, and quote-based enterprise. None of the mid-market tools charge a percentage of attributed revenue, but the total cost varies significantly once you add in AI-message overages and required upgrades to unlock the actual AI features.

Nobi sits in the flat metered camp. The base is [$25 per month](https://nobi.ai/pricing) and includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Past that, overages run $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A store doing 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month would pass through the included tier and pay roughly $500 plus the $25 base, so the math scales with traffic rather than with revenue or seat count.

Tidio uses tiered SaaS. There's a free tier, paid plans start around $29 per month, and Lyro, the AI agent that actually handles conversations, is sold either inside the Plus plan or as an add-on priced per conversation. The chat widget itself is cheap; the AI usage is where the bill grows.

Octane AI starts at $50 per month for the Basic plan with 400 quiz/chat credits, jumps to $200 per month for Plus (2,200 credits), and then $500 and up for Enterprise & Unlimited. On the higher tiers, pricing scales with Shopify store revenue.

Gorgias starts at about $10 per month on Starter, but that tier is a ticketing tool. The AI Agent that actually deflects support and sales conversations sits on higher plans, and overages run $0.36 to $0.40 per ticket once you blow past your monthly limit.

Rep AI is typically quoted from around $299 per month for the proactive sales chatbot, with custom plans above that.

Alby uses custom Shopify app pricing, including outcome-based plans tied to assisted conversions for self-serve brands. Pricing direction post-Bluecore acquisition is still settling, so confirm current terms directly.

## When is a different tool the right call instead of Nobi?

Nobi is a discovery-first platform, not a helpdesk replacement or a quiz funnel builder. There are four buyer profiles where another tool is the honest recommendation.

If your primary bottleneck is ticket deflection, returns handling, and post-purchase workflows, Gorgias is the better pick. It's built around the helpdesk: order lookups, refund and exchange flows, agent-assist on inbound tickets, and a per-ticket pricing model that fits support volume rather than search volume. Nobi's assistant helps shoppers find products and decide, but it doesn't process returns, refunds, or order modifications inside the chat. A brand whose conversational AI roadmap starts with "deflect 40% of our tickets" should start with Gorgias and pair Nobi in later if discovery becomes the next priority.

If your hero conversion surface is a quiz-led product finder, Octane AI is purpose-built for that flow. It's the dominant quiz platform on Shopify, with templated finders for routines, bundles, and personality-style flows that hand off to recommendations and email capture. Beauty and CPG brands that have already validated a quiz funnel as their main on-site conversion mechanic will get more out of Octane than out of Nobi's on-demand search and chat.

If your lever is proactive browser engagement, where the assistant intercepts disengaged shoppers before they ask, Rep AI is closer to that shape than Nobi. Nobi engages when shoppers initiate; Rep leans into the proactive nudge.

A few channel and surface gaps to be honest about. Nobi is web chat only today, so brands that need the assistant on voice, SMS, or WhatsApp will need a separate tool for those surfaces. And Nobi curates the search results page, not category or collection pages, so teams that want site-wide merchandising control will still pair it with a dedicated merchandising tool.

## What kind of results can ecommerce brands expect from a conversational commerce assistant?

Published deployments give a realistic benchmark range, and the honest answer is that results vary widely by catalog complexity, traffic mix, and how many surfaces you actually deploy. Faherty's Shop with AI surface converts at 6.3% with a $315 AOV, both well above typical sitewide ecommerce numbers where a 2 to 3% CVR is considered healthy. That gap is not magic. Shoppers who engage with an assistant are already further down the funnel, and the assistant's job is to keep them from bouncing on a product question or a sizing doubt.

Lucchese, a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus, is a useful case for a more complete picture. In its first year on Nobi, the brand attributes more than $1M in incremental revenue to the platform, with $3.46M cumulative attributed revenue and a 39x ROI. Search alone did not get them there. A cart assistant and a PDP assistant ran alongside the search surface, each catching shoppers at a different decision point: a question on a product page, a hesitation in the cart. Those incremental checkouts compound, and they are the reason multi-surface deployments tend to outperform chatbot-only tools that live in a single bubble in the corner of the screen.

Two practical caveats. First, expect a learning period. Nobi's ranking improves as it watches real shopper behavior on your catalog, and meaningful lift typically shows up after 2 to 4 weeks, not on day one. Second, your traffic mix matters. A brand with strong organic PDP traffic will see most of the lift on the PDP assistant; a brand with heavy paid traffic landing on collection pages will see more of it through search and cart. Pick the surfaces that match where your shoppers actually get stuck.

## How do you choose the right conversational commerce assistant for your brand?

Start from the single bottleneck you're trying to fix this quarter. If shoppers can't find products, a discovery-first platform like Nobi or Alby is the right starting point. If they browse but bounce without engaging, Rep AI's proactive triggers may be the higher-leverage move. If your conversion hinges on a guided quiz, Octane AI is built for exactly that funnel. If support volume is drowning the team, Gorgias handles ticket deflection and order questions natively.

Once you've named the bottleneck, pressure-test three things before you sign. First, run a two-week pilot with real traffic, not a sandbox demo. Conversion lift only shows up when the assistant sees actual shopper behavior. Second, confirm the pricing structure: flat or metered usage is predictable, but revenue-share clauses punish you for growing. Nobi's $25/month base with $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra message is an example of pricing that scales linearly, not exponentially. Third, ask for references at brands in your GMV range, not just enterprise flagships. A platform that works for a $200M brand may not be tuned for a $5M one.

One last thing to ask: how does the tool learn? Behavioral learning means the system improves on its own. Manual rule-tuning means someone on your team owns it forever. And if you need developer-controlled customization, ask about extensibility. UNTUCKit's requests shaped Nobi's Hooks API, which is now available to every Nobi customer.
