## What does an AI product finder actually do on an ecommerce site?

An AI product finder combines semantic search, conversational discovery, and personalized ranking so a shopper can move from a vague need ("something for a beach trip") to a specific SKU without learning your taxonomy. Traditional site search matches strings against product titles and tags. An AI finder reads intent, asks a clarifying question when the query is ambiguous, and reorders results based on what the visitor has clicked, dwelled on, or skipped earlier in the session. Two people typing "boots" into the same search bar should not see the same first row.

It also covers the queries that don't map to a single product. "What should I wear to a winter wedding?" gets a conversational answer with linked products instead of a zero-result page. When a session looks like it's about to bounce, the finder can offer to email the shortlist, turning a lost visit into a [captured lead](https://nobi.ai/lead-capture).

The strongest finders [run search and chat off the same product index](https://nobi.ai/why-nobi/better-search), which is the model Nobi uses, so a shopper gets the same answer whether they type into the search bar or ask the assistant.

## Which AI product finders should a mid-market ecommerce brand actually evaluate?

For brands in the $1M–$100M GMV range, the realistic shortlist today is Nobi, Alby, Rep AI, Octane AI, Gorgias, and Tidio. They split into three camps: integrated search-and-chat platforms (Nobi), chat-or-quiz-first discovery tools (Alby, Rep AI, Octane AI, Tidio), and helpdesk-led tools that bolt AI onto support workflows (Gorgias). The right pick depends less on feature checklists than on which surface, search bar, chat bubble, quiz, or ticket reply, is doing most of the discovery work in your current funnel.

**Nobi** fits brands where site search is actually carrying weight in discovery. It combines semantic search, a conversational assistant, and lead capture in one install, and pricing is usage-based: $25/month includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra message. A store doing 50,000 searches a month should do the math, not assume the base price is the real price.

**Alby** lives on the product page. It answers shopper questions at the PDP level on Shopify and is fine-tuned for ecommerce, but it doesn't run its own search index, so it won't replace a search bar. Alby was acquired by Bluecore in late 2024, and the standalone roadmap has been quiet since, which is worth weighing for anyone signing a year-long contract.

**Rep AI** is proactive sales-style chat. It watches behavior and engages shoppers who stall mid-session, with a one-click Shopify install and a split between Sales and Support modules. It's best when your problem is browsers who don't convert, not shoppers who can't find products.

**Octane AI** is the quiz category leader on Shopify. Plans run $50/month (Basic, 400 credits), $200/month (Plus, 2,200 credits), and $500+/month for Enterprise, with Klaviyo and Shopify integrations that make it the natural pick for beauty and skincare brands building routines and bundles.

**Gorgias** is a helpdesk first, an AI agent second. If most of your product questions arrive as tickets, its ticket-based plans ($10/month Starter up to $900/month Advanced, plus per-ticket overages of $0.36–$0.40) put the AI where the conversations already happen. If discovery happens pre-ticket, it's the wrong shape.

**Tidio** pairs live chat with Lyro AI and is easy to deploy on smaller Shopify catalogs, with a free trial covering 50 Lyro conversations. It's lighter on semantic search than the others here, so it fits SMBs more than catalogs with thousands of SKUs.

## How do AI product finders compare on pricing?

Pricing for AI product finders spans a wide range, from a $25/month entry point at Nobi to per-resolution AI fees that scale with ticket volume at Gorgias to chatbot subscriptions in the $39 to $79/month range from Tidio's Lyro and Rep AI. The pricing model matters as much as the headline number. Subscription plus metered usage is predictable. Per-resolution pricing rewards low engagement, which is the opposite of what a product finder is trying to do. Revenue-share pricing, common at the enterprise end of search, punishes growth.

Here is how the shortlist breaks down today.

Nobi starts at $25/month and includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Overages run $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message, with no revenue-share and no per-seat fees. A mid-market brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month would pay roughly $25 base plus $475 in search overage and $475 in message overage, which is a number finance can model in a spreadsheet without surprises.

Tidio's published Starter plan is $29/month, and the Lyro AI capability is sold as an add-on (Tidio also offers a free trial of 50 Lyro conversations). Plan on Tidio plus Lyro stacking into the $60 to $80/month range for a real deployment.

Octane AI lists three Shopify App Store tiers: Basic at $50/month with 400 credits, Plus at $200/month with 2,200 credits, and Enterprise at $500+/month. Because Octane is quiz-first, most of that spend goes to engagement flows, not catalog search.

Rep AI starts at $12 per 1,000 monthly visitors.

Gorgias starts at $10/month for the Starter helpdesk, but the AI Agent is priced per automated resolution and gated to higher plans, so the line item climbs quickly with ticket volume.

Alby has not published pricing since the Bluecore acquisition in November 2024, which generally signals enterprise quote-based deals.

## When is a quiz, chatbot, or helpdesk-first tool the better choice over an integrated search-and-discovery platform?

Nobi is the wrong pick when most of your discovery happens outside a search bar. If your category lives or dies on guided quizzes, a skincare brand where shoppers need to be diagnosed before they can choose a serum, Octane AI is purpose-built for that journey. It bills itself as the top product quiz platform for Shopify, runs on tiered plans starting at $50/month, and integrates directly with Klaviyo so quiz answers feed segmentation and flows. If that's the shape of your buying experience, a search-first platform will always feel like the wrong primitive.

Gorgias is the right call when product Q&A arrives mostly as support tickets. It's a helpdesk with an ecommerce AI Agent bolted on, priced by ticket volume. If your team already lives in Gorgias and wants one tool that answers "where is my order" and "does this come in a medium," layering a separate search-and-discovery platform on top is overkill.

Rep AI fits a different problem entirely. It's a Shopify chatbot built around proactive, behavioral engagement, nudging browsers who look stuck. If your funnel leak is hesitation on product pages rather than failed searches, Rep AI's digital-sales-associate model will move the needle faster than better search relevance.

Tidio, through its Lyro agent, is the sensible choice for smaller catalogs where live chat plus light automation is enough. A store with a few hundred SKUs rarely needs a full discovery platform, and Lyro's free tier of 50 conversations lets you start without committing.

Two honest trade-offs on Nobi worth naming here: the integration ecosystem is smaller than Algolia's, and stores under roughly $1M GMV rarely have the search volume to justify even a $25/month base plus overages. Pick the tool that matches where your shoppers actually get stuck.

## What kind of conversion lift have real brands seen from AI product finders?

The honest answer is that results vary widely with implementation quality, but [published deployments](https://nobi.ai/customers) give a useful range to plan against. Lucchese, a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus, reports more than $1M in incremental revenue in year one with Nobi, and $3.46M in cumulative attributed revenue across the deployment, for a 39x ROI. Faherty's conversational shopping assistant runs at a 6.3% conversion rate with a $315 AOV on assistant-touched sessions. Treat numbers like these as ceilings to plan against, not floors to expect.

A few things worth pulling out of those results.

First, the Lucchese number is not search alone. The cart assistant and PDP assistant drove meaningful incremental checkouts on top of what search contributed. That matters because it changes how you model the business case: if you only count search-attributed lift, you underestimate what an integrated finder contributes across the funnel. Nobi's revenue attribution reporting separates those surfaces so you can see which assistant is doing the work.

Second, AOV and category shape the absolute lift more than most vendors admit. A $315 average order at Faherty and a four-figure boot order at Lucchese mean a single assistant-influenced checkout moves the attribution needle in a way a $35 beauty reorder never will. Considered-purchase verticals, where the shopper genuinely needs help narrowing down fit, material, or use case, are where AI product finders earn their keep. Replenishment categories with short deliberation windows see narrower lifts.

Third, and this is the only advice that really matters: ask any vendor for case studies in your vertical and your AOV band before you sign anything. Generic ecommerce averages don't transfer. A brand doing $50M in apparel at a $120 AOV should not underwrite a business case on a luxury footwear deployment, or vice versa.

## How long does it take to launch an AI product finder, and what does the integration look like?

Most modern AI product finders ingest your catalog through a Shopify app, a feed URL, or an API and are technically live within a day. The longer pole is configuration: training the assistant on your brand voice, mapping your taxonomy, and aligning whoever owns merchandising on what "good results" actually look like. Budget a few hours of dev work for theme injection or App Embeds, plus 1–2 weeks of tuning before you trust the data enough to run an A/B test against your existing search.

On Shopify specifically, you have three install paths. App Embeds let a non-developer flip the widget on from the theme editor. For stores on Online Store 2.0, you can drop the finder into a section. If your theme is heavily customized or you're on a legacy Vintage theme, you're looking at a `theme.liquid` injection and a PR through your frontend repo. Catalog ingestion is almost always automatic through Shopify's Storefront API once the app is authorized, so you don't hand-maintain a feed.

Where timelines slip is on non-standard PDPs. If your product cards render variants, swatches, or inventory badges in a bespoke way, the default widget output won't match. That's exactly why Nobi built its Hooks API: UNTUCKit asked for developer-controlled display so results blended into their existing PDP grid rather than looking like a bolted-on widget. Every Nobi customer now gets those hooks.

One planning note worth taking seriously: don't declare a baseline on install day. Merchandisers need a week or two to see real queries, tweak synonyms, pin featured products, and correct obvious misses. Then run a clean split test against your current search for at least two weeks. Vendor-reported lift numbers, including ours, mean less than a result your own analytics team signs off on.

## Which AI product finder is the right fit for a $1M–$100M GMV brand?

There isn't a single right answer. There's the right answer for your funnel, and the choice should follow where the discovery friction actually lives.

If shoppers land on category pages and search bars and bounce because they can't find the right SKU, an integrated search-and-chat platform is the most direct fit. Nobi sits here, and the budgeting math is easier than most: $25/month base covers 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with overages at $0.01 per search and $0.10 per message. A brand running 50,000 monthly searches and a few thousand chat sessions is looking at a few hundred dollars a month, not a quote-only contract.

If your category is beauty, skincare, or CPG and shoppers genuinely want to be guided through a routine builder or a "find your shade" flow, Octane AI fits the journey better than a search bar ever will. If most browsers leave silently and you want something to engage them mid-session, Rep AI's proactive chat is built for that moment. If product questions land in your support inbox more than your search bar, Gorgias unifies that workflow more cleanly than bolting discovery on top. Smaller catalog, lighter conversation volume, no dedicated CX team? Tidio's Lyro is the lower-lift starting point.

One honest note for brands weighing Nobi against Algolia or Bloomreach: Nobi has less name recognition and a smaller integration ecosystem. The tradeoff is a meaningfully lower and more predictable price floor.
