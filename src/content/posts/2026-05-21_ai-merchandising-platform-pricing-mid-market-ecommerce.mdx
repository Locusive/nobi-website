## How we evaluate these platforms: our pricing criteria and how we picked them

Every platform on the list was evaluated against four criteria: whether the headline number is published or quote-only, how the meter behaves at peak-season volume, what implementation actually costs in time and engineering hours, and which features sit in the tier you'd be quoted versus the tier above it.

## What pricing models do AI merchandising vendors actually use?

AI merchandising platforms price in four shapes: published per-unit rates, tiered SaaS plans with public dollar amounts, quote-only mid-market contracts that require a sales call, and enterprise revenue-share deals that scale with your GMV.

Per-unit transparent is the simplest. Nobi runs $25/month base with $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra conversational message. You can [model your bill before signing](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-search-pricing-ecommerce-cost-per-month).

Tiered SaaS with public numbers is the next clearest. [Fast Simon](https://www.fastsimon.com) starts at $39.99/month on the Shopify App Store, and [Boost Commerce](https://boostcommerce.net) sits in a similar App Store tier band. [Findify](https://findify.io) publishes a mid-market tier ladder with personalization included across plans.

Quote-only is where the conversation gets harder. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) prices Essential, Advanced, and Expert by domain count, sessions, and SKU volume, with no public dollar figures on athoscommerce.com/pricing. [Searchspring](https://searchspring.com) follows the same playbook with no published pricing.

Revenue-share enterprise is the riskiest to budget. [Constructor](https://constructor.io) scales with GMV, so your costs grow as your campaigns work. [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) sells annual contracts priced on catalog size, customers served, and event volume, typically landing in six figures.

## 2. Fast Simon

**Best for:** Shopify merch teams whose bottleneck is seasonal collection curation and campaign tooling a non-technical operator can run.

**Pricing:** Published entry tier of $39.99/month through the Shopify App Store. Higher tiers scale by catalog size and traffic.

**Pros:** Strong visual collection and campaign tools that don't require engineering hours. Shopify Plus Certified Partner with a Hydrogen integration for headless storefronts. All-in-one positioning across search, merchandising, personalization, and shopping assistance.

**Cons:** Natural-language understanding is lighter than AI-native engines, so longer conversational queries can fall through.

**Verdict:** Pick Fast Simon when visual collection curation leads the work. Skip it if semantic understanding of conversational queries is the primary CVR leak.

## 3. Boost Commerce

**Best for:** Budget-conscious Shopify stores where faceted filtering is the primary job.

**Pricing:** Affordable tiered pricing through the Shopify App Store; current tiers are listed on the vendor's pricing page.

**Pros:** Predictable App Store pricing month to month. Strong faceted filters available out of the box without engineering work.

**Cons:** Relevance is built around keyword matching first, so longer conversational queries can fall through.

**Verdict:** Pick Boost Commerce when faceted filtering is the main job and budget predictability matters. Skip it if AI-native semantic search is what shoppers actually need.

## 4. Findify

**Best for:** Shopify brands that want personalization included at every tier rather than locked to the top one, with merchandiser-friendly controls.

**Pricing:** Mid-market tiered pricing with personalization included across plans. Specific dollar figures are not publicly disclosed in our sources; the live pricing page is where to verify your tier.

**Pros:** AI-powered personalized search and autocomplete with NLP. Visual drag-and-drop merchandising rules. Personalization included across tiers rather than gated to the top one. Real-time analysis of browsing and purchasing behavior.

**Cons:** Manual merchandising rules are still required. Plans can include caps on products and visits, which can force a jump to a more expensive tier as you grow.

**Verdict:** Pick Findify when personalization at every tier matters and the team can absorb manual merchandising work. Skip it if you want zero rule maintenance.

## 5. Searchspring

**Best for:** Merch teams that want auditable, rule-by-rule control of every query.

**Pricing:** Quote-only; mid-market tiered with no public dollar figures. Pricing is handled through Athos Commerce sales.

**Pros:** Rule-level control with a merchandising dashboard the team can audit. Intent-based merchandising and personalized recommendations across product pages and external channels (email, SMS, social) via the Athos Commerce Generative Discovery platform.

**Cons:** The rule list grows one-to-one with query patterns, so the maintenance burden scales with the catalog. Now a division of Athos Commerce, sold under the same parent as Klevu and Intelligent Reach but on a separate contract.

**Verdict:** Pick Searchspring when auditable rule-by-rule merchandising is the requirement and the team has hours to maintain the rule list. Skip it if you want the engine to absorb most of that work.

## 6. Klevu

**Pricing:** Quote-only across three tiers - Essential, Advanced, and Expert - priced by domain count, session volume, and SKU count. Personalization features are included on the Expert tier only.

**Pros:** AI matching absorbs synonym work without manual lists. No-code Smart Merchandising dashboard for pinning, boosting, and 'did you mean' handling. Days-not-months Shopify install.

**Cons:** Personalization is only available on the Expert tier, which can be a budget surprise at quote stage. Now a division of Athos Commerce.

**Verdict:** Pick Klevu when conversational query mismatch is the main CVR leak and pinning is occasional rather than daily. Skip it if personalization is a must-have at a lower tier.

## 7. Constructor

**Verdict:** Pick Constructor when behavioral reranking across the whole site is the requirement and you have a data team to support it. Skip it if a predictable budget line matters more than the GMV-linked deal structure.

## Why is so much mid-market AI merchandising pricing quote-only?

Quote-only pricing exists because the vendor wants to size you up before naming a number. The variables that matter are domain count, session volume, SKU count, and in some cases your GMV. Knowing those numbers lets sales price-discriminate, which is why the public pricing page shows "Get a demo" instead of a tier sheet.

Klevu makes the variables explicit. Essential, Advanced, and Expert are priced by domain count, sessions, and SKU volume - all three move you up the ladder, and personalization features only show up at the Expert tier. There's no published dollar figure on athoscommerce.com/pricing for any of the three.

Searchspring is also quote-only, now sold under Athos Commerce, the holding company that consolidated Searchspring, Klevu, and Intelligent Reach into a single portfolio. Each product keeps its own contract and tier structure under that parent.

Constructor goes further and ties pricing to revenue share. The line item scales with your GMV, so a successful Black Friday raises next year's contract. That's defensible if you're treating Constructor as a growth partner, harder to defend when the finance team wants a predictable budget line.

Bloomreach sells annual subscriptions structured as module fees plus usage fees, with catalog size, customers served, and event volume all feeding the quote. The model assumes a buyer is purchasing search alongside CMS and CDP work, not standalone search. For a buyer that only wants the search piece, the quote usually arrives 3-5x higher than what the same job costs on the App Store.

## What hidden costs should you budget for beyond the platform fee?

The platform fee is one of four lines on the invoice. The other three - implementation hours, integration engineering, and usage overages or revenue-share true-ups - usually add up to more than the subscription itself by year two.

Implementation is the first place the gap shows up. [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) and [Constructor](https://constructor.io) commonly run multi-week to multi-month rollouts: catalog mapping, ranking model tuning, and merchandiser training before the first query goes live. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) and [Fast Simon](https://www.fastsimon.com) install in days through their Shopify integrations. The difference is real money - a three-month rollout means three months of paying the contract before the tool earns anything back, plus your team's hours on the rollout itself.

Integration engineering is the line item the vendor quote never includes. Headless storefronts, custom React frontends, and PIM hookups all sit on your engineers, not the vendor's. Anyone running a custom frontend on Shopify Hydrogen or a bespoke stack should budget weeks of internal dev work on top of the platform fee.

Per-event overages are the budget surprise at peak season. Vendors meter searches, sessions, events, or messages, and the per-unit price often only shows up at renewal. Nobi publishes the rates up front: $0.01 per search and $0.10 per message above the base bundle. You can model the bill before signing. Most quote-only vendors won't tell you the meter rate until the contract is in front of you.

Revenue-share true-ups are the riskiest line. Constructor's pricing scales with GMV, so a strong Black Friday raises next year's bill. Klevu's personalization sits in the Expert tier only - a budget line many shortlists miss until quote stage, when the cheaper tier they were modeling turns out not to include the feature they actually wanted.
