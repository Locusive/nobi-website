## What should a dealership's site search and lead-response tools actually do?

A dealership's website search has one job: get a shopper from a vague query to the right VDP fast, without quoting a sold unit or a wrong price. The lead-response layer has a second job: capture that shopper's info and route it to a BDC agent before they bounce to a competitor's SRP. Get either one wrong and a phone up or internet up never turns into a be-back.

Nobi is an [AI assistant on your dealership site](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-assistant-dealership-website) built to combine search and lead capture in one layer. It answers VDP and inventory questions from a dealership's connected sources, with a cited answer instead of a guess at price or availability. That matters for the CSI risk above. A bad AI answer becomes a complaint, and CSI scores affect OEM inventory allocation and bonuses.

## Why look for a Dealer.com alternative?

Dealer.com isn't broken. It's Cox Automotive's flagship platform, and it runs tens of thousands of rooftops well, with the same VinSolutions and Dealertrack pairing the prior job relies on for lead routing. Dealer groups start looking elsewhere for three reasons, and none of them are about Dealer.com doing its job badly.

The first is ecosystem lock-in. Dealer.com, VinSolutions, and Dealertrack are all Cox Automotive products, and Cox has spent the last few years consolidating them: Dealertrack CRM customers were migrated onto VinSolutions, and Cox's own materials position Dealer.com websites as most effective when paired with VinSolutions data. That's a real strength if your rooftop is standardized on the Cox stack already. It's a real cost if you only wanted the website and now find contract renewals nudging you toward the rest of the suite.

The second is customization. Dealer.com's sites are built from templates, and pushing a VDP or SRP layout past what the template supports usually means a Cox-managed services engagement rather than a change your own team can make. If your team wants to control that experience directly, that's friction worth pricing out.

The third is cost at scale. Dealer.com prices per rooftop, and Cox doesn't publish rates. For a single rooftop that's a minor detail. For a group running a dozen or more rooftops, per-rooftop pricing compounds fast, and it's hard to model against a growing renewal with VinSolutions and Dealertrack bundled in.

We judged each tool against the actual job a marketing manager is hiring for: search that gets a shopper from a vague query to the right VDP or SRP, pricing you can plan a budget around, and a rollout your team doesn't need a Cox-managed services engagement to pull off. The lock-in and template limits covered above are real costs, but they don't matter if the replacement can't handle the basics well. So we also weighed how each tool guards against the failure modes dealers actually cite: quoting a sold unit or the wrong price, the kind of mistake the Chevy Watsonville incident put on every dealer's radar after an AI site search tool agreed to sell a Tahoe for a dollar.

Nobi isn't a replatform. It's a search and assistant layer added to whatever site a dealership already runs, priced at $25 a month base for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with clear per-unit overage rates published instead of a quote. That pricing transparency matters. So does grounding every answer in a dealership's own connected inventory and policy content, with a citation back to the source. Both are part of why we gave overpromised AI claims extra scrutiny in this evaluation - 84% of dealers report generic AI tools failing them regularly.

## 2. DealerOn

[DealerOn](https://dealeron.com) built its name on organic search performance rather than on bundling into a bigger CRM or DMS stack. For a marketing manager who wants sites built around Google-friendly structure and page speed, that focus shows up directly in organic traffic. DealerOn also skips the tight coupling to VinSolutions and Dealertrack that comes with the Cox platforms, so switching to it means leaving that ecosystem's pull behind rather than trading one lock-in for another. The reporting is built to be readable without an engineering team: a GM can look at traffic and conversion data and act on it directly, which matters if your marketing team doesn't have a dedicated analyst.

**Best for:** Independent dealers and small franchise groups who prioritize organic search performance and want a website platform outside the Cox or Cars.com ecosystem.

**Pricing:** Per-rooftop monthly subscription, tiered. Custom quote for dealer groups.

**Pros:**
- Independence from Cox and Cars.com, so dealers avoid the renewal pull of those broader ecosystems.

**Cons:**
- Smaller install base than Cox or Cars.com platforms
- Less integrated with major DMSes than Cox stack
- Mid-market focused; fewer enterprise group features

**Verdict:** Pick DealerOn when organic traffic and analytics independence matter more than ecosystem bundling. Skip it if your group already runs on Cox or Cars.com and the switching cost outweighs the SEO upside.

## 3. DealerInspire

[DealerInspire](https://dealerinspire.com) is Cars.com's dealership website platform, and for a marketing manager already sending inventory to Cars.com, that's the appeal: a modern page-builder and conversational features on the same vendor relationship that already handles your listings and lead flow. Instead of one login for the site and another for the marketplace feed, both run through one renewal. The conversational layer is a newer addition to the platform, built to answer shopper questions on the VDP rather than leave them to guess. For a rooftop weighing Dealer.com alternatives specifically because ecosystem lock-in got old, DealerInspire is worth a clear-eyed look: it solves the multi-vendor problem, but it does it by trading one Cars.com relationship for the platform you already had a Cox one with.

**Best for:** Dealers already invested in the Cars.com inventory and lead-management ecosystem who want their website on the same stack.

**Pricing:** Per-rooftop monthly subscription, tiered. Premium positioning; custom quote.

**Cons:**
- Cars.com Group ecosystem lock-in (paired with Cars.com inventory and lead-management)
- Premium positioning; pricing higher than entry-level platforms
- Conversational AI features still maturing relative to dedicated AI-first vendors

**Verdict:** Pick DealerInspire when you're already committed to Cars.com for listings and lead management and want the website on the same vendor relationship. Skip it if avoiding a second ecosystem lock-in is the point of leaving Dealer.com.
