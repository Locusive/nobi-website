## What is hybrid search and why are ecommerce teams adopting it?

Hybrid search runs keyword matching and vector matching together, then re-ranks the combined results. Keyword retrieval catches exact SKUs, brand names, and short tokens. Vector retrieval catches paraphrased, conversational queries - "wide-leg cropped trouser for transitional weather" - that don't share words with the product title. Pure keyword misses the long ones. Pure vector drifts off the exact matches. Hybrid handles both on the same call.

Ecommerce teams are adopting it because the query distribution on a real storefront has both ends. Shoppers type SKU codes and brand names. They also type sentences. A search engine that nails one half and fumbles the other leaves money on the table.

Most modern platforms now ship hybrid by default or as a tier upgrade. Nobi, [Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) (via NeuralSearch), [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com), [Constructor](https://constructor.io), and [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) all offer some version of it. The real choice comes down to two things: how much engineering work you want to own, and which pricing model - per-search, per-month, revenue-share, enterprise contract - actually maps to your volume.

## How did we evaluate these hybrid search tools?

We weighed five things a head of ecommerce actually has to defend in a vendor pick: relevance on long, conversational queries; the engineering and merchandising work to keep that relevance tuned; published pricing that holds up at your real search volume; implementation time from contract to live shopper traffic; and measured CVR or revenue-per-searcher lift when the vendor or a customer has shared it.

Relevance on long queries is the whole point of hybrid. Pure keyword catches the SKU; pure vector catches the sentence. We looked for evidence each platform actually handles both - vendor benchmarks where they exist, customer A/B test numbers where they don't. Nobi has two we could cite directly: [UNTUCKit](/customers/untuckit) saw a 17.1% CVR lift over its prior search tool in a two-month split, and [Kilte](/customers/kilte) saw 21.7% over Shopify's default. Algolia, Klevu, Constructor, and Bloomreach all publish customer stories of their own; we weighed those against the rigor of the test and how recently it ran.

Merchandising overhead matters because it predicts what your team does every Tuesday. Some platforms expect a merchandiser to pin and boost weekly. Others rank from the catalog and shopper behavior by default and only need a rule when you want to override something specific. We marked which is which.

Pricing got a hard test. If a vendor wouldn't publish a starter rate, we said so plainly and quoted only what we could verify. Revenue-share counts as pricing too, but you deserve to know the model before you sign anything.

Implementation time is measured contract-to-traffic, not contract-to-kickoff-call. Hours, days, weeks, or months - we named the realistic range.

## 2. Algolia

[Algolia](https://algolia.com) is a search API built for engineering teams. Rules, ranking, synonyms, and merchandising are all configured in code, and response times stay under 50ms even at catalog scale. NeuralSearch adds [semantic matching](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-semantic-search-ecommerce) on top of keyword relevance on higher tiers, which is what catches the descriptive queries keyword-only ranking drops.

**Best for:** Engineering teams that want full API control over hybrid ranking and have the developer hours to keep relevance tuned as the catalog evolves.

**Pricing:** Usage-based on search requests and records indexed, with NeuralSearch gated to higher tiers. The enterprise tier is opaque, and mid-market deployments often land at $2K+/month before custom relevance engineering.

**Pros:**
- Sub-50ms response times and fast indexing at catalog scale
- Large ecosystem of libraries, InstantSearch widgets, and integrations across every major frontend stack
- NeuralSearch adds semantic matching on top of keyword relevance for queries that don't match product titles word-for-word
- Granular API-level control over indexing, ranking, and how results render on the storefront

**Cons:**
- Relevance work scales with engineering hours, not contract size, so non-technical merchandisers can't drive ranking changes alone
- Usage-based pricing produces surprise bills during traffic spikes, exactly when CVR pressure is highest
- NeuralSearch is gated to higher tiers, so the cheapest Algolia setup doesn't include the semantic layer most ecommerce catalogs need

**Verdict:** Pick Algolia when you have a dedicated search engineering team and want full API control over hybrid ranking; skip it if non-technical teams need to drive relevance work without writing code.

## 3. Klevu

[Klevu](https://klevu.com) layers AI matching on top of keyword retrieval and ships it as a Shopify-native install. The AI reads your catalog and figures out what a shopper actually meant, so a long conversational query like "wide-leg cropped trouser" still finds the right product even when your title says "cropped wide pant." A "did you mean" layer handles the typos and misspellings shoppers reliably produce on product names, colors, and brands. When a query has no good match, merchandisers route it to a category page from the Smart Merchandising dashboard instead of filing an engineering ticket.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose main hybrid-search miss is long, conversational queries a basic keyword engine can't handle.

**Pricing:** Tiered, quoted by store size. Klevu does not publish a starter rate; pricing scales with store size and search volume.

**Pros:**
- AI matching catches long, conversational queries before they resolve to empty results
- "Did you mean" suggestions handle most typos and misspellings
- Smart Merchandising dashboard sets up category-page fallbacks without an engineering ticket
- Packaged Shopify install goes live in days rather than months

**Cons:**
- Personalization is a separately licensed add-on, not included with the base search tier
- AI matching quality is bounded by your catalog data, so sparse product descriptions weaken the layer that's supposed to catch the misses
- Now sold under Athos Commerce alongside Searchspring and Intelligent Reach

**Verdict:** Pick Klevu if your hybrid-search problem is mostly a wording mismatch on Shopify and you want a packaged install. Skip it if you also need personalization included rather than bought as a separate license. Teams shopping the broader category should also weigh [Klevu alternatives for ecommerce site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/klevu-alternatives-ecommerce).

## 4. Constructor

[Constructor](https://constructor.io) blends semantic search with results that reorder in real time as a shopper clicks, views, or adds items during a visit. The same model runs across search, browse, category pages, and recommendations, not just the search results page.

**Best for:** Enterprise retailers with an internal data team where merchandising has to move across category, collection, browse, and search together.

**Pricing:** Revenue-share with no published list price. Costs scale with GMV.

**Pros:**
- Session-signal personalization reorders results in real time, so the CVR lever moves on every individual visit, not just on average ranking quality
- Merchandising covers the full site - category, collection, browse, recommendations - not just the search results page
- A/B testing infrastructure and behavioral analytics are built in, so you can measure which change is actually working
- Quizzes, browse, and recommendations run on one platform, so signals from every channel feed the shared ranking model

**Cons:**
- Out of reach for most SMB and mid-market brands on budget alone
- Implementation runs weeks to months and requires internal data science or analytics resources to get full value
- Revenue-share pricing can surprise as GMV grows - the most common complaint in post-signing reviews

**Verdict:** Pick Constructor when behavioral personalization across the full site is the job and you have a data team and budget to match; skip it if you want transparent per-unit pricing or just the search bar without a full merchandising platform attached.

## 5. Bloomreach

[Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) brings search, merchandising, content, and customer data into one commerce experience platform. The Discovery module handles search and product recommendations, and everything plugs into unified customer profiles that drive personalization across the site.

**Best for:** Omnichannel retailers consolidating search, CMS, and CDP into one platform contract who have the appetite for a multi-quarter rollout.

**Pricing:** Enterprise-only pricing. Six-figure annual contracts are common, with multi-month implementation standard.

**Pros:**
- Search, content, marketing, and customer data in one place
- Strong semantic search with product-specific AI alongside keyword retrieval
- Personalization driven by unified customer profiles across the entire experience layer
- Mature analytics and reporting tooling for enterprise governance

**Cons:**
- Enterprise pricing and sales process out of reach for most brands
- Multi-quarter implementation requirements
- Overkill if hybrid search is the standalone problem

**Verdict:** Pick Bloomreach when you're ready to consolidate your entire commerce stack into one contract; skip it if hybrid search is a standalone problem and a multi-quarter rollout is off the table.
