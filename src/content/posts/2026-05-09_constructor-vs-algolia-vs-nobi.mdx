# Constructor vs. Algolia vs. Nobi: Which AI Search Is Right for Your Store?

This is not a review where we pretend to be neutral and then recommend ourselves. Nobi is one of the three products in this comparison, and we built it. That bias exists, and you should factor it in.

What we can do is be honest about what each tool does well and where it falls short — including Nobi. Because the real answer to "which is best" is boring but true: it depends on your team, your budget, and what you need search to do.

![Three approaches to AI search](/media/posts/constructor-vs-algolia-vs-nobi/01-hero.svg)

## The Three Philosophies

These three tools represent fundamentally different approaches to the same problem: helping shoppers find products.

**Constructor** believes search should optimize for revenue. Its ML models learn which products convert and rank them accordingly. Search is a revenue lever, not a utility.

**Algolia** believes search should be a powerful, flexible API. Give developers the tools to build anything, and they will build the right experience for their store.

**Nobi** believes search should be a conversation. Instead of forcing shoppers to formulate the perfect query, let them describe what they want and guide them there through dialogue.

None of these philosophies is wrong. But each one leads to very different products, and the right choice depends on which philosophy matches your reality.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Search Technology

**Constructor** uses machine learning trained on your store's behavioral data. It learns what converts and ranks products to maximize revenue. The more data you feed it, the better it gets. This requires significant traffic volume to work well.

**Algolia** combines keyword matching with NeuralSearch for semantic understanding. Its core strength is speed — queries return results in milliseconds. The relevance tuning is manual and requires developer configuration.

**Nobi** uses conversational AI to interpret natural language queries. Instead of returning a grid of results for a single query, it can ask clarifying questions, understand context, and narrow down options through dialogue. The experience feels more like talking to a knowledgeable sales associate than using a search engine.

**Verdict:** Constructor wins on revenue optimization for high-traffic stores. Algolia wins on raw speed and API flexibility. Nobi wins on understanding complex, natural language queries without manual tuning.

### Setup and Implementation

**Constructor** implementation takes weeks to months. It requires technical integration, data pipeline configuration, and ongoing optimization. Expect to involve your engineering and data teams.

**Algolia** can be set up in days if you have developers. Without developers, it can take weeks or stall entirely. The [InstantSearch libraries](https://www.algolia.com/doc/guides/building-search-ui/what-is-instantsearch/js/) provide building blocks, but assembly requires code.

**Nobi** sets up in minutes. Connect your product catalog, customize the appearance, and go live. No engineering ticket required.

**Verdict:** Nobi wins on speed to value. Algolia is reasonable if you have developers. Constructor requires the most investment to get running.

### Pricing

**Constructor** is enterprise-only. Expect $50K+ per year with custom contracts and multi-year commitments.

**Algolia** uses [usage-based pricing](https://www.algolia.com/pricing/). Published plans exist for smaller volumes, but costs scale with search requests and records. Traffic spikes can produce unexpected bills.

**Nobi** offers transparent, accessible pricing designed for ecommerce teams that do not have enterprise budgets. No usage-based surprises.

**Verdict:** Nobi is the most accessible. Algolia is moderate but unpredictable. Constructor is for budgets that require executive approval.

### Personalization

**Constructor** has the deepest personalization. Its ML models learn individual shopper preferences and adjust rankings in real time. This is its core competitive advantage.

**Algolia** offers personalization through its Personalization add-on. It works, but it requires developer configuration and is less sophisticated than Constructor's approach.

**Nobi** personalizes through conversation context. The AI remembers what the shopper has said in the current session and uses that context to refine recommendations. It does not yet have the cross-session behavioral modeling that Constructor offers.

**Verdict:** Constructor leads here. Nobi's conversational context is a different kind of personalization — more immediate but less historical. Algolia requires manual setup.

### Merchandising

**Constructor** provides advanced merchandising tools, including boost/bury rules, campaign management, and visual merchandising capabilities.

**Algolia** offers Rules and query-time merchandising, but configuration is primarily through the API or a dashboard that still requires technical understanding.

**Nobi** has basic merchandising capabilities that are growing. It is not yet the tool for teams that need fine-grained control over product ranking rules.

**Verdict:** Constructor wins for enterprise merchandising teams. Algolia is powerful but developer-dependent. Nobi is catching up.

### Analytics

All three provide search analytics, but the depth varies.

**Constructor** offers revenue-attributed analytics that tie search directly to conversion and revenue. This is particularly valuable for proving ROI to executive stakeholders.

**Algolia** provides solid search analytics including click-through rates, conversion tracking, and query analysis. The dashboard is usable by non-technical teams.

**Nobi** provides analytics on search queries, conversational patterns, and conversion data. The focus is on surfacing what shoppers want in their own words.

**Verdict:** Constructor wins on revenue attribution. Algolia and Nobi both provide useful analytics with different focus areas.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Constructor if:

- Your annual GMV exceeds $50M
- You have a data team that can work with ML models
- Revenue optimization is your primary search KPI
- You can commit $50K+ annually and wait weeks for implementation
- You are on a custom or enterprise commerce platform (not Shopify)

### Choose Algolia if:

- You have a strong engineering team that wants API-level control
- Speed and flexibility matter more than out-of-the-box functionality
- You are building a custom or headless commerce experience
- You can manage usage-based pricing fluctuations
- Your developers actively want to build and maintain search infrastructure

### Choose Nobi if:

- Your ecommerce team does not have dedicated developers
- You want conversational AI that understands natural language
- You need to improve search this week, not this quarter
- Predictable pricing matters to your budget planning
- You believe product discovery should feel like a conversation, not a keyword guessing game
