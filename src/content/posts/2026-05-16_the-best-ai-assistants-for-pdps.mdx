## What is a PDP assistant and what should it actually do on a product page?

A [PDP assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce) is an AI chat layer that sits on a product detail page and answers the specific questions a shopper has while they're looking at one product - fit, materials, dimensions, compatibility, shipping, returns - grounded in your catalog and policies rather than a generic site-wide script. A good one resolves the fit question on the page (cutting the apparel return rate that runs as high as 70%), shows where every answer came from with citation pills, and exposes the conversation as crawlable FAQ content so the same question earns SEO and AEO impressions later.

The bar is higher than "we put chat on the PDP." The assistant has to be grounded in your live catalog and policy pages, with a refresh measured in hours rather than weeks, so yesterday's price or policy change is in today's answer. Every answer needs an inline citation so a shopper can verify it against the official content - that's the difference between a credible answer and a confident lie on inventory or price. Suggestion prompts scoped to the page give visitors who don't know what to type a starting point. And the whole exchange should reduce WISMO and pre-sale tickets, not shift them to a different inbox.

## How did we evaluate these PDP assistants?

We looked at five AI assistants - Nobi, Intercom, Re:amaze, Zendesk, and Ada - and asked the same five questions of each: what does it actually know about the product the shopper is looking at, how fresh is that knowledge, can a shopper verify an answer without leaving the page, what does it cost per PDP conversation, and how long until it's live.

PDP-specific behavior was the first filter. Does the assistant know which product the shopper is on, and does it surface prompts tied to that product, or does the same generic widget render on every page?

Grounding and refresh was the second. The assistant should pull from your live catalog and policy pages, and a price or stock change should land in answers in hours, not at the end of a weekly sync. A stale catalog is where confident lies on inventory come from. Nobi refreshes connected sources twice a day; the helpdesks usually rely on knowledge-base articles a content team updates on its own schedule.

Citations were the third. An inline source pill, with the document name and excerpt visible, is how a shopper checks a fit or returns claim without leaving the page.

Pricing was the fourth, and we compared the per-PDP conversation cost across flat usage plans (Nobi's $25 base with $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra message), per-ticket AI charges, and per-seat helpdesk subscriptions. Implementation time was the fifth: hours for a site-side install versus quarters for an enterprise rollout.

## 2. Intercom

[Intercom](https://intercom.com)'s Fin AI Agent runs inside the Intercom Messenger and can be dropped onto a PDP like any other Intercom widget. It answers from your Help Center articles plus external sources you connect - Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, Zendesk, public URLs (up to 10 sources at 3,000 pages each), PDFs, and DOCX. For a head of ecommerce already running Intercom as the helpdesk, the appeal is that a PDP conversation lives in the same platform as your tickets, routing rules, and reporting. The catch on a product page: external sources only refresh once a week, so a price change in Notion or a policy update on a public page can sit stale in PDP answers for up to six days.

**Best for:** Ecommerce teams already running Intercom as their helpdesk who want PDP Q&A to live inside the same platform as their tickets and reporting.

**Pricing:** $29/seat/month annual ($39 monthly) on Essential, up to $132 annual / $139 monthly on Expert, plus $0.99 per Fin resolution. The underlying Intercom subscription is required.

**Pros:**
- Fin, your human agents, tickets, and reporting all live in the same platform, so a PDP conversation that escalates lands in the same inbox the team already works out of.
- Wide knowledge ingestion: native Help Center and Snippets plus public URLs, PDFs, DOCX, and direct syncs from Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, and Zendesk.
- Mature reporting and a deep app marketplace built up over a decade of SaaS adoption, useful when you want PDP conversation metrics in the same dashboards as the rest of support.

**Cons:**
- External sources only refresh once a week, so a price change in Notion or a policy update on a public page can sit stale in PDP answers for up to six days.
- Fin runs inside Intercom's Messenger, so using it on the PDP means deploying and paying for the full Intercom platform on top of the per-Fin-resolution charge.

**Verdict:** Pick Intercom Fin when Intercom is already your helpdesk and you're fine with a weekly external refresh; skip it if your prices and policies change more than once a week, or if you don't want to buy the full Intercom stack just to get a PDP assistant.

## 3. Re:amaze

[Re:amaze](https://reamaze.com) is an ecommerce-native customer support platform that runs a chat widget on the product page alongside email, social, and SMS inboxes. The widget can be deployed on PDPs as part of the broader chat install, and AI-assisted reply suggestions (branded Re:amaze Brain) help agents respond faster to the shopper questions that come through. The AI side draws from help center articles and macros wired to Shopify order data, and it leans toward drafting replies for an agent rather than answering on its own. For a head of ecommerce who already needs one platform handling tickets across email, chat, and social, putting that same platform on the PDP is the path of least resistance, with the product-page question landing in the same inbox as the rest of support.

**Best for:** DTC and mid-market ecommerce teams who want one ecommerce-aware support platform covering email, chat, social, and PDP questions, even if the PDP experience is more agent-assisted than autonomous.

**Pricing:** From $29/agent/month on Basic, $49 on Pro, and $69 on Plus, with AI features (Re:amaze Brain, AI compose) layered on top of those plans.

**Pros:**
- Ecommerce-native context on the PDP conversation: shopper history, order data, and product information sit next to the chat, which matters when the question on the product page is really "where's my order" or "can I exchange this one."
- Unified inbox covering email, chat, social, SMS, and the PDP widget, so a question that starts on the product page can continue on email without the team swapping tools.
- Quicker to install on a Shopify-led stack than enterprise-tier helpdesks, and the chat widget can be live on PDPs in a day.

**Cons:**
- The PDP experience leans on agent reply suggestions and curated FAQ rather than an LLM-grounded assistant answering shoppers directly on its own.
- The knowledge base is manually curated, so PDP answers go stale if the team doesn't keep the help docs in sync with catalog and policy changes.

**Verdict:** Pick Re:amaze when you want one ecommerce-aware platform handling email, chat, social, SMS, and PDP support in the same inbox; skip it if you need an autonomous LLM-grounded PDP assistant rather than agent-assisted replies.

## 4. Zendesk

[Zendesk](https://zendesk.com) is the enterprise support platform, and its AI Agents and Copilot features layer onto the Zendesk Suite. You can put a PDP assistant on a product page by embedding Zendesk's web widget, but the customer-facing parts have to be built rather than turned on. The AI runs inside the same tool the support team already uses, which is the appeal: a PDP conversation that escalates lands in the same inbox, with the same macros, triggers, SLAs, and routing the team already runs.

**Best for:** Mid-market and enterprise ecommerce teams whose system of record for tickets is already Zendesk and who need PDP Q&A coupled to that workflow rather than as a separate widget.

**Pricing:** Suite + Copilot Professional $155/agent/month annual; Suite + Copilot Enterprise $209/agent/month annual; standalone Copilot add-on $50/agent/month; autonomous AI Agents quote-only.

**Pros:**
- Tight integration with Zendesk tickets, macros, triggers, SLAs, and routing, so a PDP conversation that escalates flows straight into the workflow the team already runs.
- Mature reporting and SLA management that established support teams trust, useful when you want PDP-level metrics in the same dashboards as the rest of support.
- Wide app marketplace covering CRM, commerce, and internal-tool integrations, which matters when the PDP assistant has to talk to inventory or order systems.

**Cons:**
- Not built to answer questions on the PDP out of the box - the on-page experience has to be built and tuned, not just turned on, and a full rollout is measured in months rather than days.
- Suite plus AI licensing at $155-$209 per agent is a real budget line before the AI pays back, and the standalone Copilot add-on plus quote-only AI Agents stack on top.

**Verdict:** Pick Zendesk when Zendesk is already your system of record and the PDP assistant has to live inside that workflow; skip it if your real problem is answering product questions on the PDP and you don't want to build the customer-facing layer.

## 5. Ada

[Ada](https://ada.cx) runs one AI agent across web chat, in-app, email, SMS, and messaging from a single setup, and the PDP chat on a product page is just one of those channels. For a head of ecommerce whose pre-sale product questions arrive across many places at once, that breadth is the appeal. The same setup, integrations, and reasoning logic that answer on email or SMS also answer on the product page, so a shopper asking about sizing in one channel and shipping in another gets a consistent reply. Ada is built for teams handling 300,000+ customer service conversations a year - roughly 25,000 a month - and the PDP is one channel inside that wider rollout, not a quick standalone install.

**Best for:** Enterprise ecommerce teams whose PDP traffic is one channel inside a wider cross-channel rollout and who can absorb a multi-quarter implementation.

**Pricing:** Quote-only. Ada explicitly fits companies running 300,000+ annual customer service conversations, and you'll go through a sales call before you know what it costs.

**Pros:**
- One AI agent runs across PDP web chat, in-app, email, SMS, and messaging from a single setup, so the answer on the product page matches the answer everywhere else.
- Focused on resolving conversations end-to-end rather than helping agents reply faster, with API tooling for real-time catalog and order data retrieval.
- Doesn't sit on top of an existing helpdesk - Ada can be the primary AI layer on the PDP rather than an add-on to a ticketing platform.

**Cons:**
- Getting it running on the PDP is a multi-quarter project, not a days-to-live install - data integration, setup and configuration, and rollout all take real time.
- Quote-only pricing with no public numbers means you go through procurement before you know what running the PDP assistant costs, and it's less suited to teams whose PDP question volume is small or sits on a single channel.

**Verdict:** Pick Ada when PDP Q&A is one channel inside a wider 300K+ annual conversation rollout and you have the team and budget for an enterprise implementation; skip it if you need a PDP assistant live this quarter on one channel.
