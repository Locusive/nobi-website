## How do you let service customers book appointments directly from your dealership website?

Your service drive closes at 6pm. Most dealership websites do too - not literally, but functionally, because they can accept a form submission and nothing else. A customer who shows up at 8pm asking what's included in their 30k service, or whether their vehicle qualifies for an open recall, hits a wall and moves on. That's not a website traffic problem. It's repair orders going to the quick-lube down the street every week. Here is how to add a conversational assistant that answers those pre-booking questions and routes appointment requests directly to your scheduler.

## Why do service customers book at the quick-lube instead of your dealership's website?

Most dealership websites accept a service form submission but cannot answer a single follow-up question - what is included in a 30k-mile service, whether a specific recall applies to the customer's vehicle, or how long the wait typically runs. When a customer hits that wall at 8pm, they book with a shop that does answer online. That is a fixed-ops revenue leak that has nothing to do with the quality of your service department.

Service forms go unanswered after the drive closes, and customers do not leave voicemails. They find a competitor who responds.

The problem compounds in two specific ways. First, recall notifications and OEM service campaigns drive some of your highest-intent traffic - customers who already know they need an appointment. When those visitors land on a generic "contact us" form, that intent disappears. Second, fixed ops is the most predictable margin center in a rooftop. Losing a handful of ROs a week to a quick-lube down the street is not a rounding error - it adds up across the model year in ways that show up on the service absorption number.

The staffing is not the issue. Your technicians are there. Your service advisors are good. The website just cannot hold a conversation after hours or answer the two or three questions that stand between a customer and booking - and so they book somewhere else.

## What is Nobi, and how does it handle service appointment requests on a dealership website?

Those two or three questions standing between a service customer and a booking - what's covered in the 60k service, whether a recall applies to their specific VIN - are answerable around the clock when your website has the right assistant. Nobi is a conversational website assistant that answers questions from your own connected content - service menus, recall bulletins, warranty policy docs, FAQ pages. A Custom Actions layer connects Nobi to external systems when a customer is ready to book. On the service side, the same assistant that helps a shopper find a vehicle can answer "what is included in an oil change here?" It then routes the appointment request directly to your scheduler, with no phone tree and no form sitting unread until morning.

The knowledge base builds from content you already have. Connect your service menu pages, upload OEM bulletins as PDFs, point it at your FAQ routes - Nobi reads what's there and makes it answerable without any manual re-entry.

Every answer includes a citation. A customer asking whether a recall applies to their vehicle sees the source document and the exact excerpt the answer came from. They can verify the coverage before committing to an appointment, which matters when the question is warranty eligibility or what a service campaign actually includes.

For high-stakes questions like recall scope or CPO warranty coverage, query overrides return your approved text exactly as written.

Custom Actions connect Nobi to your existing service scheduler. When a customer is ready to book, the request lands in the system your service advisors already use. Your fixed-ops workflow does not change.

On service-related pages, suggestion pills appear as tappable prompts - "What is included in a 60k service?", "How long does a tire rotation take?" - giving customers a starting point before they have to type anything. A visitor who doesn't know what to ask still has a reason to stay on your site instead of driving to the quick-lube down the street.

## How does Nobi answer common service questions, oil change packages, recall eligibility, and what is covered under warranty?

Nobi builds its answers from content you connect: service menu URLs covering oil change packages and other fixed-ops items, FAQ routes, manufacturer recall bulletins uploaded as PDFs, and warranty policy documents. When a customer asks whether their 2021 Tacoma qualifies for an airbag recall, the assistant pulls from the connected recall document and returns a cited answer with the source excerpt - not "call us for details."

That citation matters. Every answer includes the source document name, the date, and the exact passage the answer came from, displayed as an inline pill the customer can hover to verify. A customer checking their CPO warranty coverage before committing to a service appointment can read the actual policy excerpt right there in the chat. There is no "trust us" involved - the source is visible.

Two questions in fixed ops carry real liability if the answer is wrong: warranty scope and recall eligibility. For those, query overrides let you lock in exact approved language. When a customer asks what the powertrain warranty covers, Nobi returns your word-for-word response every time. The AI does not paraphrase, summarize, or interpret - the override fires the exact text, full stop.

Keeping that knowledge current is automatic. Connected sources refresh twice a day, so a revised service menu price or a new OEM bulletin lands in customer answers within hours. Your service advisors are not manually updating a FAQ page every time the package prices change.

There is also a proactive angle for open recalls. On a model-specific VDP where the vehicle has an associated recall, Nobi can surface a service prompt when recall bulletins are connected and the page activation rule is set - giving a customer who came to look at a vehicle a reason to book before they leave the page. That kind of connection between sales and fixed ops activity tends to stay invisible on most dealership websites; this makes it visible at exactly the right moment.

## How do I wire Nobi to my service scheduler using Custom Actions?

Getting a customer to the point of booking is the first half. Custom Actions complete the handoff: they connect Nobi to any external system that accepts a webhook or API call, including most dealership service schedulers. When a conversation reaches a booking trigger - the customer says they want to schedule service, or confirms after seeing the details - Nobi collects the required fields and fires the action to your scheduler. Your scheduling system owns the calendar and sends the confirmation; Nobi gathers the request and passes it in a structured format your team can act on immediately.

The action fires at a defined trigger, not on every message. You configure the phrase or intent that signals the customer is ready to book. Before that trigger fires, Nobi is answering questions and building context. Once it fires, the fields required for a service write-up get collected in sequence: year, make, model, mileage, VIN, service type, preferred date and time, name, phone, and email are common starting points. The field list is configurable to match whatever your advisors collect at the write-up desk.

If your scheduler exposes an API, Nobi posts the structured request directly. If it doesn't, the action sends a formatted email or webhook to your service team's inbox while the customer still receives an immediate on-site acknowledgment. Either way, no appointment request sits as an unread form submission until morning.

Setup lives in Nobi's dashboard. There are no code changes on the dealership's website beyond the one-time site snippet your developer drops in.

The same Custom Actions layer can also trigger your CRM to create a service lead record at the same moment the booking request fires. The customer's contact details land in the system your BDC already monitors - so a no-show appointment still leaves a record your team can follow up on, rather than disappearing entirely.

## What vehicle and contact details does Nobi capture before the service request reaches your team?

What fills that CRM record - and the scheduler entry it came from - is set by the Custom Action configuration. Before submitting a service request, Nobi collects year, make, model, mileage, VIN, service type, preferred date and time, name, phone, and email. Your service advisors get a complete write-up starter, not a vague "call me back" entry with nothing useful attached.

Every field on that list is configurable. If your service drive doesn't need mileage until the car is on the lift, take it out. If your advisors always want a second contact number, add it. The field set mirrors your actual write-up process.

VIN is worth keeping if you can get it. When a customer provides their VIN, your advisors can pull the repair order before the car rolls in, cutting check-in time. The assistant asks for it conversationally - "Do you have your VIN handy? It speeds things up when you arrive" - so it doesn't feel like filling out a form.

Contact information comes last, not first. Before asking for a name and phone number, the assistant works through the service questions: is this routine maintenance or a specific concern you've noticed? How long has it been doing that? Your advisors get real context ahead of the first touch, not just a callback number with no vehicle details.

Once collected, contact details route to your scheduler and, if configured, to your CRM. That record can trigger a post-visit follow-up or a next-interval reminder depending on your setup.

If a customer states something the assistant cannot verify against your connected content - a coverage claim, for instance - it passes the note to your service team rather than treating it as confirmed. The handoff is clear about what it knows and what needs a human to verify.

## What does Nobi not handle in the service booking flow, and what stays in your scheduler and DMS?

Once that service request is in your scheduler - and the CRM has a record of it - Nobi's job is done. Bay management, technician assignment, parts availability, slot confirmation, and all appointment confirmation communications stay inside your DMS and service scheduler. Nobi takes the service request from the website and routes it to your scheduling system. It does not execute transactions, modify existing appointments, or manage service capacity. Those belong in the systems built for them.

Bay availability and open appointment slots are not visible to Nobi. Your scheduler owns that calendar and sends the confirmation back to the customer. When a visitor asks "do you have availability Tuesday afternoon?", the right answer is that the assistant routes the request to your team, not that it checks the schedule in real time.

Cancellations and reschedules work the same way. Nobi does not cancel or modify existing appointments. A customer with that kind of request should be directed to call the service drive or use your scheduler's customer-facing portal if it has one. The assistant can surface that path - "to reschedule, call the service drive at [number]" - but it cannot take the action itself.

Pricing in the assistant comes only from content you have connected. If your service menu does not publish a price for a specific job, Nobi will not quote one. That is the right behavior - quoting a labor rate that changed in your DMS last week is worse than saying you'll need to call for a quote.

There is no email channel. Appointment confirmation and the day-before reminder go out through your CRM or scheduling system, through their normal workflow. Nobi handles the website conversation; the follow-up communication is your existing systems' job.

Finally, there is no live agent transfer inside the same session. If a customer raises a warranty dispute that needs a service advisor, the right path is a phone number or click-to-call that appears in the conversation. Nobi surfaces that contact option - it does not transfer the customer mid-thread to a human agent. The assistant knows where its lane ends.

That lane boundary is also the right place to make the vendor call. Nobi is the right fit when the primary leak is after-hours website conversations that cannot take a service request and route it to your team, and when you want one assistant handling both sales and service questions without a second vendor. If you need a native connection to your DMS, SMS follow-up for no-shows, or service lifecycle orchestration across a dealer group, purpose-built tools go further than Nobi does today.

[Numa](https://numa.com) is strongest in service-bay communication: SMS-driven appointment confirmations, status updates, and service department follow-up. Pick it when your service team's primary follow-up channel is text and the goal is automating appointment confirmations, status updates, and service department follow-up via SMS.

[Impel](https://impel.io) includes service scheduling inside an automotive AI platform covering lead engagement and lifecycle marketing across sales, service, and fixed ops. Pick it when the goal is consolidating onto one vendor across the full rooftop - with the runway for a multi-month rollout.

Nobi is the right pick when you want the same assistant that handles sales floor questions to also take service bookings - one site-side install, one knowledge base, one vendor - without the complexity of a platform-level implementation.
