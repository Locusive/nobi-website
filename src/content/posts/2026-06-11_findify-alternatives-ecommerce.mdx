## Why are ecommerce teams looking for Findify alternatives?

Findify built its reputation on Shopify search and visual merchandising - pinning, boosting, zero-result redirects, and collection sorting from one dashboard.

## How did we evaluate these Findify alternatives?

We scored each on four criteria: how well each closes vocabulary mismatch without manual synonym work; whether it handles pre-purchase questions as direct answers or as results pages; what pricing looks like at entry tier; and how fast a non-technical team can go live.

## 2. Klevu

[Klevu](https://klevu.com) is AI-powered site search for Shopify, with a no-code Smart Merchandising dashboard for pinning, boosting, and zero-result redirects. The AI closes vocabulary gaps that keyword search can't touch: a shopper who types "wide-leg cropped trouser in navy" finds the right product even when the catalog title says "cropped wide pant" - no synonym rule required. The Smart Merchandising dashboard handles seasonal pushes and priority products without filing an engineering ticket. A packaged Shopify install typically goes live in days. One flag worth noting before you finalize your shortlist: Klevu is now a division of Athos Commerce, the same parent that owns Searchspring. If both tools land on your evaluation list, you're comparing two products from one owner, not two independent vendors.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose zero-result rate comes from long, descriptive queries a keyword engine can't parse, and who need occasional pinning on top.

**Pricing:** Three tiers: Essential, Advanced, Expert. Quote-only - no published dollar figures on athoscommerce.com/pricing; all tiers show "Get a demo" CTA. Third-party sources cite roughly $499-$1,598/month range. Personalization features are included in Expert tier only, not lower tiers.

**Pros:**
- AI matching closes the vocabulary gap between how shoppers describe products and how your catalog titles them - no manually maintained synonym list needed for every phrase variation

**Cons:**
- Expensive relative to brand size
- Merchandising UI is dated
- Personalization features are only in the Expert tier; Essential and Advanced tiers do not include them
- Consolidated under Athos Commerce (alongside Searchspring and Intelligent Reach) - shortlisting Klevu + Searchspring means picking between two products of the same parent company

**Verdict:** Pick Klevu when vocabulary mismatch is the main CVR leak on your Shopify store and you need occasional pinning on top; skip it if personalization is a priority at any budget level, since that feature is limited to the Expert tier.

## 3. Fast Simon

[Fast Simon](https://fastsimon.com) is a Shopify search and merchandising tool built around the job most marketing managers actually spend their time on: keeping storefronts current. Seasonal edits, campaign capsules, and gift guide layouts can go live from one dashboard, with no engineering ticket required. A non-technical team can pin products, update collection order, and adjust what shows up first on a given page - all from the same screen. Fast Simon's merchandising tooling outpaces its natural-language understanding, so a shopper who types a descriptive query like "lightweight rain jacket for trail running" may get a results page that's good enough but not as sharp as a dedicated AI search engine would return.

**Best for:** Shopify marketing teams whose main bottleneck is visual collection curation - seasonal edits, campaign capsules, gift guides - not long-tail semantic matching on natural-language queries.

**Pricing:** Shopify App Store tiered pricing (verified 2026-05). Starter plan (free, 100 sessions/mo), then paid tiers: entry at $39.99/mo, Essential at $99.99/mo, Top Pro at $299.99/mo. Scales by monthly session volume.

**Pros:**
- App Store install gets a store live in days rather than months

**Cons:**
- Primary strength is visual merchandising and collection curation; search personalization is a secondary strength
- Lighter on personalization than AI-native engines

**Verdict:** Pick Fast Simon when visual collection curation is your main discovery bottleneck and shoppers mostly stick to category browsing; skip it if a high zero-result rate on natural-language queries is where you're losing sales.

## 4. Searchspring

[Searchspring](https://searchspring.com) puts the merchandising team in charge of every query decision. Pins, boosts, zero-result redirects, and category rules all live in one dashboard - and the pitch is direct: your team, not an algorithm, decides what each search returns. For a marketing manager who needs to audit any result back to a specific rule and explain it to a stakeholder, that transparency is the main reason to look here. The trade-off is the rule list itself. It grows one-to-one with query patterns - every unusual phrasing that misses a match needs its own new entry, and the maintenance load compounds as the catalog scales. Searchspring is now a division of Athos Commerce, sold under the same parent as Klevu and Intelligent Reach but with its own contract and pricing.

**Best for:** Ecommerce teams that want exact, rule-by-rule control over what each query returns and have the merchandising bandwidth to keep that rule list current as the catalog grows.

**Pricing:** Mid-market tiered pricing.

**Pros:**
- Each result traces back to a specific rule - merchandisers can audit any query against the pin or boost that produced it, with no black-box AI decisions to reverse-engineer after a campaign
- Zero-result redirects send dead-end queries to a curated landing page rather than a generic fallback list
- Campaigns, category rules, and search all live in the same merchandising dashboard, so teams already working in Searchspring don't need to learn a separate tool

**Cons:**
- Traditional rule-based merchandising, less AI-native
- Requires manual relevance tuning
- Consolidated under Athos Commerce (alongside Klevu and Intelligent Reach) - shortlisting Searchspring + Klevu means picking between two products of the same parent company

**Verdict:** Pick Searchspring when the merch team wants total, auditable control over every query and has the bandwidth to maintain the rule list; skip it if reducing manual maintenance work is the actual goal.

## Which Findify alternative fits your ecommerce team?

The right tool depends on where shoppers are actually dropping off and what your team has the bandwidth to maintain. If the CVR problem is that shoppers use natural language your keyword engine can't parse, that's a semantic matching gap - Nobi or Klevu close it without manually maintained synonym rules. If the bottleneck is collection presentation and seasonal edits, Fast Simon addresses that directly. If the merchandising team needs exact, auditable control over every query result, Searchspring is the most defensible choice.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Klevu and Searchspring still separate products now that Athos Commerce owns both?**

They're sold and contracted independently. Each has its own pricing, onboarding, and product roadmap. The flag is awareness: when both land on your shortlist, you're evaluating two products from the same parent, not two independent competitors. It's worth confirming before you run a formal side-by-side.

**What's the most affordable Findify alternative?**

Fast Simon has a free Starter plan capped at 100 sessions per month, with paid plans from $39.99/month. Nobi starts at $25/month, covering 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with per-unit overages after that. Klevu and Searchspring don't publish pricing - both require a discovery call before you can model costs.

**Does switching from Findify require a developer?**

It depends on the tool. Fast Simon installs from the Shopify App Store with no development work required. Nobi is a small theme edit - a developer drops in the Nobi snippet and points it at the search bar and assistant slots on your store. Most teams are live the same day.
