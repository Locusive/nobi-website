## What should you look for in a Justuno alternative?

You started looking at Justuno alternatives for one of two reasons: your list growth has stalled, or your popups convert fine but shoppers still leave without buying. Those come from different gaps. A stalled list needs more people opting in, not a different tool answering questions. A shopper who leaves after seeing a popup usually had a question it couldn't answer, like whether a jacket runs true to size. What splits these alternatives is which of those two problems they actually solve.

Justuno and Privy both grow your email and SMS list with on-site popups. Justuno leans toward AI-driven product recommendations on its higher tiers and pairs well with brands that want granular targeting; Privy bundles email, SMS, and popups into one plan built for smaller Shopify and BigCommerce stores. Neither talks back when a shopper has a specific question about a product. A popup can capture an email address, but it can't tell someone whether a jacket runs true to size.

Octane AI takes a different approach: a quiz that narrows your catalog down to a recommended SKU. That works well for brands with a wide catalog and a beauty or CPG assortment, but a quiz is a fixed set of questions. It can't handle a shopper who types something the quiz didn't anticipate. If Octane AI's quiz model is on your shortlist, our [Octane AI alternatives for ecommerce](https://nobi.ai/blog/octane-ai-alternatives-ecommerce) comparison walks through how it stacks up against Nobi, Rep AI, Gorgias, and Tidio.

Nobi is a conversational assistant that answers open-ended product questions and captures a lead in the same conversation, combining product search, automated support, and lead generation. It doesn't replace an email service provider the way Privy does. If your CVR problem is unanswered questions rather than list size, that's the gap it's built for.

## How did we evaluate these Justuno alternatives?

We compared each tool on four things a marketing manager actually cares about: what job it does (list growth, discovery, or Q&A), pricing transparency, how disruptive the on-site experience is, and whether it ties back to revenue metrics like CVR and AOV rather than just impressions. Nobi is one of the tools evaluated, alongside Privy and Octane AI.

Justuno publishes four tiers, from a free plan up to a Flex tier priced by visitor traffic, with Enterprise unlisted. Privy prices by contact count and includes email, SMS, and popups in one plan. Octane AI publishes real numbers too: Basic at $50 a month for 400 credits, Plus at $200 for 2,200 credits, and Enterprise starting at $500 and up, where a credit means one completed quiz. None of these hide behind a flat "contact sales" wall, which makes them easier to budget against.

Justuno and Privy are interruption-based: a popup appears whether or not the visitor asked for anything. Octane AI's quiz is a bit softer since a visitor usually opts in to start it, but it still takes over the screen. Nobi sits available on request instead, a chat interface a visitor opens when they have a question, rather than one that opens itself on them.

Popup tools report list growth and email-driven revenue well, but that's a step removed from CVR or AOV - the hardest of the four to pin down. Octane AI's quiz data can feed into email platforms once synced, which adds a lag before it's actionable. Nobi ties directly to conversion and average order value inside the same conversation where the question gets answered, since search, support, and lead capture aren't split across separate tools reporting separately.

## Why look for a Justuno alternative?

That tie to conversion and AOV only matters if the reason your numbers are stuck is a question going unanswered, not a list that's too small. Justuno is a mature popup and banner tool with a real behavioral rules engine, and teams that just need targeted on-page popups have no reason to leave it. Teams look elsewhere when the popup itself isn't the problem. The problem is that a popup can't answer the product question a visitor actually has before they'll convert.

Justuno's rules engine does what it's built to do. Trigger a popup on exit intent, on scroll depth, on time on page, on cart value, and it fires reliably. That part of the product isn't broken, and if your only goal is growing an email list, it still works well. The gap shows up on your analytics dashboard in a specific way: bounce rate stays high on your highest-intent pages, product detail pages, sizing guides, spec sheets, even with popups active and converting at a normal rate. A popup fired at the right moment can still capture an email address from someone who leaves a minute later, because the popup never answered the thing they came to ask. Does this run small? Is it machine washable? What's the return window on a final sale item? None of that is a job a rules engine does.

Nobi answers those questions directly, pulling from your own connected product pages and policy docs with a citation back to the source, so a shopper gets a real answer instead of a discount offer that doesn't address what they asked. It doesn't compete with Justuno's list-growth job. If your funnel leak is unanswered product questions or fit confusion rather than list size, that's a different tool for a different leak, and it's worth running alongside whatever popup tool is already doing its job.

## 2. Privy

[Privy](https://privy.com) is built for one job: growing your email and SMS list through on-site popups, and it does that job well if you're already running a Shopify store. For a marketing manager whose main KPI is list size, that focus is the appeal. Privy bundles popups, email, and SMS into a single plan instead of separate tools, and setup is fast because the templates are pre-built for cart abandonment and welcome discounts. It won't tell a shopper whether a jacket runs small or answer a question about return policy on a final sale item. If your bounce rate is climbing on product and sizing pages, that gap matters more than another popup asking for an email address before the visitor has gotten anything back.

**Best for:** Marketing teams whose main KPI is email and SMS list growth, with campaigns ready to send once the list grows.

**Pricing:** Free tier; paid tiers for volume.

**Pros:**
- Single-purpose focus means less configuration than a broader platform
- Bundles popups, email, and SMS into one plan with pre-built templates for cart abandonment and welcome discounts, so setup is fast on Shopify

**Cons:**
- Popup-based, disruptive to UX
- Single-purpose tool

**Verdict:** Pick Privy when list growth is your bottleneck and popups are already converting; skip it if visitors are leaving with unanswered questions rather than an unfilled email field.

## 3. Octane AI

[Octane AI](https://octaneai.com) skips the search bar and the chat window entirely and puts a short quiz in front of the shopper instead. Answer a handful of questions about fit, use case, or occasion, and the quiz resolves to a specific SKU rather than a category page you'd have to browse. For a marketing manager with a catalog wide enough that shoppers routinely bounce between similar products, that's the appeal: the quiz does the narrowing for them. It also solves a second problem at the same time. Every completed quiz writes back to Klaviyo as a segment attribute, so a shopper who answers a sizing or occasion question lands in a lifecycle flow without you building a separate integration to make that happen.

**Best for:** Marketing teams whose catalog is wide enough that shoppers need guided questions to narrow down to the right product, and who want quiz answers feeding straight into email lifecycle campaigns.

**Pricing:** Public tiered Shopify-app pricing. Basic $50/month (400 credits), Plus $200/month (2,200 credits), Enterprise & Unlimited starts at $500+/month. Octane defines a credit as one quiz completion ("1 credit = 1 quiz engagement when someone completes your quiz"). AI features (Smart Products, Smart Copy, Image Analyzer) consume fractional extra credits per interaction.

**Pros:**
- Deep Shopify product-attribute mapping resolves quiz answers to real SKUs, not generic category pages
- Native Klaviyo integration captures quiz completions as email-segment attributes automatically
- Pre-built templates shorten time-to-launch for fit-finder and routine-style quizzes

**Cons:**
- Quiz-centric, less about open-ended conversation
- Strong in beauty and CPG, less relevant elsewhere

**Verdict:** Pick Octane AI when a guided quiz is genuinely the right discovery metaphor for your catalog and Klaviyo is your email backbone; skip it when shoppers are asking open-ended questions instead of answering multiple choice. ## How should a marketing manager pick between these Justuno alternatives?

That question, whether shoppers are asking open-ended questions or would answer a fixed set of quiz choices, is really a symptom of a bigger one: which metric is actually stuck. If email or SMS list size is flat while your popups still convert at a normal rate, the fix is more list-growth surface, and Privy's popup capture is the direct answer. If a wide catalog is confusing shoppers before they ever reach a product page, Octane AI's quiz narrows them down to a SKU. If bounce rate stays high specifically on pages visitors already found through search or ads, the popup and the quiz both miss the point: the visitor has a question nobody answered. That's the gap Nobi's conversational Q&A and lead capture cover.

Privy is the right call when your list itself is the bottleneck. It's a mature Shopify and BigCommerce tool built for that one job, and if campaigns are already queued up and waiting on more subscribers, nothing here beats it at growing the list.

Octane AI fits when the problem is choice, not information. A shopper staring at forty similar products doesn't need a Q&A session, they need to be walked to the right one, and a quiz does that faster than a search box or a chat window would.

Nobi fits when the problem is a question, not a decision tree. A shopper who wants to know if a jacket runs small or what the return window is on a final-sale item won't get that from a popup or a quiz. Nobi answers it directly and captures contact info in the same exchange.

None of this is either-or. A marketing manager can run Nobi for on-site questions and lead capture while Privy or an ESP handles outbound email and SMS campaigns. They solve different leaks in the same funnel.

## Frequently answered questions

Quick answers to what marketing managers ask most when comparing Justuno alternatives.

**Does Nobi replace Justuno's popups entirely, or run alongside them?** Nobi isn't a popup tool, so it doesn't replace Justuno's popups directly. It's a conversational assistant that answers a shopper's product question and captures contact info in the same exchange. Most teams keep Justuno running for list-growth popups and add Nobi for the questions a popup can't answer, like sizing or return policy on a specific item.

**Can Nobi send email or SMS campaigns?** No. Nobi answers in web chat and captures the lead there; it doesn't send outbound email or texts. Getting that contact into a campaign still takes an email service provider, the kind of tool Privy already feeds with the addresses and numbers it collects through popups.

**Is switching from Justuno disruptive?** No. None of these tools require re-platforming. Justuno, Privy, and Nobi all run as an added layer on top of the site you already have, so adding or dropping one doesn't touch your storefront setup underneath it.

**Do Nobi and Privy do the same job?** No. Privy grows your list through email, SMS, and popups. Nobi answers shopper questions and captures a lead in that same conversation. A marketing manager can run both: Privy handling outbound list growth, Nobi handling the on-site questions that a popup was never built to answer.
