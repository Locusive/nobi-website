## What is the difference between Nobi and Intercom for automated customer support?

Nobi and Intercom both reduce inbound support volume, but they intercept questions at different points in the funnel. Nobi grounds its answers in your live product catalog and policy pages, so a shopper asking "does this run small?" or "when will it ship?" gets resolved on the PDP from the same data the storefront already exposes - no ticket, no agent. [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) is a general customer messaging platform whose Fin AI agent layers on top of an existing helpdesk with tickets, macros, and live agents. The right pick depends on whether your support volume starts on a product page or in your support inbox.

Nobi is purpose-built for ecommerce. The assistant is grounded in your catalog and tied to site search and product discovery, so it answers the [questions shoppers ask before checkout](https://nobi.ai/blog/handling-long-tail-queries) - "does this run small," "when will it ship," "what's your return window" - without a ticket ever being created. Pricing is usage-based: $25/month base including 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. No per-seat fees, no per-resolution charges. The trade-off is that Nobi is not a ticket workflow platform - once a question escalates beyond catalog and policy answers, you still need a helpdesk to manage the case.

Intercom is a horizontal support platform. Seats run $39 (Essential), $99 (Advanced), and $139 (Expert) per month, with Fin AI Agent billed at $0.99 per resolution on top. Pick Intercom when your bottleneck is the full ticket lifecycle - inbox management, agent workflows, multi-channel messaging - and AI-powered support is one layer inside that system rather than the primary job.

## How does Nobi compare to Intercom on pricing?

Nobi starts at $25 per month flat, including 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Intercom's Essential plan starts at $39 per seat per month, and the Fin AI agent costs $0.99 per resolved conversation on top of seats. For an ecom team handling 1,000 shopper conversations over the included quota in a given month, Nobi's overage runs $100 (1,000 × $0.10) against the $990 (1,000 × $0.99) Intercom would charge for the same volume - roughly a 10x gap before seat costs are even counted. For a large support org already paying for seats, the marginal cost of adding Fin is the number that matters.

Intercom prices on two axes: seats and AI resolutions. Essential is $39 per seat, Advanced is $99, and Expert is $139, with Fin resolutions stacking on top at $0.99 each. A five-agent team on Essential starts around $195 per month before any AI usage, and 1,000 Fin-resolved conversations adds another $990. That model works when you already need the seats for a human support team and Fin runs on top of an established helpdesk. It works less well when most of your inbound is shopper product questions rather than tickets - you're paying per seat for an inbox capability you may barely use.

Nobi prices only on what shoppers do. No seats, no per-resolution charges, no tiered AI gating - the same $25 base and the same overage rates whether you have one merchandiser or ten. The honest trade-off: Nobi answers shopper questions and reduces inbound volume, but it isn't a ticket workflow platform. If your team needs case routing, macros, or returns processing, you'll still pair Nobi with a helpdesk. For a head of ecommerce whose cost driver is shopper Q&A volume rather than agent headcount, the math usually points to Nobi.

## Which is built specifically for ecommerce automated support?

The mechanism difference is what each tool grounds its answers in. Nobi grounds in your live product catalog and policy pages — variants, inventory, sizing, materials, shipping rules — pulled directly from the storefront. Intercom's Fin grounds in help-center articles and uploaded knowledge-base content — text written by a content team, indexed for search, refreshed manually. That choice cascades through where the assistant lives on your site, how it answers product-specific questions, and how much catalog plumbing you have to build yourself before automated answers actually work.

Nobi ingests your product catalog and grounds every answer in the variants, inventory, sizing, materials, and shipping policies you actually sell. The assistant sits inside the storefront search bar, on the PDP, and in a chat widget, so a shopper asking "does this run small?" gets answered against live variant data, not against a help-center article that someone wrote six months ago. Install is two JavaScript snippets dropped into the theme, which works on Shopify, BigCommerce, Magento, WooCommerce, and Salesforce Commerce Cloud. The trade-off: Nobi runs on web chat only - no SMS, WhatsApp, or voice channels - so brands whose shoppers ask questions over messaging apps need a different tool for those surfaces.

[Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) is built around the messenger bubble and Fin, its AI agent. Fin trains primarily on help-center articles, public URLs, and uploaded docs - well-suited to policy-heavy or content-heavy support orgs where the answers already live in a knowledge base. Pricing starts at $39 per seat per month on the Essential plan and runs to $139 per seat on Expert, with Fin resolutions billed at $0.99 each. Pick Intercom when your support content already lives in a Zendesk-style help center and your support team works out of a unified inbox.

## How do AI customer support capabilities compare between Nobi and Intercom?

Intercom's Fin is a strong general-purpose AI agent: it reads your help center, answers in conversational language, and reports resolution rates per conversation. Nobi's assistant is narrower and deeper - it only answers questions an ecommerce shopper actually asks (product fit, comparison, availability, order status, returns policy) and grounds every answer in your live catalog and order data. Fin is the better generalist; Nobi is the better specialist for storefront questions. Neither replaces a live agent for genuinely complex cases.

Fin learns from whatever you point it at: help center articles, macros, internal docs, public URLs. That makes it strong on policy and how-to questions ("how do I update my billing address", "what's your warranty process", "how do I reset my password") because those answers live in the help center where Fin is trained. The handoff model is its other real strength. When Fin's confidence drops, it passes the conversation to a human Intercom agent inside the same thread, with full transcript context, across web, in-app, email, and SMS surfaces. The agent picks up where Fin left off. For a support team already running Intercom as its inbox, that hybrid path is hard to beat. Fin is weaker when the question depends on live catalog state - whether a specific SKU is in stock in size medium, whether two products are interchangeable, what shipped on order #48291 - because that data isn't in the help center.

Nobi's assistant is grounded in your product catalog and order data directly, not in articles about your catalog. A shopper asking ["do you have this in petite"](https://nobi.ai/blog/handling-long-tail-queries) gets an answer from the live inventory feed, not a paraphrase of a sizing guide. Same for "where's my order" or "can I return this if I bought it on sale" - the answer comes from the order record and the policy bound to that order. That's the lane Nobi owns: the storefront questions that would otherwise become Gorgias or Zendesk tickets. The honest limit: Nobi runs AI-first on the web chat surface only, with a binary escalation when the AI can't answer. There's no live-agent drop-in mid-thread, and no SMS, email, or in-app channel. Brands who want a human jumping into an ongoing AI session, or AI customer support on channels beyond web chat, will get more from Fin.

## When is Intercom the better choice over Nobi?

Intercom is the better pick when your support team already runs a full helpdesk and the volume problem lives in the inbox, not on the storefront. If you need ticketing, macros, SLAs, agent routing, an omnichannel inbox spanning email, SMS, and in-app messenger, and a hybrid mode where a human agent jumps into an ongoing AI conversation mid-thread without a hard handoff, that's the job Intercom is built for. Brands whose customers reach support through email or messenger more often than through the website will get more lift from Fin than from a storefront-only assistant.

[Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) is a conversation-first customer service platform with deep agent-side workflow tooling. Tickets, macros, queues, SLAs, productivity reporting, multibrand support on the Expert plan, and a unified inbox across channels all sit inside the same product. Fin layers AI resolutions on top of that workflow, billed at $0.99 per resolution on top of seat pricing that ranges from $39 per seat per month on Essential to $139 per seat per month on Expert. Pick Intercom when agent workflow is the bottleneck, when one tool needs to handle SaaS-style product questions next to ecommerce questions, or when you specifically want a live agent to take over an AI thread without breaking the session.

Nobi is the wrong tool for that profile. Nobi answers shopper questions on the storefront and reduces inbound volume there, but it doesn't manage tickets, route cases to agents, run macros, or expose a hybrid live-agent-in-AI-conversation mode. It's also web chat only, with no SMS, WhatsApp, or voice channel. If your support volume sits in the inbox and your team measures itself on agent productivity inside a queue, Intercom solves that problem and Nobi doesn't pretend to.

## When is Nobi the better choice over Intercom?

Nobi is the right call when most of your inbound support volume traces back to questions a shopper would have answered themselves with better on-site information - sizing, fit, availability, order status, returns policy. In that scenario you want the assistant on the PDP and the search bar, grounded in catalog data, not a messenger bubble trained on help-center articles. Nobi also tends to be cheaper for ecommerce teams under roughly ten support agents, since seat-based plans plus per-resolution charges add up quickly against a $25/month base with $0.10 messages.

Nobi fits when the support volume is shopper-facing and product-driven. The assistant lives where shoppers actually have questions - inside search, on PDPs, in a chat surface that already knows your catalog - so "do you ship to Canada", "is this true to size", "where's my order" get answered in the buying flow instead of escalating to email. One platform handles [site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search), product discovery, and conversational Q&A on a $25/month base that includes 2,500 searches and 250 messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. No seats, so a lean two- or three-person ecom team doesn't subsidize larger orgs sharing the same tier. The trade-off worth naming: Nobi answers post-purchase questions but doesn't execute cancellations, returns, or tracking lookups inside the chat. Shoppers either self-serve via the answer or hand off to email.

Intercom is built around an agent inbox: ticket workflows, multibrand routing, deep reporting, and Fin AI resolving general help-center questions so agents can triage what's left. It's the right call when the bottleneck is agent productivity and ticket volume across multiple support teams, not shopper questions on the PDP. If you don't run a full helpdesk staffing model, the seat math and the help-center-shaped knowledge layer are sized for a different buyer.

## Frequently asked questions

A few questions come up consistently when ecommerce leaders weigh Nobi against [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) for automated customer support.

**Can Nobi replace my helpdesk?** No. Nobi answers shopper questions before they hit the inbox, but it doesn't manage tickets, run macros, or route cases to agents. If you need ticketing, pair Nobi with Gorgias or Zendesk.

**Can Intercom's Fin answer product-specific questions from a Shopify catalog?** Yes, if you feed it product content as knowledge sources. The caveat: Fin isn't grounded in live variant or inventory data the way an ecommerce-native assistant is, so answers about stock, sizing availability, or per-variant attributes can drift from what's actually on the PDP.

**How is a "resolution" counted on Fin?** Roughly, a conversation Fin handles end-to-end without escalating to a human. The exact definition has shifted over time, so check Intercom's current docs before modeling your bill.

**Does Nobi support email, SMS, or WhatsApp?** Not today. Nobi is web chat only. Brands that need messaging channels should pair Nobi with another tool or pick Intercom.

**Can I run both?** Yes. Some brands put Nobi on the storefront for pre-purchase questions (search, PDP questions, sizing, shipping policy) and keep Intercom for post-purchase ticketing and agent workflows. The two cover different jobs and don't step on each other.
