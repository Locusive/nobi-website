## What is an AI recommendation engine for ecommerce?

An AI recommendation engine surfaces the products most likely to convert each shopper, using signals like browse history, cart contents, and catalog relationships to reorder what shows up on PDPs, in carts, in email, and inside search. The point is to move CVR, AOV, and revenue per visit without making merchandisers hand-pin products to every query or audience.

The surfaces that matter are the [search results page](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-search-ecommerce-2026), PDPs, category pages, the cart, post-purchase, and email. Most engines specialize in two or three of those rather than all six, so the right pick usually depends on which surface drives the most missed revenue today. Modern engines also lean on session-signal ranking and semantic matching of catalog content, not just collaborative filtering on past orders, which is why long, conversational queries land better than they used to.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi pairs personalized search-results ranking with a [conversational assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce) that recommends products on the PDP and the homepage, all from one tool. The personalization runs on shopper signals - browse, click, add-to-cart - out of the box, so merchandisers don't have to hand-pin products to every query or audience. For a head of ecommerce, that means one engine reorders the search results page, suggests on the PDP, and answers shopper questions inside chat, instead of three separate tools on three separate bills. [Kilte](/customers/kilte) runs Nobi across the search bar, collection filters, and product discovery on a single platform. Inline numbered citation pills on every assistant answer link recommendations back to the source product or policy page, so shoppers can verify what they're being shown without leaving the chat.

**Best for:** Ecommerce teams whose recommendation work spans the search results page, PDP suggestions, and conversational answers, and who want transparent per-unit pricing instead of a revenue-share contract.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages). $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**
- Out-of-the-box personalization reorders search results and PDP suggestions on shopper signals (browse, click, add-to-cart) with no manual rule tuning
- One semantic layer powers search, conversational answers, and PDP recommendations - Kilte runs Nobi across the search bar, collection filters, and product discovery from one platform
- Inline numbered citation pills on every assistant answer link recommendations back to the source product or policy page, so shoppers can verify claims without leaving the chat
- Transparent per-unit pricing: $25 base with $0.01 per search and $0.10 per message overage, no revenue-share and no usage-based surprise bill

**Cons:**
- No site-wide merchandising across category or collection pages - Nobi curates the search results page and the assistant, not the full PLP grid
- Web chat only; brands whose shoppers convert on SMS, WhatsApp, or voice need a different tool for those channels

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when recommendations, search, and shopper Q&A all need to live in one tool with transparent per-unit pricing; skip it if your recommendation work has to drive merchandising across every category and collection page on the site.

## 2. Rebuy

[Rebuy](https://rebuyengine.com) is a Shopify-native recommendation engine built around the Smart Cart and post-purchase moments. The widgets drop into the cart drawer, the checkout, and the order confirmation page, on the bet that the recommendation surface that pays best isn't the homepage - it's the moment a shopper has already added something to cart. For a head of ecommerce on Shopify Plus, that's a specific lift mechanic: AOV from cart upsells and cross-sells, plus add-on revenue from post-purchase widgets most engines never touch.

**Best for:** Shopify Plus brands whose recommendation lift comes from cart upsells, cross-sells, and post-purchase moments rather than the search results page or PDP.

**Pricing:** Tiered Shopify app pricing scaled by order volume. Verify current numbers on Rebuy's pricing page.

**Pros:**
- Smart Cart drops into a Shopify theme in hours and starts driving AOV lift on cart upsells and cross-sells right away
- Post-purchase recommendation widgets run on the order confirmation page, capturing add-on revenue most engines never touch
- Deep Shopify Plus integration with checkout extensibility and Shop App support

**Cons:**
- Cart and post-purchase focus, not site-wide product discovery; the search results page and PDP recommendation surfaces aren't the strength
- Not a conversational assistant; shopper Q&A and grounded chat answers are out of scope

**Verdict:** Pick Rebuy when cart upsells and post-purchase recommendations are the AOV lever you're focused on; skip it if you need recommendations across search, PDPs, and a shopping assistant in one platform.

## 3. Nosto

[Nosto](https://nosto.com) is an ecommerce personalization platform that runs recommendation widgets across category pages, PDPs, the cart, and email, with segmentation rules layered on top for merch teams that want explicit control. The pitch for a head of ecommerce is breadth: one platform contract covering most of the recommendation surfaces a merch team touches in a given week, instead of stitching together separate vendors for category, PDP, and email. Onsite content personalization and pop-up overlays come bundled in the same contract.

**Best for:** Brands consolidating personalization across category, PDP, cart, and email under one platform contract instead of stitching together point tools.

**Pricing:** Mid-market to enterprise tiered pricing, with contracts negotiated directly based on GMV and surfaces enabled.

**Pros:**
- Personalization widgets run across category, PDP, cart, and email - one platform covering most of the recommendation surfaces a merch team works on
- Mature segmentation and rule-builder tools for teams that want explicit control on top of automated ranking
- Onsite content personalization and pop-up overlays bundled into the same contract

**Cons:**
- Pricing and contract length skew enterprise, which puts it above mid-market comfort when the team only needs a subset of the surfaces
- Search is a newer add-on rather than the primary product

**Verdict:** Pick Nosto when full-surface personalization under one contract is the priority and the budget supports an enterprise-leaning platform; skip it if you only need recommendations on one or two surfaces.

## 4. Dynamic Yield

[Dynamic Yield](https://dynamicyield.com) is a Mastercard-owned personalization platform that pairs recommendation widgets and behavioral targeting with a first-class A/B testing layer. For a head of ecommerce, the pitch is the experimentation workflow: every personalization change ships behind a test against control, with multivariate options when you need them, instead of running an outside testing tool stitched onto a separate recommendation vendor. The platform has a long track record on enterprise omnichannel rollouts across web, app, and email, and the segmentation depth holds up under brand-team and governance review.

**Best for:** Enterprise teams where experimentation infrastructure matters as much as the recommendations themselves, and where every personalization change has to ship behind an A/B test.

**Pricing:** Quote-only enterprise pricing; six-figure annual contracts are common, priced on traffic, surfaces enabled, and number of running experiments.

**Pros:**
- A/B testing and multivariate experimentation built into the personalization engine, not bolted on as a separate vendor
- Behavioral targeting and segmentation depth that holds up under enterprise governance and brand-team review
- Long track record on enterprise omnichannel deployments across web, app, and email

**Cons:**
- Enterprise pricing and a multi-month implementation timeline put it out of reach for most brands without a dedicated optimization team
- Heavy on experimentation and targeting tooling, lighter on conversational discovery; no native shopping assistant or grounded chat

**Verdict:** Pick Dynamic Yield when A/B testing every personalization change is the workflow you're buying; skip it if you want a packaged install or a conversational shopping layer in the same tool.

## 5. Constructor

[Constructor](https://constructor.io) pairs semantic search with personalized session-signal ranking and a recommendations layer that runs across category pages, collection pages, browse, and PDPs - not only the search results page. For a head of ecommerce running a $50M+ GMV operation with an internal data team, that breadth is the reason to look here. Personalization runs in real time on what each shopper has clicked, viewed, and added during the session, and quizzes, browse, and recommendations sit under one platform feeding the same ranking model.

**Best for:** $50M+ GMV retailers with an internal data team where recommendations have to move across category, collection, browse, and search together.

**Pricing:** Revenue-share with no published list price; costs scale with GMV.

**Pros:**
- Real-time session-signal ranking reorders products based on in-session behavior, so recommendations move on every individual session rather than only on average ranking quality
- Recommendations run across category, collection, browse, and PDPs, not only the search results page
- Built-in A/B testing infrastructure for measuring which recommendation change is actually working
- Quizzes, browse, and recommendations unified under one platform feeding the same ranking model

**Cons:**
- Revenue-share pricing means a successful recommendation campaign costs you more on the contract as GMV grows
- Implementation runs weeks to months and requires internal data science or analytics resources to get full value

**Verdict:** Pick Constructor when recommendation work has to span the full site and you have the data team and GMV to match; skip it if you want transparent per-unit pricing or a faster Shopify-native install.

Pick by the recommendation surface that's moving CVR and AOV today, then by the pricing model your finance team can sign. The five tools above each lead on a different surface and a different contract shape, so the right answer depends on where the missed revenue lives in your funnel right now.

Nobi fits when discovery, conversational answers, and PDP recommendations all need to live in one tool with transparent per-unit pricing - $25/month base with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message, plus a small theme tweak to ship.

Rebuy is the closer match when cart upsells, cross-sells, and post-purchase widgets are the AOV lever you're focused on. Tiered Shopify app pricing scales with order volume, and the cart and order-confirmation moments are where the lift comes from.

Nosto carries the work when category, PDP, cart, and email personalization all consolidate under one contract. Mid-market to enterprise tiered pricing, with contracts negotiated directly.

Dynamic Yield is the pick when A/B testing every personalization change is the workflow you're buying. Six-figure annual contracts are common, priced on traffic and number of running experiments.

Constructor goes deepest when recommendations have to span search, browse, category, and PDP at $50M+ GMV with an internal data team behind them. Revenue-share pricing scales with GMV, so the bill grows as the program works.
