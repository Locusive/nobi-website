## What does live chat actually do for an ecommerce support team?

Live chat is a real-time messaging surface on your storefront where shoppers ask questions before, during, and after they buy. For an ecommerce support team, it functions as the deflection layer in front of your ticket queue: an AI agent or a human answers shipping, returns, sizing, and product questions inside the chat window so they never become a ticket your team has to triage tomorrow morning.

The same surface does three jobs at once. It deflects the repetitive volume - "where's my order," "do you ship to Canada," "does this run small" - that would otherwise pile up in email. It converts, by answering product questions while the shopper is still on the PDP instead of after they've closed the tab. And it escalates the cases AI genuinely can't handle to a human in a structured way, with the conversation history attached so the agent isn't starting from scratch.

One thing live chat is not: a replacement for your helpdesk. Chat sits in front of Gorgias, Zendesk, or whatever you use for ticket workflow - the helpdesk still owns case routing, macros, and returns processing. Chat just keeps the easy stuff from getting there.

## Which support questions should AI handle and which need a human?

AI should handle questions whose answer already lives in your catalog or policy docs - shipping windows, return rules, product specs, sizing, availability, and store hours. Route to a human when the request requires an account-level action (cancellation, refund, address change on a shipped order), when the shopper sounds frustrated after one AI reply, or when the AI's confidence on a multi-step question is low. Most ecommerce teams escalate 20-40% of conversations and resolve the rest in chat.

The clean split is by intent type, not keyword match. "Where is my order?" is a lookup against your order system - safe for AI to answer with a tracking link. "I want to cancel my order" sounds similar but triggers a financial action and a possible refund, which belongs with a human (or at minimum a confirmation step before the action fires). Build your routing rules around the verbs the shopper is using - "find," "show," "what is" go to AI; "cancel," "refund," "change," "speak to someone" go to a human queue. Sentiment is the second filter: if the shopper's language flips negative or the same question repeats twice, escalate even if the intent type would normally stay with AI.

Tune the AI confidence threshold against your CSAT data, not your deflection rate. If AI-handled chats score more than 10 points lower than human-handled chats, you've set the bar too low - the AI is answering questions it shouldn't be. Pull the threshold up and accept a higher escalation rate until the gap closes.

Nobi sits on the AI-suitable side of this split. The assistant is grounded in your catalog and policy docs, so it answers shipping, sizing, returns, and product questions inline and deflects that volume off your support queue. It doesn't execute account-level actions inside the chat - cancellations, refunds, and tracking lookups still belong with your helpdesk. That boundary is the right one for most teams: deflect the high-volume, low-risk questions, escalate the rest cleanly.

## How do I deploy live chat without overwhelming my support team?

Start narrow. [Deploy AI chat](https://nobi.ai/blog/adding-ai-chatbot-to-ecommerce-site) on three or four high-volume question types first - shipping ETA, return policy, sizing, stock availability - then measure deflection for two weeks before expanding scope. Set a clear handoff path so agents only see escalations the AI genuinely couldn't resolve. The mistake most teams make is routing every chat into a shared inbox on day one; agents drown, response times get worse not better, and the AI investment never pays off.

The pilot scope matters more than the tool. Pick the three or four question categories that already eat the most agent time - pull the last 30 days of tickets and tag them. Instrument those categories specifically so you can watch deflection rate move week over week. Expand scope only after the rate stabilizes for the initial set. Adding a fifth question type while the first four are still wobbling makes it impossible to tell which change moved which number.

Configure the handoff path inside your helpdesk before turning AI chat on, not after. In [Re:amaze](https://www.reamaze.com), that means setting up a dedicated channel or inbox for AI escalations with the full conversation transcript attached, so agents pick up mid-thread without asking the shopper to repeat themselves. In [Zendesk](https://www.zendesk.com), the equivalent is a trigger that routes AI-handed-off conversations to a specific group with the chat history appended to the ticket. Either way, agents need one clean queue, not a firehose.

Don't have the AI try every question on day one. Have it answer what it knows confidently and silently log what it doesn't - those logs become your roadmap for what to add to the catalog or policy docs next. A useful intermediate step is a one-week dry run where the AI drafts replies and an agent reviews each one before sending. Catches catalog gaps and bad answers fast, before any shopper sees them.

One Nobi-specific caveat worth naming: Nobi's assistant runs AI-first and hands off cleanly to a human, but there's no hybrid mode where an agent jumps into an active AI session mid-thread while the AI keeps going. If your support model relies on agents and AI working the same conversation simultaneously, that's a real constraint to weigh.

## How do I measure whether live chat is actually deflecting tickets?

Track three numbers: deflection rate (chats fully resolved without an agent), CSAT on AI-only conversations, and helpdesk ticket volume before and after launch. A working AI chat layer typically deflects 40-70% of incoming volume on common ecommerce questions, with CSAT landing within 10 points of human-handled chats. If your helpdesk ticket count isn't dropping in the first month, the AI isn't catching real demand - it's likely answering questions that wouldn't have become tickets anyway.

Deflection rate is the headline number: AI-resolved chats divided by total chats received, where "resolved" means the shopper closed the conversation without escalating to a human and didn't open a ticket on the same issue within 48 hours. The 48-hour window matters - a chat that "ended" but turned into an email two hours later isn't a deflection, it's a deferral.

CSAT delta is the honesty check on that deflection number. Run a one-question survey at the end of every chat session and segment the results by AI-only versus human-handled. If AI CSAT is more than 10 points below human CSAT, the AI is closing chats that shoppers walked away from unhappy, and your deflection rate is overstating real resolution.

Ticket volume drop is the test that the deflection is structural, not cosmetic. Pull helpdesk volume by category (shipping, returns, sizing, product questions) for the 30 days before launch and the 30 days after. Categories the AI is supposed to handle should drop noticeably; categories outside its scope should hold steady. If nothing drops, the chat layer is generating new conversations rather than absorbing existing ones.

Two checks worth running monthly: sample 50-100 AI replies and have an agent rate factual accuracy (anything below 95% means hallucinations are reaching shoppers), and divide your AI tool cost by AI-resolved chats to get a cost-per-resolution you can compare against fully loaded agent cost per ticket.

## Where does AI live chat fall short for ecommerce support?

AI live chat does not replace a helpdesk. It can't manage tickets, route cases by SLA, run macros, or process refunds and cancellations inside the chat session itself. That's still helpdesk work, and most ecom teams pair an AI chat layer with [Zendesk](https://www.zendesk.com) or [Re:amaze](https://www.reamaze.com) for the agent workflow rather than trying to ship a single tool.

Zendesk is the agent-workflow incumbent: ticketing, multi-channel routing, macros, and an AI add-on. Suite tiers are priced per agent per month with the AI add-on layered on top, so the per-seat math compounds across a full support team. That's where cases, returns, and refunds actually live. Re:amaze plays in the same lane with per-user pricing starting around $20 or $40 per user per month and chat, email, social, and FAQ in one console; teams running Shopify or BigCommerce with simpler workflows often start there. AI chat sits on top of these helpdesks - it answers shoppers in real time and posts the rest into the case queue. Don't shop for an AI tool expecting it to replace ticketing.

The data side bites too. AI is only as good as the catalog feeding it. Stale product titles, missing return-window fields, or out-of-date sizing will produce confidently wrong answers, and the fix is upstream in your PIM, not the AI vendor.

Channel coverage is the other honest gap. Most AI chat tools today, Nobi included, are web only. Shoppers who want SMS, WhatsApp, or voice will need a separate channel tool, and Nobi doesn't execute cancellations, returns, or refund actions inside the chat widget either - if shoppers need to self-serve those tasks in chat, that's a different category of tool.

Brand-voice persona chat (an AI stylist, a celebrity character) is also its own category. General AI chat platforms tune for accuracy and deflection, not for performing as a branded character.

## Frequently asked questions about implementing ecommerce live chat

Common questions support leads ask before turning live chat on: whether to keep your helpdesk, how long implementation takes, what pricing looks like across the main options, and how to handle hallucination risk.

**Do I still need a helpdesk if I deploy AI live chat?**

Yes. AI deflects questions; the helpdesk handles tickets the AI escalates and runs the agent workflow - macros, SLAs, returns, refunds. AI chat sits in front of a helpdesk, not in place of it.

**How long does implementation take?**

Days for AI-first tools that load via a catalog ingest plus a script tag. Weeks to months for enterprise platforms like Zendesk if you're configuring routing rules, SLAs, macros, and agent workflows from scratch.

**What does typical pricing look like?**

Nobi starts at $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 messages included), then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Re:amaze starts around $29/agent/month. Zendesk Suite Team is $55/agent/month before the $50/agent/month Advanced AI add-on. Intercom runs roughly $74/seat/month with Fin AI priced separately at $0.99 per resolution.

**Will AI hallucinate product details?**

LLMs grounded in your live catalog hallucinate far less than [generic assistants](https://nobi.ai/blog/chatgpt-alternatives-for-ecommerce), but it's not zero. Sample 50-100 conversations a month for accuracy and feed corrections back into your product data.

**Can I run AI chat without a helpdesk at all?**

Only if ticket volume is very low. Once you're escalating more than a handful of conversations a week, you'll want ticket workflow tooling underneath.
