## What does conversational AI for ecommerce sales assistance actually do?

Conversational AI for sales assistance is software that talks to shoppers on your site to help them find products, answer pre-purchase questions, deflect the routine shopper questions that would otherwise become tickets, and nudge them toward checkout. It overlaps with site search, support chatbots, and quiz funnels, but the job is different: the goal is incremental revenue. A ticketing helpdesk wins by processing tickets through agent tooling. A sales assistant wins by moving a browsing shopper one step closer to "add to cart" and keeping their questions from ever becoming a ticket in the first place.

In practice, the category breaks into four sub-types. Shopping assistants guide discovery through natural-language product questions. Proactive sales chatbots trigger on behavior, like a shopper lingering on a product page, and open a conversation. Quiz-led product finders walk shoppers through structured questions to narrow a catalog. AI-augmented helpdesks handle pre-sale questions as a side-job to their ticket workflow.

Most vendors specialize in one of these. A few combine search and chat in one deployment, which matters if you don't want two contracts and two data silos. Before shortlisting, decide which job is primary: discovery, proactive engagement, or qualification. The right tool depends on that choice.

## How should a head of ecommerce evaluate conversational AI vendors?

Evaluate conversational AI vendors on four axes: the job the tool is actually built for, the data it's allowed to use to personalize, the implementation lift, and the pricing model. Tools that combine multiple jobs under one roof (search plus chat, or chat plus lead capture) reduce your vendor count, but they may be weaker than point tools in any single dimension. And watch for revenue-share or per-conversation pricing that scales unpredictably as traffic grows.

Start by mapping each vendor to one of the four sub-categories before you compare features. A shopping copilot, a helpdesk bot, a quiz-led finder, and a branded character chat all call themselves "conversational AI," but they win on different metrics. Comparing them feature-by-feature without first agreeing on the job produces a spreadsheet that makes every vendor look reasonable and tells you nothing.

Pricing deserves the same scrutiny. Insist on a concrete example at your actual traffic level. If a vendor won't give you a number without a sales call, that's a signal their model gets expensive at scale. For reference, Nobi [publishes pricing on the site](https://nobi.ai/pricing): $25 per month for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A brand doing 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month can do the math before the demo.

Three more questions to ask every vendor on the shortlist:

- Does the assistant learn from shopper behavior automatically, or does it require merchandiser rules to stay relevant?
- What data can it read: catalog only, or also reviews, PDP copy, and sizing content?
- What's realistic time-to-live? Enterprise tools that promise the moon often take a full quarter to deploy. Good tools should be live in days.

If a vendor dodges any of these, assume the answer is the one they don't want to give.

Pricing is ticket-volume based, which can get unpredictable with auto-replies and reopens.

## How much does conversational AI for ecommerce cost?

Entry pricing for ecommerce conversational AI runs from free (Tidio's starter) to roughly $39–$95/month at 10K monthly visitors (Rep AI's Starter), with Nobi sitting at $25/month for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Gorgias starts around $10/month, but its AI Agent capability lives on higher tiers and bills against ticket volume. Octane AI's Basic tier is $50/month for 400 quiz credits. Alby's standalone pricing has not been publicly listed since Bluecore acquired it in November 2024, so plan on requesting a quote.

The headline number is rarely what you actually pay. Tidio's free tier covers 50 Lyro conversations, but past that, Lyro is roughly $0.50 per conversation on top of your seat plan, which climbs quickly once you outgrow the trial. Gorgias bills on ticket volume, and a third-party analysis of its model flagged that auto-replies, reopened tickets, and multi-channel threads can each count as billable, so a $10 starter plan is not what a real catalog with real shopper traffic ends up paying. Octane AI's $50 Basic tier holds 400 credits; brands running quizzes as a primary discovery surface tend to land on Plus at $200/month or Enterprise at $500+. Rep AI uses visitor-based pricing: Starter runs $39–$95/month at 10K monthly visitors, Pro $280–$740/month at 125K visitors, at roughly $12 per 1,000 visitors at low volume that drops at scale. Alby has moved toward outcome-based pricing tied to assisted conversions, which sounds attractive at low volume and gets expensive as GMV scales.

A practical note for anyone running the math: watch for revenue-share clauses. They look small at $5M GMV and painful at $50M. Nobi's pricing is per event, $0.01 a search and $0.10 a message, so a brand doing 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month pays about $525, not a percentage of revenue. The bill scales with usage, not with how successful you get.

Two more honest caveats. Nobi is web-chat only today, so if shoppers talk to you primarily in SMS or WhatsApp, pair Nobi with a tool built for those channels. And Nobi curates the search results page, not category and collection pages, so a brand that needs site-wide merchandising will still want a dedicated merchandising tool alongside it.

## What proof do these tools have of actually moving sales metrics?

Ask every vendor for named-customer revenue, CVR, and AOV numbers, not generic "lift" percentages from anonymous composites. Public, attributable case studies are the cleanest signal that a tool actually moves the needle, because a brand willing to attach its logo and specific dollar figures to a result is a brand that has verified the math internally.

For Nobi, [Lucchese is the concrete reference](https://nobi.ai/customers). The luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus reported $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one, a 39x ROI, and $3.46M in cumulative attributed revenue across the deployment. That deployment is search plus a cart assistant and a PDP assistant, so the assistant surfaces contribute alongside the search results page rather than standing alone. Faherty's Shop with AI, a conversational shopping assistant deployment, reports a 6.3% CVR with a $315 AOV, which is meaningful context for an apparel buyer trying to forecast what a chat surface can plausibly drive on their own storefront.

When you collect numbers from any vendor on your shortlist, push on methodology. Was the lift measured against a holdout group or an A/B test, or was it a before-and-after comparison that conveniently coincided with a site redesign, a paid media push, or a seasonal peak? A 20% CVR lift measured against a non-exposed holdout is a different piece of evidence than a 20% CVR lift measured year over year.

Be skeptical of vendors who will only share aggregated "across our customers" percentages without naming brands. Aggregate numbers are marketing artifacts, not evidence. If a vendor can't point you to a named retailer, a specific metric, and a defensible measurement approach, treat the number as directional at best, and weight the shortlist toward the tools whose case studies you can actually pressure-test with a reference call.

## How fast can a ecommerce team actually get conversational AI live?

Time-to-live ranges from a single afternoon for Tidio's free chat widget to a full quarter or more for enterprise platforms that demand data-engineering work. Most Shopify-native tools in this roundup, Nobi, Alby, Rep AI, and Octane AI, install and configure in days through their app-store listings. The catch: shopper-behavior personalization usually needs one to two weeks of live traffic before rankings and recommendations stabilize, so plan on a soft-launch window before you judge performance.

Helpdesk-style implementations like Gorgias take longer because you're configuring a ticketing system at the same time as the AI agent, which means macros, routing rules, and channel connections before the bot answers its first shopper.

Where vendors really diverge is customization beyond defaults. Themed widgets and out-of-the-box flows work for many brands, but some merchandising teams need engineering control over how products render inside the assistant. Ask every shortlisted vendor whether they expose a developer-controlled customization path, not just a CSS editor. Nobi ships a [Hooks API](https://nobi.ai/custom-actions) for exactly this. The capability originated from UNTUCKit, who wanted control over product display inside the assistant, and is now available to every Nobi customer. A ecommerce team on Shopify can reasonably expect Nobi live in a week, with personalization dialed in by week three.
