## What does a chatbot actually do for an automotive dealership?

On a dealership site, the assistant's real job is narrower than the marketing pitch. It answers questions a shopper has while staring at a VDP - is this trim in stock, what's the payment, can I book a test drive Saturday - and captures their contact info before they bounce. The best ones also help shoppers find the right vehicle in the first place, handling fuzzy year/make/model queries that [stock site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search) fumbles. Three jobs: lead capture, after-hours coverage, and inventory discovery. Most dealerships don't own their site code, so the tool has to drop in as a widget.

The market splits two ways. Nobi sits in the search-and-assistant lane: vehicle discovery plus a conversational layer that answers shopper questions and captures intent, installed via two JavaScript snippets on the existing storefront. [Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) is proactive sales chat, with behavioral AI that intervenes mid-session to push toward a lead form. [Tidio](https://tidio.com)'s Lyro agent leans into support automation, useful when after-hours service questions dominate. [Octane AI](https://octaneai.com) owns the quiz-led product finder lane on Shopify - a fit for dealer accessory stores more than the main inventory site. Pick by the job your site actually loses traffic on.

## 1. Nobi

## 2. Rep AI

For a dealership marketing team, that means the same person running the year-end sale or a new model launch can wire up a campaign-specific chat flow without filing an engineering ticket. A live-agent drop-in lets a sales rep join an active AI conversation mid-thread while the AI keeps participating, instead of forcing a binary handoff. Pricing starts at $12 per 1,000 visitors on the Pay As You Grow tier. One caveat worth confirming up front: Rep AI was built for Shopify, so a dealership running on a dedicated automotive website platform should validate deployment fit before signing.

**Best for:** Dealership marketing teams who want to script campaign-specific conversation flows (year-end sales, new model launches, service-special outreach) without engineering, or who want a sales rep to jump into an active AI chat mid-thread.

**Pricing:** $12 per 1,000 visitors on the Pay As You Grow tier (unlimited sessions, 500-product catalog cap). At ~125K monthly sessions, Pro tiers run $280/mo (Support), $616/mo (Sales), $740/mo (Bundle). 30-day free trial.

**Pros:**
- Flow Studio visual builder lets the marketing team design conversation flows for promotions or model launches without engineering tickets
- Live-agent drop-in is a true hybrid - a sales rep can join an active AI conversation while the AI keeps participating, rather than a binary handoff
- Post-Order Tasks Agent handles after-purchase requests inside the chat, which can extend to service-appointment lookups in an automotive context
- Drop-off-reasons analytics tell you why a shopper bailed mid-conversation, not just that they did

**Cons:**
- Discovery happens entirely through the chat surface - shoppers find vehicles by asking the assistant rather than through a dedicated browse or filter UI
- Built for Shopify; deploying on a dedicated automotive website platform may require custom work or won't be supported

**Verdict:** Pick Rep AI when scripted campaign flows and AI-plus-human hybrid chat are the priority and you can deploy it on your site; if your bigger problem is shoppers not finding the right vehicle, pair it with a search tool or pick something else.

## 3. Tidio

[Tidio](https://tidio.com) is a live-chat platform with Lyro layered on top as the AI responder for first-touch and after-hours questions. The paradigm matters for a dealership GM evaluating this category: Tidio assumes humans still own the conversation and AI fills the gaps, not the other way around. If your BDC team already staffs a chat widget during sales hours and the real problem is what happens at 9pm or on Sunday morning, that framing maps cleanly to how the tool behaves day-to-day. The agent inbox, keyboard shortcuts, and multi-channel routing are built for human operators first. Lyro is positioned as the safety net that catches questions when reps are off the floor or at capacity.

**Best for:** Dealerships whose priority is a 24/7 staffed chat widget with BDC reps in the loop and AI covering after-hours and first-touch questions.

**Pricing:** Free tier available. Paid plans from around $24/month (Starter, 100 conversations) and $49/month (Growth, 250 conversations). Lyro AI is available as an add-on to any Tidio plan or included in the Plus plan, with a free trial of 50 Lyro conversations.

**Pros:**
- Well-designed agent inbox with keyboard shortcuts that BDC and internet-sales teams will be productive in
- AI and human modes are complementary rather than competing, so the after-hours-to-business-hours handoff feels native
- Multi-channel coverage beyond web chat (Messenger, Instagram, email) for shoppers who reach out across surfaces
- Lowest-friction free tier in this list for a dealership that wants to pilot AI shopper support before committing budget

**Cons:**
- Lyro is first-touch oriented rather than a deep conversational discovery agent - shoppers hit the ceiling on detailed inventory or financing questions quickly
- Weaker on semantic vehicle discovery than tools whose primary surface is search, so it's not the right pick if browse-and-find on the VDP is the conversion gap

**Verdict:** Pick Tidio when the job is staffing a 24/7 chat widget with humans plus an AI safety net; skip it when the real problem is helping shoppers find the right vehicle in the first place.

## 4. Octane AI

[Octane AI](https://octaneai.com) is a quiz-led product finder built primarily for Shopify, where the quiz is the hero discovery surface rather than open-ended chat or site search. For a dealership GM, the practical question is whether a guided vehicle-finder quiz - "how many passengers, towing needs, body style, budget" - is the right way to route a shopper to inventory. If shoppers land on your site without a model in mind, a scripted quiz tree can narrow a 200-vehicle lot to three credible matches in under a minute. The catch is platform fit: Octane AI is concentrated in Shopify and in beauty and CPG verticals, so any automotive deployment hinges on confirming the platform can ingest a vehicle inventory feed and map answers to attributes like drivetrain, seating, and towing capacity.

**Best for:** Dealerships whose primary discovery surface is a guided vehicle-finder quiz - typically broad-inventory lots where shoppers don't yet know the model they want.

**Pricing:** Basic at $50/month (400 credits), Plus at $200/month (2,200 credits), Enterprise & Unlimited at $500+/month.

**Pros:**
- Quiz builder with deep product-attribute mapping that can translate to vehicle attributes (body style, drivetrain, seating, towing capacity) when configured against an inventory feed
- Pre-built quiz templates shorten time-to-launch versus building a vehicle-finder flow in-house
- Native Klaviyo integration captures quiz answers as email-segment attributes, useful for lifecycle nurture after a quiz completion
- Lower engineering overhead than building a custom vehicle-finder on top of a dealer-platform CMS

**Cons:**
- Quiz-centric, so it doesn't help shoppers who already know the model they want and just want to search inventory directly
- Built primarily for Shopify and concentrated in beauty and CPG verticals - automotive deployment will require confirming the platform supports a vehicle inventory feed

**Verdict:** Pick Octane AI when a guided vehicle-finder quiz is genuinely the right discovery metaphor for your shoppers; skip it if the bottleneck is direct year/make/model search or open-ended chat.

## Frequently asked questions

Common questions dealership GMs and operations leads ask before picking an AI assistant for the website. Quick answers below.

**Will an ecommerce-built tool deploy on DealerOn, Dealer.com, or DealerInspire?** Most ecom-native assistants assume Shopify or BigCommerce themes. They can usually be embedded via a script tag, but DMS-tied page templates and inventory widgets often need vendor support to wire up cleanly. Confirm platform compatibility in writing before you sign.

**How do these tools handle year/make/model queries against an inventory feed?** Automotive-specific vendors ingest your inventory feed and parse Y/M/M filters natively. Generic ecom search treats "2022 RAV4 Hybrid AWD under 30k" as free text and routinely misses matches. Ask for a live demo against your own feed.

**Can it push captured leads into VinSolutions, DealerSocket, or Elead?** Native CRM connectors are the dividing line. Without one, you are posting via webhook or Zapier - workable but fragile and a known source of dropped leads.

**Does it handle SMS?** Most ecom-built assistants, Nobi included, are web chat only. If text is the channel your shoppers actually use, pick a tool with native SMS.

**How long does implementation take?** Days for snippet-based installs (Nobi installs by dropping two JavaScript snippets), weeks to months for anything requiring DMS or custom CRM mapping.

**What happens after-hours?** The AI answers the question, books the test drive, and writes the lead to your CRM so the BDC has it on open.
