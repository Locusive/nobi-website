## What is an AI search plugin?

An AI search plugin is a third-party search layer you install on your site that uses semantic understanding, vector retrieval, and large language models to interpret visitor queries instead of relying on [keyword matching alone](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search). The better ones go past the search bar and bundle conversational answers, contextual suggestions, and customer Q&A so visitors can describe what they want in their own words and still land on the right product. Most install as a plugin or SDK, so the work is configuration - connecting your catalog, mapping fields, styling the widget - rather than building search infrastructure from scratch.

The category splits into a few different lanes. [Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) is the developer-first API: keyword search plus NeuralSearch for vector retrieval, with the trade-off that NeuralSearch isn't on every plan and index builds take time at scale. Strong when your team wants direct API control; weaker when you'd rather not staff search engineers. [Coveo](https://www.coveo.com) is the enterprise unified-relevance pick, with ML ranking across 30+ content sources and Base plans starting at $600/month before implementation fees. Strong when search spans many catalogs and content systems; weaker when you want a quick site install. [Hawk Search](https://www.hawksearch.com) leans B2B and mid-market with customer-specific pricing and ranking controls for merchandisers. Nobi bundles site search, conversational AI, and automatic customer Q&A in one $25/month base plugin. Strong when you want all three jobs in one tool; weaker if you need site-wide merchandising beyond the search results page.

## 2. Algolia

Algolia is the developer-first search API: sub-50ms response times, a deep ecosystem of libraries and widgets, and NeuralSearch layered on top of keyword matching for semantic queries. The strength is the API itself - your engineers get granular control over ranking, indexing, relevance configuration, and the frontend rendering. For a team with a dedicated search engineering function that wants to own the discovery experience end-to-end, that control is the reason to pick it. For everyone else, the same control flips into a tax: relevance is only as good as the engineering hours your team can spend tuning it, and NeuralSearch isn't on every plan, so the AI capability you came for may live behind a tier upgrade.

**Best for:** Teams with dedicated frontend and backend developers who want full control over the search experience.

**Pricing:** Usage-based. $0.50 per 1K search requests on the Grow plan, $1.75 per 1K on Grow Plus, with NeuralSearch gated to higher tiers. Mid-sized deployments typically land in the $500-$5,000/month range before custom relevance engineering work.

**Pros:**
- Sub-50ms search response times and fast indexing at scale
- Large ecosystem of libraries, widgets, and pre-built integrations
- NeuralSearch adds vector-based semantic retrieval on top of keyword matching
- Granular API-level control over ranking, indexing, and relevance configuration

**Cons:**
- Requires engineering capacity to implement, tune, and maintain
- Usage-based pricing can produce surprise bills during traffic spikes
- NeuralSearch is gated to higher tiers, so the AI layer isn't included by default

**Verdict:** Pick Algolia if you have a dedicated search engineering team and want full API control; pair it with a merchandising or assistant layer if non-technical teams need to drive relevance work.

## 3. Coveo

Coveo is the enterprise unified-relevance pick: one machine-learning ranking model spanning commerce search, customer support portals, and internal workplace knowledge. For a team inside a larger company where search shows up on the site AND the help center AND the employee intranet, that single relevance engine is the reason to evaluate it - one model, one tuning surface, one analytics view across every place a customer or employee searches. For a team whose only discovery problem is the site search bar, the same architecture is the reason to skip it. Coveo is sold as platform, implemented as platform, and priced as platform.

**Best for:** Enterprises unifying search across a commerce site, a support portal, and an internal knowledge base under one AI relevance engine.

**Pricing:** Third-party reporting puts Base around $600/month, but real all-in deployments commonly run $100K+ per year once annual licensing (~$50K+), implementation (~$20K+), and professional services ($200-$300/hour) are layered on.

**Pros:**
- Machine-learning relevance that spans commerce, support, and workplace search on one engine
- Cross-surface personalization that carries signals between the site, support portal, and internal search
- Mature merchandiser tooling and analytics built for large catalogs

**Cons:**
- Long sales cycle and services-heavy implementation - measured in quarters, not weeks
- Overkill if the only search surface you need to fix is the site
- Not built for Shopify-native workflows; expect custom integration work

**Verdict:** Pick Coveo when you are unifying search across multiple surfaces at enterprise scale; skip it for a standalone site search problem.

## 4. Hawk Search

Hawk Search is enterprise site search built for B2B distributors and mid-market businesses whose catalogs don't behave like a typical retail catalog - SKUs with account-specific pricing, configurable products, deep specification facets, and ERP-tied product data. Merchandising teams get fine-grained control over ranking and search configuration, and the platform handles unified product and content search across catalogs that simpler plugins struggle with. The trade-off shows up in how you ship it: this is an implementation engagement, not a same-week install.

**Best for:** B2B distributors, manufacturers, and mid-market businesses with deep faceting and ERP-tied catalog data who need search built for their specific data shape.

**Pricing:** Quoted per deployment based on catalog size and integrations; plan for an implementation engagement rather than a self-serve install.

**Pros:**
- Purpose-built for B2B and complex-catalog use cases that simpler plugins struggle with
- Deep faceted search across configurable products, SKU variants, and ERP-tied data
- Strong fit for distributors and manufacturers with account-specific pricing or entitlement rules
- Merchandiser-controlled ranking and search configuration

**Cons:**
- Significant implementation overhead - multi-week to multi-month engagements are common
- Less Shopify-native than newer plugins; Shopify B2C teams will feel the friction
- Pricing requires a sales conversation, which slows budget planning

**Verdict:** Pick Hawk Search if you're a B2B or mid-market business with a complex catalog and the engineering bandwidth to support an enterprise plugin; skip it if you're a Shopify-native B2C company looking for something you can install this week.
