## What is an MCP server, and why does it matter for an ecommerce store?

An MCP server is a callable endpoint that speaks the Model Context Protocol, an open standard AI agents like Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity use to discover and query external data and actions. For an ecommerce store, it's what lets an agent ask your business directly: what's in stock, what does this product cost, what's your return policy, what fits a 6'1" runner. As more shopper research moves into AI agents, exposing a callable endpoint is becoming the agent-era equivalent of having a website. Most ecommerce tools, including the chat and helpdesk tools listed below, don't yet expose one.

A typical ecommerce MCP server makes products, inventory, pricing, policies, and FAQ answers available to agents through a standard the agent already knows how to call. ChatGPT Operator, Claude with tool use, and Perplexity Shopping are early examples consuming endpoints in this shape.

## How did we evaluate these AI-agent endpoints for ecommerce?

Four criteria did the heavy lifting: whether the tool ships a callable endpoint an AI agent can discover and query, how often its connected sources refresh, what it actually costs at real volume, and how its answers are grounded so an agent won't repeat a fabricated price or policy. The four tools we looked at are Nobi, Rep AI, Alby, and Gorgias - the ones ecommerce teams actually deploy when they want to answer shopper questions or expose store data programmatically.

The callable-endpoint question is the first cut, because it's binary. Either an agent like Claude or Perplexity can call the tool over a standard protocol or it can't. Most chat and helpdesk tools today don't expose one - they were built to render a widget on the storefront, not to answer an API call from somewhere off-site.

Refresh cadence is the second cut, and it matters more for agents than for human shoppers. A human reading a stale FAQ page will at least notice the date in the footer. An agent will quote whatever it's told, so a knowledge base that syncs once a week ships week-old prices into agent answers. We checked how often each tool re-reads connected sources.

Pricing is the third cut. "Contact sales" doesn't help anyone modeling a bill at 50,000 messages a month, so we pulled concrete numbers where they're published and flagged the gaps where they aren't.

Grounding is the fourth. For each tool we looked at what its answers are anchored to and whether the source is visible to the end shopper or just to the operator.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi runs as on-site search and an [AI shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce), and the same endpoint that grounds shopper chats also answers agent queries through the Model Context Protocol. There's no second integration, no separate API to maintain, no parallel content pipeline.

What makes this compounding is what happens to those shopper conversations. Every question a real visitor asks on a product page gets cleaned, sanitized, and added to a structured FAQ that lives on that page and becomes part of what the endpoint serves. A shopper who asks about sizing at 2pm is contributing to the answer an AI agent surfaces at 8pm - and the answer the next agent gets three months from now. The more people interact with your store through Nobi, the richer and more specific your store's answers get, and the better it performs when AI agents query it.

The answer engine reads from your product catalog and the knowledge sources you connect (product pages, FAQ routes, policy docs, PDFs), refreshes those sources twice a day, and ships every answer with inline citation pills back to the exact document and excerpt it came from.

**Best for:** Ecommerce teams who want one platform that answers shopper questions on the storefront and exposes a callable endpoint that ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity agents can discover and query.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages). $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**
- Every Nobi deployment is also an MCP endpoint with no extra integration work; the agent-callable surface ships alongside the on-site shopper experience.
- Shopper conversations automatically build a structured, crawlable FAQ on your product pages - so your store gets more findable and answerable to AI agents the more customers interact with it.
- Connected knowledge sources refresh twice a day, so a price or policy change lands in agent answers within hours instead of waiting on a weekly sync.
- Inline numbered citation pills on every answer; hovering shows the source document name, date, and the exact excerpt, so an agent (or anyone auditing one) can trace any claim back to your published content.
- An optional second AI review checks each draft answer against the raw cited sources before it sends, on by default for high-consideration categories.

**Cons:**
- Doesn't execute in-chat post-purchase transactions like cancellations, returns, or order tracking; those still need a helpdesk or a dedicated post-order task agent.
- Personalization today is limited to personalized placeholder text and starter messages; behavioral reranking that reorders results by individual shopper history isn't shipped yet.

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when you want one platform handling on-site shopper discovery and a callable agent endpoint in the same deployment; pair it with a helpdesk or a post-purchase task agent if shoppers also need to cancel or return inside chat.

## 2. Rep AI

[Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) is a proactive-chat tool built for Shopify whose strongest move is in-chat post-purchase task execution. Its Post-Order Tasks Agent lets a shopper cancel an order, start a return, or check tracking inside the chat widget on the storefront, without a handoff to an email queue or a helpdesk ticket. Marketing-ops teams can also script campaign-specific conversation flows in Flow Studio, a visual no-code builder, so a Black Friday flow or a product-launch pitch can go live without filing engineering tickets. A live-agent drop-in feature lets a human join an ongoing AI session while the AI keeps handling it, instead of a hard handoff that pauses the AI.

**Best for:** Shopify merchants who want shoppers to self-serve cancellations, returns, and tracking inside the chat widget, or marketing-ops teams who want to script campaign-specific conversation flows without engineering.

**Pricing:** $12 per 1,000 visitors on the Pay As You Grow tier (unlimited sessions, 500-product catalog cap). At ~125K monthly sessions, Pro tiers run $280/mo (Support), $616/mo (Sales), $740/mo (Bundle). Enterprise custom above ~500K sessions. 30-day free trial.

**Pros:**
- Post-Order Tasks Agent lets shoppers self-serve cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups inside the chat widget
- Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder, so marketing can design conversation flows for promos, product launches, and abandoned-cart rescue without engineering
- Live-agent drop-in is a true hybrid: a human can join an AI session while the AI keeps handling it, instead of a binary handoff
- Drop-off-reason analytics tell you why shoppers abandoned, not just that they did

**Cons:**
- Sales-chat only, with no site search; product discovery on category and search pages isn't covered
- The 500-product catalog cap on the entry Pay As You Grow tier is tight for many ecommerce catalogs
- Personalization is scoped to the chat session, not the wider storefront experience

**Verdict:** Pick Rep AI when in-chat post-purchase task execution or no-code flow design is the conversion lever you most need.

## 3. Alby

[Alby](https://alby.com) answers shopper questions right on the Shopify product detail page, at the moment someone is staring at a specific item and weighing whether to add to cart. The widget is built for human shoppers on a PDP, with shopper intent already narrowed to a specific SKU, and its UX is tuned for that moment of add-to-cart consideration. Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024, so the product now sits inside Bluecore's identity and predictive-marketing stack rather than running standalone. For brands already on Bluecore, that means PDP Q&A becomes one more layer of the platform they already use.

**Best for:** Shopify brands already on Bluecore who want product-page Q&A inside that stack.

**Pricing:** Bundled into Bluecore platform pricing after the acquisition; no standalone public price.

**Pros:**
- Focused UX for product-page Q&A, where shopper intent is already locked to a specific SKU
- Plugs into Bluecore's identity and predictive-marketing stack without a second integration
- Cuts pre-purchase support email volume by answering common PDP questions inline

**Cons:**
- Chat only, with no search; shoppers earlier in the discovery funnel aren't covered by the widget
- Scope is the product page; category, collection, and search-page moments aren't part of the product
- Standalone roadmap is uncertain after the Bluecore acquisition; future direction depends on Bluecore's strategy

**Verdict:** Pick Alby if Bluecore is already your predictive-marketing platform and PDP Q&A is the one gap; look elsewhere if you need anything beyond the product page.

## 4. Gorgias

[Gorgias](https://gorgias.com) is an ecommerce helpdesk built around agent-side workflow: tickets, routing, macros, returns, and refund processing, with Shopify order context rendered directly inside each ticket so an agent can cancel, refund, or modify an order without leaving the case. A newer AI Agent layer sits on top on higher-priced plans and handles repetitive inbound tickets. The AI runs inside Gorgias's own workflow to help agents close tickets faster - the focus is on the support team's inbox, not on programmatic access from outside systems.

**Best for:** Support-heavy brands whose bottleneck is ticket workflow, case routing, and structured returns handling rather than pre-purchase discovery.

**Pricing:** Tiered by ticket volume. The base plan starts low; the AI Agent add-on sits on higher-tier plans. Gorgias publishes the plan-by-plan breakdown on its pricing page.

**Pros:**
- Deep Shopify order context surfaced inside every ticket, so agents can cancel, refund, or modify an order from the ticket view
- Mature agent-side automation: macros, routing rules, SLA tracking, multi-brand tenant support
- Structured returns and refund workflows that AI-chat-only tools don't replicate
- Multi-channel coverage across email, chat, and social for teams consolidating support inboxes

**Cons:**
- No product discovery or site search; this is a helpdesk for managing tickets, not a pre-purchase shopping experience
- AI features only ship on higher-tier plans, so total cost jumps once you turn them on

**Verdict:** Pick Gorgias when agent-side support workflow is the problem you're solving - tickets, routing, macros, returns processing.

## Which agent endpoint fits the job you're actually trying to do?

Start with the buyer, not the tool. If your job is exposing store data to external AI agents that research and shop on behalf of users, Nobi is the only tool in this list whose endpoint is also an MCP server out of the box. If the job is shopper-side post-purchase task execution inside a chat widget on Shopify, Rep AI is the strongest pick - its Post-Order Tasks Agent handles cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups. If you're already on Bluecore and the gap is PDP Q&A, Alby plugs in without a second integration. If your bottleneck is agent-side ticket throughput - returns, refunds, case routing - Gorgias is the mature answer.

Most teams end up running two of these together - Nobi for [product discovery](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-product-finders-ecommerce) and agent-endpoint coverage, plus a helpdesk like Gorgias for structured post-purchase workflows.

## Frequently asked questions

**What is MCP, in one sentence?** Model Context Protocol is an open standard AI agents use to discover and call external data sources and tools, so a ChatGPT or Claude session can query your store without a custom integration for each agent.

**Do AI agents actually drive traffic and purchases today?** It's still early, but the direction is clear. ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity all now run shopping-style queries on behalf of users, and the brands whose data agents can actually reach are the ones showing up in those answers. Exposing a callable endpoint is the agent-era equivalent of having a sitemap.

**Does Shopify already give me an agent endpoint?** Shopify is rolling out agent-facing surfaces, but they cover catalog basics like products and prices. They don't expose your FAQ, return policy, sizing notes, or any custom knowledge content. A dedicated assistant like Nobi handles that layer.

**What should an ecommerce MCP server expose?** Products, inventory, pricing, shipping and return policies, and the FAQ content shoppers actually ask about. Anything an agent might quote on your behalf belongs in the connected sources.

**How do I keep an agent from quoting wrong prices or stale policies?** Pick a tool that grounds answers in your live sources and cites them. Nobi refreshes connected sources twice a day and ships every answer with a citation back to the document and excerpt it came from, so a wrong answer leaves an audit trail rather than a mystery.
