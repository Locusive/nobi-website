## What does an AI shopping assistant actually do on an ecommerce site?

An AI shopping assistant sits between the shopper and your catalog and handles some mix of five jobs: understanding natural-language search queries, recommending products in context, answering product questions, deflecting pre- and post-purchase support volume, and capturing leads or guiding checkout. The category stretches from pure search engines with a chat skin to conversational agents that replace the help widget.

Most tools are strong at one or two of those jobs, not all of them. Nobi is the exception in this article - it covers search, discovery, Q&A, and support deflection under one platform. Rep AI focuses on proactive chat that intercepts browsers mid-session. Octane AI is the category leader for quiz-led product finders. Gorgias is a helpdesk-first ticket workflow tool for the agent side. Tidio is primarily live human chat with an AI responder. Alby was a Shopify PDP Q&A tool, now part of Bluecore.

The useful question isn't which demo is shiniest. It's which job is the real bottleneck on your site, and whether you want one platform covering the biggest slice of it or multiple point solutions stitched together.

## When do Rep AI or Octane AI fit alongside Nobi?

Both of these are specialized tools that handle one job really well. They're not substitutes for Nobi on search, Q&A, or deflection - use them alongside Nobi when your bottleneck is the specific job they're built for.

**Rep AI** fits three specific jobs Nobi doesn't do, plus one softer fourth.

First, **Flow Studio** - Rep AI's visual no-code builder for scripted conversation flows. Marketing ops teams can design deterministic conversation trees per campaign: a Black Friday flow that pitches the seasonal bundle, a product-launch sequence that walks shoppers through the new SKU, an abandoned-cart rescue that triggers on exit intent with specific recovery copy. Nobi's assistant is LLM-grounded in your catalog - more flexible for open-ended shopper questions, less controllable when you want a specific path for a specific moment. If your team wants deterministic conversation paths they design and control, Rep AI's Flow Studio is the better fit.

Second, **live-agent drop-in on AI conversations.** A human agent can jump INTO an ongoing AI thread mid-session while the AI continues handling it. That's a true AI+human hybrid, not a binary "handoff" that pauses the AI. Useful for stores that want a human safety net on high-value sessions or edge cases while the AI handles volume. Nobi's assistant runs AI-first without that hybrid mode.

Third, **Post-Order Tasks Agent** - Rep AI's shopper-facing in-chat execution of post-purchase transactions: cancellations, returns, tracking lookups inside the chat without a human agent. This is different from Gorgias, which is agent-side workflow tooling; Rep AI's version is self-serve for the shopper. Nobi deflects post-purchase questions (answers them) but doesn't execute the transaction inside the chat widget. If "shoppers self-serve a return inside chat" is a required feature, Rep AI is the pick.

Fourth (softer), **drop-off-reasons analytics.** Rep AI's insights surface WHY shoppers abandon in behavioral terms, not just the fact that they did. If that analytical lens is a primary lever for your team, Rep AI's reporting is more targeted at it than Nobi's search + CVR-focused analytics.

Pricing is tiered by monthly visitors - starting at $12 per 1,000 visitors at the smallest tier, dropping as volume scales. A 10K-visitor Starter plan runs $39 to $95 per month depending on plan type (Support, Sales, or Bundle). A 125K-visitor Pro plan runs $280 to $740.

One note on proactive chat: Rep AI is the most mature purpose-built proactive-chat tool available today, and if that's your bottleneck right now, it fits the job. Nobi is also building proactive triggers into its assistant - brands who don't need proactive deployed this quarter may prefer to wait for Nobi's proactive rollout so they get search, discovery, Q&A, deflection, and proactive chat under one platform instead of running Rep AI as a separate vendor.

**Octane AI** is the better pick for beauty, skincare, and CPG brands where a guided quiz is the hero discovery flow. Shoppers in those categories often don't know whether they want the retinol serum or the peptide one, and a five-question quiz is a better answer than a search box. Octane's Basic plan starts at $50 per month with 400 credits, scaling to Plus at $200 per month with 2,200 credits, and Enterprise from $500+. Pair that with the Klaviyo handoff and the quiz doubles as a discovery surface and list-building engine. Nobi doesn't ship quiz-led product finders, so if a quiz funnel is the front door of your store, buy Octane.

## What about Gorgias, Tidio, and Alby - when are those the right pick?

These three tools each own a different lane that isn't product discovery, and each is worth picking for the right reason.

**Gorgias** is the right call when agent-side workflow tooling is the bottleneck. It's a full ecommerce helpdesk with deep Shopify order context and purpose-built ticket management - case routing, macros, returns processing, refund handling, the whole post-purchase operational surface that a support team works inside. Gorgias pricing is ticket-based, from roughly $10/month at Starter up to $900/month at Advanced, with overages around $0.36–$0.40 per ticket and the AI Agent billed per automated resolution on top.

Worth being clear on the Nobi-vs-Gorgias framing: Nobi deflects support volume by answering shopper questions without an agent, so it reduces ticket inflow. Gorgias is what your agents work inside once a case reaches them. The two are complements, not substitutes. If "our agents spend two hours a day on WISMO" is your sentence, Nobi deflects the inbound; Gorgias is the workflow your team runs the rest on.

**Tidio** fits brands whose priority is live human-agent chat with handoff, not AI-led product discovery. Its Lyro AI responder handles first-touch questions when an agent isn't online, and agents pick up the rest live. The free tier lets you test with 50 Lyro conversations before committing; paid plans start at $29/month with Lyro from $39. If the bottleneck is staffing a 24/7 live-chat widget with human handoff (not discovery or deflection at scale), Tidio is approachable. If the bottleneck is shoppers failing to find products in a large catalog, Nobi is the better fit.

**Alby** is listed for completeness but isn't a recommendation. Its lane was Shopify PDP product Q&A, which Nobi covers natively as part of the assistant. Alby was acquired by Bluecore in November 2024, and the independent product roadmap has been uncertain since. It's only worth considering if you're already committed to the Bluecore ecosystem and want the integrated Q&A surface there.

## How do AI shopping assistants price, and where do the hidden costs show up?

Pricing in this category falls into three buckets: flat SaaS, usage-based with clear unit economics, and revenue-share or per-resolution pricing that scales with your success. The hidden costs almost always show up in the same three places: revenue-share tiers that tax your upside, AI features gated to higher plans, and engineering time required to launch.

Nobi publishes exact unit pricing. $25/month base includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. After that, $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month pays roughly $995, with no revenue share on top. You can model cost at any traffic level before you sign, and the bill doesn't rise faster than your traffic does.

Rep AI publishes a session-slider calculator at hellorep.ai/pricing. Per-visitor rate starts at $12 per 1,000 visitors at the smallest tier (that's the highest unit rate) and drops as volume scales. Concrete anchors: 10K visitors on the Starter plan runs $39 to $95 per month depending on plan type; 125K on Pro runs $280 to $740.

Octane AI publishes three plans starting at $50/month (Basic, 400 credits), $200/month (Plus, 2,200 credits), and $500+/month for Enterprise. Credits apply to quiz interactions, so cost scales with quiz engagement rather than outcomes.

Gorgias is where the math gets trickier. The helpdesk starts at $10/month for Starter, but AI Agent is billed per automated resolution on top of ticket-based pricing, and third-party buyers have flagged double-charging when a ticket touches both a human and an AI. Model the resolution volume at your projected ticket load before you sign.

Tidio has a free tier; paid plans from $29 per month with Lyro AI from $39. Alby doesn't publish pricing at all post-acquisition.

The head-of-ecommerce lens: a revenue-share or per-resolution deal that looks cheap at $5M GMV can turn punitive at $50M. Model the unit economics at your 18-month projected volume before you sign anything.

## How long does it actually take to launch an AI shopping assistant?

Time-to-launch varies more than vendors admit, and the honest answer depends on what you're deploying. A chat-only tool like Tidio can go live in an afternoon: drop in a script, point it at your Shopify catalog, write a few FAQ answers, and you're taking conversations the same day.

A search-plus-assistant deployment is a different animal. You're indexing the full catalog, mapping attributes (fit, fabric, occasion, use case), and reviewing merchandising defaults before the first shopper types a query. With Nobi, that work typically runs 2 to 4 weeks for a full deployment. Enterprise search platforms usually run 2 to 4 months once integration, indexing, and merchandising rules are agreed on, reviewed, and QA'd, largely because the configuration surface is bigger and the implementation is often gated behind a services contract.

The variable that blows up timelines is customization. Product teams routinely want search results to render differently than the vendor's default grid, which is where most implementations stall. Nobi's Hooks API exists specifically for this. UNTUCKit asked for developer-controlled logic over how products display in the results panel, and that request became the Hooks capability every Nobi customer now has access to. It covers most bespoke rendering requests without forcing a full custom frontend build.

One honest caveat: if you want to own the ranking logic yourself, or embed search inside a heavily custom storefront where you control every pixel, an API-first developer platform will fit you better than Nobi. Hooks handles rendering, not ranking algorithms you want to write from scratch.

## What results should I expect from deploying an AI shopping assistant?

Expectations depend on which job the assistant is doing and the shape of your catalog. On an apparel catalog, Faherty's Shop with AI experience runs at a 6.3% conversion rate with a $315 AOV, well above typical site-wide ecommerce averages. On a multi-surface deployment, Lucchese paired Nobi search with a PDP assistant and a cart assistant and reported $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one, $3.46M cumulative revenue attributed, and a 39x ROI. The cart-assistant and PDP-assistant surfaces drove meaningful incremental checkouts alongside search - a useful data point if you're debating whether to roll out beyond the search box on day one.

Treat those numbers as anchors, not forecasts. The same assistant can produce very different economics across verticals: what works on a $500 Western boot won't automatically work on a $20 consumable where shoppers don't need help deciding.

The honest way to model this: stand the assistant up on an A/B traffic split, measure incremental revenue against your current baseline CVR and AOV, and run it long enough to clear seasonal noise. Compare against your own numbers, not the vendor's best-case customer logo. If the lift holds at a fraction of Faherty's or Lucchese's results, the math usually still works.
