## What is a vehicle recommendation AI for a car dealer?

A vehicle recommendation AI is software that sits on a dealership's website (and sometimes its phone, text, and social channels) and helps a shopper narrow down to the right vehicle in inventory. It reads year, make, model, trim, body style, and budget cues from the conversation or search query, then surfaces matching VDPs from your live inventory feed and answers questions about price, financing, availability, and trade-in - the same questions a salesperson would handle on the floor. The job is shopper-to-inventory matching: turning a vague "I want a midsize SUV under $35k" into a short list of VDPs the shopper can actually act on. Because the tool sits on top of the live feed, recommendations stay current as cars sell or get added to the lot.

This is different from a general-purpose website chat widget. Dealership tools handle year/make/model queries, VDP context, appointment booking, and trade-in conversations natively. Some focus on the website surface; others extend to SMS, phone, social, and CRM follow-up. The shortlist worth knowing includes [Nobi](https://nobi.ai), [DealerAI](https://dealerai.com), [Impel](https://impel.ai), [Matador AI](https://matador.ai), and [DriveCentric](https://drivecentric.com), each leading in a different part of the workflow.

## 2. DealerAI

[DealerAI](https://dealerai.com) is a multi-agent generative AI platform built specifically for car dealerships. The product calls itself a Multi-Agent Generative System, or MAGS, and splits work across separate Sales, Service, Parts, and Finance agents instead of routing every shopper conversation through a single assistant. The on-site jobs it focuses on are appointment scheduling, inventory inquiries, and answering shopper questions on a VDP before the visitor bounces off the page. The base tier covers the dealership website plus Facebook, Instagram, and SMS; voice and phone live in higher tiers. For a rooftop whose CRM and BDC stack already works and whose website-and-Meta chat is the weak link, DealerAI is aimed at exactly that piece of the workflow.

**Best for:** Rooftops that want a dealership-native multi-channel chat layer covering website, Facebook, Instagram, and SMS in the entry tier without a CRM swap, with a published per-site price.

**Pricing:** Public per-site tiers as of April 2026: entry tier at $599/month (website, Facebook, Instagram, and SMS), Reach at $1,499/month, All-In-One at $2,299/month. Dealer-group and OEM tiers are quote-only.

**Pros:**
- Purpose-built for dealerships, so inventory inquiry and appointment-booking flows feel native rather than retrofitted onto a generic chat tool
- Multi-agent architecture splits Sales, Service, Parts, and Finance into separate agents so each gets the right tone and routing
- Published per-site pricing on the website, so a GM can model cost without a sales call
- Multi-channel from the entry tier (website, Facebook, Instagram, SMS), so the BDC is not getting fragments across separate tools

**Cons:**
- Voice and phone coverage is a higher-tier upsell, not in the $599/month base
- Less depth on lifecycle CRM and lead routing than Impel or DriveCentric
- Per-site billing means dealer groups scale linearly without a published group discount

**Verdict:** Pick DealerAI when you want a dealership-native multi-agent chat layer covering website plus Meta plus SMS without a CRM swap; skip it if voice and phone in the base tier is non-negotiable, or if your real problem is CRM workflow rather than the chat layer.

## Frequently asked questions

Short answers to questions GMs ask when shortlisting a vehicle recommendation AI for the dealership website - covering channel coverage, CRM fit, pricing models, and what happens to inventory accuracy as cars sell.

### What channels does this cover beyond the website?
Depends on the tool. Some are website-only (Nobi falls here - no SMS, phone, or social today). Others lead with SMS and phone or layer Facebook and Instagram on top of web chat. Pick by where your shoppers actually message, not by the feature checkbox.

### Will it work with the CRM I already run?
Most tools in this category plug into the dealership CRMs you already run (VinSolutions, DriveCentric, Elead) rather than replacing them. Confirm the integration path during the demo, including whether leads write back with full conversation context or only as a basic notification.

### How does pricing usually work?
Two patterns. Published per-message or per-site pricing you can model from current website traffic - Nobi sits here at $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included), $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Or quote-only per-rooftop pricing that needs a sales call before you see numbers. Published pricing lets a GM build the internal case without a vendor in the room.

### What happens to inventory accuracy as cars sell?
The tool reads from your live inventory feed, so a sold vehicle drops out of recommendations as the feed refreshes. Ask the vendor how often the feed syncs and whether VDP detail (price, photos, options) refreshes on the same cadence; a daily sync on a fast-moving used lot will trail reality.
