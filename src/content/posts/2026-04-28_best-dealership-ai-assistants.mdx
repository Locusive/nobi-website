## What does an AI assistant for a dealership actually do?

An AI assistant for a dealership handles shopper conversations the way a strong floor or BDC rep would: answering vehicle questions on the website, qualifying leads by phone or text, booking test drives and service appointments, and following up on cold leads until they re-engage.

Nobi is a website-based AI shopping assistant and search layer that answers VDP and inventory questions inline, from $25/month base plus usage. Strong when on-site shopper questions are the bottleneck; weaker on SMS, voice, or phone qualification (web chat only today). DealerAI is a multi-agent generative AI assistant for dealership websites, handling appointment scheduling and inventory questions on-site. Strong on the site; weaker on phone, SMS, and lifecycle CRM. Matador AI automates sales and service conversations across phone, text, email, social, and web chat for 1,500+ dealerships. Strong when calls and texts are leaking; weaker as a website-only fit. DriveCentric is an AI-first CRM with automated follow-ups, service reminders, and trade-in offers, priced by rooftop and units sold. Strong when CRM workflow is the gap; weaker as standalone web chat. Impel is a broader Automotive AI Operating System spanning lead engagement, service scheduling, and marketing AI after its 2024 Outsell acquisition. Strong if you want one vendor across the funnel; weaker when you'd rather buy best-of-breed for each lane.

## How did we pick the AI assistants in this list?

We picked tools that are actively used at production dealerships, name automotive as a core vertical rather than a side audience, and publish enough about how they work that a GM can evaluate them without sitting through a sales call. Five filters did the work: which job in the funnel each tool actually owns (lead response, conversational sales, service scheduling, on-site Q&A); how many channels it covers (web only versus phone, SMS, social); whether it can answer year/make/model questions about the dealer's current inventory; how transparent the pricing is (published numbers versus quote-driven); and how heavy the implementation lift is - days of JavaScript snippets versus months of CRM replacement. Nobi is on this list because we built it and we'd rather name that than pretend we're a neutral panel; the other four earned their spots on the merits.

## 2. Impel

[Impel](https://impel.ai) positions itself as an Automotive AI Operating System covering lead engagement, service scheduling, and customer lifecycle marketing under one contract. The 2024 acquisition of Outsell layered marketing AI on top of the existing engagement and service modules, which is why dealer groups looking to consolidate point tools onto a single vendor keep landing on Impel's customer list. For a multi-rooftop GM running parallel contracts across BDC chat, service reminders, and equity-mining email, the pitch is straightforward: collapse three vendors into one and run a unified customer record across the lifecycle. Direct integrations with major OEM customer journey platforms make that consolidation easier to defend with the franchise side of the house.

**Best for:** Dealer groups consolidating multiple point AI tools onto a single vendor across sales, service, and lifecycle marketing.

**Pricing:** Custom; enterprise dealer-group quote (no public starter tier).

**Pros:**
- Suite breadth across lead engagement, service scheduling, and marketing AI under one contract since the Outsell acquisition
- Direct integrations with major OEM customer journey platforms
- Track record at large multi-rooftop groups, not just single-store pilots

**Cons:**
- Enterprise sales motion and integration footprint - implementation timelines run in months, not days
- Suite breadth means most modules are competent rather than strongest for any single job

**Verdict:** Pick Impel for a multi-rooftop group that wants one vendor across the lifecycle; if you need a single sharp tool for one part of the funnel, a focused option will be lighter to deploy.

## 3. DriveCentric

[DriveCentric](https://drivecentric.com) is an AI-first dealership CRM with autonomous engagement agents and multi-channel follow-up built into the core platform rather than added as a side module. For a dealership GM, that distinction matters: instead of layering an AI assist on top of an aging CRM, the AI is the CRM. Lead response, text/email/voice outreach, automated follow-ups, service reminders, and trade-in offers all live in one workflow your salespeople and BDC already work inside. The interface is modern by automotive-CRM standards, which lowers the day-to-day friction your team feels compared with the legacy enterprise systems most rooftops are running today. The trade-off is scope: this is a CRM replacement decision, not a bolt-on, and the change-management bill comes due across every desk on the floor.

**Best for:** Rooftops that are actively replacing the CRM, not just bolting an AI layer onto an existing one.

**Pricing:** Custom; priced per rooftop and units sold, centered on the Engagement Hub CRM with add-on solutions available.

**Pros:**
- AI follow-up and engagement are native CRM features, not a separate vendor to integrate
- Multi-channel outreach (text, email, voice) lives inside the CRM instead of a layered tool
- Modern interface compared with the legacy enterprise CRMs most dealerships run today

**Cons:**
- Replacing the CRM is a far bigger change-management lift than adding a chat or BDC tool
- Less depth on shopper-facing product Q&A on the website itself; the strength is sales-floor and BDC workflows

**Verdict:** Pick DriveCentric when the CRM itself is what's broken and you want the AI native to it; choose a layered tool if your CRM is fine and you only need a specific assist on top.

## 4. Matador AI

[Matador AI](https://matador.ai) is a conversational AI platform purpose-built for dealerships, automating sales and service conversations across phone, text, email, social, and web chat. For a dealership GM whose BDC is buried under inbound leads and outbound follow-ups, the appeal is breadth: one vendor handling the channels your team currently juggles across three or four tools. The TCPA compliance engine and SMS broadcast tooling are the parts BDC managers tend to call out, because automated texting at dealer scale is exactly the kind of thing that ends up in front of a lawyer if it goes wrong. With 1,500+ dealerships using the product, the conversation playbooks are tuned to automotive workflows rather than retrofitted from another industry.

**Best for:** Dealerships whose BDC is the bottleneck across multiple channels, especially high-volume SMS follow-up.

**Pricing:** Custom; per-dealership pricing.

**Pros:**
- Channel breadth - one vendor covering phone, text, email, social, and web chat
- TCPA compliance engine for SMS, addressing real legal exposure dealers worry about with automated texting
- 1,500+ dealership install base, so the conversation playbooks are dealer-specific rather than ported from another vertical

**Cons:**
- Sales- and conversation-focused; less depth on inventory-aware product discovery on the website itself
- Designed to plug into an existing CRM rather than replace one, so it adds a layer rather than consolidating tools

**Verdict:** Pick Matador AI when multi-channel follow-up is the job and the BDC is overwhelmed; pair it with something inventory-aware on the website if shopper discovery on the lot pages is also a gap.

## 5. DealerAI

[DealerAI](https://dealerai.com) is a multi-agent generative AI assistant built specifically for dealership websites, with separate agents handling appointment scheduling, inventory inquiries, and general shopper questions on-site. For a dealership GM whose CRM and BDC are already running clean, the gap is often just the website itself - shoppers landing on inventory pages, asking a question, and bouncing when nothing answers. DealerAI sits on that surface and gives each intent (sales, service, inventory) its own specialized prompt rather than routing every question through a single generic chat. The trade-off is scope: the base product stays on the website and doesn't extend to phone, SMS, or service-bay coverage, so it works best as a focused fix rather than a lifecycle replacement.

**Best for:** Dealers whose CRM and BDC are already in place and the gap is specifically a smarter website chat experience.

**Pricing:** Custom; per-rooftop pricing, quoted by DealerAI directly.

**Pros:**
- Purpose-built for dealership website use cases out of the box - appointment booking, inventory questions, and shopper Q&A
- Faster to deploy than a full lifecycle suite because the scope stays on the website
- Multi-agent architecture routes service, sales, and inventory intents to specialized prompts instead of one generic agent
- Store-level customization lets regional groups tune interactions per rooftop

**Cons:**
- Website-only in the base product - no phone, SMS, or service-bay coverage
- Less depth on lifecycle CRM, lead routing, and BDC workflows than full-suite competitors

**Verdict:** Pick DealerAI when website chat is the only gap.

Match the tool to the job that's actually losing you deals. If your BDC is buried in multi-channel follow-up, [Matador AI](https://matador.ai) is built for that. If the CRM itself is the bottleneck, [DriveCentric](https://drivecentric.com) replaces it. Most rooftops end up running two of these together rather than one, because no single product covers every channel a shopper uses.

Nobi fits when on-site discovery is the gap: shoppers asking "AWD under $30k with third-row seating" should get a real answer on the VDP or search bar, not a [dead-end results page](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search). $25/month base with 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included, $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message. Web chat surface only - if your shoppers live on SMS or phone, you'll need a second tool.
