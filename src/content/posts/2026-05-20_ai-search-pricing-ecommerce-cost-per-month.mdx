## Which AI search platforms for ecommerce publish their pricing?

Three platforms in this comparison publish concrete starting prices. Nobi lists $25/month base on its pricing page, covering 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. [Fast Simon](https://www.fastsimon.com) shows an entry paid tier of $39.99/month and an Essential tier at $99.99/month on the Shopify App Store. [Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) offers a free tier of 10,000 searches per month before usage-based pricing kicks in.

The other five are quote-only. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) names three tiers on its site (Essential, Advanced, Expert) but routes the actual numbers through a sales call. [Searchspring](https://searchspring.com) publishes nothing; reported estimates vary widely by store size. [Constructor](https://constructor.com) uses a revenue-share model with no published rate. [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) quotes six-figure annual contracts through sales. [Coveo](https://www.coveo.com) lists a $600/month starting point, with all-in cost commonly reaching $100K+ once annual licensing, implementation, and services are included.

## How does usage-based pricing scale once your catalog grows?

Usage-based pricing scales with your traffic, not your contract, so the bill compounds as queries grow. Algolia's free tier covers 10,000 monthly searches and 1 million records. Past that, you pay per search request and per record. A peak-season traffic spike produces a surprise bill exactly when search matters most.

Algolia bills two ways at once: monthly search requests and record count. NeuralSearch, [the semantic layer most catalogs actually need](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-semantic-search-ecommerce), is only available on the top-tier enterprise plan. It's not included in Build, Grow, or even the Premium tier. A growing catalog hits both meters at the same time, and adding the semantic layer means a full plan upgrade, not a feature toggle.

Fast Simon scales along similar lines across its App Store tiers, from $39.99/month at entry to $99.99/month for Essential and $299.99/month for Top Pro. Pricing tracks monthly session volume, so a promotional traffic spike can push you into the next tier. The entry number you see at signup is not always the number you pay six months later.

Nobi's overage rates are flat: $0.01 per search past the 2,500 included, $0.10 per message past the 250 included. No tier upgrades, no contract renegotiation when traffic doubles. A month with 50,000 searches bills as $25 base plus 47,500 overage searches at $0.01 each - $500 total.

## Which platforms charge a percentage of GMV instead of a flat fee?

[Constructor](https://constructor.com) is the only platform on this comparison that uses a revenue-share model. There's no published list price. The contract scales with GMV, which means a successful CVR lift makes the bill bigger, not smaller. The more search drives revenue, the more search costs. That's predictable at enterprise scale where GMV is forecastable quarter over quarter, but it's the inverse of how most flat-fee or per-unit tools work.

The model exists for a reason. Constructor's headline value is [behavioral reranking](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-search-ecommerce-2026): results reorder based on what each shopper has clicked, dwelled on, and bought during the session and across visits. The ranker improves with traffic - more sessions, more click data, better ordering. Pricing the tool against the outcome it produces is internally consistent, and it aligns the vendor and merchant on growth in a way per-seat or per-search pricing doesn't.

The catch shows up at two ends. Peak season is the first. A November traffic spike that doubles GMV doubles the bill. That bill lands in finance's quarterly review at the moment CVR pressure is already at its highest - right next to search tools that had a fixed cost. Traffic volume is the second. Behavioral reranking needs a lot of click data to perform well. [Smaller brands without significant query volume](/blog/constructor-alternatives-ecommerce) often don't generate enough session data to give the ranker useful signal. The math gets worse at low traffic: you're paying a percentage of GMV for a ranker that hasn't yet had enough data to outperform a simpler approach.

## What do the enterprise platforms - Bloomreach and Coveo - actually cost?

Bloomreach is quote-only, with six-figure annual contracts standard. Coveo's base is reported at $600/month by third-party sources, but the all-in number tells a different story: annual licensing commonly lands at $50,000 or more, and implementation and services costs stack on top. The full first-year cost often clears $100,000 before the engine is live. Both vendors run sales-led procurement, and full-platform implementations can take several months from contract to launch.

Bloomreach is not just a search engine. The platform bundles search, a customer data platform, and a content management system. You're paying for the full stack, not for Discovery in isolation. If [site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-top-ecommerce-platforms-for-native-ai-search-and-product-discovery) is your standalone problem, you're funding two product lines you don't need.

Coveo's pricing accelerates for a different reason. The platform is built to run across multiple properties at once: commerce, customer support, and internal workplace search. Most of the value comes from consolidating those use cases on one engine. [Buying Coveo just for ecommerce search](/blog/coveo-alternatives-ecommerce) means paying for capabilities you won't use, which is why the published $600/month base and the actual contract sit so far apart.

## How to narrow the list in 10 minutes

Eight platforms is a lot to evaluate seriously. You can cut it to two or three before booking any demos. Four questions will get you there, and you probably know the answers already.

**Do you have developer time to own the search experience?** If yes, Algolia gives you the most control. Rules, ranking, synonyms, and merchandising all live in code - an engineering team can tune every detail. If that bandwidth isn't there, look at platforms that configure on install. Nobi, Klevu, and Fast Simon all go live in days without an engineering sprint.

**Is your main problem query understanding or visual merchandising?** Klevu and Nobi focus on query understanding. A shopper types "wide-leg cropped trouser" and gets the right product even if the catalog title says something different. Fast Simon and Searchspring are stronger at visual merchandising - how collections are structured, which products surface in a given campaign. If shoppers find the right results but don't convert, merchandising is likely the bottleneck. If shoppers can't find what they're describing in the first place, query understanding is.

**Does reranking by individual shopper behavior matter to you?** Constructor makes that its primary job. Results reorder in real time based on what each shopper has clicked, dwelled on, and bought during the session and across visits. It's the only platform in this comparison built around that idea. The trade-off is revenue-share pricing and a traffic requirement - the ranker needs enough session data to outperform simpler approaches. Klevu includes personalization in its Expert tier. Nobi's personalization today is starter messages and placeholder text, not click-signal reranking.

**What's your monthly budget for search?** If you need a published, predictable number before you start talking to anyone, the options are Nobi at $25/month, Fast Simon from $39.99/month, and Algolia's free tier for under 10,000 monthly searches. All three post their rates publicly. Klevu, Searchspring, and Constructor all require a quote. Bloomreach and Coveo start at six-figure annual contracts and fit buyers who need search bundled with other platform tools, not search alone.

Run those four filters and you're probably down to two options. That's when a vendor call actually moves fast.

It fits best at high GMV volumes where the ranker has enough session data to work well, and where revenue-share pricing makes sense against a forecastable GMV base. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) pairs AI-powered search with a no-code merchandising dashboard on Shopify, with personalization features in the Expert tier. Nobi at $25/month base is the only platform here that publishes per-unit overage rates, making it the predictable pick when transparent pricing and a built-in shopping assistant matter more than behavioral reranking. Its personalization today is limited to starter messages and placeholder text, not click-signal reranking.

[Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) is the right pick when an engineering team owns the rule engine and wants full API control over ranking. [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com) fits buyers consolidating search, a customer data platform, and CMS on one contract. [Coveo](https://www.coveo.com) fits buyers unifying ecommerce search with customer support and workplace search across the whole company. ## What to ask before you sign

The headline price is rarely what you end up paying. Most surprises come from the same handful of questions buyers forget to ask before the contract is signed.

**Does the plan you're evaluating include semantic search?** This matters most with Algolia. NeuralSearch is gated to the top-tier enterprise plan. Every lower tier - including the free tier - is keyword-only matching. If shoppers search for "wide-leg cropped trouser" and your product title says "cropped wide pant," a keyword engine misses the sale. Klevu includes AI matching across tiers but gates personalization to the Expert plan. Nobi includes semantic search at $25/month. Before you start a trial, ask which plan unlocks AI-powered relevance - not just fast keyword matching.

**What does a peak-season month actually cost?** Usage-based tools bill on actual monthly volume, so a high-traffic month means a high bill. Black Friday arrives at the worst possible moment: maximum pressure to convert, maximum invoice. Ask the vendor to run the numbers at your peak estimated query volume before you sign anything. With Nobi, the per-unit rate stays flat regardless of volume, so a spike is calculable in advance. With Algolia and Fast Simon, the same spike can push you into a new tier or produce an invoice you didn't budget for.

**What does implementation cost beyond the license?** Coveo and Bloomreach both carry professional services fees on top of annual licensing, and implementation can take months. Constructor needs data-team hours after launch to tune the ranker as click data accumulates. Algolia is developer hours rather than a services contract - no separate line item, but the time is real. Nobi, Fast Simon, and Klevu install in days. When comparing a published price against a quote, add the full implementation cost to the quote side of the comparison, not just the license.

**Is there a minimum contract term?** Usage-based tiers can still require a 12-month commitment at a discount, or carry a minimum monthly spend. Ask whether you can exit after 30 days or whether you're locked in for a year. The answer changes the real cost of a wrong pick.

**What data can you take with you if you switch?** Synonym lists, search analytics, and behavioral click data often stay with the vendor when you leave. Ask every quote-only vendor to show you the data export process before you sign - not after you've decided to move on.
