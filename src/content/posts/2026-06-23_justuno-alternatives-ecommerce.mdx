## Is Justuno still the right tool for your ecommerce brand?

[Justuno](https://www.justuno.com) is a conversion optimization platform built around on-site popups and banners. It handles exit-intent overlays, spin-to-win promotions, and behavioral targeting rules well. For brands focused on list growth through traditional display overlays, it still does that job. Popup fatigue is the documented cost: 71% of consumers abandon a brand after a single negative interaction (per Qualtrics XM research), and disruptive overlays are a known contributor.

Teams look for alternatives when they want lead capture woven into product discovery and Q&A - not sitting on top of the browsing experience as a separate interruption. Three other triggers come up often: needing email and SMS send without a separate ESP, wanting quiz-based capture tied to Klaviyo segments, or wanting [AI-grounded conversations](https://nobi.ai/blog/chatgpt-alternatives-for-ecommerce) that generate warmer leads than a cold form fill.

Nobi approaches lead capture as one part of a broader setup that also handles product search and shopper Q&A.

## How did we evaluate these Justuno alternatives?

That gap - capture woven into the browsing experience rather than on top of it - shaped four evaluation criteria: what each tool actually does, how transparent the pricing is, how well it connects to Shopify and Klaviyo without engineering help, and whether it reduces overlay friction or swaps one popup for another. Nobi is on this list; we publish it, so weigh our section accordingly.

[Privy](https://www.privy.com) is an email and SMS capture tool for Shopify stores. Klaviyo integration is native, and there's no annual contract. The format is still popup overlays - the same model Justuno uses. Pick it when you want a lower-cost tool with email and SMS send built in. Skip it if the overlay format is what you're trying to move away from.

[Octane AI](https://www.octaneai.com) handles quiz-led product discovery on Shopify, starting at $50/month for 400 quiz completions. Quizzes pass zero-party data into Klaviyo, which sharpens segmentation downstream. It's Shopify-only, and quiz attribute data can take a few minutes to appear in Klaviyo after a profile syncs. Pick it when a quiz is your primary capture mechanism. Skip it if you want open-ended shopper Q&A instead of a structured flow.

[Conversica](https://www.conversica.com) runs persistent AI follow-up after capture - email, SMS, and chat - until a lead responds or opts out. It's a post-capture tool, not an on-site one. Pricing is quote-only with no public starter tier, so expect a multi-week sales cycle before committing budget. Pick it when your bottleneck is lead follow-up, not list building. Skip it if you're still trying to grow the list.

Nobi handles on-site capture as part of a broader setup that also covers product search and automated shopper Q&A. Pricing is transparent: $25/month base, $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional conversational message. The current gap is personalization - behavioral reranking based on individual click history isn't available yet. Pick Nobi when you want capture, search, and Q&A in one product. Skip it if a quiz funnel or post-capture outreach is the primary need.

## 2. Privy

[Privy](https://privy.com) covers the full popup toolkit Justuno users already know: exit-intent overlays, flybars, spin-to-win wheels, embedded forms, and announcement bars. For a marketing manager who wants a straight Justuno replacement without rebuilding their entire setup, the bigger appeal is what comes after capture - Privy includes native email and SMS sending, so the contacts you collect start getting nurture emails without routing them through a separate ESP for basic sends. Targeting rules tie to real Shopify purchase behavior, not just generic page visits, so triggers like "shopper who viewed this product three times but didn't add to cart" are straightforward to configure. The trade-off is that the core model is still popup-based. Switching tools doesn't change the format, and popup fatigue follows the form, not the vendor.

**Best for:** Shopify brands that want a direct Justuno replacement - popup capture with email and SMS send already included - without overhauling their marketing stack.

**Pricing:** Free tier; paid email plans scale by contact count (current tiers listed on the [Shopify App Store](https://apps.shopify.com/privy-email-pop-ups-overlays)).

**Pros:**
- The full range of popup formats (exit-intent, flybar, spin-to-win, embedded) with targeting rules Justuno users will recognize - the shortest migration path of any option on this list.

**Cons:**
- Popup-based, disruptive to UX
- Single-purpose tool

**Verdict:** Pick Privy when you want the fastest Justuno replacement with email and SMS send built in; skip it when you want lead capture to extend into conversational product discovery and automated shopper Q&A.

## 3. Octane AI

[Octane AI](https://octaneai.com) is a quiz-led product discovery platform built for Shopify, and the quiz is the whole mechanism. Shoppers work through five to ten diagnostic questions about fit, use case, or occasion, and the platform maps their answers to real product attributes in your catalog to surface specific SKUs - not a generic category page. For a marketing manager sitting on a wide catalog where shoppers often don't know what they want, that guided structure is the value. Octane runs natively on Shopify, ships pre-built templates for fit finders and routine-style flows, and connects quiz completions directly to Klaviyo as email-segment attributes. A shopper who tells you their fit preferences and use case during a quiz is a more actionable contact than one who dismissed a cold overlay.

**Best for:** Ecommerce teams whose primary path to email capture is a diagnostic quiz - where shoppers need guided questions to narrow a wide catalog to the right SKU, and quiz completions are the Klaviyo segments worth building on.

**Pricing:** Public tiered Shopify-app pricing. Basic $50/month (400 credits), Plus $200/month (2,200 credits), Enterprise & Unlimited starts at $500+/month. Octane defines a credit as one quiz completion ("1 credit = 1 quiz engagement when someone completes your quiz"). AI features (Smart Products, Smart Copy, Image Analyzer) consume fractional extra credits per interaction.

**Pros:**
- Quiz builder with deep Shopify product-attribute mapping, so quiz answers resolve to real SKUs rather than generic category pages
- Native Klaviyo integration captures quiz answers as email-segment attributes, so a quiz completion flows straight into lifecycle email without a separate integration step

**Cons:**
- Quiz-centric, less about open-ended conversation
- Strong in beauty and CPG, less relevant elsewhere

**Verdict:** Pick Octane AI when a diagnostic quiz is genuinely your main path to email capture and quiz completions are the Klaviyo segments you want to build; skip it when shoppers need open-ended Q&A or free-form browsing to find what they want.

## 4. Conversica

[Conversica](https://conversica.com) builds AI agents for persistent lead follow-up. The agent reaches out by email - and now SMS - after a shopper or prospect makes contact, then keeps following up until they reply or opt out. Founded in 2013 with automotive roots, Conversica now covers multiple verticals including ecommerce. For a marketing manager whose capture tool is working but whose list goes cold before converting, this is the post-capture layer: it automates the follow-up sequence that would otherwise require manual outreach on high-consideration purchases where a single email rarely does the job.

**Best for:** Ecommerce and DTC teams with high-intent captured leads that go cold without persistent outreach - particularly high-consideration purchases where a buyer inquires and then disappears for days.

**Pricing:** Quote-only - per-conversation enterprise pricing model with no public starter tier published on conversica.com. Expect a multi- week sales cycle before signing.

**Pros:**
- Keeps following up automatically until the lead replies or opts out, which matters on any purchase where one outreach isn't enough to re-engage someone who went quiet
- Email-first with SMS now layered in, so teams can reach warm leads on whichever channel actually gets a reply
- Conversational AI tuned specifically for sales follow-up, not a general-purpose assistant retrofitted into a nurture sequence

**Cons:**
- Email-first; later added SMS but channel breadth still narrower than newer multi-channel entrants
- Enterprise sales cycle and per-seat pricing; expensive for small-location independent brands
- Originally automotive-focused; the automotive-specific depth that defined early Conversica now competes with roadmap priorities across other verticals

**Verdict:** Pick Conversica when qualified leads are going cold and you need a persistent agent to follow up; skip it if you need an on-site capture tool or can't absorb a multi-week enterprise sales cycle.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Privy a good direct replacement for Justuno?

Privy supports the same core popup formats - exit-intent, flybar, and spin-to-win - with Shopify-native targeting and built-in email and SMS sending. It's the most direct migration path for Justuno users who want to stay in the popup model.

### Can Nobi replace a popup tool entirely for lead capture?

Nobi captures leads through exit-intent prompts, toast notifications, and prompt cards that open a product conversation rather than a form overlay. Whether it fully replaces a popup tool depends on how much of your current list growth comes from popup-specific formats like spin-to-win versus shoppers already browsing your catalog who are asking product questions.

### Does Octane AI integrate with email platforms other than Klaviyo?

Octane AI's native integration is Klaviyo. Brands on other ESPs should verify supported integrations before committing, since the segmentation value of quiz data depends on that connection working reliably.

### Is Conversica designed for ecommerce?

Conversica was founded with automotive roots and now serves multiple verticals including ecommerce. Its strongest fit is high-consideration purchases with longer follow-up cycles. Impulse-buy ecommerce with low AOV and no sales motion is a weaker fit - the persistent outreach model adds more process than a low-touch purchase warrants.

### What is the risk of popup fatigue for ecommerce brands?

Disruptive overlays are a documented friction point. 71% of consumers report abandoning a brand after a single negative interaction, and aggressive overlays are a common trigger. Conversational capture tools like Nobi and quiz-led tools like Octane AI aim to generate leads from shoppers already engaged with the catalog, so the capture mechanism itself is less likely to be the experience that drives someone away.
