## What does a shopping chatbot actually do for an ecommerce site?

A shopping chatbot is a conversational widget that sits on your store and does three things in the same session: answers product questions, recommends items, and captures an email or phone number before the visitor leaves. For a marketing manager, it's a middle-funnel asset. It catches the shopper who's stuck on a PDP comparing two sweaters, or scanning a collection page without clicking anything, and nudges them toward an add-to-cart, a saved cart, or a list signup.

The bot earns its keep on high-intent surfaces: PDPs, collection pages, and the cart. That's also why marketing increasingly owns the buying decision instead of support. Assisted revenue and email capture land in the marketing dashboard, not the helpdesk.

Faherty's "Shop with AI" assistant, for example, converts at 6.3% with a $315 AOV, well above typical site averages. Useful framing, with one caveat: a chatbot won't rescue thin PDP copy, broken merchandising, or a checkout that's leaking for unrelated reasons. It amplifies a store that already works. It doesn't fix one that doesn't.

## How do you choose the right shopping chatbot for your brand?

Start by naming the funnel problem you're actually trying to fix. Is it bounce on product detail pages, thin email capture, shoppers failing to find the right product, or a rising support queue? Each of those points to a different shortlist. Then run every candidate through four filters: pricing model, implementation timeline, integration with your email and analytics stack, and whether ranking learns from shopper behavior or needs your team to hand-tune rules.

Pricing model matters as much as the sticker. Rep AI uses a modular Sales and Support setup, and pricing for that kind of behavioral-AI sales bot often lands in revenue-share or bundled deals that are harder to forecast once you scale. Octane AI publishes tiered credit-based pricing: $50/month for 400 credits, $200/month for 2,200, and $500+/month for 7,250 or unlimited, which works if your quiz volume is predictable. Tidio's Lyro sells as an add-on with roughly $0.50 per resolved conversation after a free trial. Nobi's approach is flat and usage-based: $25/month for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra message. A mid-market brand pushing 50,000 searches and 10,000 messages a month is closer to a few hundred dollars, not $25, so do the math on your real volume before you compare stickers.

Check the integrations list against your actual stack. Octane AI connects directly to Klaviyo and Shopify, which matters if quiz-captured emails feed your flows. Tidio is strong on Shopify-native support. Then ask the harder question: does the platform's ranking get smarter on its own, or does someone on your team have to maintain merchandising rules every week? Finally, be honest about scale. If you're under about $1M GMV, none of these will pay back yet.

## What does a shopping chatbot implementation actually look like?

Most modern chatbots install through a Shopify app or a single script tag and reach a working state in days, not months. The real work isn't code. It's content and scope: which pages the bot appears on, what it can answer, how it hands off to email or a human, and which CTAs it offers. Budget one to two weeks for setup and content seeding, then a two-to-four-week tuning window before you judge results.

Here's what a sane rollout sequence looks like:

1. **Install and connect the catalog.** Drop in the app or script and point it at your product feed. If the vendor can't pull your Shopify catalog automatically on day one, that's a red flag.
2. **Decide page-level placement.** Don't enable the widget everywhere at once. Start on PDPs and cart, where intent is highest. Add collection pages and blog posts in week two, once you've seen what questions actually come in.
3. **Seed answers for the top 20 product questions and top 10 policy questions.** Sizing, fit, materials, shipping windows, return policy, gift cards. This is where most launches stall, because nobody owns writing the answers. Assign it before kickoff.
4. **Wire the email handoff into Klaviyo or your ESP.** Captured leads should land in a real nurture flow with the product context attached, not a generic welcome series.
5. **Set up GA4 event tracking.** You want chatbot-assisted sessions and assisted revenue visible next to your other channels, otherwise you can't defend the line item at renewal.

One detail that matters more than it sounds: ask whether you can customize display behavior without filing a vendor ticket. UNTUCKit pushed Nobi to expose developer hooks so their team could control how products rendered in-widget, and that feedback became the Hooks API every Nobi customer now gets. Fast implementation is only useful if you're not stuck waiting on someone else's roadmap six months in.

## How do you measure whether your shopping chatbot is working?

Track four metrics: assisted revenue (sessions that touched the chatbot and converted), assisted AOV, email opt-in rate from chat, and containment rate for support questions. Don't judge the launch on raw chat volume. A quiet, well-targeted bot that lifts CVR on product pages is worth more than a loud one with no funnel impact.

Assisted revenue and assisted CVR are the headline numbers for a marketing-owned chatbot. They answer the only question your CFO cares about: did the bot move money that wouldn't have moved without it. Set up attribution so you can filter to sessions where a shopper actually engaged the chat widget, then compare conversion and revenue per session against a matched control. If your platform can't split assisted from non-assisted sessions, that's the first integration gap to close, because everything else downstream depends on it.

AOV lift is the tell that the bot is doing real recommending rather than just deflecting shipping questions. Faherty's Shop with AI surface, for example, ran a 6.3% conversion rate with a $315 average order value, which only happens when the assistant is actively pairing items and suggesting upgrades. If your AOV from chat sessions looks identical to your site-wide AOV, the bot is answering FAQs, not selling.

[Email opt-in rate from chat](https://nobi.ai/lead-capture) is the soft-conversion lever most marketing teams end up leaning on hardest. A chat that collects 8 to 15% of engaged sessions into your ESP is feeding your flows, your lookalikes, and your retargeting pool at a cost per lead that usually beats paid social.

For an upper-bound reference, Lucchese's deployment on Shopify Plus generated over $1M in incremental year-one revenue at a 39x ROI across search and assistant surfaces. That's a ceiling, not a forecast. Nobi's assisted revenue attribution makes the honest version of this math visible in your dashboard from day one, so you're reporting real lift instead of vanity chat counts.

## What are the most common shopping chatbot mistakes to avoid?

The most common failure modes are launching everywhere at once, leaving the bot to answer with stale or generic content, and never wiring it into email or analytics. A chatbot that captures leads but doesn't drop them into a Klaviyo flow is a measurement gap, not a marketing program, and it's the single mistake that kills more pilots than any technical limitation.

Start with the rollout. Going site-wide on day one means you're debugging tone, product recommendations, and handoff logic across every page at once, with no baseline to compare against. Pick three to five high-intent pages, usually a couple of category pages, your two best-selling PDPs, and the cart, then expand once you see what shoppers actually ask. The data from that first month will reshape your assumptions about which questions matter.

Next, edit the defaults. The greeting, the fallback responses, the product card copy: ship anything generic and customers clock it inside two messages. Write the opener in your brand voice, swap the canned "I didn't understand that" for something that routes them to a human or a specific collection, and review chat transcripts weekly for the first six weeks.

Skipping the ESP handoff is the quiet killer. If captured emails sit in the chatbot's own dashboard instead of flowing into a Klaviyo welcome series or abandoned-browse trigger, you've built a list with no nurture. Confirm the integration on day one, not after launch.

Watch the right metric. Raw chat volume tells you the widget is visible, not that it's working. Assisted revenue, email signups, and the abandonment-recovery rate are the numbers that justify the line item.

Finally, read the pricing model carefully. Revenue-share deals and opaque per-resolution billing can produce a Q4 invoice that erases the program's margin. [Nobi's $25 base](https://nobi.ai/pricing) covers 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with $0.01 and $0.10 overages from there, so a mid-market brand doing 50,000 searches a month should model the real number before signing anything, ours included.

## When is a shopping chatbot the wrong investment for your store?

If your primary problem is support ticket volume rather than discovery or conversion, a helpdesk like Gorgias is a better first investment than a shopping chatbot. Gorgias is built around a unified support inbox for Shopify brands, with ticket-based pricing and AI agents tuned to deflect repetitive questions. If most of your inbound is "where is my order" and refund requests, that shape of tool will move the numbers Nobi can't.

The same logic applies to Octane AI. If you're a beauty or CPG brand whose entire CRO program is built on a guided quiz, Octane's quiz-led approach (positioned as a Shopify product quiz platform with Klaviyo integration for follow-up flows) will outperform a generic chat layer. A chatbot can complement a quiz, but it won't replace one as the hero conversion mechanic.

Hold off entirely if you're under roughly $1M in GMV. The traffic math doesn't work yet for any of these tools, including Nobi. Even at $25/month, you need enough sessions for the assisted-revenue signal to mean something, and paid, email, and SEO usually pay back faster at that stage.

One last honest note: enterprise buyers may still default to better-known names like Algolia. Nobi has less name recognition, and that's a fair reason to put Algolia on your evaluation list alongside it.
