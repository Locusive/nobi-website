## What is car dealership lead response software?

Car dealership lead response software captures inbound shopper interest from your website, phone, SMS, email, or chat and turns it into a qualified lead a salesperson can act on. The category spans on-site shopping assistants, AI-first CRMs, multi-channel conversational platforms, and full automotive AI suites. Speed-to-lead is the metric they're measured against, and because most dealerships don't run their own websites, the tool has to drop into an existing site and CRM rather than replace them.

Nobi runs on-site as a [JavaScript-installed shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/adding-ai-chatbot-to-ecommerce-site) that answers inventory, financing, and service questions in chat and captures shopper contact information when the thread warrants sales follow-up. Strong when website engagement is the gap; weaker if you need outbound SMS or voice channels.

[Impel](https://impel.ai) is a full automotive AI suite covering chat, voice, and merchandising across the dealership footprint. Strong when you want one vendor for every customer touchpoint; weaker if you already have tools you like in some of those slots.

[DriveCentric](https://drivecentric.com) is an AI-first automotive CRM with the Genius assistant built into pipeline and follow-up workflows. Strong when you're replacing your CRM anyway; a heavier lift to layer on top of an existing CRM.

[Matador AI](https://matador.ai) is a conversational AI for dealership BDC teams that automates routine sales and service conversations. Strong when BDC bandwidth is the bottleneck; weaker when on-site product discovery is the gap.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi is an [AI shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search) that lives on your dealership website and turns browsing shoppers into structured leads for your sales team. It sits on the homepage, on VDPs, and on inventory pages, answering the specific questions car shoppers actually ask - "do you have a 2023 RAV4 Hybrid Limited under $35k", "what's the payment on this F-150", "is this still available" - and capturing intent before the shopper bounces. When a conversation surfaces a real buying signal, Nobi pushes a structured lead into your CRM so a salesperson has something concrete to call on. For a GM watching website traffic without seeing matching lead volume, it closes the gap between visitors and pipeline without adding a phone-room headcount or a multi-month rollout.

**Best for:** Dealerships whose biggest leak is website shoppers bouncing before a rep ever sees the lead.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages). $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message. No per-rooftop licensing and no revenue share.

**Pros:**
- Captures intent from real inventory questions ("do you have a 2023 RAV4 Hybrid Limited under $35k") instead of static contact forms
- Goes live in days via two JavaScript snippets in the site theme, not the multi-month rollout of a full automotive AI suite
- Posted prices you can budget against, not a custom quote tied to dealer-group size
- Combines site search, conversational AI, and automatic shopper Q&A in one platform, so you are not stitching three vendors together

**Cons:**
- Web chat surface only - no phone, SMS, or service-bay channels, so a store where most leads come through text or calls will need a different tool
- Not a CRM and not a replacement for one - leads get pushed to your CRM, but Nobi will not run your sales pipeline

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when website shoppers are dropping off before they ever become a lead; skip it if your lead-response problem is mostly on the phone or in SMS.

The 2024 acquisition of Outsell pulled marketing AI into the same suite, and Impel maintains established integrations with the major OEM customer journey platforms that smaller automotive AI vendors typically can't match. It positions itself as the AI layer powering modern automotive retail, with a footprint built for dealer groups rather than single-store operators.

**Best for:** Multi-rooftop dealer groups consolidating onto one AI vendor across sales, service, and marketing.

**Pricing:** Custom; quoted per dealer group based on rooftop count and modules selected. Not posted publicly.

**Pros:**
- Breadth - one stack for lead engagement, service scheduling, and lifecycle marketing instead of three separate vendors
- Established OEM integrations that smaller automotive AI competitors do not have
- The Outsell acquisition extended the suite further into marketing automation
- Strong fit for groups whose procurement teams want a single AI vendor of record

**Cons:**
- Enterprise-leaning - the integration footprint and rollout length are heavier than a single-purpose lead tool
- More than a single-rooftop store typically needs if the only goal is faster lead response

**Verdict:** Pick Impel when you are running a dealer group and want one AI vendor across the full lifecycle; skip it if you are a single rooftop trying to plug a website lead leak this quarter.

For a GM, that means lead response speed and follow-up cadence are a property of the CRM itself, not a separate layer you bolt on top. The trade-off is scope: this is a CRM swap, not a quick add-on to your current stack. If your dealership is already evaluating a CRM change, DriveCentric folds the AI engagement work into that same project. If you are happy with your incumbent CRM, the lift to migrate is the main thing standing between you and the AI features.

**Best for:** Dealerships that are already planning a CRM swap and want the AI engagement layer baked in instead of bolted on.

**Pricing:** Custom; quoted per rooftop. Not posted publicly.

**Pros:**
- AI agents live inside the CRM, so follow-up actions and pipeline state stay in one system
- Multi-channel follow-up across email, SMS, and chat runs off the same customer record reps already work in
- Mature integration story with common DMS systems, backed by a large dealership install base

**Cons:**
- Replacing an incumbent CRM is a heavier project than adding a chat or assistant layer to your existing site
- Less depth on shopper-facing inventory Q&A than tools that specialize in the website surface

**Verdict:** Pick DriveCentric when you are ready to replace your CRM and want AI follow-up built in; skip it if you are not in the market for a new CRM.

For a GM whose lead volume actually lives in SMS threads and inbound calls rather than website chat, that channel mix matters - most lead-response tools cover one or two surfaces well and punt on the rest. Matador AI also ships a TCPA compliance engine and SMS broadcast tooling, which directly addresses the legal exposure that comes with running text outreach at scale across thousands of contacts. The platform is in use at 1,500+ dealerships and is designed to plug into your existing CRM rather than replace it, so rollout is closer to bolting on a conversation layer than swapping the system of record.

**Best for:** Dealerships where SMS and phone are the dominant lead channels and TCPA compliance is a real concern.

**Pricing:** Custom; quoted per store. Not posted publicly.

**Pros:**
- Channel coverage that web-only assistants do not match - phone, text, email, social, and chat in one platform
- TCPA compliance engine takes a real legal risk off the GM's plate when running SMS at scale
- SMS broadcast tooling fits dealerships that already lean on text for service reminders and event invites
- Designed to plug into an existing CRM rather than replace one, so adoption is lighter than a CRM swap

**Cons:**

- Still depends on your CRM as the system of record, so it does not solve a broken pipeline process

**Verdict:** Pick Matador AI when SMS and phone are where your leads actually live; skip it if your bottleneck is shoppers leaving your website before any conversation starts.

## How should a dealership GM pick between these lead response tools?

Start with the channel where you are actually losing leads. Pull last month's lead source report, look at where the drop-off happens between first touch and a salesperson making contact, and pick the tool that closes that specific gap. Most single-rooftop GMs do not need an enterprise suite. They need to fix the one channel that is leaking.

If the leak is on your website - shoppers landing on VDPs and inventory pages, browsing two or three vehicles, and bouncing without a form fill - Nobi is the wedge. It answers VDP and inventory questions inline so a shopper asking about trim differences, financing terms, or trade-in policy gets a real answer before they leave. Pricing is $25/month base with 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. The honest limit: Nobi runs on web chat only, so phone and SMS leads still flow through whatever you use today.

If the leak is on SMS and phone, Matador AI is the right pick. It is purpose-built for dealership BDC teams and automates sales and service conversations across text and voice, which is where most lead volume at a single rooftop actually lives. The trade-off is that Matador does not touch on-site discovery, so VDP bouncers stay bouncers.

If the CRM is the bottleneck - leads land in the system but follow-up is slow or inconsistent - DriveCentric replaces the CRM itself with one that has AI follow-up and the Genius assistant built in. Heavier swap than adding a chat layer, but it fixes the root cause when the CRM is what is dropping leads.

If you run a dealer group and want one AI vendor across sales, service, and marketing, Impel is the consolidation play. Match the tool to the leak, not to the demo polish.

## Frequently asked questions

A few questions come up in almost every dealership evaluation - on speed to live, CRM fit, TCPA, and pricing. Short answers below.

**How fast can a single rooftop be live?** It depends on the channel. SMS-first tools that sit on top of an existing CRM (Matador AI, Podium-style messaging) typically stand up a single rooftop in a week or two once carrier registration and CRM API credentials are sorted. A full CRM swap (DriveCentric replacing your existing platform) is a different project - expect a multi-week migration with data mapping, user training, and process changes. Ask the vendor for a reference rooftop on your same DMS and CRM combo and call them.

**Does the tool replace the CRM or sit alongside it?** Both options exist. DriveCentric IS the CRM. Matador AI sits alongside VinSolutions, Elead, DealerSocket, and others, pushing activity back into the CRM record. Pick based on whether you want to consolidate the stack or keep your current CRM and add an AI layer.

**What does TCPA compliance actually require for dealership SMS?** Prior express written consent for marketing texts, clear opt-out language, quiet-hours respect, and an audit trail of consent per lead. Vendors vary in how much of this they automate. Ask to see the consent record on a sample lead.

**How is pricing typically structured?** Per-rooftop subscriptions on the dealer side. Most vendors quote on a call rather than publishing rates.
