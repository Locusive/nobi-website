## What is an MCP server, and why does it matter for your business?

An MCP server is a callable endpoint that speaks the Model Context Protocol - an open standard AI agents like Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity use to discover and query external data. It's what lets an agent ask your business directly: what are your hours, what does this cost, what's your return policy, what options do you have for someone in situation X. The user stays in their AI interface. Your business answers in real time, as if it's part of the conversation.

As more research and discovery moves into AI agents, exposing a callable endpoint is becoming the agent-era equivalent of having a website. Most chat and helpdesk tools don't yet expose one - they were built to render a widget for a human visitor, not to answer an API call from an agent operating on someone's behalf.

## How did we evaluate these tools?

Four things mattered: whether the tool builds and exposes a knowledge base AI agents can actually find, whether it ships a callable endpoint an agent can query directly, how fresh its content stays, and how its answers are grounded so an agent won't repeat something fabricated. The four tools we looked at are Nobi, Rep AI, Alby, and Gorgias - the ones businesses most often deploy when they want to answer customer questions through AI.

The knowledge-base question comes first, because it's the discovery layer. An agent that can't find structured, specific content about your business won't surface it. The tools differ sharply here: some build a growing FAQ from real customer conversations, others rely on content the team manually maintains.

The callable-endpoint question is the second cut. Either an agent like Claude or Perplexity can call the tool over a standard protocol, or the user has to go browse. Most chat and support tools don't expose one - they were built for a human in a widget, not an agent calling an API.

Refresh cadence is the third cut. A person reading stale content will notice the date. An agent will quote whatever it finds. A knowledge base that syncs once a week puts week-old information into AI answers.

Grounding is the fourth. We looked at what each tool's answers are anchored to and whether the source is visible to whoever reads the answer.

## 2. Rep AI

[Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) is a proactive-chat tool built for Shopify whose strongest move is in-chat post-purchase task execution. Its Post-Order Tasks Agent lets a customer cancel an order, start a return, or check tracking inside the chat widget, without a handoff to an email queue or a helpdesk ticket. Marketing teams can also script campaign-specific conversation flows in Flow Studio, a visual no-code builder. A live-agent drop-in feature lets a human join an ongoing AI session while the AI keeps handling it, instead of a hard handoff that pauses the conversation.

**Best for:** Shopify businesses who want customers to self-serve cancellations, returns, and tracking inside chat, or marketing teams who want to script campaign-specific conversation flows without engineering.

**Pricing:** $12 per 1,000 visitors on the Pay As You Grow tier (unlimited sessions, 500-product catalog cap). At ~125K monthly sessions, Pro tiers run $280/mo (Support), $616/mo (Sales), $740/mo (Bundle). Enterprise custom above ~500K sessions. 30-day free trial.

**Pros:**
- Post-Order Tasks Agent handles cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups inside the chat widget
- Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder for campaign-specific conversation flows
- Live-agent drop-in is a true hybrid: a human can join an AI session while the AI keeps going
- Drop-off-reason analytics show why visitors left, not just that they did

**Cons:**
- Chat only, no site search or discovery layer
- Not designed to be queried by external AI agents; built for a human visitor in a browser
- The 500-product cap on the entry tier is tight for larger catalogs

**Verdict:** Pick Rep AI when in-chat post-purchase task execution or no-code flow design is the primary job.

## 3. Alby

[Alby](https://alby.com) answers visitor questions on the product detail page, at the moment someone is looking at a specific item and deciding whether to buy. The widget is built for a human visitor on a product page, with intent already narrowed to a specific item, and its UX is tuned for that moment of consideration. Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024, so the product now sits inside Bluecore's identity and predictive-marketing stack rather than running standalone.

**Best for:** Businesses already on Bluecore who want product-page Q&A added to that platform.

**Pricing:** Bundled into Bluecore platform pricing after the acquisition; no standalone public price.

**Pros:**
- Focused Q&A at the moment a visitor is considering a specific item
- Plugs into Bluecore's stack without a second integration
- Reduces pre-purchase support volume by answering common questions inline

**Cons:**
- Chat only, no search; visitors earlier in the discovery process aren't covered
- Scope is the product page; broader discovery moments aren't part of the product
- Not designed to be queried by external AI agents
- Standalone roadmap uncertain after the Bluecore acquisition

**Verdict:** Pick Alby if Bluecore is already your platform and product-page Q&A is the one gap; look elsewhere if you need discovery, search, or agent-endpoint coverage.

## 4. Gorgias

[Gorgias](https://gorgias.com) is a helpdesk built around support-team workflow: tickets, routing, macros, returns, and refund processing, with order context rendered directly inside each ticket. A newer AI Agent layer on higher-priced plans handles repetitive inbound tickets, helping support teams close cases faster. The focus is on the support team's inbox, not on being queryable by external AI agents.

**Best for:** Support-heavy teams whose bottleneck is ticket routing, case management, and structured returns processing.

**Pricing:** Tiered by ticket volume. The base plan starts low; the AI Agent add-on is on higher-tier plans. Gorgias publishes the breakdown on its pricing page.

**Pros:**
- Mature support workflow: macros, routing rules, SLA tracking, multi-brand support
- Structured returns and refund workflows that chat-only tools don't replicate
- Multi-channel coverage across email, chat, and social

**Cons:**
- No discovery layer or site search
- Not designed to be queried by external AI agents
- AI features only on higher-tier plans

**Verdict:** Pick Gorgias when the problem is support ticket throughput, case routing, and returns processing - not discoverability or agent-endpoint coverage.

## Frequently asked questions

**What is MCP, in one sentence?** Model Context Protocol is an open standard AI agents use to discover and call external data sources and tools, so a ChatGPT or Claude session can query your business directly without a custom integration for each agent.

**Do AI agents actually drive real interactions today?** It's still early, but the direction is clear. Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity all now handle research and discovery queries on behalf of users, and the businesses whose information agents can actually reach are the ones showing up in those answers. A callable endpoint is the agent-era equivalent of having a website.

**What should my business expose to AI agents?** Pricing, availability, policies, and the specific Q&A content your customers actually ask about. Anything an agent might quote on your behalf belongs in connected, refreshing sources - not in a static document that gets stale.

**How does the FAQ content from customer conversations help discoverability?** Every real question a customer asks and gets answered adds to a structured knowledge base that AI agents can find and query. The more specific and rich that content gets, the more accurately an agent can surface your business when someone asks something relevant. It compounds - the FAQ you have six months from now is more valuable than the one you launched with.

**How do I keep an agent from quoting wrong or outdated information?** Pick a tool that grounds answers in live, connected sources and refreshes them often. Nobi refreshes connected sources twice a day and ships every answer with a citation back to the exact document and excerpt it came from - so stale content gets corrected automatically rather than getting quoted for weeks.
