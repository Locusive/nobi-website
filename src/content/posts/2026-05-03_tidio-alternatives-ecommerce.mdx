## Why look for a Tidio alternative?

Tidio is built around the idea that humans own the conversation and AI fills the gaps after hours. It's a poor fit when the real conversion problem is pre-purchase product discovery on the storefront. Lyro, Tidio's AI agent, is first-touch oriented rather than a deep conversational discovery agent, so shoppers asking nuanced product questions hit the ceiling quickly.

The pricing reflects the priorities. Lyro is sold as an add-on starting at $32.50/month for 50 conversations on top of a Tidio base plan, so brands that want serious AI volume end up stacking two line items before they get there. And because [Tidio](https://www.tidio.com/) was designed first as a live-chat inbox, semantic product discovery isn't its strong suit; tools whose primary job is [AI search for ecommerce](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-search-ecommerce-2026) or conversational sales assistance go deeper.

## 2. Rep AI

[Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) is a proactive sales chat tool built for Shopify, organized around behavioral AI that anticipates shopper intent and intervenes mid-session. Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder that lets non-engineers design deterministic conversation flows. A live-agent drop-in lets a human join an active AI session without forcing a binary handoff. An in-chat agent handles cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups. Drop-off-reason analytics tell you why a shopper bailed, not just that they did.

**Best for:** Marketing ops and CX teams who want to script campaign-specific conversation flows without engineering, or who need a human agent to jump into an active AI session mid-thread.

**Pricing:** $12 per 1,000 visitors on the Pay As You Grow tier (unlimited sessions, 500-product catalog cap). At ~125K monthly sessions, Pro tiers run $280/mo (Support), $616/mo (Sales), $740/mo (Bundle). Enterprise custom above ~500K sessions. 30-day free trial.

**Pros:**
- Flow Studio visual builder lets non-engineers design deterministic flows for promos, product launches, and abandoned-cart rescue
- Live-agent drop-in is a true hybrid - a human can join an AI session while the AI keeps handling it, rather than a binary handoff
- Post-Order Tasks Agent lets shoppers self-serve cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups inside the chat widget
- Drop-off-reason analytics answer why shoppers abandoned, not just that they did

**Cons:**
- Sales-chat-only - no site search, so discovery outside the chat widget still needs a separate tool
- Personalization is scoped to the chat session; ranking and merchandising anywhere outside the chat widget aren't part of what Rep AI does

**Verdict:** Pick Rep AI when you need a no-code flow builder, an AI plus human hybrid chat mode, or in-chat post-purchase task execution; pair it with a search tool if on-site discovery is also a priority.

## 3. Octane AI

[Octane AI](https://octaneai.com) is a quiz-led product finder built primarily for Shopify, where the quiz itself is where shoppers engage rather than open-ended chat or a search bar. For a head of ecommerce in beauty, skincare, or supplements, that focus is the point: shoppers answer five to ten diagnostic questions about skin type, goals, or routine, and Octane maps the answers to product attributes in your Shopify catalog to recommend a regimen. Octane positions itself as the leading product quiz platform for Shopify and ships pre-built templates for skincare routines, supplement regimens, and fit finders, plus a Klaviyo handoff that turns quiz answers into email-segment attributes.

**Best for:** Beauty, skincare, and CPG brands whose primary discovery funnel is a diagnostic or routine-builder quiz.

**Pricing:** Basic at $50/month (400 credits), Plus at $200/month (2,200 credits), Enterprise & Unlimited at $500+/month.

**Pros:**
- Quiz builder with deep Shopify product-attribute mapping, so recommendations resolve to real SKUs and attributes
- Pre-built templates for common vertical use cases - skincare routines, supplement regimens, fit finders - which shortens the time it takes to launch
- Native Klaviyo integration captures quiz answers as email-segment attributes, so quiz completion flows straight into lifecycle email
- Lower engineering overhead than building quiz logic in-house on top of a headless storefront

**Cons:**
- Quiz-centric, so it's less effective for shoppers who prefer open-ended search or conversational browsing over a scripted question tree
- Vertical fit narrows outside beauty and CPG categories, where scripted diagnostic questions don't map cleanly to catalog attributes

**Verdict:** Pick Octane AI if a quiz is genuinely the right discovery metaphor for your category; skip it if your catalog is too varied or technical for a scripted question tree to cover.

## 4. Gorgias

[Gorgias](https://gorgias.com) is an ecommerce helpdesk built around agent-side workflow: tickets, routing, macros, returns, and refund processing, with Shopify order context rendered directly inside each ticket so an agent can cancel, refund, or modify an order without leaving the case. A newer AI Agent layer sits on top of that helpdesk on higher-priced plans and handles repetitive inbound tickets. If your queue is drowning in "where's my order" and returns questions and you need structured workflow tooling across email, chat, and social, this is the mature answer.

**Best for:** Support-heavy brands whose bottleneck is ticket workflow, case routing, and structured returns handling rather than pre-purchase discovery.

**Pricing:** $10/month Starter tier for basic helpdesk; plans scale to $900/month Advanced, with the AI Agent add-on gated to higher-tier plans and overage charges of $0.36-$0.40 per ticket once you exceed your monthly limit.

**Pros:**
- Deep Shopify order context surfaced inside every ticket, so agents can cancel, refund, or modify an order from the ticket view
- Mature agent-side automation: macros, routing rules, SLA tracking, multi-brand tenant support
- Structured returns and refund workflows that AI-chat-only tools don't replicate
- Multi-channel coverage across email, chat, and social for teams consolidating support inboxes

**Cons:**
- No product discovery or site search; this is a helpdesk, not a shopping assistant
- AI features are gated behind higher-tier plans, so total cost jumps once you turn them on

**Verdict:** Pick Gorgias when agent-side support workflow is the problem you're solving; skip it if what you actually need is a pre-purchase shopping assistant on the storefront.
