# Nobi vs Findify: Which Site Search Actually Lifts Ecommerce Conversion Rates?

## What Nobi and Findify Actually Do

**Findify** is an AI-powered search and merchandising platform built for ecommerce. It personalizes search results, auto-generates smart collections, and serves product recommendations based on shopper behavior. Findify has been around longer and is a familiar name for Shopify Plus merchants who want to replace their store's default search with something that actually understands what shoppers are looking for.

**Nobi** is an AI shopping assistant that combines site search, conversational AI, and lead capture in a single tool. It returns product results like any search app, but it can also walk shoppers through a conversation — answering questions about fit, materials, compatibility, or use cases — the way a good sales associate would on a shop floor.

The key difference comes down to scope:

- **Findify** handles search and merchandising. If you need chat support, conversational product guidance, or lead capture, you'll need to add separate tools.
- **Nobi** covers search, merchandising, and guided shopping in one product. It handles more of the buyer journey without requiring you to stitch together multiple apps.

This matters because every additional tool you bolt onto your stack adds cost, complexity, and another vendor to manage. Whether that tradeoff is worth it depends on how much of the shopping experience you want a single product to own.

## AI Search Quality: How Each Platform Handles Real Shopper Queries

Both Nobi and Findify use AI to rank search results, but the way each platform learns — and how much work it requires from your team — differs significantly.

**Findify** combines machine learning with manual merchandising rules. Its algorithms analyze shopper behavior, but you still need someone configuring relevance ranking, setting boost/bury rules, and tuning results for key queries. If you have a dedicated search merchandiser, this gives you fine-grained control. If you don't, those rules don't write themselves, and default results may underwhelm.

**Nobi** takes a more hands-off approach. Its AI learns directly from shopper signals — clicks, add-to-carts, purchases — and adjusts result ranking automatically. No one on your team needs to manually configure which products rise or fall for a given query. For lean ecommerce teams running without a full-time search merchandiser, that difference in setup and maintenance time is real.

The bigger gap shows up in how each platform handles **natural-language queries**. Shoppers increasingly type full phrases like "black dress for a summer wedding under $200." Nobi's conversational AI layer parses that intent — occasion, color, price constraint — and returns filtered, relevant results. Findify handles synonym matching and typo tolerance well, and it's strong at structured merchandising tasks like boosting collections or pinning featured products. But when a query is unstructured and intent-heavy, keyword-matching logic hits its limits.

This plays out in the numbers. Faherty, a lifestyle apparel brand, saw a **12.3% search conversion rate** with Nobi compared to 10.1% with a previous AI search tool — a 2.2 percentage point lift attributed to better result relevance. That kind of gap compounds fast on high-traffic stores where thousands of searches happen daily.

The short version: Findify gives merchandisers manual control over search results. Nobi bets on AI that learns without that manual layer. Which matters more depends on your team's size and how much time you want to spend tuning search.

## Personalization: Out-of-the-Box vs Configuration-Heavy

Findify personalizes search results and product recommendations using AI, but getting it to perform well usually means hands-on work. You'll configure merchandising rules, define customer segments, and iterate on the setup — often in collaboration with Findify's team. That process can take weeks before personalization is actually tuned to your store's shoppers.

Nobi takes a different approach. It starts personalizing results for each visitor immediately, using behavioral signals like browsing patterns and click history. There's no upfront configuration required. The AI adapts to individual shoppers from the first session, so stores see personalized results on day one rather than after a setup period.

That doesn't mean Nobi locks you out of control. Merchandising teams can step in whenever they want:

- **Pin specific products** to the top of results for certain queries
- **Boost or bury products** based on margin, inventory, or seasonal priorities
- **Set custom actions** to override the AI for specific campaigns or categories

The key difference is that these controls are refinements, not prerequisites. The default experience is already personalized, so you're adjusting a system that works rather than building one from scratch.

**Which approach fits your team?** If you have a dedicated merchandising team that prefers setting rules and segments from the start, Findify's model will feel familiar — it gives you that granular control upfront. If you'd rather have personalization working immediately and fine-tune over time as you learn what your shoppers respond to, Nobi requires significantly less setup to get there.

## Pricing and Implementation: What Each Platform Actually Costs

**Findify does not publish pricing on its website.** You'll need to get on a sales call to learn what it costs. Historically, Findify has used usage-based pricing that scales with your traffic volume and catalog size. That model has a predictable downside: your bill goes up when your store gets busy. Black Friday, holiday rushes, a viral product moment — the months where you're making the most money are also the months where your search costs spike the hardest.

**Nobi publishes its pricing upfront.** There's no revenue share, no per-query fees, no usage-based billing that punishes you for growth. You know the number before you sign. It stays the same whether you have a slow Tuesday or your best sales day of the year.

On the implementation side, the two platforms diverge significantly:

- **Findify** integrations can stretch into weeks, particularly if you're running a complex merchandising setup or working on a less common ecommerce platform. Much of that time goes toward manual configuration — setting up synonym lists, tuning ranking rules, mapping product attributes.
- **Nobi** typically goes live in days. The AI handles most of the configuration work that Findify requires a human to do manually. Less setup time means less internal resource cost and a faster path to seeing whether the tool actually moves your metrics.

**One thing to know:** Nobi is built for mid-market ecommerce brands. If your store does under roughly $1M in GMV, the investment probably doesn't make sense yet. At that scale, a simpler native search tool or a lower-cost option like Findify may be the better fit until your traffic and catalog complexity justify a more capable system.

## Head-to-Head Results: What Happened When Brands Tested Both

Case studies are easy to cherry-pick, so the most useful data point here is a direct head-to-head test.

**UNTUCKit ran Nobi and Findify side by side on the same store, at the same time, with real shoppers.** This wasn't a before-and-after comparison where seasonal trends or site changes could muddy the results — it was a controlled A/B test. Nobi drove a **15.0% to 17.4% conversion rate increase** over Findify. For a brand doing significant volume through site search, that gap translates directly to revenue.

Other brand results add context, even without the same head-to-head setup:

- **Lucchese**, a western boot brand, switched to Nobi and generated **over $1M in incremental revenue in year one**. Their previous search wasn't handling the long-tail queries their customers actually typed — things like specific boot styles, leathers, or toe shapes. Once search started returning relevant results for those queries, more shoppers bought.
- **Kilte** saw a **21.7% conversion improvement** with Nobi compared to Shopify's default search. This matters if you're currently on Shopify's native search and weighing whether a dedicated search tool is worth the cost. That's the kind of lift that pays for itself quickly.

A few caveats worth being honest about. These numbers won't apply uniformly to every store. How much lift you see depends on several factors:

- **Catalog size.** Larger catalogs with more SKUs tend to benefit more from better search because there's more room for results to go wrong — or right.
- **How broken your current search is.** If shoppers already find what they need most of the time, the ceiling for improvement is lower. Stores where search is actively losing sales will see the biggest gains.
- **Traffic patterns.** Stores with a high percentage of search-using visitors will feel the impact more than stores where most shoppers browse by category.

The UNTUCKit test is the strongest evidence here because it isolates the variable. The other results show what's possible but reflect different starting points.

## Where Nobi Falls Short (And Where Findify Might Be the Better Fit)

No product is the right pick for every store. Here's where Nobi has gaps worth knowing about.

**Brand recognition is still limited.** Nobi doesn't show up on the same analyst reports or G2 grids as Algolia, Bloomreach, or even Findify in some verticals. If your procurement process requires vendors to clear a shortlist built from industry reports or peer polls, Nobi may get filtered out before anyone runs a head-to-head test. That's a real obstacle — even if performance data tells a different story once you evaluate directly.

**Teams that want manual control over every ranking signal may prefer Findify.** Nobi leans on AI to handle merchandising logic automatically, and for most mid-market brands that's a time saver. But some organizations have dedicated search teams with strong opinions about how every rule, boost, and bury should work. If your team wants to hand-tune ranking weights and doesn't trust a model to make those calls, Findify's more configurable approach gives you that lever.

**Nobi isn't built for smaller stores.** If your store does under roughly **$1M in annual GMV**, the math on a dedicated search tool gets hard to justify. At that scale, Shopify's native search or a lightweight app will cover most of what you need without the added cost.

**Check integration pages before you decide.** Both platforms ship updates regularly. If your evaluation hinges on a specific integration or feature — say, a particular reviews provider or loyalty platform — verify the current state on each vendor's site. Gaps that existed six months ago may not exist today.

## The Bottom Line for Ecommerce Teams Evaluating Site Search

The right pick depends on how your team actually works.

**If you don't have a dedicated search merchandiser** — and most mid-market ecommerce teams don't — Nobi is the shorter path to results. It learns from shopper behavior on its own, so you're not writing manual rules or tweaking synonyms lists for months before seeing impact. You also get conversational AI and lead capture in the same platform, which means fewer vendor contracts and fewer integration points.

**If you already staff a search and merchandising team** that wants granular, rule-by-rule control, Findify gives them the levers they're used to pulling. That control costs real time, though — both at launch and on an ongoing basis.

Neither of those summaries should be the thing that decides your budget.
