# Nobi vs Algolia: AI Site Search for Ecommerce Compared

**TL;DR:** Two different shapes of search tool, not two versions of the same one:

- **Nobi** - AI site search plus a shopping assistant and lead capture in one platform, $25/mo base with $0.01 per extra search. Pick when you want relevance that works without a merchandiser tuning rules every week and an implementation measured in days.
- **Algolia** - developer-first search infrastructure with deep APIs, SDKs, and InstantSearch UI libraries; pricing starts free and scales with queries and records. Pick when an engineering team is going to build a bespoke search experience and wants full control over ranking, indexing, and the frontend.

## What's the core difference between Nobi and Algolia for ecommerce site search?

[Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) is search infrastructure; Nobi is a finished ecommerce search product. Algolia ships APIs, SDKs, and InstantSearch UI components that your engineering team assembles into a search experience you own end-to-end. Nobi ships a working search results page and a conversational shopping assistant that activate once you connect your catalog, with no frontend build required. The real question is whether you want to buy search as a product or build one on top of a platform.

Algolia is the right pick when you have engineering capacity and a specific ranking, faceting, or UI pattern you need to control. Its AI relevance layer, NeuralSearch, is a separate add-on and isn't included on every pricing plan, so teams that want semantic search have to opt into that tier. Implementation is an engineering project: APIs, SDKs, and InstantSearch components assembled into the finished search UI, then maintained as the catalog and ranking needs evolve.

## How do Nobi and Algolia compare on pricing?

Nobi starts at $25 per month, which includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Overages run $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message - one usage-based line item. Algolia is free up to 10,000 search requests per month; its Grow tier then runs $0.50 per additional 1,000 search requests plus $0.40 per additional 1,000 records. Grow Plus is $1.75 per additional 1,000 search requests with the same per-record fee, and Improve is custom. NeuralSearch (Algolia's AI relevance layer) isn't on every plan, so turning on AI search at scale can push a brand into a higher tier.

Algolia's pricing rewards low-volume stores. Under 10,000 search requests a month with no NeuralSearch, the free tier is hard to beat. It gets expensive when volume, record count, and AI relevance all scale together, because each is metered separately. A catalog with 500,000 records and 500,000 monthly searches on Grow pays for both sides of that equation, and NeuralSearch sits behind a higher plan. Pick Algolia when you want the developer platform and can model a multi-variable bill.

Nobi's bill is one usage-based line: searches and messages. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month lands around $995 - the $25 base plus $475 in search overage plus $495 in message overage. No per-record indexing fee, no AI-relevance upcharge, no revenue-share. The trade-off is breadth: Nobi is a finished search and conversational discovery product, not an API-first platform for teams that want to build their own ranking stack.

## Which delivers better AI relevance out of the box?

Nobi ships semantic relevance as the default behavior. A shopper searching for "brown boots" surfaces a product the merchandiser named the Cappuccino Boot, with no synonym list to maintain and no tuning ticket to file. Algolia's keyword engine is fast and configurable, but its AI relevance layer (NeuralSearch and Dynamic Re-Ranking) sits in a higher tier and needs an engineering team to wire it in and tune it against query logs.

Nobi's semantic layer runs without a rule library. Kilte, a DTC fashion brand with brand-voice color names (Cappuccino, Ivory, Midnight, Écru), saw a 21.7% conversion lift in an A/B test against Shopify's default search once Nobi's color-normalization [mapped shopper vocabulary to catalog language](https://nobi.ai/blog/handling-long-tail-queries) - "brown" finding the Cappuccino Boot even though the word "brown" never appears in the product record. The engine keeps learning from clicks and purchases to sharpen ranking over time, so the merchandiser isn't hand-pinning products to queries each week.

Algolia's base product is a tokenized keyword engine with strong filtering, faceting, and typo tolerance - the foundations of fast search at scale. Its AI relevance lives in NeuralSearch, which combines keyword matching with vector-based semantic search and isn't available on every plan. NeuralSearch is an opt-in layer that engineers wire in and tune against query logs, so semantic relevance is configured rather than enabled by default. Best when an engineering team wants to own ranking logic, query-log tuning, and a custom frontend.

One honest concession: if your team wants to build bespoke ranking rules or embed search inside a highly custom frontend, Algolia's developer platform is the right shape for that work.

## How do implementation and ongoing engineering requirements compare?

Nobi installs on Shopify in days, not months. You connect the catalog, drop the search bar into the theme, and the assistant works against your products without anyone writing custom frontend code or modeling search records by hand. Algolia is a build: an engineering team integrates the API, models the index records, configures ranking rules, and assembles a search UI from the InstantSearch libraries, then keeps maintaining all of it as the catalog and ranking needs evolve. Both vendors run hosted infrastructure - the difference isn't where the search runs, it's what the brand has to build and own on top of it.

Algolia gives engineering teams precise control over how search behaves. Records, attributes, ranking formula, typo tolerance, synonyms, faceting, and the frontend search UI are all configurable, and a team that wants to write their own ranking logic or embed search in a bespoke storefront has the API surface to do it. The trade-off is the work itself. Setup means schema design, indexing pipelines, ranking configuration, and a frontend assembled from InstantSearch components; catalog changes typically mean reindexing and re-tuning ranking; ongoing relevance work sits with whoever owns the integration. Best when the brand has engineering capacity to own setup, ranking iteration, and continuous relevance work, since maintenance is ongoing rather than one-time configuration.

Nobi takes the opposite approach. The platform is the finished search experience - semantic relevance, the conversational assistant, the search UI, and the merchandising controls are already built, and the assistant adapts as the catalog updates without a reindex-and-retune cycle. Faster implementation than enterprise alternatives is the lane. The honest limit: Nobi is not an API-first developer platform. Teams that want to write custom ranking logic or embed search in a highly bespoke frontend will prefer Algolia. Teams that want a working AI search experience live in days, with merchandisers (not engineers) tuning it from there, are the ones Nobi is built for.

## How do merchandising and personalization controls compare?

Algolia gives merchandisers a dedicated Merchandising Studio for pinning products to queries, building rules, and running A/B tests, with personalization and related-item modules sold as a separate product called Algolia Recommend. Nobi handles personalization automatically per visitor based on behavior and intent, with no rule library for merchandisers to maintain. The honest trade-off is scope: Nobi curates the search results page, not category or collection pages, so brands that need true site-wide merchandising still want a dedicated tool.

Algolia's merchandising surface is built for teams who want explicit control. A merchandiser can pin specific products to a query, create rules that promote or demote items based on attributes or inventory status, and A/B test variants to see which ranking performs better. Personalization sits in Algolia Recommend and is configured and priced as a separate product. If you have a merchandising team that thinks in rules and wants to express specific business logic ("always promote new arrivals on 'summer dress' queries through June 30"), Algolia gives you the surface to do that. The cost is operational overhead: rules are written and maintained by humans, and the library compounds over time.

Nobi takes the opposite approach. Personalization is visitor-aware out of the box, so the products each shopper sees are tuned to their behavior without anyone writing a rule. Kilte uses Nobi across three discovery surfaces - the search bar, collection filters, and product discovery - so the same semantic layer compounds wherever shoppers land, not just in one box. The limit worth naming: Nobi does not curate category or collection pages themselves. A brand running hand-tuned collection grids for seasonal campaigns (the Valentine's edit, the back-to-school capsule) still needs a dedicated merchandising tool for those pages. For search itself, the automatic behavior usually lands closer to what a shopper wants than a hand-built rule library would.

## When is Algolia the better choice over Nobi?

Algolia is the right call when an engineering team is going to own the search experience and wants the deepest possible API surface to build on. If your roadmap includes a custom-ranked search UI, your developers want to express ranking logic in code, your stack already runs on Algolia in other surfaces, or you need a third-party integration marketplace broader than what a newer vendor can offer, Algolia's developer platform fits the job better than Nobi.

[Algolia](https://www.algolia.com/) is built as a search API first and a merchandising surface second. The strength shows up when an engineering team wants to wire ranking signals, custom rules, and UI components together in code rather than configure them in a vendor dashboard. Pricing scales with that posture: a Free tier covers 10K search requests and 100K records per month, then Grow charges $0.50 per additional 1K requests, Grow Plus runs $1.75 per additional 1K, and Improve is custom-quoted with volume discounts. NeuralSearch (the AI-relevance layer) isn't bundled into every plan, so the "buy Algolia and get AI relevance for free" math doesn't always hold - check the plan. The honest weakness is that the developer-first surface is operational work for the engineering team that owns it. Relevance tuning, rule maintenance, and ranking iteration are ongoing engineering responsibilities, not one-time configuration. Best when the brand has dedicated search engineers who want maximum control and are budgeted to maintain the system over time.

Nobi is the wrong tool for that job and we'll say so plainly. Nobi is not an API-first developer platform - teams building bespoke frontends with hand-coded ranking logic will hit the ceiling fast. Nobi's third-party integration marketplace is also smaller than Algolia's, which matters if your stack already standardises on Algolia connectors elsewhere. Pick Nobi when you want search relevance to work without an engineering owner; pick Algolia when you have one.

## When is Nobi the better choice over Algolia?

Nobi is the right call when on-site discovery is the bottleneck and the team buying the tool sits on the ecommerce or merchandising side, not engineering. Brands that need semantic relevance, a [conversational shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce), and lead capture working in days - without a quarterly cycle of rule tuning or a separate AI add-on bill - get there faster with Nobi. Nobi fits ecommerce teams whose primary friction is search relevance and who don't have dedicated engineering capacity to maintain a search stack. The product ships AI relevance, personalization, and a conversational assistant in one platform on a usage-based bill: $25/month base including 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. No revenue-share, no per-seat fees, no separate invoice when you want the AI features turned on. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month lands around $995. Implementation is days on Shopify, and merchandisers don't spend their week hand-pinning products to queries to hit relevance targets. The honest trade: Nobi curates the search results page, not category or collection pages, so brands that need merchandising across the entire site will still pair it with a dedicated tool for those surfaces.

[Algolia](https://www.algolia.com/) is the better pick when an engineering team owns the search experience end to end and wants the deepest API surface, custom ranking logic, and a full merchandising studio for rule-based pinning. Pick Algolia when API flexibility and developer control are the priority; pick Nobi when merchandiser time and time-to-launch are the constraint.

## Frequently asked questions

A few questions come up every time a head of ecommerce compares Nobi and Algolia for site search: how AI relevance is priced, whether either tool replaces a merchandising platform, what migration actually looks like, and how a conversational shopping assistant fits in. Short answers below.

**Does Algolia's free tier include AI relevance?** No. Algolia's free plan covers keyword search up to 10K requests and 100K records per month. NeuralSearch, Algolia's AI vector search layer, isn't bundled on every tier - access depends on the plan and it's metered separately from base search requests. [If AI relevance is the reason you're evaluating](https://nobi.ai/blog/choose-ai-search-tool-ecommerce), budget for the higher tier rather than the free one.

**Will Nobi or [Algolia](https://www.algolia.com) replace a full merchandising tool?** Neither covers site-wide collection-page merchandising. Both focus on the search results surface. Brands that need to merchandise category and collection pages beyond search typically pair their search tool with a dedicated merchandiser.

**How long does migration take?** Nobi installs in days on Shopify via the app. Algolia ships through its official Shopify integration plus storefront engineering work, and teams commonly spend weeks to months depending on how much custom ranking, faceting, and UI work they want.

**Does Algolia include a conversational shopping assistant?** No. Algolia is search infrastructure. A conversational discovery layer is a separate build on top, using Algolia's APIs plus an LLM. Nobi ships site search and the conversational assistant in one platform on the web chat surface.
