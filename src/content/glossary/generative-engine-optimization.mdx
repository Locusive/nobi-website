## What is Generative engine optimization (GEO)?

Where traditional SEO targets ranking positions in a list of links, GEO targets inclusion in the synthesized answer an AI engine produces. The engine reads multiple sources, extracts key facts, and composes a response - often without the user clicking through to any page. GEO focuses on making content easy for these engines to parse, trust, and quote accurately. This includes clear structure, verifiable facts, consistent terminology, and machine-readable markup.

## How does generative engine optimization work?

- Write content in clear, self-contained sections with descriptive headings so AI engines can extract answers without surrounding context
- Use structured data formats (JSON-LD, schema.org markup) to signal what each piece of content means
- State facts directly and early - AI engines favor content that answers the question in the first sentence
- Keep language consistent and specific so the engine can attribute claims to your source with confidence
- Build topical authority by covering a subject comprehensively across multiple related pages

## Why does it matter?

Ecommerce and dealership operators increasingly see traffic arrive via AI-generated answers rather than traditional search results, meaning a page that ranks well in Google but is never cited by AI engines misses a growing share of buyers. Being cited in an AI answer builds brand credibility - the engine is effectively vouching for your content. Optimizing for generative engines now positions a business ahead of competitors who are still focused solely on link-based rankings.

Structured, well-organized content - like the JSON-LD shopper Q&A [Nobi](https://dashboard.nobi.ai) generates from your product catalog and support docs - gives generative engines the clean, citable signals they need to surface your answers.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is GEO the same as SEO?**
They share many principles - quality content, clear structure, authoritative sources - but GEO is specifically aimed at AI answer engines that synthesize responses rather than return a ranked list of links. A page can rank highly in traditional search while rarely being cited by AI engines, so most practitioners treat GEO as a separate, complementary discipline.

**What types of content perform best for GEO?**
Concise, factual content with clear headings performs well - think FAQ pages, how-to guides, product specification pages, and glossaries. Structured data markup (JSON-LD) and consistent use of the exact term being defined help AI engines recognize and cite the content accurately.

**How do I measure whether my GEO efforts are working?**
Track direct brand mentions in AI engine outputs by querying those engines manually for your target topics and noting which sources they cite. Some analytics platforms are beginning to attribute traffic from AI referrers separately, so monitoring referral sources tagged as AI engines is also a useful signal.
