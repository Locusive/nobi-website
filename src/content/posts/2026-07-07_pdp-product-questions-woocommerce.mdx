## How do I answer shopper questions directly on a WooCommerce product page?

A shopper on your WooCommerce product page asks something specific - will this run small, does this work with the router they already own, is this fabric machine washable - and the answer isn't anywhere on the page. They check the description, maybe scroll to reviews, and when nothing matches what they typed, they either open a support ticket or close the tab. Install Nobi on that product page and it answers that question directly, pulling from the exact SKU's own product data and content instead of a generic store-wide FAQ. Ask the same question enough times and Nobi turns it into a visible FAQ block right on the page, then outputs it as FAQPage JSON-LD, so the same content that saves you a ticket can also land you a rich result in search.

## Why does a generic FAQ page fail to answer product-specific questions?

A store-wide FAQ page answers questions about the brand, not about the specific pair of boots or the specific chair a shopper is currently looking at, so sizing, material, and compatibility questions go unanswered right where the add-to-cart decision happens. That vocabulary mismatch - the shopper's exact question doesn't match any FAQ entry's wording - is what pushes them to abandon the page, open a support ticket, or just leave.

Your FAQ page is written at the store level: shipping windows, return policy, payment methods, maybe a paragraph on sizing philosophy. None of that tells a shopper whether this specific fabric runs small, whether this specific chair fits under a standard desk, or whether this specific part is compatible with the model they already own. A generic FAQ can't scale to SKU-level answers without turning into a directory nobody reads. So the shopper lands on the PDP with a real, product-specific question, checks the FAQ link out of habit, finds nothing that matches, and gives up on the FAQ entirely.

From there they take one of two paths, and both cost you. They file a support ticket and wait for an answer that should have taken five seconds, or they close the tab. Either way, that's revenue per visit walking out the door. Fit and compatibility doubt is a common driver of cart abandonment, and it's just as often the reason a product comes back after purchase.

## How does Nobi keep answers scoped to the exact product a shopper is viewing?

Nobi reads the WooCommerce product context the widget is embedded on: the specific SKU, its attributes, and its variations, and scopes retrieval to that product's own data plus any content connected for it. A question asked on one product page pulls facts about that product, not a different one in the catalog. That scoping is what stops the AI assistant from confidently giving a wrong answer. Instead of guessing or blending facts across SKUs, the assistant either answers from that product's grounded content or says it doesn't know.

This matters most on sizing and fit questions, since those carry the highest return risk. A men's jacket listing and a women's jacket listing might use similar language in their descriptions, but per-SKU scoping keeps a sizing answer on one page from leaking fit notes that belong to the other. The assistant only draws from the product the shopper is actually looking at, so a "does this run small" answer on the men's jacket page can't accidentally pull a note that was written for a different garment with a different cut.

Some questions are too high-stakes to leave to a model's phrasing on the fly. A compatibility claim or a warranty term might get asked a dozen different ways, and merchants don't want the wording to drift between those variations. Query overrides handle that: a merchant can lock an exact, verbatim answer to a known question, and it fires the same way every time a shopper's question matches, no paraphrasing, no variation.

Scoping to live data only holds up if the data itself stays current, and that's the same refresh cycle covering every SKU, not a separate schedule per product. That's a meaningfully tighter loop than the stale, multi-week sync some help-desk-layered AI tools run on, where a sold-out item can keep getting recommended long after it's gone.

## How do product-page questions become a crowd-sourced FAQ?

When shoppers keep asking the same question on a product page, a merchant can pin the exact answer to that question with a query override, and it appears as a visible entry on that same page. Instead of a merchandiser guessing what belongs there, the FAQ reflects what shoppers actually asked. That visible block does two jobs at once. It works as social proof, since other shoppers clearly had the same question, and it means the next shopper who wonders the same thing finds the answer already sitting on the page instead of asking again.

Because the FAQ comes from real questions, it naturally uses the words shoppers use, not the words a copywriter would pick. If shoppers keep asking whether a jacket "runs small" instead of asking about its "fit," that's the phrasing that ends up in the FAQ. A hand-written FAQ almost never anticipates that exact vocabulary mismatch. A crowd-sourced one can't help but match it, since it's built from the mismatch itself.

The effect compounds over time. The first time a question gets asked, Nobi answers it in the chat. Once a merchant has pinned it as a standing entry, the assistant (and the shopper) never has to work through it from scratch again.

It's also specific to the product, not the store. The FAQ that builds up under a hiking boot will cover traction, break-in time, and trail versus pavement use. The FAQ under a dining chair will cover weight limits, assembly, and whether it fits under a standard table. Each product page earns its own long tail of questions, shaped by what that specific item makes shoppers wonder, which is exactly the gap a store-wide FAQ page could never close.

## How does the product-page Q&A also help SEO and AEO?

That accumulating, product-specific long tail doesn't just sit on the page for shoppers. Nobi outputs the crowd-sourced FAQ as FAQPage JSON-LD schema on the product page, so the same question-and-answer pairs answering shoppers directly are also structured for Google's rich results and for AI answer engines like AI Overviews and Perplexity that look for question-shaped, sourced content to cite. One feature does the job that used to require a separate schema-markup project.

Search engines and LLMs don't parse a paragraph of marketing copy looking for a direct answer. They parse structured question-and-answer pairs, because that's a format built for extraction. A product page with three flowing paragraphs about "premium comfort" gives a crawler nothing to lift. A product page with "Does this run small?" paired with a specific answer is exactly the shape an answer engine is scanning for.

The part that would normally make this expensive is keeping the markup current. A manual FAQ schema project means someone writes the questions, someone else checks the answers still match the product, and both need revisiting every time a spec or a policy changes. Nobi's FAQ schema doesn't add that maintenance cost, because the markup is generated straight from the same pinned answers the merchant already curated. When the underlying content updates, the FAQ updates with it, and the markup follows without a separate pass.

So a feature built to answer questions on the page, cutting into ticket volume, turns into an incremental organic-visibility win on that same page, at no extra setup cost. You're not running two projects, one for shopper-facing Q&A and one for search markup. You're running one, and the schema is a byproduct of shoppers actually using it. The FAQ that builds itself from real questions is the same FAQ Google and AI answer engines read.

## When is a product-page assistant like Nobi the wrong tool for a WooCommerce question?

The FAQ block you just saw is built from real questions, but it only covers questions Nobi can actually answer. Nobi answers pre-purchase and post-purchase questions in a web chat widget on the product page, but it doesn't execute transactions. It won't process a return, look up a live order status, or run a scripted campaign flow inside the chat. If a shopper asks whether a jacket runs small, Nobi answers from your connected content. If that same shopper asks Nobi to actually start a return on the jacket they bought last week, that's outside what the assistant does. It can tell them the return window and the steps, pulled from your policy page, but the return itself still runs through whatever system already owns that job.

That split matters when you're deciding what to install where. Nobi reduces the volume of questions that would otherwise become tickets, but it hands off anything transactional to the tool built for it. Pricing reflects that scope too: $25 a month covers 2,500 searches and 250 messages, with $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra message beyond that. It's a flat, transparent add-on, not a usage-gated support platform you have to negotiate a contract for.

If the real problem on your product pages turns out to be a workflow problem, tickets piling up unrouted, no macros, no clean agent handoff, that's a job for a helpdesk, not a product-page assistant. And if you need a visual, no-code way to script a specific campaign flow (a Black Friday sequence, an abandoned-cart rescue), that's a different tool too. Nobi answers the question in front of the shopper. What happens after the question, that's still yours to route.
