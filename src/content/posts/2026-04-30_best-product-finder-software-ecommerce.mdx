## What is product finder software, and how does it differ from default site search?

Product finder software helps shoppers find the right item when default search returns zero results or makes them wade through a category page. Shopify and WooCommerce search match on keywords, so a typo or a synonym often returns nothing. The category splits across smart site search, knowledge platforms, conversational assistants, and quiz-led flows.

Nobi is a smart site search and [shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-shopping-assistants-for-ecommerce) that ranks results by shopper behavior, not hand-pinned merchandising rules. Pick Nobi when search relevance and conversational product Q&A are the bottleneck; the trade-off is that Nobi curates the search results page, not category pages, so site-wide merchandising still needs another tool.

[Yext](https://www.yext.com) is a knowledge platform that publishes one canonical answer across site search, FAQ pages, and partner surfaces. Strong when consistent answers across channels matter; weaker as a pure on-site product discovery layer.

[Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) is a customer messaging platform with the Fin AI Agent layered on top of the Intercom inbox. Fin pulls from Help Center articles plus external syncs (Notion, Confluence, Zendesk, public URLs) and answers questions inside the Intercom Messenger. Strong when an existing helpdesk and inbox already anchor the support stack; weaker as a lightweight standalone Q&A surface.

[Re:amaze](https://www.reamaze.com) is a multi-channel customer support platform with live chat, FAQs, and automated messaging in one inbox. Strong when shopper questions span email, chat, social, and SMS and need a unified inbox; built for tickets and conversations rather than on-site product discovery.

## 2. Intercom

[Intercom](https://intercom.com) is a customer messaging platform with the Fin AI Agent layered on top of an Intercom helpdesk. Fin reads from Intercom Help Center articles plus external sources - public URLs, PDFs, Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, Zendesk - and answers shopper questions inside the Intercom Messenger. The platform itself is a full customer messaging stack: the inbox, the seats, the macros, proactive messages, product tours, and a mobile SDK all sit alongside the AI layer. Shopify integrations bring order context directly into the conversation, so an agent reviewing an escalation has the order details inline.

**Best for:** Teams already running Intercom as their full ticketing and messaging platform who want an AI Q&A layer inside that existing stack.

**Pricing:** Seat-based starting around $39/seat/month for Essential, plus resolution-based fees for Fin AI Agent on top of the subscription.

**Pros:**
- Fin pulls from native Help Center content alongside external syncs (Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, Zendesk), so an existing knowledge base isn't wasted
- Tight integration with the Intercom inbox keeps escalations, macros, and reporting in one place
- Mature messenger with proactive messages, product tours, and a mobile SDK if you already run an app

**Cons:**
- Built around the Intercom Messenger and inbox, so the AI lives inside Intercom's chat surface rather than as a standalone widget on your storefront
- Not ecommerce-specific, so you'll pay for SaaS-oriented features a founder won't use
- Costs climb fast at scale because Fin runs on top of a seat-based subscription rather than as a standalone product

**Verdict:** Strongest when the team already lives inside Intercom and wants Fin riding on the seats and inbox they're already paying for.

## 3. Re:amaze

[Re:amaze](https://reamaze.com) is a customer support platform built for ecommerce, with a unified inbox that pulls email, live chat, social, and SMS into one place. On Shopify and BigCommerce stores, order data sits next to the conversation by default, so an agent can look up tracking, issue a refund, or check return eligibility without flipping tabs. The platform also includes a help center, automated messaging, and FAQ tooling, giving a small team coverage across the questions that don't need a human at all. For a founder running support solo or with one or two teammates, that breadth matters more than a deeper feature set in any single channel: one tool, one bill, one place to look when a shopper sends a message.

**Best for:** Stores whose shopper questions are mostly order status, shipping, and returns, and whose primary need is a fast, ecommerce-native inbox rather than on-site product discovery.

**Pricing:** $20 or $40 per user per month on the published Basic and Pro tiers, with a higher Enterprise tier that adds channels and automation.

**Pros:**
- Ecommerce-native from the inbox up: order lookups, refunds, and tracking links live alongside the conversation
- One inbox for email, chat, social, and SMS, so a small team isn't switching dashboards to keep up
- Predictable per-user pricing is friendly to founders who want to know the bill before they commit

**Cons:**
- Support-only, with no on-site search or product discovery surface, so a shopper who can't find a product still can't find it
- Less AI-forward than purpose-built AI assistants; you'll write more macros than you would on a tool that grounds answers in your catalog automatically

**Verdict:** Best when most of your shopper questions are post-purchase and you want a clean, ecommerce-aware support inbox; pair it with [a product discovery tool](https://nobi.ai/blog/ecommerce-product-discovery-beyond-filters) if shoppers also struggle to find products on the site.

## 4. Yext

[Yext](https://yext.com) is a knowledge graph and answers platform that publishes one canonical set of facts - products, FAQs, locations, policies - to search engines, partner directories, and your own site. Yext earns its keep when shoppers, search engines, and partner surfaces all need to see the same authoritative facts and you have a deep content footprint to govern. The natural-language answers layer is strong on FAQ-heavy questions grounded in structured content, and the approval workflows fit teams that need editorial review before anything goes live.

**Best for:** Brands publishing a deep, authoritative content footprint - many SKUs, many locations, many policies - who want one source of truth across owned and partner surfaces.

**Pricing:** Quoted on annual contracts, scoped to the size of your knowledge graph and the surfaces you syndicate to. Yext doesn't publish a self-serve plan, so expect a sales conversation before you see a number.

**Pros:**
- One canonical knowledge graph keeps product facts, FAQs, and policies in sync across owned and partner surfaces
- Strong on natural-language answers grounded in structured content, which fits FAQ-heavy categories
- Mature governance and approvals workflow for teams that need editorial review before content goes live

**Cons:**
- Not purpose-built for ecommerce product discovery - the answer to "what's your return policy" is excellent, but ranking products on a search results page isn't the focus
- Heavier setup and contracting than a self-serve search add-on; budget weeks, not days, for a founder-led rollout

**Verdict:** Best when authoritative content across many surfaces is the strategic asset; for a founder who just needs better on-site search and product Q&A, a focused ecommerce tool is a faster path.

## Frequently asked questions

**How fast can a product finder tool go live?** It depends on the install path. A site-side install - paste in a snippet and drop a placeholder where the search bar or assistant should appear - is usually a few days of developer work. Tools that require a full platform migration or custom ranking models take weeks to months. Nobi sits in the days-not-months camp on Shopify, BigCommerce, Magento, and WooCommerce.

**Will the tool replace my native site search?** Most product finder tools replace the default search bar entirely. You swap out the native theme search, and the new tool renders results in its place. Conversational assistants and quiz-led flows usually sit alongside search rather than replacing it, so you may end up running both surfaces.

**How is pricing typically structured?** Three models show up in this category: per-seat (priced by team size), usage-based (priced by searches or messages handled), and annual enterprise contracts. Nobi is in the usage camp at $25/month base, with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message past the included quota. Per-seat works for support-heavy stacks; usage works when traffic is the main variable.

**Do I need a developer to install one?** Yes, even for drop-in installs. Someone has to edit the theme and place the surface where the widget should render. Budget a few hours of dev time at minimum.
