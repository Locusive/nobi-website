# How to Implement a Shopping Chatbot for Ecommerce Success

**TL;DR:** Adding a shopping chatbot to an ecommerce site comes down to four steps:

1. **Pick a platform built for the job you actually need** - search, conversational discovery, scripted campaign flows, or post-order support.
2. **Install and ground it in your live product catalog** so it answers from real inventory, not hallucinated SKUs.
3. **Instrument a holdout segment before launch** so you can attribute revenue uplift cleanly.
4. **Measure incremental revenue against that holdout** - not chat volume, not deflection rate in isolation.

Nobi starts at $25/month and includes 2,500 searches plus 250 conversational messages.

## What does a shopping chatbot actually do on an ecommerce site?

A shopping chatbot is an [AI-powered conversational interface](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce) that sits on an ecommerce site and helps visitors find products, answer pre-purchase questions, capture email or SMS leads, and deflect common support questions. The modern versions are grounded in your actual product catalog and shopper data, so when a visitor asks "do you have this in a wide fit under $80?" the bot recommends real SKUs with real availability instead of hallucinating a product that doesn't exist.

Surfaces vary by platform. The baseline is a chat widget on every page. On more capable platforms, the same conversational layer also powers the search results page (a shopper types a query and gets a ranked list plus an answer), the product detail page (a Q&A assistant that answers fit, sizing, compatibility, and returns questions inline), and sometimes the cart.

Worth naming up front where a shopping chatbot isn't the right category - because the surfaces above get confused with helpdesk tools all the time. A shopping chatbot is not a helpdesk. Helpdesks like Gorgias or Zendesk manage tickets agent-side - they're the interface your support team uses to handle cases, macros, and returns. A shopping chatbot talks to the shopper directly, before a ticket would ever get filed.

## How much does a shopping chatbot cost, and what pricing models should you expect?

Shopping chatbot pricing breaks into three shapes, and the one you pick matters less than whether you model your actual volume before signing. Flat subscription with included usage and per-unit overage is the most predictable. Custom GMV-indexed quotes scale your bill with revenue rather than chatbot-influenced sessions. Helpdesk-bundled pricing puts chat inside a broader ticketing platform, usually with AI features gated behind higher tiers.

Nobi sits in the flat-plus-overage camp. $25/month base, including 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Beyond that, $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month lands around $995. No revenue share, no per-seat add-ons, no AI gating between tiers.

Rep AI and Octane AI both lean toward quotes indexed to store size or GMV. Octane AI publishes three tiers - Basic at $50/month for 400 credits, Plus at $200/month for 2,200 credits, and Enterprise/Unlimited starting at $500+/month - with custom Enterprise pricing for larger stores. Credit-based plus Shopify-store-sized quoting means the variable bill tracks revenue more than actual chatbot sessions. Rep AI doesn't publish a public price sheet; quotes vary by Shopify store size.

Gorgias and Tidio are helpdesk-bundled. Gorgias uses a ticket-based model from $10/month (Starter) up to $900/month (Advanced), plus a custom Enterprise option, with overage charges of $0.36-$0.40 per ticket once you exceed your monthly limit. Tidio offers a free trial including 50 Lyro conversations; paid AI sits in Lyro-specific tiers on top of the base chat product.

Alby is the one outlier - Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024 and its standalone pricing is no longer published; post-acquisition, Alby's PDP Q&A is effectively bundled into Bluecore's contract, so the pricing conversation happens at the Bluecore level rather than line-item per feature.

Before you sign anywhere, model your six-month bill at projected traffic. GMV-indexed quotes and per-ticket overages can look cheap at tier one and balloon by month three.

## How do you plan a shopping chatbot rollout before you pick a tool?

Plan the rollout in three stages: scoping, technical prep, and go-live. Skip any of them and the tool you pick will feel wrong inside a month.

Scoping means writing down the single primary job the chatbot has to do on your site. Product discovery, lead capture, post-purchase deflection, or a specific campaign flow are all valid jobs, but the best tool for each is different. A platform that's great at conversational product discovery will do a mediocre job of ticket deflection, and a support-first bot will not move your AOV. Pick one primary job, rank the second and third as stretch goals, and shortlist against the primary. Teams that try to solve all four jobs at once end up with a tool that's adequate at each and great at none.

Technical prep is the part most teams skip. Verify catalog feed quality first: missing attributes, wrong inventory counts, and broken image URLs will show up in the chatbot's recommendations faster than they show up anywhere else on your site. Instrument analytics before the tool goes live so you can measure CVR and AOV on chatbot-interacted sessions against a holdout segment. Decide widget placement, trigger rules, and what the bot does when it can't answer before the vendor asks you in the implementation call.

Go-live should be a soft launch to 10-25% of traffic for one to two weeks before rolling out wide. That window catches tone, recommendation-quality, and fallback issues while the blast radius is small. Model your projected six-month cost at expected traffic and message volume, not the brochure number. The budget leak at this stage is picking a tool whose pricing scales with GMV and then discovering months later that the variable bill tracks total revenue rather than actual chatbot-influenced sessions. Run the overage math before you sign.

## What does the implementation timeline look like from install to launch?

Modern LLM-grounded shopping chatbots install on Shopify in days, not months. A typical week-one timeline: catalog sync the same day you install, tone and fallback configuration over the next 2-3 days, analytics wiring so you can attribute revenue (another 2-3 days), and a soft launch to 10-25% of traffic by the end of week one. Helpdesk-bundled tools take longer because the rollout includes training the agent team on new ticket workflows, macros, and case routing, not just dropping in a chat widget. Plan on 1-2 weeks for a modern shopping chatbot and 4-8 weeks for anything bundled with a helpdesk migration.

Rep AI installs from the Shopify App Store and pulls catalog and order data through the native Shopify integration on day one. Tone and proactive-engagement triggers are configured in-app, so most brands are running a soft launch within a week. The longest part of a Rep AI rollout is usually deciding which proactive intervention rules to enable, not the technical install.

Octane AI is faster still for its specific job. Quiz-led product discovery is the hero surface, and the AI quiz generator builds a first-draft quiz from your catalog in minutes. Brands using Octane AI as their primary discovery flow can have a quiz live in 2-3 days; the remaining time goes into Klaviyo handoff wiring and reviewing the recommendation logic against actual catalog inventory.

Gorgias is the long-pole option because it isn't really a chat-widget rollout, it's a support-ops migration. Plans run from $10/month Starter to $900/month Advanced, and the 4-8 week timeline reflects ticket-system cutover, macro rebuilding, and agent training, not chatbot installation itself. If your team is replacing an existing helpdesk at the same time, budget the longer end.

The step brands skip most often is analytics wiring. Without a holdout group and revenue attribution in place before launch, you'll have a chatbot in production and no way to prove what it's worth.

## How do you measure whether your shopping chatbot is actually working?

Measure incremental revenue from chatbot sessions against a holdout, not chat volume. The three numbers that tie to marketing KPIs: conversion rate on sessions that interacted with the chatbot versus a matched holdout that didn't, average order value on chatbot-assisted checkouts, and email or SMS captures attributable to the chat surface. Set the holdout up on day one. If you wait, you'll spend a quarter arguing about whether the chatbot caused the lift or whether those shoppers would have converted anyway.

[Published benchmarks](https://nobi.ai/blog/state-of-ecommerce-site-search-2026) give you something to anchor against. Faherty's Shop with AI assistant runs at a 6.3% CVR with a $315 AOV, which is a useful yardstick for a conversational discovery surface on a premium apparel catalog. Lucchese generated over $1M in incremental revenue in year one at a 39x ROI using Nobi across search, a cart assistant, and a PDP assistant, and has since accumulated $3.46M in attributed revenue. The cart and PDP assistant surfaces drove meaningful incremental checkouts on top of search, which matters when you're deciding whether to expand beyond the search box.

Three vanity metrics to ignore in isolation: total messages handled, raw engagement rate, and deflection rate. Messages handled scales with traffic, not value. Engagement rate flatters tools that interrupt shoppers who were already going to convert. Deflection rate as a standalone number rewards any chatbot that answers anything, even when the answer sent the shopper away from a purchase. Pair deflection with downstream CVR on the same sessions or don't report it.

One differentiator that matters here: Nobi's analytics are built around search and CVR, not shopper drop-off reasons. If you need behavioral drop-off analytics as your primary lens, that's a real gap worth naming before you commit.

## When is a shopping chatbot NOT the right move for your store?

A shopping chatbot is the wrong investment in a few specific cases, and it's worth naming them before you sign a contract.

If your site gets under a few thousand monthly sessions, you don't have the sample size to move conversion rate in a way you can measure. Fix landing-page bounce first - chatbot lifts show up once traffic is stable, not before. If your conversion angle hinges on a branded voice or celebrity character in chat, most platforms including Nobi aren't purpose-built for that persona work and you'll need a bespoke build. And if your shoppers live on WhatsApp, SMS, or voice rather than your site, Nobi is web chat only, so you'll need a messaging-channel tool to reach them there.

The vendor-specific cutouts matter too. If marketing ops wants to design scripted conversation trees per campaign - a Black Friday flow, a product-launch pitch, an abandoned-cart rescue - without engineering help, Rep AI's Flow Studio gives ops a visual builder with one-click Shopify integration for that job. If a product quiz is your hero discovery surface, Octane AI is the specialist: AI-powered quiz generation, 50+ integrations including Klaviyo and Recharge, Basic at $50/month. And if your real bottleneck is agent-side ticket workflow rather than shopper-facing chat, pair a chatbot with Gorgias, which centralizes tickets, automates repetitive cases, and runs macros inside a Shopify-native helpdesk.
