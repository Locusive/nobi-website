## Why look for a Shopify Search and Discovery alternative?

Shopify's free Search & Discovery app handles synonyms, redirects, and basic filters from the admin, but it's a keyword-matching layer with no semantic understanding, no way to track zero-result rate in the dashboard, and no conversational layer. Heads of ecommerce outgrow it when long descriptive queries land on null pages, when search abandonment shows up as a CVR drag, or when a merchandiser spends hours each week building synonym groups that the next vocabulary mismatch still misses. Personalization and behavioral ranking aren't part of the native app either.

## How did we evaluate these tools?

We picked the four alternatives a head of ecommerce on Shopify would actually shortlist after deciding the native app isn't enough: Nobi, Klevu, Searchspring, and Fast Simon. Each one is graded on the same five criteria: semantic matching quality, analytics depth, merchandising workflow, pricing transparency, and time to live on a Shopify store.

Semantic matching is the first cut. Shopify's native app does keyword matching, so long descriptive queries like "wide-leg cropped trouser in linen" land on null pages when the title says "cropped wide pant." We graded each tool on how it resolves conversational queries without a merchandiser writing a synonym rule for every variant. Nobi and Klevu both [rank semantically by default](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-semantic-search-ecommerce). Searchspring leans on rule-based relevance with category-level custom sorts that let merchandisers pin promoted SKUs into specific positions. Fast Simon ships multimodal search (text and image) with drag-and-drop merchandising on top.

Analytics depth matters because zero-result rate, search abandonment, and query-to-CVR are the metrics a head of ecommerce actually reports on. We looked at whether each tool surfaces those numbers to merchandisers in a dashboard, or buries them in logs an engineer has to pull.

Merchandising workflow is the weekly cost of running the tool. How many synonym groups, pins, and dead-end fixes does someone on your team write per week to keep search honest? AI-led platforms cut that work; rule-based platforms don't.

Pricing transparency separates the tools you can model before signing from the ones that need a sales call. Nobi publishes $25/month base with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Fast Simon publishes Shopify App Store tiers from $39.99/mo. Klevu and Searchspring, both now divisions of Athos Commerce, are quote-only.

Time to live is hours for a Shopify App Store install versus quarters for an enterprise rollout. We graded each tool on how fast a real store gets it running.

## 2. Klevu

[Klevu](https://klevu.com) is AI-powered Shopify search with a no-code merchandising dashboard. The AI matching reads your catalog and figures out what shoppers actually mean, so a long query like "wide-leg cropped trouser in navy" still surfaces the right product even if the title says "cropped wide pant." A "did you mean" feature handles the typos shoppers reliably produce on product names and branded ingredients, so most misspellings resolve without a merchandiser writing a rule for each one. Klevu is now a division of Athos Commerce alongside Searchspring and Intelligent Reach - the three are still sold separately with their own contracts and pricing, so shortlisting Klevu and Searchspring side by side means picking between two products of the same parent.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose biggest CVR leak is conversational query mismatch and who want a packaged App Store install rather than a custom build.

**Pricing:** Quote-only across three tiers (Essential, Advanced, Expert), priced by domain count, sessions, and SKU volume. Contact Athos Commerce for current rates.

**Pros:**
- AI matching catches long, conversational queries and synonyms before they resolve to empty results, so merchandisers aren't writing synonym lists for the common stuff
- "Did you mean" handles most typos and misspellings automatically
- Merchandiser-friendly dashboard handles pinning, redirects, no-results rules, and recommendation slots without an engineering ticket
- Packaged Shopify install ships in days compared to a custom-built setup

**Cons:**
- Behavioral personalization is included in the Expert tier only; Essential and Advanced tiers don't include it
- AI matching is only as good as your catalog data; sparse product info weakens the layer that's supposed to prevent empty pages

**Verdict:** Pick Klevu when conversational vocabulary mismatch is the main miss reason on your Shopify catalog and a packaged install matters more than bundled conversational Q&A. Skip it if you also need a grounded shopping assistant on the same platform.

## 3. Searchspring

[Searchspring](https://searchspring.com) puts the merch team in charge of every query. Pins, boosts, zero-result redirects, and category rules all live in one merchandising dashboard, and the pitch is that the team, not an AI model, decides what each query returns. For a head of ecommerce whose merchandisers want to audit any result back to a specific rule, that exact control is the reason to look here. The trade-off is the rule list itself - it grows one-to-one with query patterns, so every unusual long-tail query that misses needs its own new rule.

**Best for:** Merchandising teams that want exact, rule-by-rule control over what each query returns and have the bandwidth to maintain that rule list as the catalog grows.

**Pricing:** Quote-only, tiered for the mid-market by store size. Contact Searchspring (or Athos Commerce) for current rates before budgeting.

**Pros:**
- Rule-level control means merchandisers can audit any result back to a specific rule, useful for promoted SKUs and seasonal pinning
- Zero-result redirects send dead-end queries to a curated landing page instead of a generic fallback list
- Lives inside the same merchandising dashboard the team already uses for campaigns and category rules, so adoption is fast for merch-led teams

**Cons:**
- The rule list grows one-to-one with query patterns, so every unusual query that misses needs its own new rule, and the maintenance load compounds with catalog size
- Less AI-native than newer engines, so long, conversational queries remain a weak spot
- Pricing isn't published, so budgeting requires a sales conversation before you can model the cost

**Verdict:** Pick Searchspring when exact rule-by-rule control over a Shopify catalog is the requirement and you have the merch team to maintain it; skip it if conversational queries are your main miss reason or you want published pricing.

## 4. Fast Simon

[Fast Simon](https://fastsimon.com) is a Shopify-tuned merchandiser's toolkit that bundles AI-assisted search with product recommendations and visual merchandising. For a head of ecommerce on Shopify, the appeal is operational: seasonal edits, campaign capsules, holiday collections, and category-level curation all run from one dashboard a non-technical merchandiser can manage daily. Pinning, boosting, and collection layouts live on the same screen, so a marketing-led team can update the storefront month over month without filing an engineering ticket. The trade-off is that natural-language understanding is lighter than the merchandising tooling, so long descriptive queries are where Fast Simon tends to miss.

**Best for:** Shopify teams whose bottleneck is visual collection curation by season, holiday, or campaign rather than long-tail semantic relevance.

**Pricing:** Shopify App Store tiered pricing. A free Starter plan covers 100 sessions/month; paid tiers begin at $39.99/month (entry), then $99.99/month (Essential) and $299.99/month (Top Pro), scaling by monthly session volume.

**Pros:**
- Strong Shopify integration and visual merchandising tools a non-technical team can run daily
- Quick install through the App Store, so a store goes live in days rather than months
- Visual product card editing lets merchandisers adjust how items display in a collection - image, badge, price prominence - without touching the theme

**Cons:**
- Long descriptive queries are where Fast Simon misses most often; visual merchandising and collection curation are what it does best
- Personalization is a secondary strength, not a headline capability

**Verdict:** Pick Fast Simon when visual collection curation leads your discovery model and search needs are straightforward; skip it if conversational shopper queries are what's costing you sales.

## Frequently asked questions

**When does it make sense to replace Shopify's native Search & Discovery app?**
When zero-result rate climbs into the [10-30% benchmark range](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-search-tools-zero-results-ecommerce), when merchandisers spend hours each week writing synonym groups, or when long descriptive queries keep landing on null pages. The native app does keyword matching only; it can't resolve vocabulary mismatch on its own.

**What's the difference between AI-powered search and Shopify's native synonym groups?**
Synonym groups need a merchandiser to write every variant pairing by hand. AI-powered search reads your catalog and figures out that "wide-leg cropped trouser" means "cropped wide pant" without the rule. Nobi and Klevu rank semantically by default; Searchspring leans on rule-based relevance.

**How much does a semantic search tool cost per month for a Shopify store?**
Nobi publishes $25/month base with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Fast Simon's Shopify App Store tiers start at $39.99/month for the entry paid plan. Klevu and Searchspring, both divisions of Athos Commerce, are quote-only.

**Can you migrate from the native app without breaking existing redirects and synonyms?**
Most alternatives import your existing redirects and synonym groups during install. Run the import in a staging theme first, verify the rules carry over, then cut traffic to the new tool once the spot checks pass.

**Do these alternatives also handle the shopping assistant or chat layer, or only the search bar?**
Only Nobi bundles semantic site search and a shopping assistant on one platform, answering pre-purchase questions in the same widget that runs search. Klevu, Searchspring, and Fast Simon focus on the search bar and merchandising; conversational shopper Q&A is a separate tool.
