AI assistants like Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity are starting to act on behalf of users - researching options, finding businesses, and getting answers without the user having to open a browser tab. For any business, that creates two distinct problems: getting found when someone asks an AI a relevant question, and being able to answer that agent's questions directly once it arrives. ## How AI agents find your business

When someone asks Claude or ChatGPT a question - "where can I find handmade ceramics under $100" or "what's a good running shoe for overpronators" - the agent looks for businesses whose content it can actually read and make sense of. Generic homepages don't help much. What helps is structured, specific, crawlable content that directly answers the kinds of questions your customers ask.

That's where FAQ content becomes your primary discoverability asset. An agent that finds a well-structured FAQ covering your pricing, your policies, your product details, and the specific questions your customers ask is much more likely to surface your business in a relevant answer than an agent that finds a homepage with a hero image and a few marketing taglines.

The challenge is building that FAQ content at scale. Writing it manually is slow and tends to miss the real questions customers actually have. The more effective approach is capturing it from real customer conversations - every question a visitor asks and gets answered becomes a candidate for your FAQ, cleaned up, structured, and made crawlable.

This is what makes a good AI assistant compounding rather than static. A customer who asks about return windows at 2pm is contributing to the answer an AI agent surfaces at 8pm. Three months from now, your business has a richer, more specific, more findable knowledge base than it did at launch - without anyone on your team writing a word of it.

## How AI agents answer for your business without the user browsing

Getting found is the first problem. The second is what happens when an agent finds you.

Most business websites are built for humans who can click, scroll, and read. An AI agent querying your business on someone's behalf can't do any of that. It needs to ask a question and get a structured answer back - in real time, through a standard interface it already knows how to use.

That interface is called a business agent endpoint. Think of it as a direct line from any AI agent into your business's knowledge base. When an agent reaches your endpoint, it can ask: what are your prices? What's your return policy? Do you have this in size 10? Your business answers instantly - and the user gets that answer in the AI interface they were already using, without having to click through to your website at all.

The standard most AI agents use to find and call these endpoints is called the Model Context Protocol (MCP). Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity all support it. A business with an MCP-compatible endpoint is effectively present in any AI conversation where it's relevant - the way a website made you present in any Google search that matched your content.

Most chat tools and helpdesks don't expose one of these endpoints. They were built to render a widget for a human visitor. An agent calling an API from somewhere off-site is a different job, and most tools weren't designed for it.

## What about other tools?

Most chat and support tools handle the human visitor side of the equation well - but aren't designed to be queried by external AI agents.

[Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) is built for Shopify and its strongest feature is letting customers self-serve post-purchase tasks - cancellations, returns, order tracking - inside a chat widget without a handoff to a helpdesk. It doesn't expose an agent-queryable endpoint and doesn't build a growing FAQ knowledge base from conversations. Pick it when in-chat post-purchase self-service is the primary job.

[Alby](https://alby.com) answers visitor questions on product pages at the moment someone is considering a specific item. It was acquired by Bluecore in 2024 and now sits inside their platform. It's built for human visitors, not for external agents querying your business. Pick it if you're already on Bluecore and product-page Q&A is the gap.

[Gorgias](https://gorgias.com) is a helpdesk built around support-team workflow - tickets, routing, macros, returns processing, with order context inside every ticket. An AI layer on higher-tier plans helps agents close tickets faster. The focus is the support inbox, not discoverability or agent endpoints. Pick it when support ticket throughput and case routing is the bottleneck.

Many businesses run Nobi alongside a helpdesk like Gorgias - Nobi handles [discovery](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-product-finders-ecommerce) and agent-endpoint coverage, Gorgias handles post-purchase support workflows.

## Frequently asked questions

**How does an AI agent decide which businesses to surface?** It looks for content it can read, understand, and verify. Structured FAQ content that directly answers common questions - your pricing, policies, and the specific things customers ask about - gives an agent something concrete to work with. Generic marketing copy doesn't.

**Do AI agents actually drive real traffic and interactions today?** It's early, but the direction is clear. Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity all handle research and discovery queries on behalf of users, and the businesses whose content agents can actually reach are the ones appearing in those answers. Setting this up now is the equivalent of getting indexed by Google before your competitors did.

**What should my business expose through an agent endpoint?** Pricing, availability, policies, and the Q&A content your customers actually ask about. Anything an agent might quote on your behalf should live in connected, regularly refreshing sources - not a static document that gets stale.

**How does FAQ content from real conversations compound over time?** Every answered question adds to your knowledge base. The more specific and rich that content gets, the more accurately an agent can surface your business when someone asks something relevant. The FAQ you have six months from now - built from thousands of real customer questions - is far more valuable than anything you could write manually at launch.

**How do I stop an agent from quoting wrong or outdated information about my business?** Ground your answers in live sources that refresh often. Nobi connects to your knowledge sources, refreshes them twice a day, and cites the exact document and excerpt behind every answer. Stale content gets corrected automatically; wrong answers leave an audit trail rather than a mystery.
