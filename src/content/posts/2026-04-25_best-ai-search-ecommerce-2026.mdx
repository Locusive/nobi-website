# Best AI Search Solutions for Ecommerce in 2026

**TL;DR:** Real AI site search reads shopper intent, ranks by real behavior, and personalizes out of the box — no hand-written synonym rules. The ecommerce shortlist worth considering in 2026: Nobi, Algolia, Klevu, Searchspring, Constructor, Bloomreach, Coveo, Fast Simon, and Findify. Which one fits depends on your platform, engineering bandwidth, and whether you want search, merchandising, and conversational AI under one contract or stitched together. Shopify-native brands typically land on Nobi, Klevu, or Fast Simon. Enterprise teams with their own engineering orgs weigh Algolia, Constructor, or Bloomreach.

The ecommerce search market has matured fast. What used to be a simple text box is now a battleground of machine learning models, natural language processing, and conversational interfaces. The problem is, most comparison articles are written by vendors pretending to be journalists. So here is an honest look at nine AI search solutions, what they actually do well, and where each one falls short — including specific A/B test numbers and per-unit pricing instead of the usual hand-wavy "enterprise-grade" language.

If your search bar still returns "no results found" when a shopper types "blue running shoes for flat feet," you are leaving money on the table. [Research from Baymard Institute](https://baymard.com/blog/ecommerce-search-query-types) shows that 72% of ecommerce sites fail basic search usability benchmarks. [According to Forrester](https://www.forrester.com/), search users convert at 2-3x the rate of browsers. And controlled A/B tests at apparel brands consistently show AI-native search delivering 15–22% conversion lifts over default platform search — UNTUCKit posted a 17.1% lift after switching to Nobi, and [Lucchese attributed over $1M in incremental year-one revenue](https://nobi.ai/customers) to the combined search + assistant deployment.

The stakes are clear. The question is which tool fits your team.

## 1. Constructor — Enterprise Revenue Optimization

**What it does:** Constructor uses machine learning to optimize search and product discovery for revenue, not just relevance. It learns from user behavior to rank products that are most likely to convert.

**Best for:** Large enterprise retailers with $50M+ in annual GMV who want search to directly drive revenue growth.

**Pricing tier:** Enterprise. Revenue-share with no published list price — deals typically run mid-five-figures and up annually. The most common complaint in post-signing reviews is surprise bills as GMV grows.

**Pros:**
- Revenue-focused ranking that goes beyond keyword matching
- Strong personalization and A/B testing infrastructure
- Quizzes, browse, and recommendations all in one platform

**Cons:**
- Out of reach for most SMBs and mid-market brands
- Implementation takes weeks to months
- No Shopify-native integration
- Requires internal data science or analytics resources to get full value

Constructor is the right choice if you have the budget and the team. For everyone else, it is aspirational pricing attached to enterprise-grade tooling.

## 2. Algolia — Developer-First Search API

**What it does:** Algolia provides a powerful, fast search API that developers can customize extensively. It has expanded into AI recommendations and NeuralSearch, but its core strength remains the API layer.

**Best for:** Teams with dedicated frontend and backend developers who want full control over the search experience.

**Pricing tier:** Usage-based. $0.50 per 1K search requests on the Grow plan, $1.75 per 1K on Grow Plus, with NeuralSearch gated to higher tiers. Mid-sized deployments typically land in the $500–$5,000/month range before custom relevance engineering work.

**Pros:**
- Extremely fast search indexing and response times
- Massive ecosystem of libraries, widgets, and integrations
- NeuralSearch adds semantic capabilities on top of keyword matching

**Cons:**
- Requires developers to implement and maintain
- [Usage-based pricing](https://www.algolia.com/pricing/) can produce surprise bills during traffic spikes
- Configuration complexity means your search is only as good as your engineering time
- Not built for non-technical ecommerce teams

Algolia is a powerful engine. But handing a Formula 1 car to someone without a racing team does not end well. If you do not have developers, look elsewhere.

## 3. Bloomreach — Full-Stack Commerce Experience

**What it does:** Bloomreach combines search, merchandising, content, and customer data into a full commerce experience platform. Its Discovery module handles search and product recommendations.

**Best for:** Enterprise omnichannel retailers who want search, CMS, and CDP in one platform.

**Pricing tier:** Enterprise contracts in the $60K–$250K+/year range, priced on catalog size, customers served, and events. Multi-month implementation standard.

**Pros:**
- True full-stack: search, content, marketing, and data in one place
- Strong semantic search with product-specific AI
- Personalization driven by unified customer profiles

**Cons:**
- Enterprise-only pricing and sales process
- Heavy implementation requirements
- Overkill if you just need better search
- Not ideal for lean ecommerce teams

Bloomreach makes sense when you are ready to consolidate your entire commerce stack. If you just want search that understands your shoppers, it is a lot of platform for one problem.

## 4. Searchspring (now Athos) — Mid-Market Search and Merchandising

**What it does:** Searchspring provides search, merchandising, and product recommendations for mid-market ecommerce stores. It has recently rebranded under the Athos umbrella after acquiring other commerce tools.

**Best for:** Growing Shopify and BigCommerce stores that want solid search with visual merchandising controls.

**Pricing tier:** Mid-range. Typically $799/month and up depending on traffic and modules, with annual-pay discounts and special rates for early-stage and MWBE brands. Quote-based but accessible to growing brands.

**Pros:**
- Native integrations with Shopify, BigCommerce, and Magento
- Visual merchandising tools that non-technical teams can use
- Solid analytics and reporting

**Cons:**
- AI and NLP capabilities are still basic compared to newer entrants
- Semantic understanding is limited — still leans on keyword matching
- The Athos rebrand has created some market confusion

Searchspring is a reliable mid-market option if you need merchandising more than you need AI. But if natural language understanding matters, it lags behind.

## 5. Klevu — AI Search for Merchandisers

**What it does:** Klevu focuses on AI-powered search and product discovery with a particular strength in merchandising tools. It uses its own NLP engine to understand search intent.

**Best for:** Merchandising-led ecommerce teams that want AI search without writing code.

**Pricing tier:** Tiered plans starting around $249/month. More accessible than enterprise options, but Smart Category Merchandising is a paid add-on and personalization is typically priced as a separate module — real all-in costs climb quickly past the entry tier.

**Pros:**
- Strong NLP for product search
- Visual merchandising dashboard that non-technical users love
- Good Shopify and Magento integrations
- Supports multiple languages

**Cons:**
- Some features still require developer involvement
- Learning curve for the full feature set
- Not conversational — search is still query-and-results

Klevu hits a sweet spot for merchandising teams who want better search without an enterprise contract. The gap is in conversational capabilities, which the market is moving toward.

## 6. Nobi — Conversational AI Shopping Assistant

**What it does:** Nobi replaces the traditional search bar with a conversational AI shopping assistant. Instead of query-and-filter, shoppers describe what they want in natural language and get guided to the right products through dialogue.

**Best for:** Ecommerce teams and DTC brands that want to improve product discovery without hiring developers.

**Pricing tier:** Accessible and transparent. [Starts at $25/month](https://nobi.ai/pricing) for 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. No revenue-share, no per-seat fees, no AI features gated behind higher tiers. A store running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages per month lands around $975/month — math you can do yourself before signing anything.

**Pros:**
- Conversational interface that goes beyond search-and-filter
- No-code setup — integrates with your Shopify catalog in minutes
- Understands natural language queries without synonym management ([Kilte's "brown boots" to Cappuccino mapping](https://nobi.ai/why-nobi/better-search) is a good example — no rule-writing required)
- Predictable per-unit pricing, no usage-based surprise bills
- Proof-point CVR lifts: UNTUCKit +17.1%, Kilte +21.7%, Faherty 12.3% vs. competing AI search at 10.1%

**Cons:**
- Newer platform with a growing feature set
- Less enterprise merchandising tooling than Constructor or Bloomreach
- Not an API-first tool — less customizable for developer teams
- Web chat only today; no native SMS, WhatsApp, or voice channels

Nobi is the choice if you believe ecommerce search should feel like a conversation, not a keyword matching exercise. It is the most accessible entry point to AI-powered product discovery — [Lucchese](https://nobi.ai/customers), a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus, attributed over $1M in incremental revenue in year one and a 39x ROI from the combined search and assistant deployment. If you want the depth of Constructor's ML models, Nobi is not there yet. But most stores do not need that depth — they need search that works.

## 7. Fast Simon — Shopify-Native Visual Merchandising

**What it does:** Fast Simon provides AI-assisted site search, product recommendations, and visual merchandising tools tuned for Shopify. It positions itself as a merchandiser's toolkit with search and recommendations bundled in.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose bottleneck is collection curation and visual merchandising, not semantic relevance.

**Pricing tier:** Accessible. Starts around $99/month via the Shopify App Store, scaling with catalog and traffic.

**Pros:**
- Strong Shopify integration and visual merchandising tools
- Quick install through the App Store
- Collection curation workflows non-technical merchandisers can use daily

**Cons:**
- Lighter on natural-language understanding than AI-native engines
- Personalization is a secondary strength, not a headline capability
- Will not match Nobi or Klevu on long-tail semantic queries

Fast Simon is a solid pick if your merchandising team leads with visual curation and your search needs are basic. If natural-language queries are the unlock, it is not the right engine.

## 8. Coveo — Enterprise AI Across Commerce and Workplace

**What it does:** Coveo provides AI-powered relevance across commerce search, customer support, and internal knowledge. Its ecommerce module uses ML to unify search with service and self-help surfaces.

**Best for:** Enterprises that want one AI relevance engine spanning their ecommerce site, support portal, and internal knowledge base.

**Pricing tier:** Enterprise. Third-party references put base plans around $600/month, but real all-in deployments commonly run $100K+ per year once annual licensing (~$50K+), implementation (~$20K+), and professional services ($200–$300/hour) are added.

**Pros:**
- Powerful ML relevance that spans commerce + support + workplace search
- Strong cross-surface personalization
- Mature enterprise tooling and analytics

**Cons:**
- Long sales cycle and services-heavy implementation
- Overkill if you only need ecommerce search
- Not built for Shopify-native workflows

Coveo makes sense when you are unifying search across multiple surfaces at enterprise scale. For a standalone ecommerce search problem, it is a lot of platform for one job.

## 9. Findify — Shopify Search + Merchandising

**What it does:** Findify is a Shopify-focused search, merchandising, and product-feed engine. It offers Smart Collections, ranked product feeds, and personalization claims.

**Best for:** Shopify Plus brands that want a mature packaged app with personalization claims out of the box.

**Pricing tier:** Essential at $499/month, Professional at $799, Enterprise at $1,399.

**Pros:**
- Mature Shopify app with plug-and-play AI ranking
- Smart Collections and ranked product feeds
- Personalization features included rather than bolted on

**Cons:**
- Lost head-to-head CVR test against Nobi at UNTUCKit (16.5% vs. 17.4%) and was replaced in production
- Merchandising workflows still require manual tuning
- Pricing climbs past $1,000/month fast on Professional or Enterprise tiers

Findify is a reasonable packaged pick for Shopify Plus brands that want a single-vendor option with merchandising included. Worth running head-to-head against Nobi on your own traffic before signing anything longer than a quarter.

## The Numbers: What Conversion Lift Is Realistic

Most "AI search" comparison articles cite vendor-reported averages with no control group. Those numbers are marketing copy, not evidence. Below are CVR lifts from controlled A/B tests with a real holdout — the only way to know whether a tool actually moves the number or just produces prettier reports.

UNTUCKit ran a two-month split test against the search tool they were already using. Nobi posted a 17.6% conversion rate vs. 15.0%, a 17.1% lift. Revenue per searcher rose 21.3% ($39.17 vs. $32.30) and AOV ticked up 3.3% ($222 vs. $215). After the test ended, they moved Nobi to 100% of traffic. Separately, UNTUCKit ran Nobi head-to-head against Findify, their paid incumbent — Nobi won again, 17.4% vs. 16.5% CVR, and replaced Findify in production.

Kilte saw a 21.7% CVR lift over Shopify's default search. Most of the gain came from semantic color normalization: brand color names like Cappuccino and Écru were invisible to shoppers typing "brown boots" or "off-white." Nobi mapped the brand vocabulary to the shopper vocabulary without anyone writing a synonym rule.

Faherty measured 12.3% search CVR against a competing AI search tool's 10.1% in a head-to-head. Lucchese, the luxury Western boot brand, [attributed over $1M in incremental revenue](https://nobi.ai/customers) in year one and a 39x ROI from the combined search and assistant deployment.

The honest filter when reading any vendor's case studies: if there is no control group, the lift number is marketing copy. Ask for the A/B methodology, the sample size, and the duration before you trust any number — including the ones above.

## How to Choose

There is no single best AI search tool. There is only the best tool for your specific situation.

**Choose Nobi** if you want AI search and a conversational assistant live in days with transparent per-unit pricing, and your team can deploy it without developers. Proof points at UNTUCKit, Kilte, Faherty, and Lucchese span Shopify Plus apparel through luxury Western boots.

**Choose Algolia** if you have a dedicated search engineering team and want full API control over ranking, indexing, and the frontend. A Formula 1 engine for teams that can drive it.

**Choose Constructor** if you are an enterprise retailer with a large catalog, a data team to shape behavioral training, and you are comfortable with revenue-share pricing that scales with GMV.

**Choose Bloomreach** if you are consolidating search, personalization, content, and CDP into one enterprise contract, not just buying a search tool.

**Choose Coveo** if you are unifying ecommerce search with support and internal knowledge search across the business at enterprise scale.

**Choose Searchspring** if your merchandising team needs manual control over category and collection pages site-wide, not just the search results page, and merchandising matters more than AI sophistication.

**Choose Klevu or Fast Simon** if you want a search-only Shopify vendor and do not want a conversational assistant bundled in. Both are strong on Shopify and focus narrowly on search and merchandising.

**Choose Findify** if you want a mature packaged Shopify app with personalization claims included. Worth A/B testing against Nobi before committing past a quarter.

The worst decision is no decision. Every month you run a search bar that does not understand your shoppers, you lose the [15% of visitors who search and generate 45% of revenue](https://www.nosto.com/).

## What to Ask Every AI Search Vendor Before Signing

Most evaluations go sideways because buyers ask about features instead of operating realities. A short list of pointed questions separates real AI search from rebranded keyword engines:

- **Show me the A/B test.** Controlled split with a real holdout group, not a before-and-after case study that conveniently lacks one. Ask for methodology, sample size, and duration.
- **What does my bill look like at 2x my current traffic?** In writing. This is where revenue-share and per-query pricing diverge from flat plans.
- **How long from contract signature to live in production?** Modern tools should ship in days to a few weeks. A six-month services engagement is a different category of purchase.
- **What do I have to do when 500 new SKUs land in the catalog?** AI-native tools adapt from behavior; keyword engines make your team write synonym rules every week.
- **Is personalization included or priced as a separate module?** Common gotcha with Klevu and several enterprise vendors.
- **How do you handle zero-result queries?** Fallback page, conversational clarification, or dead-end? The answer predicts real-world UX more than any demo does.
- **What does the hand-off look like between search and a conversational assistant for higher-consideration questions?** If search and chat use different ranking models, you end up with two merchandising systems that contradict each other.
- **Where does the tool stop?** Honest vendors name their limits. For Nobi: curates the search results page, not site-wide collection merchandising — brands that need full-site merchandising should pair it with a dedicated tool.
