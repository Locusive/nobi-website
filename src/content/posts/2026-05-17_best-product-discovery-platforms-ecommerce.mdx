## What is a product discovery platform?

A product discovery platform is the software layer that helps a visitor get from "I'm on your site" to "this is the SKU I want." That work shows up across three different parts of the funnel - [site search and faceted browse](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-search-ecommerce-2026), a conversational chat or assistant, and guided experiences like quizzes or PDP Q&A. Most teams end up running one tool per part unless a platform covers more than one.

The shared job is reducing zero-result rate, dead-end sessions, and the invisible churn from shoppers who can't find what they came for.

Nobi is one of the tools in this list. Job fit was the first filter. Most "product discovery" tools win at one part of the funnel and either skip the rest or bolt on a weaker version. Nobi covers an [AI shopping assistant and site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-top-ecommerce-platforms-for-native-ai-search-and-product-discovery) from the same connected catalog, so the search bar and the chat widget answer from the same source. Rep AI sits on the chat widget and watches session behavior to intervene mid-browse, which is a different job than answering a typed query. Octane AI runs quiz-led product finders on Shopify, useful when guided discovery beats free-text search. Alby answers shopper questions on the product detail page itself, so the depth shows up where a visitor is already weighing a SKU.

Pricing came next, and we looked for published numbers rather than "contact us." Grounding mattered more than any feature flag. We checked whether answers pull from your live catalog and policy pages or from static training data that goes stale. Nobi's personalization today is placeholder text and starter messages; behavioral reranking is on the roadmap, not in production. Finally, implementation effort: a small theme drop-in that ships in hours versus a multi-month rollout, and whether merchandisers can run the tool day-to-day without filing engineering tickets.

## 2. Rep AI

[Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) is a proactive-chat tool built for Shopify, and a fit for ecommerce teams whose discovery bottleneck is browsing abandonment mid-session - shoppers loading a few PDPs and bailing before they add to cart. Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder that lets marketing script deterministic conversation flows for promos, product launches, and abandoned-cart rescue. A live-agent drop-in lets a human join an active AI session without forcing a binary handoff. An in-chat Post-Order Tasks Agent handles cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups inside the widget. Drop-off-reason analytics tell you why a shopper bailed, not just that they did.

**Best for:** Marketing ops and CX teams who want to script campaign-specific chat flows without engineering, or who need a human agent to jump into an active AI session mid-thread.

**Pricing:** $12 per 1,000 visitors on the Pay As You Grow tier (unlimited sessions, 500-product catalog cap). At ~125K monthly sessions, Pro tiers run $280/mo (Support), $616/mo (Sales), $740/mo (Bundle). Enterprise quoted above ~500K sessions. 30-day free trial.

**Pros:**
- Flow Studio visual builder lets non-engineers design deterministic flows for promos, product launches, and abandoned-cart rescue without filing engineering tickets
- Live-agent drop-in is a true hybrid - a human can join an active AI session while the AI keeps handling it, rather than a binary handoff that pauses the AI
- Post-Order Tasks Agent lets shoppers self-serve cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups inside the chat widget, useful where return volume is high
- Drop-off-reason analytics answer why shoppers abandoned, not just that they did

**Cons:**
- Sales-chat only - no site search, so discovery outside the chat widget still needs a separate tool
- Personalization is scoped to the chat session; ranking and merchandising anywhere outside the chat widget aren't part of what Rep AI does

**Verdict:** Pick Rep AI when you need a no-code flow builder, an AI-plus-human hybrid chat mode, or in-chat post-purchase task execution. Skip it when the bottleneck sits outside the chat widget on the search bar or product pages.

## 3. Octane AI

[Octane AI](https://octaneai.com) is a quiz-led product finder built primarily for Shopify, where the quiz is what customers actually engage with - not a search bar or a chat widget. For a head of ecommerce, the fit is narrower than it sounds: this is the right tool when your hero path to product discovery is a guided diagnostic, common in beauty, supplements, apparel fit finders, and structured CPG catalogs. Customers answer five to ten questions, and the platform maps their answers to product attributes in your Shopify catalog to recommend specific SKUs. A native Klaviyo handoff turns those answers into email-segment attributes, so a quiz completion flows straight into lifecycle email rather than dead-ending on a thank-you screen.

**Best for:** Brands whose main path to product discovery is a guided diagnostic or routine-builder quiz, common in beauty, supplements, apparel fit finders, and structured CPG catalogs.

**Pricing:** Basic at $50/month (400 credits, where one credit covers one quiz completion), Plus at $200/month (2,200 credits), Enterprise & Unlimited at $500+/month. AI features (Smart Products, Smart Copy, Image Analyzer) consume fractional extra credits per interaction.

**Pros:**
- Quiz builder with deep Shopify product-attribute mapping, so diagnostic answers resolve to real SKUs and attribute combinations
- Pre-built templates for fit finders, skincare routines, and supplement regimens shorten time-to-launch for common use cases
- Native Klaviyo integration captures quiz answers as email-segment attributes, so a completion flows straight into lifecycle email
- Lower engineering overhead than building quiz logic in-house on a headless site

**Cons:**
- Quiz-centric - no real-time chat or search bar, so the value sits inside the quiz funnel rather than spreading across the rest of the site
- Best fit is structured catalogs where scripted diagnostic questions map cleanly to attributes; freeform open-ended questions don't always land

**Verdict:** Pick Octane AI if a quiz is genuinely the right discovery metaphor for your catalog; skip it if customers are typing open-ended product questions you'd rather answer directly with chat or search.

## 4. Alby

[Alby](https://alby.com) is a Q&A layer that lives on the Shopify product detail page, answering shopper questions inline at the moment they're staring at a specific item and weighing add-to-cart. Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024, so the product now sits inside Bluecore's identity and predictive-marketing stack rather than running standalone. For a head of ecommerce, the practical question is whether Bluecore is already your platform. If it is, Alby plugs PDP Q&A into a stack you've already bought. If it isn't, you're effectively evaluating Bluecore underneath the Alby layer, which is a much bigger decision than picking a product-page assistant.

**Best for:** Shopify brands already on Bluecore who want product-page Q&A inside that stack without adding a second integration.

**Pricing:** Not publicly listed post-acquisition; bundled into Bluecore platform pricing. Contact Alby directly for current rates.

**Pros:**
- Focused UX for product-page Q&A, where shopper intent is already locked to a specific SKU
- Plugs into Bluecore's identity and predictive-marketing stack without a second integration
- Cuts pre-purchase support email volume by answering common PDP questions inline

**Cons:**
- PDP Q&A only, with no site search, so it can't help shoppers earlier in the funnel before they reach a product page
- Standalone roadmap is uncertain after the Bluecore acquisition; future direction depends on Bluecore's strategy

**Verdict:** Pick Alby if Bluecore is already your predictive-marketing platform and PDP Q&A is the one gap; look elsewhere if you need a standalone assistant or anything beyond the product page.

## How should a head of ecommerce pick between these product discovery platforms?

Start with the part of the funnel where discovery is actually failing - search, chat, quiz, or PDP Q&A - and pick the tool anchored there. If discovery is failing in more than one place, lean toward a platform that covers more than one job so you're not running three integrations and three contracts to fix one problem.

If shoppers can't find what they typed - high zero-result rate, low click-through from search results, vocabulary mismatch on synonyms - the gap is search, and Nobi is the fit. The search bar pulls from your live catalog, results rank automatically from what shoppers actually click on, and the same connected sources answer the open-ended questions that follow in chat. One platform, one bill, one place to look at the analytics.

If shoppers are typing open-ended questions about materials, sizing, compatibility, or store policy, and you want grounded answers with citations they can verify on the page, Nobi's conversational assistant and [automatic shopper Q&A](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-best-ai-assistants-for-pdps) are the fit. Every answer carries an inline citation pill back to the source document, date, and exact excerpt, which is the direct counter to the confident-lies failure mode operators worry about.

If your conversion drop happens mid-browse and you want non-engineers to script proactive chat flows for promos, product launches, and abandoned-cart rescue, Rep AI is the fit. Flow Studio gives marketing a no-code builder; the live-agent drop-in lets a human join an active AI session without pausing it.

If your hero path to discovery is a diagnostic quiz that maps answers to SKUs, Octane AI is the fit. Beauty regimens, supplement stacks, and apparel fit finders all fall here.

If you're already on Bluecore and PDP Q&A is the one gap, Alby is the fit, since it plugs into a stack you've already bought.
