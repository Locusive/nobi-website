## Can an AI assistant on my dealership website answer shopper questions around the clock?

Car shoppers do most of their research after dinner. Your BDC closes at six. When someone lands on a VDP at 10pm wanting to know whether the certified pre-owned warranty covers the powertrain or whether this trim ships with Apple CarPlay, there's nobody to answer them, just a lead form telling them to wait until morning. Most shoppers don't wait. They find a rooftop whose site can tell them what they need to know right now. An AI assistant grounded in your own inventory feed, VDPs, and policy pages can close that gap: specs, hours, trade-in process, warranty coverage, around the clock, from your content, with every answer traced back to a source. This guide walks through how to set one up.

## Why do car shoppers leave dealership websites without getting their questions answered?

Speed to lead is one of the highest-impact metrics in dealership sales. The longer the gap between a shopper's question and a real answer, the lower the appointment set rate and the worse the show rate.

Putting an AI assistant on your site is the obvious fix. Most dealers who consider it run into the same objection.

The fear dealers most often cite about AI on their site is the Chevy Watsonville incident: a generic ChatGPT-powered bot that agreed to sell a Tahoe for $1 because it had no connection to the dealer's actual inventory or policies and no guardrails on what it could say. That incident is a real cautionary tale, but it points at the wrong conclusion. The risk isn't AI on your site. It's ungrounded AI on your site: a general-purpose model answering from its training data instead of your content. Grounding is the answer, not avoidance.

## What is Nobi, and how does it answer vehicle and dealership questions from your own content?

Grounding is exactly what Nobi is built around. Nobi is a conversational website assistant that answers shoppers' questions from the content you connect to it: your inventory feed, your VDPs, your hours and location page, your CPO warranty docs, your F&I FAQ. It doesn't reach into a general knowledge base to fill gaps; it retrieves answers from your sources and cites the exact document and excerpt it drew from. That tether to your published content is what keeps answers from drifting into territory you haven't approved.

Setup is a one-time connection. Point Nobi at your inventory feed URL, your VDP pages, your policy docs, your financing FAQ. It indexes them and keeps them current automatically, so your connected content stays in sync without anyone touching a config.

Every answer carries numbered citation pills. A shopper can hover over any pill to see the source document and the exact passage Nobi used. They can check the answer against your official content without leaving the chat. That transparency matters for vehicle and warranty questions, where a shopper who second-guesses an AI answer is one click away from bouncing.

For high-stakes questions (a CPO warranty scope, an F&I FAQ item, your return policy), you can lock a verbatim response. That override fires whenever a shopper's question matches, with no paraphrasing and no variation: just the dealer-approved answer. Every draft answer also passes through a second AI review that reads the cited source and flags inaccuracies before anything reaches the shopper. It's on by default for high-consideration industries like customer support, healthcare, and financial services, off by default for others, and worth enabling for a dealership deployment handling warranty and F&I questions.

The assistant handles the question. Your BDC handles the relationship. For anything that requires a person (negotiating price, running credit, scheduling a test drive), Nobi routes to your BDC contact rather than attempting an answer.

## What questions can the assistant actually answer from my dealership's connected content?

The tether to your own content that keeps answers grounded is also what defines the scope. The assistant can answer anything a shopper would find in the pages you connect: vehicle specs and package details from your VDPs, dealership hours and location, a description of your financing and trade-in process, and certified pre-owned warranty terms from your policy pages. It cannot quote interest rates, approval odds, or estimated monthly payments, because those depend on live lender data and individual credit profiles that aren't in your connected content; it will direct shoppers to call or visit for those answers rather than generate a number.

That scope covers most of what shoppers actually ask on a VDP. "Does this F-150 come with a tow package?" "Is the sunroof standard on the Sport trim or an add-on?" "What engine is in the base model?" Those answers come straight from the VDP you connected. Hours, location, and directions come from your dealership pages. Questions about the financing process (how to apply, what documents to bring to the F&I office, what the general steps look like) get answered from your finance FAQ. The assistant describes the process. It does not quote a rate or make an approval call.

The same boundary applies to trade-ins. The assistant can walk a shopper through how to request a valuation, what to bring, and when to schedule an appraisal. It describes the process because that's what's in your connected content. It won't generate a KBB ICO estimate, because that estimate isn't in your content: it's a live calculation. CPO warranty coverage, service intervals, and maintenance plan terms all answer cleanly from whatever warranty or policy documents you connect.

What the assistant will not do is equally important. It won't quote a specific APR. It won't promise a monthly payment. It won't confirm a vehicle is available if your inventory feed hasn't captured the sale yet. When a question falls outside what your content can support, it says to call for current pricing or availability rather than fill the gap with a figure it invented.

Every answer carries numbered citation pills a shopper can hover over to see exactly which document and passage the answer came from. For warranty and F&I questions (the ones where a wrong answer costs a deal or a CSI point), that audit trail lets a shopper verify any answer against your official content before acting on it.

## How do I make sure the assistant never quotes a wrong price or a sold vehicle?

That citation trail (the one that keeps warranty and F&I answers honest) does the same work for pricing and inventory. Every answer traces back to your connected content, and when a shopper asks about something not covered in those sources, the assistant directs them to contact the dealership rather than generate a figure. That applies whether the question is about warranty scope or whether the F-150 on that VDP is still on the lot.

Pricing is handled the same way. If your VDP lists an MSRP, the assistant surfaces that figure and notes it's the listed price. If the VDP doesn't include a price, the assistant directs the shopper to contact the dealership rather than generate a number. It doesn't estimate. It doesn't pull a figure from somewhere else.

A sold car works the same way. The assistant's knowledge of a specific vehicle is only as current as the content you've connected. When a vehicle sells and the VDP comes down, the assistant can no longer confirm that car. The twice-daily content refresh means a sold VDP clears out of the assistant's view within hours. For rooftops moving inventory fast, keep your feed update frequency in line with your sales pace so the assistant's picture of your lot stays current.

For high-stakes questions where the answer can't vary (whether a vehicle is certified, what a warranty covers, your return policy), query overrides let you lock a verbatim response. The assistant delivers that exact wording every time, no paraphrasing. That's the right tool for anything with legal or CSI implications.

The CSI protection goes beyond accurate answers. The assistant doesn't apply pressure, manufacture urgency, or make commitments on your behalf. When a question needs a person (negotiated pricing, trade-in value, a credit decision), it routes to your BDC rather than attempt an answer. A frustrating AI interaction costs a CSI point the same way a wrong answer does, and an assistant that hands off cleanly at its limits is the one that protects your score.

## How does Nobi get installed on my dealership website?

All of that (the citation trail, the verbatim overrides, the clean handoff to your BDC) runs from one small change to your dealership site. Your website vendor, whether that's DealerOn, Dealer Inspire, or whoever manages your template, drops in the Nobi snippet and points it at the page slot where the assistant should appear. You don't need to own or control your site's code to make it happen. Most rooftops are live the same day the snippet goes in.

After that, you connect your content from the Nobi dashboard. Paste in your inventory feed URL, add your policy and FAQ page URLs, and upload any PDFs: warranty terms, CPO agreements, F&I disclosures. Any URL Nobi can reach becomes part of what it can answer. Connected sources update automatically, so content changes reach shopper answers without any manual step on your end.

The widget's placement and trigger timing are yours to configure. You can scope suggestion pills to specific pages (a trade-in prompt on your valuation page, a financing FAQ prompt on your SRP and inventory pages) so visitors have a starting point rather than a blank input box.

Pricing starts at $25 a month, which covers 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Additional searches run $0.01 each and additional messages $0.10 each. There's no platform subscription and no revenue-share; the cost scales with actual usage, not a commitment you make at signing.

The one thing to confirm before committing: if your website vendor restricts third-party additions to your template, verify they can drop in the Nobi snippet before you sign up. That is the only technical hurdle. Source connections, widget placement, and suggestion pill settings are all handled from the dashboard; no further development after the initial install.

## How do I use the questions shoppers are actually asking to improve my VDPs and policy pages?

Once the assistant is live, it logs every question shoppers ask and flags the ones it couldn't answer confidently because the relevant information wasn't in your connected content. Those low-confidence flags are a prioritized list of exactly what to add to your VDPs and policy pages, written in the words your shoppers use rather than the terms your marketing team assumed they would use.

Review it weekly. Clusters reveal where your content is thin. If multiple shoppers on different VDPs ask whether a vehicle has heated seats, your trim-level specs aren't listed consistently. That's a content fix, not an AI fix: add the specs and the assistant handles it automatically.

CPO pages are a common gap source. If questions about powertrain coverage or inspection standards keep coming back low-confidence, the detail isn't in the page you connected. Expand it, reconnect it, and the gap closes within hours.

F&I questions deserve separate attention. Shoppers asking about gap insurance, extended warranties, or add-on packages before they walk in are doing pre-write-up research. Pass those patterns to your F&I manager; they show which products shoppers are already weighing before the desk.

After-hours questions reveal friction costing you appointments. If "is this certified?" tops the list on your CPO VDPs, adding a CPO badge and a short coverage summary to each one removes that barrier before it becomes a bounced shopper.

The questions shoppers type into your assistant are also the phrases they search on Google. Adding those answers explicitly to your VDPs and policy pages improves what the assistant can say and lifts your organic visibility for long-tail spec queries at the same time.

## When should I look at a different tool instead of Nobi for my dealership?

All of that content work pays off when Nobi is solving the right bottleneck for your rooftop. Nobi solves one problem well: answering shopper questions on your dealership website, grounded in your own content, around the clock. If the real gap is somewhere else (managing leads across your full BDC pipeline, adding AI voice to your phones, or running a multi-channel internet-up operation across SMS and Meta), a tool built for that specific job is the better fit.

For a dealer group consolidating sales, service, and lifecycle marketing under one contract, [Impel](https://impel.io) is built for that scope. It covers lead engagement, service scheduling, and lifecycle marketing in one platform, integrates with major OEM customer-journey systems, and added a marketing layer through its 2024 Outsell acquisition. The rollout is a multi-month group-level project. That's the right call for groups tired of stitching point tools together; it's the wrong one for a single rooftop trying to close one gap fast.

If your internet ups fragment across website, Facebook, Instagram, and SMS and you want them handled in one place at a published per-site price, [DealerAI](https://www.dealerai.com) covers all of those from its base tier at $599 per month, with separate agents for Sales, Service, Parts, and Finance.

Two other limits worth naming. Nobi has no native mode for a BDC rep to jump into an active chat session mid-conversation. And it answers questions from your connected content only; it won't pull live data from your DMS or process in-chat transactions. For BDC workflow tooling, add that capability at the CRM layer.
