## How does Nobi compare to Matador AI for dealerships?

A shopper lands on a VDP at 9 p.m., can't get a straight answer on trim or availability, and closes the tab. Down the hall, a lead from three weeks ago is still sitting untouched in the CRM, because nobody ever called back. Both are speed-to-lead problems, but they're not the same problem, and a tool built for one won't fix the other. Nobi is an AI website assistant that answers shopper questions from your connected inventory and policy content and captures leads on the site itself, before that visitor bounces off the page. Matador AI is a multi-channel BDC layer. It works phone, text, email, social, and web chat to follow up on ups wherever the conversation is happening, and it has a TCPA compliance engine built in for the SMS side of that work.

Matador AI's job starts once a lead exists. It's used by more than 1,500 dealerships to automate sales and service conversations across channels, and it's built to plug into your existing CRM rather than replace it. Where it's thinner is inventory-aware product discovery: it's a conversation and follow-up tool, not a search layer for the shopper actively browsing your lot online.

Nobi's job is narrower and earlier: product search, shopper Q&A on VDPs, and lead capture for the visitor who's live on your site right now.

## How do pricing and setup compare between Nobi and Matador AI?

Nobi publishes a flat $25/month base with published per-unit overage rates (see the table above). A marketing manager can model the cost before ever picking up the phone with a rep. Matador AI's pricing is tiered by dealership size and channel usage, and it's quoted only after a sales conversation.

Nobi is an AI website assistant, so setup runs through the site itself. It connects to your existing inventory feed, VDPs, FAQ, and policy pages rather than asking you to replace your CRM or DMS, and it answers questions and captures leads from content you already publish. The overage rates apply as site traffic grows, and there's no revenue share or per-seat charge layered on top, so a rooftop pushing more shoppers through the assistant during a sales event isn't guessing at what the next invoice looks like.

Because it spans phone, text, email, social, and web chat with a TCPA compliance engine for SMS, its setup touches more systems than a single site install, and that's part of why the price is quoted rather than posted. For a BDC evaluating a multi-channel follow-up tool, a custom quote is normal for the category. For a marketing manager who wants a number before booking a demo, Nobi's published rate is the one you can act on today.

## How do Nobi and Matador AI cover lead follow-up channels?

Matador AI follows a lead across phone, text, email, social, and web chat, all from one tool. That reach is the bigger footprint for working aged and orphaned leads sitting in your CRM. Nobi covers the website chat and search on your dealership site only. It doesn't call, text, email, or message a lead once they've left the page.

Matador AI is built for BDC teams whose job is finding the up wherever they went quiet: a phone call after a missed appointment, a text after a be-back, an email for a lead that's gone cold, a social message, a web chat catch if they come back to the site. Where it comes up short is the shopper live on your site right now, about to close the tab. That's a lead-capture moment, not a re-engagement one, and Matador AI isn't reading your VDPs or inventory to catch it.

Nobi's job stops at the site's edge. It answers questions and captures a lead while the shopper is on the page, but once that visitor leaves, Nobi has no way to call, text, or email them back. For the work of reactivating leads that are already sitting in the CRM and just going untouched, Matador AI's multi-channel reach is the more complete tool.

## How do Nobi and Matador AI guard against AI mistakes like wrong pricing or hallucinated inventory?

Nobi grounds every website answer in your connected inventory feed and policy content, and shows an inline citation pill a shopper can hover to check the source. For a question with real downside, like return policy or warranty terms, a dealer can lock an exact, company-approved answer so there's no paraphrasing risk. Matador AI's compliance engine solves a different problem: TCPA consent for SMS broadcast and follow-up, not accuracy on the questions a shopper asks about a specific vehicle.

Nobi's query overrides are the control that matters most here. A dealer pins an exact answer to a specific high-stakes question, and that pinned answer fires every time a shopper's question matches it, with no model paraphrasing in between. That's the kind of guardrail that prevents a repeat of incidents like the Chevy Watsonville bot agreeing to sell a Tahoe for a dollar. Nobi also offers an optional second AI review: a separate pass that checks each draft answer against the source content it was built from before the answer reaches a shopper, catching mismatches the first pass missed.

Matador AI's guardrail sits somewhere else entirely. Its TCPA compliance engine keeps SMS broadcast and follow-up campaigns from turning into a legal problem, which matters for a BDC texting hundreds of aged leads a week. It isn't built to check whether an answer about price or inventory on your site is accurate, because it isn't answering those questions in the first place.

Neither tool fixes a VDP with the wrong price on it or a policy page that's gone stale. Nobi and Matador AI are only as accurate as the inventory feed and content each one reads from, so keeping that source material current stays the dealer's job either way.

## How does website product Q&A compare between Nobi and Matador AI?

Nobi is built for the moment a shopper is on a [VDP or SRP with a question](https://nobi.ai/blog/nobi-vs-toma). It answers from your connected inventory and policy content, and routes fast product searches through a separate instant-search path. Matador AI is built for sales and service conversations, not inventory-aware product discovery on the site itself.

Nobi routes each incoming query automatically. A short product search, like a trim level or a color, gets fast, ranked results straight from your inventory feed. A real question, like whether a specific unit still has all-wheel drive or what the CPO warranty covers, gets a cited conversational answer instead of a results list. The knowledge base behind both paths pulls directly from your VDPs, FAQ routes, and policy PDFs, and it refreshes twice a day, so a price change or a unit that sold this morning shows up in answers within hours, not whenever someone remembers to update a script.

Matador AI's strength sits somewhere else. It's a conversation and follow-up engine built to work an up across channels once contact exists, and that's genuinely useful for a BDC chasing aged and orphaned leads. But it isn't reading your VDPs or SRP to answer a shopper's question about a specific vehicle on the lot right now. A shopper typing a question into Matador AI's chat is talking to a sales-conversation layer, not a search index tied to your live inventory.

## When is Matador AI the better choice over Nobi?

Matador AI is the better pick when the leak isn't the website. It's phone ups and SMS threads going cold, or aged leads sitting untouched in the CRM while your VDPs and search index stay accurate. If SMS broadcast campaigns against those aged leads are part of how your BDC runs, Matador AI's TCPA compliance engine matters too, since a compliance misstep on a broadcast text is a legal problem, not just a missed follow-up.

Matador AI fits a rooftop whose real gap is reach, not accuracy. A team working hundreds of orphaned leads a month needs one tool that calls, texts, and emails across that whole list without a human dialing each one, and Matador AI is built for exactly that job. Where it falls short is the shopper sitting on your site right now with a question about a specific unit. Matador AI isn't reading your inventory feed to answer that, because answering on-site questions was never the job it was built for.

Nobi solves a narrower problem: the shopper who's live on the page. It answers from your connected inventory and policy content and captures the lead before that visitor closes the tab, but it has no channel back to a lead once they leave your site. If your team's actual bottleneck is chasing conversations that already went cold across other channels, Nobi's job never touches that gap.

## When is Nobi the better choice over Matador AI?

Nobi is the better pick when the shopper is still on the site, either stuck on a VDP question that never gets answered or running a search that comes back empty. That's the moment Matador AI isn't built to catch, since its job starts once contact already exists. If a shopper's about to close the tab because they can't confirm a trim's still in stock or what the warranty covers, Nobi is what answers that question and captures the lead before the visitor leaves the page.

High-stakes questions push the case further. A shopper asking about financing terms, warranty coverage, or a return policy needs an answer the dealership actually stands behind, not a paraphrase that might drift from what's published.
