## What does ecommerce site search software actually need to do?

Ecommerce site search needs to match what shoppers actually type, including long, descriptive, or misspelled queries, to the right products, and increasingly needs to answer pre-purchase questions those shoppers ask instead of just returning a results page. Tools in this category get judged on zero-result rate, how much manual rule-tuning they demand, and whether they can ground answers in live catalog and content data.

[Searchanise](https://searchanise.io) is a Shopify-first search and filter app, and it covers the basics well: fast typeahead, filtering, and merchandising controls at a low monthly price. The catch is that synonyms and pins are manual work. Someone on your team maintains the list mapping "wide pant" to "wide-leg trouser," and that list grows every time you add SKUs. It's a solid pick if your catalog is small and stable; it gets harder to keep up as the catalog grows.

Klevu takes an AI-native approach: it [matches on meaning](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-semantic-search-ecommerce), so a shopper's phrasing doesn't need to hit an exact synonym rule. Searchspring takes a different approach: merchandisers control ranking directly through pins, boosts, and rules they write and maintain by hand.

Nobi, a conversational website assistant, extends past the results page. It answers pre-purchase questions directly, with citations, instead of stopping at a product list.

## Why look for a Searchanise alternative?

Searchanise is upfront about what it is: a Shopify merchandising app built on hand-configured synonyms, pins, and boosts, not AI-driven matching. That's a fine setup when a catalog is small and holds still. It gets harder to keep current as SKUs pile up and shoppers keep finding new ways to phrase the same query, and it doesn't help at all when the question isn't about finding a product but understanding one, like sizing, materials, or a return window.

Searchanise covers the basics of Shopify search and filtering at a low monthly price, and for a straightforward catalog that doesn't change much, the rule-based approach works fine. The tradeoff shows up as the catalog grows: every new way a shopper phrases a query is a synonym someone has to add, and every product that isn't surfacing correctly is a pin or boost someone has to set by hand. None of that makes Searchanise a bad tool. It's a fit question, not a quality one, and the fit gets tighter as maintenance piles up.

Nobi takes a different approach: it ranks results from the catalog and shopper behavior automatically, so there's no growing list of synonym rules to maintain by hand. It also answers the questions a results page can't, citing the source page it pulled the answer from so a shopper can check it. The tradeoff is scope. Nobi curates the search results page itself; it doesn't extend merchandising controls across category or collection pages the way a dedicated merchandising tool would. For a brand whose real problem is rule upkeep and unanswered shopper questions, that's a fair trade. For a brand that needs merchandising across the whole site, it's a gap worth knowing about going in.

Pricing is tiered by store size with no published rate, another quote-only wall before you know the cost.

Nobi solves the gap after the results page: pre-purchase questions like sizing, materials, or a return window that a product list can't answer, plus capturing shoppers as leads while they're already asking. ## 2. Klevu

[Klevu](https://klevu.com) reads what a shopper actually typed, not just the words that happen to match your catalog. A search for "wide-leg cropped trouser in navy" still lands on the product even if the title says "cropped wide pant," which matters most once your zero-result rate climbs because nobody's kept the synonym list current as SKUs pile up. The no-code merchandising dashboard lets your team push a seasonal collection or pin a launch without filing an engineering ticket, and a Shopify install is usually live within days. For a marketing manager weighing this against a manual rules engine, the appeal is closing vocabulary mismatch without turning search maintenance into a full-time job.

**Best for:** You're on Shopify, your zero-result rate comes from long, descriptive queries a keyword engine can't parse, and you need occasional pinning on top.

**Pricing:** Three tiers: Essential, Advanced, Expert. Quote-only - no published dollar figures on athoscommerce.com/pricing; all tiers show "Get a demo" CTA. Third-party sources cite roughly $499-$1,598/month range. Personalization features are included in Expert tier only, not lower tiers. Do NOT describe personalization as a "separately licensed module" - it is a tier feature, not an add-on SKU.

**Pros:**
- AI matching closes the vocabulary gap between how shoppers describe products and how your catalog titles them, with no synonym list to maintain by hand for every phrasing variation

**Cons:**
- Expensive relative to brand size
- Merchandising UI is dated
- Personalization features are only in the Expert tier; Essential and Advanced tiers do not include them
- Consolidated under Athos Commerce (alongside Searchspring and Intelligent Reach)

**Verdict:** Pick [Klevu](https://nobi.ai/blog/klevu-alternatives-ecommerce) when vocabulary mismatch is the main zero-result driver on your Shopify store and you need occasional pinning on top; skip it if personalization matters and Expert-tier pricing is out of range.

## 3. Searchspring

[Searchspring](https://searchspring.com) puts every search decision in the merchandising team's hands. Pins, boosts, zero-result redirects, and category rules all live in one dashboard, so nothing about a result comes from a black-box algorithm. For a marketing manager who needs to explain why a query returned what it did, that traceability is the draw. The catch shows up in upkeep: someone still has to write and maintain the rules that map how shoppers search to what the catalog contains, and that work grows every time SKUs get added or a shopper phrases a query in a way the rules don't cover.

**Best for:** Ecommerce teams that want exact, rule-by-rule control over what each query returns and have the merchandising bandwidth to keep that rule list current as the catalog grows.

**Pricing:** Mid-market tiered pricing.

**Pros:**
- Each result traces back to a specific rule, so merchandisers can audit any query against the pin or boost that produced it instead of reverse-engineering a black-box decision
- Zero-result redirects send dead-end queries to a curated landing page instead of a generic fallback list
- Rule-level control means no surprise reranking after a catalog update or seasonal push

**Cons:**
- Traditional rule-based merchandising, less AI-native
- Requires manual relevance tuning
- Consolidated under Athos Commerce (alongside Klevu and Intelligent Reach)

**Verdict:** Pick Searchspring when the merch team wants total, auditable control over every query and has the bandwidth to maintain the rule list. Skip it if reducing manual maintenance work is the actual goal.
