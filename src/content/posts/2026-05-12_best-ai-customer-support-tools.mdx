## What counts as an AI customer support tool for a team?

The category splits cleanly into two jobs. The first is answering customer questions on the site - product, fit, policy, and shipping questions - before they ever turn into a ticket. The second is helping agents work through tickets that already arrived, with auto-resolution, routing, and assist inside a helpdesk. Most tools lean hard one way or the other, and a team's shortlist usually pulls from both.

[Nobi](https://nobi.ai) sits in the first job. It runs in a web chat widget on your site and answers product, fit, policy, and shipping questions from the pages you connect, with a citation pill on every answer that links back to the source. [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) Fin and [Zendesk](https://www.zendesk.com) AI Agents take the second path - they run inside the messenger and the ticket platform your team already uses, so the AI works on the same conversations your agents do. [Ada](https://www.ada.cx) is built to handle high volume across web, app, email, and messaging from one autonomous agent. [Forethought](https://forethought.ai) layers onto Zendesk, Salesforce, or Kustomer to auto-resolve and assist on tickets after they land.

Very few tools do both jobs well. Most teams pair one of each.

## How did we evaluate these AI customer support tools?

We weighted three things a support manager actually cares about: how clearly the pricing models out before you sign, how fast the tool can be live and answering questions, and whether the AI is grounded in content your team can verify and update. Nobi is one of the five tools on this list - we built it, and we're including it because it answers a real part of this category (questions on the website before they become tickets) that the helpdesk-native tools don't focus on.

Pricing first. We counted only what a vendor publishes. Nobi lists $25/month base with $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra message, so a team can model their bill without a sales call. Intercom publishes per-seat pricing plus $0.99 per Fin resolution. Zendesk publishes Suite tiers per agent. Ada and Forethought are quote-only - real options at scale, but you can't run the numbers yourself.

Speed to live separates the field too. Intercom and Zendesk ship faster if your team already runs their inbox; if you don't, the lift is real. Ada and Forethought take longer because they integrate with your data and tune to your tickets. Nobi installs with a small site change, hours instead of months.

Source grounding decides a lot on the AI side. Nobi indexes the pages a team connects (help center, FAQ, product pages, PDFs) and refreshes them twice a day, with a citation on every answer that links back to the source. Intercom Fin, Ada, and Forethought all build knowledge bases from sources you connect; the differences are how often they refresh and whether the AI shows its work to the customer.

The last weight was job fit. Nobi sits on the website and answers customer questions before a ticket opens. Intercom Fin and Zendesk AI Agents work inside the helpdesk your team already runs. Ada spans web, app, email, and messaging from one autonomous agent. Forethought layers onto an existing helpdesk to assist on tickets that already arrived.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi is AI customer support built into your site - it sits in a web chat widget on product pages, FAQ routes, and policy pages, and answers customer questions about product details, fit, returns, shipping, and policy before they ever turn into a ticket. Every answer is grounded in the content your team has connected (help docs, policy pages, product pages) and carries an inline citation pill back to the source. Hover the pill and you see the document name, date, and the exact excerpt the answer came from.

**Best for:** Teams whose biggest support cost is customers asking pre-purchase product and policy questions on the site, and who want answers grounded in their official content with verifiable citations.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included), $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**
- Inline numbered citation pills on every answer - hover one and you see the source document name, date, and the exact excerpt the answer came from, plus a sources sidebar with direct links so a customer can verify any answer without leaving the chat.
- Connected sources refresh twice a day, so a pricing or policy change reaches customer answers within hours instead of waiting on a weekly external sync.
- Transparent flat-base pricing with concrete per-search and per-message overage rates - no per-seat fees and no per-ticket charges to model.
- Optional second AI review that re-checks every draft answer against the cited sources before it sends; toggle is on by default for high-consideration industries.

**Cons:**
- Web chat only - no email channel and no SMS, so teams whose support inbox is primarily email will still need a helpdesk for that traffic.
- Not a ticket workflow platform - it doesn't manage routing, macros, SLAs, or post-order actions like cancellations and returns inside the chat.

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when customer questions on your site are driving the ticket volume you want to cut; pair it with a helpdesk like Gorgias or Zendesk if you also need email handling and ticket workflow.

## 2. Intercom

[Intercom](https://intercom.com) is the customer messaging platform a lot of teams already use as their inbox, with the Fin AI Agent layered on top. Fin learns from your Help Center articles and Snippets along with external content you connect: public URLs, PDFs, DOCX, and direct syncs from Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, and Zendesk. The whole thing runs inside Intercom's Messenger, so the AI answer and the human handoff happen in the same place your agents already work.

**Best for:** Teams already running Intercom as their ticketing inbox who want the AI agent layered into the same platform rather than a separate widget.

**Pricing:** $29/seat/month annual ($39 monthly) on Essential, up to $132/$139 on Expert, plus $0.99 per Fin resolution. The underlying Intercom subscription is required.

**Pros:**
- Fin, your agents, tickets, and reporting all live in the same platform, so the AI-to-human handoff is native rather than cross-tool.
- Wide knowledge ingestion: native Help Center and Snippets plus public URLs, PDFs, DOCX, and direct syncs from Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, and Zendesk.
- Mature reporting and a deep app marketplace built up over a decade of SaaS adoption.

**Cons:**
- External sources (Notion, Confluence, Guru, public URLs) refresh weekly, not in real time, so a policy change can sit stale in Fin's answers for up to six days.
- Fin runs inside Intercom's Messenger on top of a seat-based subscription, so the bill adds up fast: per-seat Intercom plus $0.99 per Fin resolution.

**Verdict:** Pick Intercom when Intercom is already your inbox; skip it if you don't want to buy the full messenger and helpdesk just to get an AI layer.

## 3. Zendesk

[Zendesk](https://zendesk.com) is the customer support platform many mid-market and enterprise teams already run as their ticketing system, and its AI Agents and Copilot features layer autonomous resolution and agent assistance on top of the Suite the team uses day to day.

**Best for:** Mid-market and enterprise support teams whose system of record for tickets is already Zendesk and who want AI tightly coupled to that ticket workflow.

**Pricing:** Suite + Copilot Professional $155/agent/month annual; Suite + Copilot Enterprise $209/agent/month annual. Standalone Copilot add-on $50/agent/month. Autonomous AI Agents are quote-only.

**Pros:**
- Tightest integration with Zendesk tickets, macros, triggers, SLAs, and routing - the AI lives next to everything else the team uses.
- Mature reporting, SLA breach alerts, first-reply and resolution-time tracking, and CSAT surveys across channels - tools support managers are already trained to read.
- Wide app marketplace and connector ecosystem covering CRM, commerce, and internal-tool integrations.
- Strong fit when the ticket workflow itself - routing, macros, SLAs, case management - is the actual bottleneck and the AI needs to live next to those rules.

**Cons:**
- Suite + Copilot licensing plus autonomous AI Agents on top is a real line item before the AI starts paying back.
- Full Suite + AI rollout is measured in months, not days, and the customer-facing parts need to be built rather than turned on.

**Verdict:** Pick Zendesk when the ticket workflow is your bottleneck and Zendesk is already your system of record; skip it if a separate website assistant would cut more tickets than agent-side AI ever will.

## 4. Ada

[Ada](https://ada.cx) is an AI customer service platform that runs one autonomous agent across web, app, email, SMS, and messaging apps. The same set of intents, integrations, and reasoning logic powers every channel, so a question that arrives by email gets answered the same way as one that arrives in the in-app chat. Ada is built for companies running 300,000 or more customer service conversations a year, roughly 25,000 a month.

**Best for:** Support teams with high conversation volume across many channels (300,000+ conversations a year) who need one autonomous AI agent answering across web, app, email, SMS, and messaging.

**Pricing:** Quote-only. Ada explicitly fits companies running 300,000+ annual customer service conversations and requires a sales call before you see a number.

**Pros:**
- One AI agent runs across web chat, in-app, email, SMS, and messaging apps from one set of intents and integrations.
- Focused on resolving questions end-to-end rather than just helping agents close tickets faster, with deep API tooling for real-time data retrieval and actions on behalf of the customer.
- Doesn't need an existing helpdesk underneath it. Ada can be the primary AI support layer rather than an add-on.
- Reports automated resolution rate by channel, so you can see which channel the AI is actually carrying.

**Cons:**
- Enterprise-leaning rollout. Data integration, intent tuning, and launch are typically a multi-quarter project rather than a days-to-live setup.
- Pricing isn't published, so you can't model your bill without a sales conversation.
- Less suited for teams whose volume is concentrated on one channel. The reach across many channels is what you're paying for.

**Verdict:** Pick Ada when you genuinely need autonomous AI across many channels at enterprise volume and have the budget and team to run an enterprise rollout; skip it if your volume sits on one channel and you need to be live this quarter.

## 5. Forethought

[Forethought](https://forethought.ai) is built for the opposite end of the problem: tickets that have already landed. It layers onto Zendesk, Salesforce, or Kustomer to auto-resolve tickets, route them, and give agents suggested replies and summaries inside the helpdesk they already use. Nothing moves - your workflows, macros, and routing rules stay in place, and the AI shows up inside the same inbox your team already works in.

**Best for:** Teams whose helpdesk choice (Zendesk, Salesforce, or Kustomer) is set in stone and who want AI added on top without a migration.

**Pricing:** Quote-only. A platform access fee plus committed usage on AI auto-resolution volume and ticket volume routed to agents. Basic, Professional, and Enterprise tiers all require a sales call before you see a number.

**Pros:**
- Layers onto an existing helpdesk without replacing it, so the change-management burden is much lower than a platform swap.
- Covers both autonomous ticket resolution and agent assistance (suggested replies, summarization, sorting) inside the helpdesk your team already uses.
- Works with whichever of the three supported helpdesks you run rather than locking you into one vendor's AI.

**Cons:**
- No customer-facing deployment - all the impact is inside the helpdesk after tickets land, so it doesn't reduce how many tickets arrive.
- Requires you to already be on Zendesk, Salesforce, or Kustomer, and the bill needs a sales call to model.

**Verdict:** Pick Forethought when your helpdesk is non-negotiable and the bottleneck is how long agents spend on tickets that already arrived; skip it if your bigger problem is how many tickets get filed in the first place.

## How should you pick between these AI customer support tools?

Match the tool to the job that's actually driving your support cost. If customer questions on the site (sizing, materials, return policy, shipping cutoffs) are filling your inbox, you need an assistant that answers them on the page before a ticket opens. If the ticket workflow itself is the bottleneck, you need [AI built into the helpdesk your team already runs](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-top-ai-assistants-that-plug-into-existing-help-desks). Most teams end up with two tools, not one.

Nobi is the pick when pre-purchase questions on your site are what you're trying to cut. It sits on product pages, FAQ routes, and policy pages, answers from the content you connect, and shows a citation on every answer. Pair it with a helpdesk for email and post-order tasks.

Intercom is the pick when your inbox is already Intercom. Fin runs inside the messenger your team logs into anyway, so adoption is the easiest part of the project.

Zendesk is the pick when Zendesk is already your system of record for tickets and the ticket workflow itself is the slow part. AI Agents and Copilot plug into the routing, macros, and SLAs you already run.

Ada is the pick when your volume is genuinely spread across web, app, email, and messaging at 300,000 conversations a year or more, and you have the budget and timeline for an enterprise rollout.

Forethought is the pick when your helpdesk is locked in (Zendesk, Salesforce, or Kustomer) and you want AI on top without a migration.
