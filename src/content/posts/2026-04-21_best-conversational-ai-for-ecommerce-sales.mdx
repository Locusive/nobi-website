## What does a conversational AI sales assistant actually do on an ecommerce site?

A conversational AI sales assistant sits on your storefront and helps visitors find products, compare options, answer pre-purchase questions, and move from browse to checkout without waiting for a human rep. The good ones pull from your live catalog, learn from shopper behavior, and can be dropped on PDPs, in the cart, or as a persistent widget on the homepage. Each surface drives a different behavior: a collection-page widget rescues undecided browsers, a PDP assistant answers sizing and fit questions before the add-to-cart, and a cart assistant handles last-mile objections (shipping, returns, bundle fit) right before checkout. Faherty's Shop with AI deployment ran at a 6.3% CVR with a $315 AOV, and Lucchese saw incremental checkouts specifically attributable to the cart and PDP assistants alongside search.

Sales assistance is a different job than support. The metrics that matter are conversion rate, AOV, and revenue per session, not first-response time or ticket deflection. Weak assistants reply from a static FAQ. Better ones ingest the full catalog, read product attributes, and can also capture email or SMS mid-conversation for lifecycle follow-up, a secondary but real source of return.

## When is Gorgias a better choice than Nobi for conversational AI?

Gorgias is the better pick when your team already routes every shopper conversation through a ticketing inbox and you want one AI agent handling both pre-sale chat and post-purchase support in the same queue. Its Shopify integration and ticket-centric workflow are hard to beat if support owns the conversation function end to end. Nobi is the wrong choice for that job. Nobi focuses on on-site discovery and sales, not ticket triage, refund workflows, or return merchandise authorizations.

The decision often comes down to who owns the project. If your head of CX is the buyer, Gorgias fits their daily workflow: tickets, macros, SLAs, agent productivity dashboards. If your head of ecommerce is the buyer and the goal is lifting conversion rate and AOV on the storefront, a discovery-first tool like Nobi maps cleanly to that P&L. Same shopper, different job to be done.

One thing to budget for with Gorgias: the AI features sit on higher plans. Public third-party pricing coverage puts plans between roughly $10/month at the Starter end and $900/month at Advanced, with ticket overages around $0.36 to $0.40 each once you blow past your monthly allowance, plus add-ons for voice and SMS. If your ticket volume spikes during a sale or a shipping delay, the bill follows. Model the overage math against your peak months, not your average ones.

These tools can also coexist. A common setup: Nobi runs the on-site assistant, search, and lead capture for pre-purchase shoppers at $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages), and Gorgias handles post-purchase tickets in the helpdesk. The handoff is clean because each tool stays in its lane.

## What are Rep AI, Octane AI, Alby, and Tidio each good at?

Each of these tools solves a narrower slice of the conversational AI problem. Rep AI focuses on proactive sales-chat triggers for browsing visitors. Octane AI is built around product-finder quizzes, particularly strong in beauty and CPG. Alby answers product questions on Shopify PDPs but has been uncertain since Bluecore's November 2024 acquisition. Tidio is a live-chat-first tool with an AI add-on, best suited to smaller Shopify merchants who want chat before they want a full shopping assistant.

**Rep AI** is built around behavioral triggers. It watches how a visitor moves through the store and surfaces chat interventions on product pages and in the cart when signals suggest hesitation. If your specific gap is that shoppers browse and leave without engaging anyone, Rep AI is a reasonable pick. It is Shopify-native and leans hard on proactive engagement as its wedge.

**Octane AI** is the category leader for quiz-led recommendation flows, especially in beauty, skincare, and CPG. Shoppers answer a product-finder quiz and get routed to a curated set of SKUs. Pricing starts at $50/month for 400 credits on the Basic plan and scales to $200/month (Plus) and $500+/month (Enterprise). Klaviyo integration is tight, which matters if quiz responses need to feed your email segmentation.

**Alby** is a lightweight PDP Q&A widget that answers buyer questions inline on the product page. The functionality is narrow but genuinely useful for high-consideration SKUs. The caveat: Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024, and the roadmap direction is not yet clear. Weigh that before signing a multi-year deal.

**Tidio** pairs live chat with its Lyro AI agent, available on the Plus plan or as an add-on. It is an affordable starting point for smaller Shopify merchants who want chat coverage first and AI second.

None of these four combine site search and conversational AI in one platform the way Nobi does.

## What results should a head of ecommerce expect from deploying a conversational AI?

Expect to measure impact in conversion rate, average order value, and revenue per session, not message volume or response time. Those operational metrics tell you the bot is running. They don't tell you it's selling. The only numbers a head of ecommerce should defend to a CFO are the ones tied to revenue on the cohort that actually saw the assistant.

Benchmarks vary by placement, catalog depth, and traffic source, but [published customer numbers](https://nobi.ai/customers) give you a useful floor. Faherty reports a 6.3% conversion rate and a $315 AOV on its shop-with-AI surface running on Nobi, which is meaningfully above typical apparel site-wide CVR. Lucchese, a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus, has attributed more than $1M in incremental year-one revenue and $3.46M in cumulative revenue to its Nobi deployment, a 39x ROI against spend. Their setup pairs site search with a cart assistant and a PDP assistant, and both assistant surfaces drove meaningful incremental checkouts alongside search, not just search alone.

Two measurement habits separate a clean rollout from a noisy one. First, track CVR and AOV on the assistant-exposed cohort specifically, not site-wide averages. Site-wide numbers mix in traffic that never touched the assistant and will understate impact. Second, attribute both assisted and last-click revenue. Conversational AI often assists the path to purchase rather than closing it in a single session, so last-click alone will credit the wrong touchpoint.

Run any rollout as an A/B test on a real traffic slice before you deploy site-wide, and give it 60 to 90 days. Shorter windows get distorted by seasonal traffic, promo cadence, and paid media shifts. If the exposed cohort is lifting CVR and AOV together after a full cycle, the assistant is earning its keep.
