## What does an AI shopping assistant actually do on an ecommerce site?

An AI shopping assistant sits between the shopper and your catalog and handles some mix of four jobs: understanding natural-language search queries, recommending products in context, answering product questions, and capturing leads or guiding checkout. The category stretches from pure search engines with a chat skin to conversational agents that replace the help widget, and the tool you pick should match whichever of those jobs is the real bottleneck on your site.

Most tools are strong at one or two of the four, not all of them. Alby focuses on Shopify product Q&A and related-product discovery. Rep AI leans into proactive chat that nudges browsers toward checkout. Octane AI is the category leader for quiz-led product finders. Gorgias is a helpdesk first, with an AI Agent bolted on for support and sales.

If you run a Head of Ecommerce org, the useful question isn't which demo is shiniest. It's which job closes the biggest conversion gap for your traffic. Search-led discovery points to Nobi or Alby. Proactive sales nudges, Rep AI or Tidio. Quiz-led funnels, Octane AI. Support deflection, Gorgias.

## Which AI shopping assistant is the best fit for a mid-market ecommerce brand?

For a mid-market brand in the $5M–$100M GMV range that wants one tool to cover site search, a conversational assistant, and [lead capture](https://nobi.ai/lead-capture), Nobi is the most defensible starting point. You get three surfaces from one vendor instead of stitching together a search platform, a chat vendor, and an email capture popup that never talk to each other. Results rank based on what shoppers actually click and buy, so the merchandising team isn't rewriting synonym rules every Monday to fix last week's misses.

Pricing is where Nobi separates from enterprise incumbents. [The base plan is $25/month](https://nobi.ai/pricing) and includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Beyond that, searches are $0.01 each and messages are $0.10 each. No revenue share, no per-seat fees, no tiered AI gating behind a sales call. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month lands around $995, which still undercuts most enterprise search contracts by an order of magnitude, and you can model the bill before you sign. Implementation runs in days, not the multi-month kickoffs typical of enterprise search replatforms.

Two honest limits worth flagging before you shortlist. Nobi curates the search results page but isn't a site-wide merchandising tool for category and collection pages, so brands that need to control ranking across the entire site will still want a dedicated merchandising layer. And the assistant is a web-chat surface only: no voice, no SMS, no WhatsApp.

## When is Rep AI or Octane AI a better pick than Nobi?

If your core bottleneck isn't search, a more specialized assistant will outperform Nobi on that specific job. Rep AI is the better pick when paid-traffic bounce is the problem and you want a chatbot that proactively intercepts browsers before they leave. Octane AI is the better pick for beauty, skincare, and CPG brands where a guided quiz is the hero discovery flow and the assistant needs to feed a Klaviyo-driven lifecycle program. Nobi does not ship quiz-led product finders, so if a quiz funnel is the front door of your store, you'll want Octane.

Rep AI is built around proactive engagement on Shopify. Its pitch is anticipating shopper intent and acting on it before the visitor bounces, which matters most when the majority of paid sessions exit without adding to cart. If your conversion math hinges on rescuing paid sessions in real time, a chatbot designed around behavioral triggers will move that number more than a better search bar will.

Octane AI is the #1 product quiz platform on Shopify, and that's the right framing. Beauty and CPG brands lean on quizzes because shoppers genuinely don't know whether they want the retinol serum or the peptide one, and a five-question quiz is a better answer than a search box. Octane's Basic plan starts at $50 per month with 400 credits, scaling to Plus at $200 and Enterprise at $500+. Pair that with their Klaviyo handoff and the quiz becomes both a discovery surface and a list-building engine.

The real question isn't which assistant is best. It's which bottleneck you're solving: browse-to-buy rescue (Rep AI), guided first-visit discovery (Octane AI), or open-ended search and conversational discovery across the catalog (Nobi).

## Should I use Gorgias, Tidio, or Alby instead of a discovery-focused assistant?

Gorgias, Tidio, and Alby each own a different job that isn't product discovery. Pick the one whose job matches your actual bottleneck, not the one that sounds closest to "AI assistant."

**Gorgias** is the right call when support ticket volume is your real pain. It's a full ecommerce helpdesk with deep Shopify order context baked in, and its AI Agent is built to autonomously handle returns, order status, refunds, and the rest of the post-purchase queue. Pricing is ticket-based (third-party reporting puts plans roughly $10/month at the entry tier up to $900/month at Advanced, with overages around $0.36–$0.40 per ticket), which makes ticket deflection the natural KPI. If "we're drowning in WISMO emails" is the sentence you keep saying, Gorgias is the answer, not a search tool. Nobi doesn't manage tickets, returns, or refunds, and isn't trying to.

**Tidio** fits smaller Shopify stores where live chat matters more than semantic search. Its Lyro AI agent learns from your support content and answers customer questions, and Tidio offers a free trial with 50 Lyro conversations included so you can test before committing. It's approachable for a team that wants live-agent handoff plus a lightweight AI responder, less appropriate if your conversion problem is shoppers failing to find products in a 10,000-SKU catalog.

**Alby** is focused on product Q&A embedded near the buy button on Shopify PDPs, fine-tuned for ecommerce sales and support questions. The honest caveat: Alby was acquired by Bluecore in November 2024, and the independent product roadmap since then is uncertain. Worth diligencing the post-acquisition direction before signing.

Nobi's gap here is real. It's not a helpdesk replacement, doesn't close the post-purchase loop, and isn't built to be a heavily branded "AI stylist" persona. If any of those three is your #1 job, buy the tool that does it.

## How do AI shopping assistants price, and where do the hidden costs show up?

Pricing in this category falls into three buckets: flat SaaS, usage-based with clear unit economics, and revenue-share or per-resolution pricing that scales with your success. The hidden costs almost always show up in the same three places: revenue-share tiers that tax your upside, AI features gated to higher plans, and engineering time required to launch.

Nobi publishes exact unit pricing. $25/month base includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. After that, $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month pays roughly $500, with no revenue share on top. You can model cost at any traffic level before you sign, and your bill doesn't rise faster than your traffic does.

Octane AI publishes three plans starting at $50/month (Basic, 400 credits), $200/month (Plus, 2,200 credits), and $500+/month for Enterprise. Credits apply to quiz interactions, so cost scales with quiz engagement rather than outcomes.

Tidio's live chat has a free tier, and Lyro, its AI responder, is priced per conversation with 50 free to start. That's clean on paper, but the meaningful AI features sit on higher plans.

Gorgias is where the math gets trickier. The helpdesk starts at $10/month for Starter, but AI Agent is billed per automated resolution on top of ticket-based pricing, and third-party buyers have flagged double-charging when a ticket touches both a human and an AI. Rep AI uses traffic-tiered pricing, so cost scales with sessions, not sales. Alby doesn't publish pricing at all.

The head-of-ecommerce lens: a revenue-share or per-resolution deal looks cheap at $5M GMV and punitive at $50M. Model the tax at your 18-month projected volume before you sign anything.

## How long does it actually take to launch an AI shopping assistant?

Time-to-launch varies more than vendors admit, and the honest answer depends on what you're actually deploying. A chat-only tool like Tidio or Alby can go live in an afternoon: drop in a script, point it at your Shopify catalog, write a few FAQ answers, and you're taking conversations the same day. Tidio advertises a free trial with 50 Lyro conversations so teams can test before committing, which tells you something about how quick the setup is meant to be.

A search-plus-assistant deployment is a different animal. You're indexing the full catalog, mapping attributes (fit, fabric, occasion, use case), and reviewing merchandising defaults before the first shopper ever types a query. With Nobi, that work typically runs 2 to 4 weeks for a mid-market catalog. Enterprise search platforms usually run 2 to 4 months once integration, indexing, and merchandising rules are agreed on, reviewed, and QA'd, largely because the configuration surface is bigger and the implementation is often gated behind a services contract.

The variable that blows up timelines is customization. Product teams routinely want search results to render differently than the vendor's default grid, which is where most implementations stall. Nobi's Hooks API exists specifically for this. UNTUCKit asked for developer-controlled logic over how products display in the results panel, and that request became the Hooks capability that every Nobi customer now has access to. It covers most bespoke rendering requests without forcing a full custom frontend build.

One honest caveat: if you want to own the ranking logic yourself, or embed search inside a heavily custom storefront where you control every pixel, an API-first developer platform will fit you better than Nobi. Hooks handles rendering, not ranking algorithms you want to write from scratch.

## What results should I expect from deploying an AI shopping assistant?

Expectations depend on which job the assistant is doing and the shape of your catalog. On a mid-market apparel catalog, Faherty's Shop with AI experience runs at a 6.3% conversion rate with a $315 AOV, well above typical site-wide ecommerce averages for apparel. On a multi-surface deployment, Lucchese paired Nobi search with a PDP assistant and a cart assistant and reported $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one, $3.46M cumulative revenue attributed, and a 39x ROI. The cart-assistant and PDP-assistant surfaces drove meaningful incremental checkouts alongside search, which is a useful data point if you're debating whether to roll out beyond the search box on day one.

Treat those numbers as anchors, not forecasts. The same assistant can produce very different economics across verticals: what works on a $500 Western boot won't automatically work on a $20 consumable where shoppers don't need help deciding.

The honest way to model this: stand the assistant up on an A/B traffic split, measure incremental revenue against your current baseline CVR and AOV, and run it long enough to clear seasonal noise. Compare against your own numbers, not the vendor's best-case customer logo. If the lift holds at a fraction of Faherty's or Lucchese's results, the math usually still works.
