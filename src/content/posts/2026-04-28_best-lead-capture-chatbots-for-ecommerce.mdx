## What does a lead capture tool actually do for an ecommerce site?

A lead capture tool turns anonymous site visitors into known contacts you can re-engage, usually by collecting an email or phone number tied to some signal of intent. In ecommerce, those tools split into three rough shapes: overlay popups that interrupt the page to ask for an opt-in, quiz funnels that trade product recommendations for a profile, and conversational shopping assistants that surface shopper questions a brand can act on. Picking the right one starts with naming the underlying job, because "emails added to your list" can be the wrong yardstick if discovery, not list size, is your actual bottleneck.

Nobi is the [AI site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search) and shopping assistant in this set: it answers shopper questions on the storefront and search bar from your connected knowledge sources, with contextual suggestion pills that give visitors a starting point. $25/month base includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Strong when shoppers can't find products or get a straight answer on the storefront; weaker when overlay-driven list growth is the metric, since Nobi has no popup builder.

[Privy](https://www.privy.com) is the overlay-first option: email and SMS popups, exit-intent triggers, banners, and spin-to-win, from $30/month with 250 free SMS credits included. Strong when list growth itself is the goal and your traffic already knows what it wants; weaker when shoppers are bouncing because they can't find the right product.

[Octane AI](https://www.octaneai.com) owns the quiz-led product finder lane on Shopify, from $50/month on the Basic plan (400 credits), with Klaviyo and Recharge integrations to push profiles into downstream flows. Strong when your hero discovery surface is a quiz; weaker when shoppers won't sit through five questions before they get a recommendation.

[Justuno](https://www.justuno.com) sits between the two: rule-engine targeting, segmentation, and exit popups with founder-led onboarding. Strong when you want fine-grained promo targeting layered on top of capture; pricing is modular and not posted publicly, so expect a quote.

## 2. Octane AI

[Octane AI](https://octaneai.com) is a quiz-led product finder built primarily for Shopify, and the quiz is the hero surface, not open-ended chat or site search. For a head of ecommerce in beauty, skincare, or supplements, that focus is the point: shoppers answer five to ten diagnostic questions about skin type, goals, or routine, and the platform maps answers to product attributes in the Shopify catalog to recommend a regimen. The lead-capture moment lives on the result page - the email is the trade for "here's your personalised routine" - and Klaviyo picks up the quiz answers as segment attributes for the lifecycle flow that follows. If your discovery problem is "shoppers don't know which of our 80 SKUs is right for them," a scripted quiz tree is a reasonable answer.

**Best for:** Beauty, skincare, and CPG brands on Shopify whose primary discovery surface is a diagnostic or routine-builder quiz funnel, with email capture happening as the trade for the result page.

**Pricing:** Basic at $50/month (400 credits), Plus at $200/month (2,200 credits), Enterprise and Unlimited at $500+/month.

**Pros:**
- Quiz builder with deep Shopify product-attribute mapping, so recommendations resolve to real SKUs rather than generic categories
- Pre-built templates for common vertical use cases - skincare routines, supplement regimens, fit finders - which shortens time-to-launch
- Native Klaviyo integration captures quiz answers as email-segment attributes, so quiz completion flows straight into lifecycle email
- Lower engineering overhead than building quiz logic in-house on top of a headless storefront

**Cons:**
- Quiz-centric, so it's less effective for shoppers who prefer open-ended search or conversational browsing over a scripted question tree
- Vertical fit narrows outside beauty and CPG categories, where scripted diagnostic questions don't map cleanly to catalog attributes

**Verdict:** Pick Octane AI if a quiz is genuinely the right discovery metaphor for your category and Klaviyo segmentation is the downstream goal; skip it if your catalog is too varied for a scripted question tree to cover.

## 3. Privy

[Privy](https://privy.com) is the most direct expression of the popup-first lead capture pattern in this list: a Shopify-focused email and SMS capture tool built around on-site overlays. Exit-intent popups, spin-to-win wheels, signup bars, embedded forms, welcome modals - the full overlay library, tuned for ecommerce and launchable without engineering help. Sending is built in too, so a captured contact can flow into a Privy-native email or SMS welcome series without bolting on a second platform. For a head of ecommerce whose named bottleneck at the leadership review is "the list isn't growing fast enough," Privy is the tool that maps cleanly to that job. It is single-purpose by design - capture and send - and it sits next to, not inside, the discovery surface a shopper actually browses.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose primary, named bottleneck is list size - more email and SMS subscribers, captured via overlays at moments of intent like exit, scroll depth, or cart abandonment.

**Pricing:** From $30/month with 250 free SMS credits included on the starter tier; add-on tiers are available for pop-ups and displays from around $24/month and for advanced features from $199/month.

**Pros:**
- Mature library of overlay templates - exit-intent, spin-to-win, signup bars, embedded forms - tuned for ecommerce and launchable without engineering help
- Direct Shopify integration with cart-aware triggers (abandoned cart value, page URL, customer tag), so a popup fires on a real signal of intent
- Built-in email and SMS sending means a captured contact can be welcomed or recovered without adding a second platform for very small teams
- Public starting price ($30/month) with 250 free SMS credits included makes it straightforward to model the bill before committing

**Cons:**
- Popup-first surface is interruptive by design, and overusing overlays can hurt the on-site experience and Core Web Vitals scores
- Single-purpose: Privy captures contacts and sends messages, but it doesn't help shoppers find products, so it pairs with rather than replaces a discovery tool

**Verdict:** Pick Privy when "grow the list via overlays" is the literal job and you want capture and sending in one place; skip it if interruptive popups are off the table or if discovery is the real underlying problem.

## 4. Justuno

[Justuno](https://justuno.com) is a conversion optimization platform built around popups, banners, sliders, and embedded on-site messages, with rule-based audience segmentation across URL, traffic source, on-site behavior, and cart contents. For a head of ecommerce, the appeal is consolidating every overlay surface (email capture, free-shipping bars, promo announcements, exit messages) into one toolbox with shared targeting rules. It is broader than Privy in format coverage and stronger on segmentation, at the cost of a steeper setup curve. Where Privy keeps the surface tight, Justuno gives a conversion team room to run several messages for several shopper segments concurrently, and ties campaign performance back to A/B tests inside the platform rather than through a separate experimentation tool.

**Best for:** Conversion-team brands who want a single toolbox for multi-format on-site messaging - email capture, promotion banners, free-shipping bars - with audience targeting across the funnel.

**Pricing:** Modular billing that scales with campaign scope and integrations; paid plans start around $39/month and tier up from there.

**Pros:**
- Granular audience segmentation by URL, traffic source, on-site behavior, and cart contents lets one platform run different messages for different shopper segments
- Format breadth covers popups, banners, sliders, and embedded messages, so a single tool handles email capture, free-shipping bars, and promotion announcements
- A/B testing and analytics across campaigns are built into the platform, not bolted on through a separate experimentation tool
- Integrates with major email platforms (Klaviyo, Mailchimp, and others) so captured contacts flow into the brand's existing lifecycle program

**Cons:**
- Popup-first surface like Privy, so the same UX trade-offs around interruption and Core Web Vitals apply at scale
- Steeper learning curve than Privy for a small team - the segmentation rules are powerful but require setup time to use well

**Verdict:** Pick Justuno when you want one platform for every overlay and banner with audience targeting; skip it if conversational discovery is the actual bottleneck or if a smaller team needs the simplest possible popup tool.

## Frequently asked questions

### What's the difference between popup-based and conversational lead capture?

Popup tools (Privy, Justuno) trade a discount or content offer for an email or SMS at the right trigger - exit, scroll, time on page. Conversational and quiz tools (Octane AI quizzes, Nobi's assistant) help shoppers find the right product first, with email collection happening on result pages or signup forms downstream of the discovery experience.

### Does Nobi replace a popup tool like Privy?

No. Privy and Justuno own the visitor-trigger surface: timed popups, exit overlays, banner bars, segmented email and SMS offers. Nobi covers the search bar and on-site assistant. Discount-driven email-list growth still needs a popup tool.

### How does pricing scale with traffic and contact volume?

Privy starts at $30/month with 250 free SMS credits and tiers up from there. Justuno bills modularly with paid plans starting around $39/month. Octane AI runs from $50/mo Basic (400 credits) up to $500+/mo Enterprise. Nobi is $25/month base with 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message.

### How long does install take on Shopify?

Octane AI, Privy, and Justuno all install as Shopify apps: connect, configure, ship in a day. Nobi installs by dropping two JavaScript snippets into the theme - one site-wide, one on the search and assistant surfaces. The same path works on Shopify Plus, BigCommerce, Magento, WooCommerce, and Salesforce Commerce Cloud.
