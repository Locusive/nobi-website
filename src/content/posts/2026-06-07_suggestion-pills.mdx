## How do search suggestion prompts guide undecided shoppers to products?

Shoppers often arrive at your store without a specific search query ready. A blank search box gives them nothing to start with. Most leave without typing a word. That drop-off never shows in your zero-result log. This guide covers how to set up suggestion pills in Nobi, which prompts convert best, and how to measure the revenue impact downstream.

## Why does a blank search box cause undecided shoppers to bounce?

Most visitors who land on your store haven't decided what to buy yet. A blank search bar with a flashing cursor asks them to supply intent they don't have - and when they can't, they leave.

The failure mode most teams track is [zero-result pages](/blog/fix-zero-result-searches) - searches that return nothing. The larger failure is the visitor who never types a word. You can measure null results. You can't measure everyone who saw an empty box and bounced before searching. That invisible churn is where most of the real loss is.

Paid and email traffic makes this worse. Those visitors arrive warm but still without a specific query ready. A blank box wastes that acquisition spend before you even know the visitor was there.

Vocabulary mismatch is a related problem. A shopper searching "slim fit chinos" on a store whose catalog uses "tailored trousers" hits a dead end even when the product exists.

## What is Nobi, and how do its suggestion pills turn a blank search box into a guided starting point?

Nobi is an AI-powered site search and shopping assistant that pairs a traditional search bar with a conversational assistant grounded in your live catalog and knowledge base. Suggestion pills appear alongside that blank box: LLM-generated, tappable prompt buttons that give visitors a starting point.

What appears depends on where the visitor is. Homepage pills tend toward broad starting questions - shipping policy, gift-finding, popular categories. Pills on a product page shift to questions about that specific item: sizing, materials, what pairs with it. Cart pills address the items already in the bag - care instructions, warranty, return policy. Nobi's LLM generates each set from the current page context; no one has to write or maintain a template for each page type.

Clicking a pill doesn't just fill the search box and wait. The assistant opens with the answer already loading. Nobi pulls from the sources you've connected - product pages, FAQ docs, policy PDFs - not from a training set that was frozen at build time. Every answer carries an inline citation pill. Hover it to see the document name, date, and the exact excerpt that grounded the answer.

Connected sources refresh twice a day, so a policy update or pricing change lands in pill answers within hours - not whenever someone remembers to edit a static FAQ.

[Lucchese](/customers/lucchese) put the PDP and cart assistant live alongside search. Their cart assistant and product page assistant together drove meaningful incremental checkouts.

## How do I add suggestion pills to my Nobi assistant?

Adding those grounded pill prompts to your store starts in the assistant configuration panel. Suggestion pills are a launch component - enable them, pick which page types should show them, and Nobi generates the prompts automatically from your connected catalog and knowledge base. No manual list to write and no maintenance once they're live.

Which page types you enable determines what prompts appear. Nobi generates each set from your current page context; you turn each type on or off in the settings panel.

The prompts draw from whatever sources you've connected: product pages, FAQ routes, policy docs, PDFs, help-center articles. Connect a URL or upload a file and it becomes part of what the pills can surface.

A developer adds the Nobi runtime (the Quick Start guide covers this) and places the pill component on the relevant pages. The default renders three pills in a carousel:

```html
<Nobi-suggestion-pills pill-count="3"></nobi-suggestion-pills>
```

For more pills or a wrapped grid layout (up to ten pills per component):

```html
<Nobi-suggestion-pills pill-count="6" multi-row="true"></nobi-suggestion-pills>
```

You can also scope pills to specific types - cross-sell suggestions, answerable Q&A questions, or custom slugs:

```html
<Nobi-suggestion-pills pill-count="3" pill-types="cross-sell,qa"></nobi-suggestion-pills>
```

On Shopify, the product ID auto-detects from the page. On other platforms, pass it explicitly:

```html
<Nobi-suggestion-pills pill-count="3" product-id="YOUR_PRODUCT_ID"></nobi-suggestion-pills>
```

Suggestion pills are part of the standard assistant - no premium tier required. The base plan is $25/month and includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Overages run $0.01 per search and $0.10 per conversational message, so you can estimate your cost at volume before committing.

## How do I choose suggestion pill prompts that match my catalog and shopper intent?

The pills that convert are grounded in questions shoppers actually have at the moment they see them - and that moment changes depending on where they are in the store.

**Homepage pills** should match broad discovery intent. Questions like "What works best for trail running?" or "What are your most popular gifts under $100?" meet them where they are.

**PDP pills** should be SKU-specific. The product page already covers headline features; the pill covers what it doesn't - sizing and fit, materials, compatibility with other items, and dimensions that matter for the buyer's specific use case. [Lucchese](/customers/lucchese) put a product page assistant live alongside site search and attributed $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one.

**Cart pills** address the last hesitation before checkout. WISMO (where is my order) questions, return policy details, and promo-code validation are the ones that stall a purchase at the final step. A shopper on the fence about a $300 boot doesn't need inspiration - they need one fast answer to a specific concern.

To find your highest-confidence pill candidates, check your zero-result log and internal search term report. The queries shoppers already type are the questions they actually have.

One boundary worth noting: Nobi answers questions but doesn't process cancellations or returns inside the assistant. Avoid prompts that require transactional execution - "cancel my order" or "start a return" - since the assistant can explain the process but can't complete it.

## Can I customize suggestion pills for different pages or use cases?

Nobi scopes pill generation to the page a visitor is on, so a homepage visitor sees different prompts than someone deep in a PDP.

On a product page, pills automatically tie to the product currently in view. Nobi reads the page context and generates questions grounded in that item.

For cases where you want tighter control - a seasonal campaign page, a high-margin category, or a high-stakes policy question - query overrides let you pin an exact merchant-approved answer to a specific prompt. When a shopper clicks that pill, the assistant fires the verbatim response every time - no AI rephrasing, no variation. Return policy questions, warranty terms, and promo-code pills during a sale are all good candidates.

An optional second AI review gives you an extra accuracy check on top. Before an answer sends, a second model checks the draft against the raw content from the cited sources and flags anything that looks off. It's off by default in ecommerce and toggleable per merchant - useful when a wrong answer in a specific category is costly enough to warrant the check.

[UNTUCKit](/customers/untuckit)'s requests shaped Nobi's Hooks API, which gives developers control over how the assistant surfaces products inside pill-triggered answer flows. That API is now available to all Nobi customers - useful when a campaign page needs specific products to surface in a particular order, or when a high-margin category calls for a more structured discovery path.

## How do I measure whether suggestion pills are actually driving more conversions?

Track three layers: pill click-through rate (are shoppers tapping at all?), post-click engagement (do they continue searching or buying?), and CVR and AOV in pill-initiated sessions versus the rest.

Pill CTR is your engagement floor. If it's low, the instinct is to move the widget somewhere more prominent. That's almost always the wrong fix. Low CTR means the prompts don't match what that page's visitors actually want - not that the pills are in a bad spot. Swap the prompts before you move anything.

Post-click engagement depth tells you whether the answer satisfied the shopper. A visitor who clicks a pill and continues to a PDP or adds to cart got what they needed. One who drops off right after the first response either got a bad answer or saw the wrong question to begin with.

CVR and AOV in pill-initiated sessions is the headline revenue metric. Nobi's analytics surface what shoppers searched, clicked, and bought - run a segment comparison between pill-initiated sessions and the rest to isolate the lift. [UNTUCKit](/customers/untuckit) ran that comparison - sessions that engaged with the assistant converted at 17.1% higher rates and generated 21.3% more revenue per searcher than sessions that didn't.

A/B testing prompt copy is one of the cheapest improvements available. "Do your boots run true to size?" and "What size should I order if I'm between sizes?" can shift CTR meaningfully on the same page - no engineering work required, just swap the copy and watch the numbers.

One honest limit: Nobi's analytics focus on search and CVR metrics. If understanding specifically why shoppers abandon mid-funnel is a primary need, [Rep AI](https://www.rep.ai) has purpose-built analytics for that.

## When is a different tool a better fit than Nobi's suggestion pills?

Suggestion pills work best when shoppers need an open-ended guided answer grounded in your catalog.

If your conversion bottleneck is a scripted campaign flow with a fixed decision tree - a Black Friday promo sequence or a product-launch walk-through - Rep AI's Flow Studio gives non-engineers a no-code visual builder for that without an engineering ticket. Rep AI also lets a human specialist join an active AI session mid-thread, a mode where the AI keeps running while the human is in the chat.

If your primary discovery approach is a diagnostic quiz - five to ten questions mapping shopper attributes to specific SKUs, common in beauty and CPG - [Octane AI](https://www.octaneai.com)'s quiz builder is purpose-built for that.

Nobi's personalization today covers placeholder text and starter messages only. If behavioral reranking - results reordering based on individual shopper click and purchase history - is the capability you need, look at Constructor or Klevu until Nobi ships that feature. Nobi answers questions through a chat widget on your site and doesn't send or receive email; if post-visit shopper follow-up by email is your primary gap, that requires a different tool category. And if your team needs agent-side ticket workflow - routing, macros, returns processing - Gorgias is a dedicated helpdesk built for that.
