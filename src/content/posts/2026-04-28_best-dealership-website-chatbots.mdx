## What is a dealership website assistant?

These are AI website assistants that sit on your VDPs, inventory pages, and homepage and answer shopper questions in real time - which trims are in stock, what financing looks like on a specific F-150, whether a service slot is open Saturday morning. The current generation runs on large language models grounded in the inventory and policy pages they've indexed, so answers come from your actual stock instead of a static FAQ. The good ones capture contact details on intent signals and route a qualified shopper to sales while interest is still warm, the difference between a browser bouncing to a competing dealer and a lead landing in your CRM.

## How did we evaluate these dealership tools?

We weighed each tool on the jobs a dealership GM actually buys for: how well it answers year/make/model questions against live inventory, whether it captures and routes leads into the CRM, what channels it covers beyond the website, how transparent the pricing is, and how heavy the rollout is for a team that does not own its own website code. Nobi is in the list too - we publish this article, and our entry follows the same template as the others, including the limits.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi is an [AI site search and shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce) for the dealership website. It indexes the pages and documents you connect - web pages, FAQ and help-center articles, and PDFs such as policy documents and brochures - then answers shopper questions in real time with inline numbered citation pills that link straight back to the source. For a GM, that means every answer is auditable: hover a citation to see the source document, its date, and the exact excerpt the answer came from, and the sources sidebar lists every reference for the conversation. Connected knowledge sources refresh twice a day, so a pricing change or new incentive published on a connected page lands in shopper answers within hours. Page-aware suggestion pills give shoppers a starting point on the homepage, VDP, or financing pages when they don't know what to type.

**Best for:** Dealers whose website is the bottleneck - shoppers can't get a clear answer to a product or financing question fast enough and bounce before they ever fill out a lead form.

**Pricing:** $25/month base, including 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**
- Search and chat that handles open-ended shopper questions against the pages you connect, without manual rule tuning each week
- Inline citation pills on every answer plus a sources sidebar, so a GM can audit which page on the dealership site each answer came from
- Connected knowledge sources refresh twice a day, so a pricing or incentive change reaches shopper answers within hours
- Transparent published pricing with metered overage rates instead of a custom-quote conversation

**Cons:**
- Web chat surface only - not currently a phone, SMS, or WhatsApp channel, so dealerships whose shoppers live on text will need a separate tool there
- Lead routing, follow-up cadences, and BDC workflows live in your CRM, not in Nobi
- Smaller third-party integration marketplace than enterprise incumbents, so very long-tail DMS or vendor connections may need a custom build

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when the leak is on the website itself - shoppers bounce before getting an answer to a product question. Skip it if your primary channel is SMS or phone, or if you need a full CRM and BDC workflow stack.

## 2. DealerAI

[DealerAI](https://dealerai.com) is a multi-agent generative AI platform built specifically for dealership websites. Instead of one monolithic assistant, it runs a stack of specialized agents that hand off between intents - inventory questions, appointment scheduling, financing FAQs, general shopper Q&A - so each conversation gets routed to the agent best suited to answer it. For a dealership GM whose biggest gap is the website chat layer, that purpose-built focus means a faster rollout than rolling out an end-to-end automotive AI suite. The trade-off is scope: DealerAI sits on the site, not on the phone line, the SMS thread, or the service-bay schedule.

**Best for:** Dealers buying the website chat layer specifically and not looking to replace their CRM or take on a multi-channel rollout in the same project.

**Pricing:** Custom; quoted per rooftop. Public pricing isn't posted, so an apples-to-apples cost comparison takes a sales call.

**Pros:**
- Multi-agent architecture purpose-built for dealership website use cases, with specialized agents for inventory, appointments, and shopper FAQ
- Covers the most common on-site jobs - inventory questions, appointment booking, shopper FAQ - in a single widget
- Focused scope means a lighter rollout than an end-to-end automotive AI suite that bundles website, phone, and BDC together

**Cons:**
- Website-only in the base product - no phone, SMS, or service-bay integration
- Less depth on lifecycle CRM, BDC routing, and post-lead follow-up than tools that started life as automotive CRMs
- Custom-quote pricing means no public starter number, which slows side-by-side cost evaluation against vendors that publish tiers

**Verdict:** Pick DealerAI when the website chat layer is the entire scope of what you're buying; look elsewhere if you also need phone, SMS, or a full CRM stack in the same purchase.

## 3. Impel

[Impel](https://impel.ai) bills itself as an Automotive AI Operating System: one vendor spanning website lead engagement, service scheduling, and customer lifecycle marketing, with deep integrations into the major OEM customer-journey platforms franchise groups already use. The company acquired Outsell in 2024 to add a marketing AI layer alongside its conversational and service tooling, which makes Impel one of the broadest single-vendor footprints in the dealership AI category.

**Best for:** Dealer groups looking to consolidate website chat, service AI, and lifecycle marketing under one vendor instead of stitching point tools together.

**Pricing:** Custom; quoted per dealer group. Public pricing isn't published, so plan on a sales conversation before you can scope a pilot.

**Pros:**
- Single vendor across website chat, service scheduling, marketing AI, and customer lifecycle in one contract
- Deep OEM integrations make it a natural fit for franchise groups already aligned to a manufacturer's customer-journey platform
- Well-known incumbent with a large install base, which simplifies vendor approval inside larger groups

**Cons:**
- Enterprise-leaning - smaller single-rooftop dealers may find the suite heavier than they need
- Broad scope means a heavier integration footprint and longer time to value than a drop-in website assistant
- Pricing isn't transparent, which makes it harder for a GM to scope an initial pilot without going through sales

**Verdict:** Pick Impel when you are running a multi-rooftop group consolidating vendors. Skip it if you are a single store looking for a fast on-site conversation win.

## 4. Matador AI

[Matador AI](https://matador.ai) is a conversational AI platform purpose-built for dealerships, automating sales and service conversations across phone, text, email, social DMs, and web chat. For a GM whose shoppers split their time between the website and SMS - and who has watched a salesperson get tied up texting one lead while three more sit unanswered in Facebook Messenger - the appeal is that the same AI handles all of those threads under one roof. Matador also ships a TCPA compliance engine and SMS broadcast tooling, which matters once outbound texting volume gets high enough to attract legal scrutiny. The vendor markets itself to 1,500+ dealerships and pitches a 24/7 AI plus human BDC model, so the product is built around the conversation lifecycle rather than just the website widget.

**Best for:** Dealers whose shoppers split their time between SMS, social DMs, and the website, and who need TCPA-safe texting alongside web chat.

**Pricing:** Custom; quoted per rooftop. Public pricing isn't published, so scoping requires a sales call before you can compare costs against other vendors.

**Pros:**
- True multi-channel coverage across phone, text, email, social DMs, and web chat in one tool, so handoffs between channels stay coherent
- TCPA compliance engine and SMS broadcast tooling reduce legal risk on outbound texting at volume
- Built around the full conversation lifecycle, including a 24/7 AI plus human BDC model for nights and weekends

**Cons:**
- Sales-conversation focused, with less depth on inventory-aware product discovery on the VDP itself
- Designed to plug into an existing CRM rather than replace one, so the CRM decision sits separate from the AI decision
- No public pricing means you can't ballpark cost against alternatives without a discovery call

**Verdict:** Pick Matador AI when SMS and social DMs are as important as the website for your shoppers and TCPA compliance on outbound texting is a real concern. Skip it if your bottleneck is on-site product discovery and inventory-aware answers on the VDP rather than cross-channel sales conversations.

## 5. DriveCentric

[DriveCentric](https://drivecentric.com) is an AI-first automotive CRM that bundles conversational engagement, autonomous AI agents, and multi-channel follow-up into a single platform. For a dealership GM, the pitch is consolidation: instead of bolting a website chat layer onto an existing CRM, the chat surface, lead routing, and follow-up cadences all live inside the same system. The Genius assistant handles inbound conversations, autonomous AI agents work leads across SMS, email, and the website, and the CRM holds the activity history in one place.

**Best for:** Dealers who are already planning a CRM replacement and want chat, follow-up automation, and AI agents fused into the same platform rather than wired together.

**Pricing:** Custom; priced by rooftop and units-sold count, with the Engagement Hub CRM as the core SKU and additional modules layered on.

**Pros:**
- Tight fusion between the chat layer, the CRM record, and autonomous follow-up agents, so a website conversation flows into a structured lead and a cadence without a handoff
- Built-in multi-channel follow-up (SMS, email, in-platform chat) reduces the number of tools your BDC has to log into
- Purpose-built for dealerships, with playbooks shaped around the automotive sales cycle rather than retrofitted from a generic CRM

**Cons:**
- Replacing an existing CRM is a substantially heavier project than dropping in a website chat widget, with data migration and team retraining attached
- Less depth on shopper-facing product Q&A grounded in your website's inventory pages, F&I disclosures, and policy content
- Custom pricing requires a sales conversation to scope, so you cannot model the bill from a public page

**Verdict:** Pick DriveCentric when a CRM swap is already on the table and you want chat plus AI agents inside one system; skip it if your current CRM is staying and you only need a sharper website conversation layer.

## Which dealership website assistant is right for your store?

Pick by the leak in your funnel today, not by which demo looked slickest. If shoppers land on the website and bounce before they get a clear answer to a product or financing question, Nobi is the sharpest fit: [AI site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search) and grounded shopper Q&A on the website, $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 messages included; $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message), so a pilot fits inside discretionary spend and skips procurement. Web chat only is the honest limit - phone and SMS live elsewhere.

If conversations are split between the website, phone, and SMS in roughly equal measure, Matador AI is built for that surface area and handles TCPA-bound texting natively; pick it when SMS volume is the real bottleneck.

If you run a multi-rooftop group and the goal is consolidating CLM, service scheduling, and marketing AI under one vendor, Impel is the candidate built for that lifecycle span; the Outsell acquisition added the marketing layer.

If a CRM replacement is genuinely on this year's roadmap, DriveCentric folds chat and autonomous AI agents into the CRM itself, priced by rooftop and units sold.

If you want only the on-site chat layer and nothing further, DealerAI is the most narrowly scoped pick in this list.
