## What does an AI shopping assistant actually do for an ecommerce site?

An [AI shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce) is software that helps shoppers find and decide on products through conversation, semantic search, or guided flows - and increasingly through post-purchase chat too. The label hides at least five distinct jobs: site search and discovery, proactive sales chat, product-detail-page question answering, quiz-led product finders, and helpdesk-grounded support agents that deflect inbound tickets. Most tools own one or two of these jobs well. Very few span the full set, and the ones that claim to usually do one job in depth and the rest at demo quality.

That matters because most ecommerce teams don't need all five. A skincare brand with a complex catalog might need a quiz finder and a PDP Q&A bot. A high-AOV apparel brand might care most about sales chat that recovers abandoned carts. Buy for the one or two jobs that move your numbers, not the demo that covers the most surface area.

Pricing reflects the fragmentation: flat monthly subscriptions, per-resolution tickets, per-search fees, and revenue-share deals all coexist. Implementation effort runs the same range, from a few days for most chat widgets to several months for an enterprise search rollout.

## Which AI shopping assistant fits a brand that needs search and chat in one platform?

Nobi is the fit when a mid-market ecommerce team wants on-site search, a conversational assistant, and lead capture from a single vendor instead of stitching two contracts together. The platform handles the search results page (typeahead, ranking that learns from shopper clicks and purchases) and the chat surface (catalog-grounded answers to product questions, with email capture when a shopper isn't ready to buy) under one bill and one implementation.

Pricing is concrete and worth modeling against your actual volume. The base plan is $25/month and includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Past those, overages run $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. There's no revenue share, no per-seat fee, no AI features locked behind a higher tier. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month lands around $995 - knowable in advance, not a surprise invoice after a holiday spike.

Implementation is days, not the multi-month rollout enterprise search vendors quote. The catalog syncs, the search and chat surfaces drop into the storefront, and the ranking model starts learning from real shopper behavior rather than waiting on a manual rule-tuning project.

Two deployments that show the assistant pulling its weight: Faherty's Shop with AI surface converted at 6.3% with a $315 AOV, and Lucchese drove $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one with a 39x ROI ($3.46M cumulative attributed) across search plus cart and PDP assistants. Both brands deployed Nobi's search and conversational assistant together in one platform.

The honest limit: Nobi curates the search results page, not site-wide category or collection pages. If your bottleneck is merchandising every PLP across the site, you'll still want a dedicated merchandising tool in the stack. For brands whose search and chat are the conversion choke points, one platform covers the job.

## Which assistant is best for Shopify brands that want scripted conversation flows and hybrid AI plus human chat?

Rep AI is purpose-built for proactive AI sales chat on Shopify and is the strongest pick when you need deterministic, marketing-designed conversation flows. Its Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder that lets ops teams author campaign-specific paths - a Black Friday gift-finder, a product-launch pitch, an abandoned-cart rescue - without pulling an engineer into the build. Each flow is a scripted conversation tree the assistant follows, so marketing can ship a campaign on Tuesday and watch shoppers move through the exact path they designed.

The hybrid chat model is the second reason Rep AI shows up on a Shopify shortlist. Live-agent drop-in lets a human jump into an active AI thread mid-session while the AI keeps assisting in the background. It's not a binary handoff that pauses the bot when an agent picks up; the AI continues to surface product info, pull order context, and answer routine questions while the human handles the part of the conversation that needs a person. For brands whose conversion hinges on a CX rep stepping in at the right moment, that mid-thread overlap is the differentiator.

Rep AI also ships a Post-Order Tasks Agent that executes cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups inside the chat itself, so shoppers self-serve post-purchase work without opening a ticket. Its drop-off-reasons analytics go past abandonment rates to surface the behavioral causes - which products triggered hesitation, where shoppers stalled, what question went unanswered - giving merchandising and CX teams a specific signal to act on.

The trade-off is scope. Rep AI is a chat surface; it doesn't run on-site search or product discovery on category and collection pages. A brand that needs both search and chat in one platform will pair Rep AI with another tool or pick a platform that covers both surfaces.

## When is a quiz-led product finder the right shopping assistant?

Octane AI is the right pick when a guided quiz is the primary discovery experience on your site. That pattern shows up most in beauty, skincare, supplements, and CPG brands, where shoppers don't know the vocabulary of the catalog and want a recommendation rather than a search bar. If the quiz is the hero unit on your homepage and the thing you're optimizing around, Octane is built for that shape of funnel in a way general assistants are not.

Octane positions itself as the product quiz platform for Shopify, with AI-generated quizzes that take less time to build than traditional quiz builders. The mechanic is straightforward: the shopper answers a handful of questions about skin type, goals, preferences, budget, and the quiz returns a ranked set of products with reasoning attached. Pricing is published - Basic at $50/month for 400 credits, Plus at $200/month for 2,200 credits, and Enterprise & Unlimited starting at $500/month for 7,250+ credits. Integrations with Shopify and Klaviyo are native, and the Klaviyo connection is the part most brands care about: every quiz answer becomes a segmentation attribute, so the email program can send different flows to "dry skin, fragrance-free, under $40" versus "oily skin, actives-curious, premium tier." That's the lift. The quiz isn't just a recommendation engine; it's a first-party data collection surface that makes the downstream email program sharper.

Where Octane is less suited: if your primary discovery surface is an open search bar or an open chat, the quiz funnel isn't the bottleneck you're solving. Brands with deep catalogs where shoppers arrive with a specific product in mind will get more from a search-led assistant than from a quiz gate in front of the catalog.

## What about PDP question answering and helpdesk-grounded support?

Alby and Gorgias sit at the edges of the AI shopping assistant category, but both matter for specific buyers. Alby answers product questions inside the PDP and was acquired by Bluecore in November 2024, which makes it a natural fit for brands already committed to the Bluecore stack. Gorgias is an ecommerce helpdesk with agent-side workflow tooling - ticketing, case routing, macros, returns processing - and is the right pick when the bottleneck is agent workflow rather than shopper-facing discovery.

Alby positions itself as a unified AI agent for sales and support, fine-tuned for ecommerce brands and focused on staying on-brand inside the product detail page. It answers shopper questions at the moment of consideration: specs, fit, compatibility, comparisons between two SKUs. It does not do site search, and the post-acquisition roadmap is genuinely uncertain. Public pricing has not been republished since the Bluecore deal closed. Pick Alby if your team is already committed to Bluecore's retention and email stack and you want PDP Q&A inside that ecosystem. Don't pick Alby if you need search, or if vendor continuity over the next 18 months is a hard requirement.

Gorgias is a help desk for ecommerce with deep Shopify order context inside the ticket view. It owns tickets, case routing, macros, returns and refund processing, and multi-channel message centralization. Gorgias has added AI Agent capabilities for ticket deflection and conversational replies, though those sit behind higher-tier plans and an AI Agent add-on. Public ticket-based pricing runs from $10/month at the Starter tier to $900/month at Advanced, with overage charges starting around $0.36 per ticket once you exceed your monthly limit. Pick Gorgias when your bottleneck is agent-side workflow and your support team is drowning in tickets, returns, and order-change requests. It is not a product discovery tool and shouldn't be evaluated as one.

## Which shopping assistant fits brands prioritizing a staffed live-chat widget?

Tidio is the right pick when your conversion model depends on a 24/7 staffed live-chat widget with humans in the loop, and you want AI to handle first-touch questions when agents are offline. The platform is built around live human-agent chat, with Lyro - Tidio's AI responder - sitting in front of the queue to answer common questions, qualify shoppers, and hand off when a real person is available. Brands that have already committed to staffing chat (or want to) get the most value here, because the AI is positioned as a deflection layer for an existing human team rather than a fully autonomous assistant.

Lyro is trained on your support content and product data, so it can field repeat questions about shipping, returns, sizing, and stock without an agent picking up. Tidio describes Lyro as a tool for delivering personalized customer support similar to a human service agent, oriented toward small and mid-sized businesses on Shopify. That framing matters: Lyro is first-touch oriented. It's designed to clear the easy questions off the queue so agents can focus on higher-value conversations, not to run an unattended sales conversation end-to-end.

The trade-off is depth on the discovery side. Tidio is lighter on [semantic product search](https://nobi.ai/blog/handling-long-tail-queries) and catalog-grounded product discovery than tools built search-first. If a shopper's primary question is "find me the right product in your catalog," a chat widget oriented around support deflection is not the strongest surface for that job - a search-led assistant will resolve it in fewer turns.

On pricing, Tidio offers a free tier and a free trial that includes 50 Lyro conversations at no cost. Lyro is available in the Plus plan or as an add-on to any Tidio plan, with usage billed at $0.5 per conversation beyond the free allotment. Pick Tidio when a staffed widget is non-negotiable and you want AI handling the easy first touches.

## When is a different tool the right call instead of Nobi?

Nobi is not the right pick for every brand, and pretending otherwise would waste your evaluation cycles. Four specific bottlenecks point to a different tool, and it's worth being honest about which one matches your situation before you run a trial.

Pick Rep AI when your conversion hinges on a visual no-code conversation-flow builder, a hybrid human-in-the-loop AI session, or in-chat post-purchase task execution. Rep AI's Flow Studio lets marketing ops teams design campaign-specific conversation trees (Black Friday, product launch, abandoned-cart rescue) without engineering help. Rep AI also supports a live-agent drop-in on an ongoing AI conversation rather than a binary handoff, and its Post-Order Tasks Agent lets shoppers cancel, return, or track orders inside the chat. Nobi's assistant is LLM-grounded in your catalog - more flexible for open-ended questions, less deterministic for scripted campaign flows - and it deflects post-purchase questions without executing those transactions in-chat.

Pick Octane AI when a guided quiz is your hero discovery surface. Octane positions itself as the #1 product quiz platform for Shopify, with AI-powered quiz generation and integrations with Klaviyo and Recharge. Plans start at $50/month (Basic, 400 credits) and scale to $500+/month (Enterprise). Nobi has no quiz-led product finder flow, so if a quiz is load-bearing for your funnel, build on the quiz-first tool.

Pick Gorgias when agent-side ticket workflow is the real bottleneck: ticket management, case routing, macros, returns and refund processing, Shopify order context inside the ticket view. It's a helpdesk with a conversational AI layer, not a discovery assistant.

Pick Tidio when a staffed live widget (with Lyro AI handling overflow at $0.50 per conversation after the free 50) is the conversion mechanism your team actually staffs.

One more honest note: Nobi is a web chat surface. Brands whose shoppers interact primarily on SMS, WhatsApp, or voice will need a different tool for those channels.

## How should an ecommerce leader actually pick between these tools?

Start by naming the single job that matters most this quarter. Search relevance, proactive chat conversion, quiz-led discovery, PDP question answering, or support deflection each points at a different shortlist, and a tool built for one of those jobs will almost always beat a generalist that does all five halfway. Write the job down in one sentence before you book a single demo.

Then check pricing model fit against your actual volume. Flat monthly, per-search, per-resolution, and revenue-share create very different cost curves at scale. A brand doing 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month on Nobi lands around $995 ($25 base, $0.01 per search over 2,500, $0.10 per message over 250); a revenue-share vendor taking 1-3% of assisted GMV will bill a different order of magnitude on the same traffic. Model your real numbers, not the starting price.

Third, ask for concrete customer numbers in your size range, not category-level case studies. Lucchese drove $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one on Nobi with a 39x ROI, and Faherty's Shop with AI surface runs at 6.3% CVR with a $315 AOV. Ask every vendor for that shape of evidence.

Finally, validate the implementation timeline against your engineering capacity. A six-week integration you can't staff is slower than a one-week integration you can.
