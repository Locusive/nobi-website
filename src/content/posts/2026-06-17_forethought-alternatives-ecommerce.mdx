## Why are ecommerce brands looking for Forethought alternatives?

[Forethought](https://forethought.ai) adds AI to an existing helpdesk: auto-resolving tickets, routing them, and helping agents close them faster inside your existing helpdesk. For ecommerce brands, that's useful for tickets that arrive. The problem is scope: it only works after a ticket is filed. Shoppers who can't get a straight answer on a return policy, hit a circular response on sizing, or see wrong inventory status often abandon without ever opening a ticket.

A few other reasons brands go looking: Forethought runs inside a helpdesk, not on your storefront, so there's no shopper-facing widget for on-site Q&A. It needs an existing helpdesk underneath. It integrates with 70+, including Zendesk, Salesforce, Intercom, and Gorgias, so it's an add-on, not a standalone tool. Pricing is quote-only with no self-serve option. Zendesk reportedly acquired Forethought in March 2026, which adds uncertainty for Salesforce and Kustomer customers.

That said, if you're on Zendesk or Salesforce and the bottleneck is how long tickets take to close, Forethought still fits.

## How did we evaluate these Forethought alternatives?

We evaluated four tools for teams whose bottleneck runs beyond the agent inbox: Nobi, Re:amaze, Intercom, and Kustomer. We scored each on where in the support funnel it operates, how well it connects to ecommerce workflows, whether pricing is public, and how long it takes to go live. We didn't treat these as head-to-head with Forethought across the board. Forethought's depth inside a helpdesk and Nobi's on-site Q&A answer different problems, and treating them as equivalent would mislead a buyer. Nobi is our own product; its weaknesses appear here alongside the others.

**Nobi** is ecommerce-native website search and support assistant that lives on your storefront, answering shopper questions before they become tickets. Pricing is public: $25/month base, with overages at $0.01 per search and $0.10 per conversational message. A quick theme drop-in gets it live in hours. The real gap: it doesn't manage the agent inbox or handle ticket routing once something escalates.

**Forethought** operates inside your existing helpdesk, auto-routing tickets and resolving them before an agent picks up. Its strength is what happens after a ticket arrives, not before. Pricing requires a sales call; deployment runs 30-90 days.

**Re:amaze** is a helpdesk with native Shopify and BigCommerce integrations. Per-seat pricing starts at $29/seat/month. An order lookup bot is available on Pro and Plus plans; AI Agent resolutions are metered at 5-20 per user per month, with $0.85 per additional resolution.

**Intercom** bundles an inbox, team seats, and its Fin AI Agent in one platform. Fin resolves conversations at $0.99 each, on top of seat costs starting at $29/seat/month annually. It covers chat, email, voice, and more, but Fin requires the full Intercom subscription.

**Kustomer** is a customer experience CRM. Its AI agents can take actions (order lookups, refunds) through Shopify and Stripe. Pricing is quote-only with no self-serve trial.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi lives on your storefront, not inside a helpdesk. It combines product search and shopper Q&A in the same widget, so a shopper asking about sizing, return windows, or materials gets an answer while they're still on the page. The answer comes from your own connected content: product pages, policy docs, PDFs, and help-center articles. The key difference is grounding: every answer comes from sources you've connected, with numbered citation pills showing exactly where the information came from. That audit trail matters for questions where accuracy carries weight, like return policy terms or warranty coverage. Connected sources update twice a day, so a policy change lands in customer answers within hours, not weeks.

**Best for:** Ecommerce brands that want to reduce ticket volume by answering product questions, return policy queries, and sizing questions directly on the storefront - no helpdesk subscription required.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included). $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**
- Query overrides let you pin exact merchant-approved text to high-stakes questions like warranty terms or return windows - no AI paraphrasing on the answers that carry the most legal weight
- Usage-based pricing starts at $25/month with no helpdesk subscription required

**Cons:**
- Not a ticket workflow platform; doesn't route or manage escalations once they're filed.
- No live agent drop-in on AI conversations.
- No shopper-facing in-chat post-purchase transactional execution.
- Website chat only - no email channel.
- Limited personalization today.

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when you want to answer shopper questions on-site before they become support tickets; skip it if you need AI built into an existing Zendesk or Salesforce helpdesk workflow.

## 2. Re:amaze

[Re:amaze](https://reamaze.com) is an ecommerce-native helpdesk that pulls email, live chat, social, and SMS into one inbox with Shopify order data already connected. When a shopper emails about a delayed order at the same time an Instagram DM arrives about a return, your team handles both from the same place - with order history visible right there, no tab-switching required. The AI layer, called Re:amaze Brain, drafts suggested replies for agents to review and send. On Pro and Plus plans, an Order Bot handles WISMO lookups automatically.

**Best for:** DTC and ecommerce brands that want email, live chat, social, and SMS managed from a single Shopify-connected inbox, with agents handling each conversation rather than relying on fully autonomous AI resolution.

**Pricing:** Per-agent monthly pricing: Basic $29/agent/month ($26.10 annual), Pro $49/month ($44.10 annual), Plus $69/month ($62.10 annual). Starter plan $59 flat/month for unlimited team members (500 conversations/month cap). AI resolutions are metered: 5/10/20 included per plan per month, $0.85 per resolution above the limit. Pricing is public on reamaze.com.

**Pros:**
- Shopify order data and shopper history appear inside every ticket - agents handle WISMO and post-purchase questions without switching to a separate order management tool
- The chat widget can be live on a Shopify store in a day - no complex integration work required to connect it to your order data

**Cons:**
- Support-only - no search or product discovery
- The AI drafts suggested replies for agents to review; it does not auto-resolve conversations autonomously the way Intercom Fin or Kustomer AI Agents can

**Verdict:** Pick Re:amaze when you want every support channel in one ecommerce-aware inbox with Shopify order data already connected; skip it if you need an AI that answers and resolves shopper questions without an agent drafting each reply.

## 3. Intercom

[Intercom](https://intercom.com)'s Fin AI Agent runs inside the same platform your team already uses for ticketing. It pulls answers from your native Help Center articles and Snippets, and also connects to external sources you point it at: Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, Zendesk, public URLs, and PDFs. If your team already handles customer conversations in Intercom, that's a genuine advantage: same login, same routing rules, same reports you already know, with AI layered into the workflow rather than sitting in a separate tool. The trade-off is platform commitment. Fin isn't sold standalone - you're buying (or already running) the Intercom subscription that comes with seats, a messenger, and a helpdesk inbox. And external sources like Notion and public URLs only refresh once a week, so a policy change can sit stale in Fin's answers for up to seven days.

**Best for:** Teams that already run Intercom as their ticketing inbox and want AI layered into the same platform rather than managing a separate tool on top.

**Pricing:** Seat-based plus usage, with a meaningful annual discount. Essential is $29/seat/month annual or $39 monthly; Advanced is $85 annual / $99 monthly; Expert is $132 annual / $139 monthly. Fin AI Agent billed at $0.99 per resolved conversation on top of seat costs. Advanced includes 20 free Lite Seats; Expert includes 50.

**Pros:**
- Handoff, routing, and escalation tooling are built into the helpdesk - the AI-to-human transition is native, not cross-tool.

**Cons:**
- Not ecommerce-specific - lots of unused SaaS-oriented features
- Expensive at scale; Fin AI Agent runs on top of seat-based Intercom subscription, not as a standalone product
- Fin runs inside Intercom's Messenger - using it as your AI customer-support layer requires deploying the full Intercom platform

**Verdict:** Pick Intercom Fin when Intercom is already your helpdesk and you want AI inside that same platform; skip it if you don't want to run the full messenger and helpdesk stack just to get the AI layer, or if your knowledge sources change frequently and a weekly external sync isn't fast enough.

## 4. Kustomer

[Kustomer](https://kustomer.com) is a full customer experience platform with two AI products built directly into it. AI Agents for Customers resolves shopper questions on their own (order lookups, refunds, post-purchase follow-ups) without routing to a human. AI Copilot sits next to your agents inside the same platform, surfacing context as they work. If you're already replacing your helpdesk, Kustomer's appeal is timing: you get both AI products built into the platform you're already migrating to. The reverse case is just as real - if your helpdesk is working and the real bottleneck is WISMO volume or on-site shopper questions before a ticket ever gets filed, Kustomer is [more change than the problem calls for](https://nobi.ai/blog/kustomer-alternatives-ecommerce).

**Best for:** Teams already planning to replace their helpdesk who want autonomous AI on the customer side and a Copilot for agents built into the new platform from the start.

**Pricing:** Enterprise per-agent pricing; custom quote.

**Pros:**
- Every customer interaction (chat, email, prior tickets) shows up in a single chronological view, which cuts the context-switching agents do when handling post-purchase follow-ups
- One vendor for the platform and the AI, so you're not managing two contracts or two roadmaps

**Cons:**
- Full CX platform; replacing an existing helpdesk is a multi-quarter migration
- Heavier-weight than a focused AI customer-support layer

**Verdict:** Pick Kustomer when you're already replacing your helpdesk and want autonomous AI built into the new platform; skip it if you just want an AI layer added to an existing workflow without a platform migration.

## Which Forethought alternative fits your ecommerce support stack?

The right tool depends on where your support problem lives in the funnel - before a ticket arrives, during agent handling, or at the platform level. Forethought and the tools compared here don't all solve the same problem, so picking by feature list instead of identifying where your bottleneck sits leads to misfit purchases. Map the gap first, then the tool.

Nobi answers questions during the shopping session, grounded in your connected catalog and policy content. A shopper asking about a return window or size chart gets an answer on the page - without ever filing a ticket. If your goal is reducing the volume that arrives in the first place, Nobi addresses it at the source.

Re:amaze connects email, live chat, social, and SMS with Shopify order data visible in every conversation. Your team handles the tickets that do arrive from one inbox, with order history there when they need it. The AI drafts suggested replies; a human still sends each one.

Intercom with Fin is the path of least resistance if your team already runs an Intercom inbox. The AI lives inside the platform you know, with no new tool to onboard. The trade-off is [the full Intercom subscription](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-intercom-alternatives-technical-support) plus a $0.99-per-resolved-conversation charge on top of what you already pay.

Kustomer makes sense when you're replacing your helpdesk anyway. AI Agents and a Copilot come built into the new platform from the start, so you're not managing an AI layer as a separate contract once the migration wraps.

Forethought remains a reasonable fit if you're staying on your existing helpdesk and the bottleneck is how long each ticket takes to process. It adds AI inside the platform you're keeping, without requiring your team to learn a new inbox.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does Forethought work with Shopify?**

Forethought layers onto your existing helpdesk - not directly onto Shopify. Those helpdesks can connect to Shopify through integrations, but Forethought has no shopper-facing widget for on-site Q&A. Re:amaze and Nobi are the ecommerce-native options here.

**Can AI handle WISMO questions without a human agent?**

Yes - where-is-my-order questions are among the highest-volume and most repetitive in ecommerce, which makes them a strong automation candidate. The AI needs access to live order data or connected shipping and policy content. Nobi answers WISMO-adjacent questions grounded in your shipping policies and FAQ pages; Re:amaze's Order Bot handles order lookups after a customer has already reached out through the helpdesk.

**What separates answering a question before a ticket arrives from resolving a ticket?**

Answering a shopper's question before they file a ticket means the issue never enters your helpdesk. Resolving a ticket means handling one that has already arrived. Nobi operates at the first stage; Forethought, Intercom Fin, Re:amaze, and Kustomer all operate primarily at the resolution stage.

**How quickly can I go live with these tools?**

Nobi can be configured in hours. Re:amaze's chat widget is typically live on Shopify in a day. Adding Fin to an existing Intercom account is faster than a full platform deploy from scratch. Kustomer is a full helpdesk migration measured in months.
