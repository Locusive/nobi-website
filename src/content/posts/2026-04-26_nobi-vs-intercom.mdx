## What is the difference between Nobi and Intercom for automated customer support?

Nobi and Intercom both reduce inbound support volume, but they intercept questions at different points. Nobi grounds its answers in your live business data and policies, so a customer asking a question on your website gets resolved on the page from the same data your site already exposes - no ticket, no agent. [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) is a customer messaging platform whose Fin AI agent layers on top of an existing helpdesk with tickets, macros, and live agents. The right pick depends on whether your support volume starts on your website or in your support inbox.

Nobi is built to prevent tickets at the source. The assistant lives on your website and answers customer questions against your live business data - pricing tiers, policies, availability, account status, the same source of truth your site already serves. Pricing is usage-based: $25/month base including 250 conversational messages, then $0.10 per additional message. No per-seat fees, no per-resolution charges. The trade-off is that Nobi is not a ticket workflow platform - once a question escalates beyond what live data and policy answers can resolve, you still need a helpdesk to manage the case.

Intercom is a horizontal support platform. Seats run $39 (Essential), $99 (Advanced), and $139 (Expert) per month, with Fin AI Agent billed at $0.99 per resolution on top. Pick Intercom when your bottleneck is the full ticket lifecycle - inbox management, agent workflows, multi-channel messaging - and AI-powered support is one layer inside that system rather than the primary job.

## How does Nobi compare to Intercom on pricing?

Nobi starts at $25 per month flat, including 250 conversational messages, with $0.10 per additional message. Intercom's Essential plan starts at $39 per seat per month, and the Fin AI agent costs $0.99 per resolved conversation on top of seats. On 1,000 conversations over the included quota: $100 on Nobi vs $990 on Intercom (1,000 × $0.10 vs 1,000 × $0.99) - roughly 10x, before seat costs are even counted. For a large support org already paying for seats, the marginal cost of adding Fin is the number that matters.

Intercom prices on two axes: seats and AI resolutions. Essential is $39 per seat, Advanced is $99, and Expert is $139, with Fin resolutions stacking on top at $0.99 each. A five-agent team on Essential starts around $195 per month before any AI usage, and 1,000 Fin-resolved conversations adds another $990. That model works when you already need the seats for a human support team and Fin runs on top of an established helpdesk. It works less well when most of your inbound is questions your live business data could answer - you're paying per seat for an inbox capability you may barely use.

Nobi prices only on what customers do. No seats, no per-resolution charges, no tiered AI gating - the same $25 base and the same overage rates whether you have one operator or ten. The honest trade-off: Nobi answers customer questions and reduces inbound volume, but it isn't a ticket workflow platform. If your team needs case routing, macros, or returns / refund processing, you'll still pair Nobi with a helpdesk. For a team whose cost driver is conversation volume rather than agent headcount, the math usually points to Nobi.

## How do AI customer support capabilities compare between Nobi and Intercom?

The mechanism difference is what each tool grounds its answers in. Nobi grounds in your live business data - pricing, policies, availability, account status - pulled directly from the same source of truth your website already serves. Intercom's Fin grounds in help-center articles and uploaded knowledge-base content - text written by a content team, indexed for search, refreshed manually. That choice cascades through where the assistant lives, how it answers specific questions, and how much knowledge-base maintenance you have to do before automated answers actually work.

Intercom's Fin is a strong general-purpose AI agent: it reads your help center, answers in conversational language, and reports resolution rates per conversation. Nobi's assistant is narrower and deeper - it only answers questions that can be resolved from your live business data and policies, and it grounds every answer there directly. Fin is the better generalist; Nobi is the better specialist for live-data questions. Neither replaces a live agent for genuinely complex cases.

Fin learns from whatever you point it at: help center articles, macros, internal docs, public URLs. That makes it strong on policy and how-to questions ("how do I update my billing address", "what's your warranty process", "how do I reset my password") because those answers live in the help center where Fin is trained. The handoff model is its other real strength. When Fin's confidence drops, it passes the conversation to a human Intercom agent inside the same thread, with full transcript context, across web, in-app, email, and SMS surfaces. The agent picks up where Fin left off. For a support team already running Intercom as its inbox, that hybrid path is hard to beat. Fin is weaker when the question depends on live data - current account state, real-time availability, individual order or session details - because that data isn't in the help center.

Nobi's assistant is grounded in your live business data directly, not in articles describing it. A customer asking about their current state, an availability check, or a policy decision gets an answer from the same data your site already exposes, not a paraphrase written six months ago. That's the lane Nobi owns: questions that resolve from live data and would otherwise become tickets. The honest limit: Nobi runs AI-first on the web chat surface only, with a binary escalation when the AI can't answer. There's no live-agent drop-in mid-thread, and no SMS, email, or in-app channel. Teams who want a human jumping into an ongoing AI session, or AI customer support on channels beyond web chat, will get more from Fin.

## When is Intercom the better choice over Nobi?

Intercom is the better pick when your support team already runs a full helpdesk and the volume problem lives in the inbox, not on your website. If you need ticketing, macros, SLAs, agent routing, an omnichannel inbox spanning email, SMS, and in-app messenger, and a hybrid mode where a human agent jumps into an ongoing AI conversation mid-thread without a hard handoff, that's the job Intercom is built for. Teams whose customers reach support through email or messenger more often than through the website will get more lift from Fin than from a website-only assistant.

[Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) is a conversation-first customer service platform with deep agent-side workflow tooling. Tickets, macros, queues, SLAs, productivity reporting, multibrand support on the Expert plan, and a unified inbox across channels all sit inside the same product. Fin layers AI resolutions on top of that workflow, billed at $0.99 per resolution on top of seat pricing that ranges from $39 per seat per month on Essential to $139 per seat per month on Expert. Pick Intercom when agent workflow is the bottleneck, when one tool needs to handle a wide mix of question types, or when you specifically want a live agent to take over an AI thread without breaking the session.

Nobi is the wrong tool for that profile. Nobi answers customer questions on your website and reduces inbound volume there, but it doesn't manage tickets, route cases to agents, run macros, or expose a hybrid live-agent-in-AI-conversation mode. It's also web chat only, with no SMS, WhatsApp, or voice channel. If your support volume sits in the inbox and your team measures itself on agent productivity inside a queue, Intercom solves that problem and Nobi doesn't pretend to.

## When is Nobi the better choice over Intercom?

Nobi is the right call when most of your inbound support volume traces back to questions a customer would have answered themselves with better information on your website. In that scenario you want the assistant on your site, grounded in live business data, not a messenger bubble trained on help-center articles. Nobi also tends to be cheaper for teams under roughly ten support agents, since seat-based plans plus per-resolution charges add up quickly against a $25/month base with $0.10 messages.

Nobi fits when the support volume is question-driven and live-data-driven. The assistant lives where customers actually have questions - on your website, in a chat surface that already knows your live data - so questions like account state, availability, or policy get answered in the moment instead of escalating to email. Conversational Q&A on a $25/month base that includes 250 messages, then $0.10 per additional message. No seats, so a lean two- or three-person team doesn't subsidize larger orgs sharing the same tier. The trade-off worth naming: Nobi answers questions but doesn't execute transactional changes (account modifications, refunds, cancellations) inside the chat. Customers either self-serve via the answer or hand off to email.

Intercom is built around an agent inbox: ticket workflows, multibrand routing, deep reporting, and Fin AI resolving general help-center questions so agents can triage what's left. It's the right call when the bottleneck is agent productivity and ticket volume across multiple support teams, not customer questions on your website. If you don't run a full helpdesk staffing model, the seat math and the help-center-shaped knowledge layer are sized for a different buyer.

If your support team needs an AI agent inside a full ticketing workflow with a multi-channel inbox, pick Intercom and turn on Fin. If you're an enterprise organization with complex routing and compliance requirements, Zendesk is the conventional choice despite the implementation overhead.

## Frequently asked questions

A few questions come up consistently when teams weigh Nobi against [Intercom](https://www.intercom.com) for automated customer support.

**Can Nobi replace my helpdesk?** No. Nobi answers customer questions before they hit the inbox, but it doesn't manage tickets, run macros, or route cases to agents. If you need ticketing, pair Nobi with a helpdesk like Zendesk.

**Can Intercom's Fin answer questions about my live business data?** Yes, if you feed it that content as knowledge sources. The caveat: Fin isn't grounded in live system data the way an assistant pulling from your source of truth is, so answers about current state can drift from what's actually true at the moment of the question.

**How is a "resolution" counted on Fin?** Roughly, a conversation Fin handles end-to-end without escalating to a human. The exact definition has shifted over time, so check Intercom's current docs before modeling your bill.

**Does Nobi support email, SMS, or WhatsApp?** Not today. Nobi is web chat only. Teams that need messaging channels should pair Nobi with another tool or pick Intercom.

**Can I run both?** Yes. Some teams put Nobi on the website for live-data questions and keep Intercom for post-conversation ticketing and agent workflows. The two cover different jobs and don't step on each other.
