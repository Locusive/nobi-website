## What should a head of ecommerce look for in an AI retail personalization platform?

The first job is behavioral reranking: results reorder in real time based on what each shopper clicks, views, and adds during a visit. [Constructor](https://constructor.io) and [Nosto](https://www.nosto.com) lead here; [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) offers it on the Expert tier. The second job is conversational and contextual personalization: page-aware suggestion pills, grounded shopper Q&A, and semantic search that reads descriptive queries. Nobi sits here. The third job is recommendation and segmentation: widget-based product picks and segment-targeted campaigns. Nosto and [Dynamic Yield](https://www.dynamicyield.com) are built around this work.

Two other things matter as much as features. Pricing model: revenue-share and quote-only enterprise contracts can swing your bill hard when GMV scales; transparent per-unit pricing holds steady. And implementation: from a few hours for Nobi to a few days for Klevu to multiple quarters for the enterprise platforms - match the rollout to the calendar pressure you're actually under.

## How did we evaluate these platforms?

We compared five platforms across four things that actually matter when a head of ecommerce is signing a contract: the type of personalization each one ships today, the pricing model, the time to go live, and an honest read on where each one fits.

Personalization type is the first cut because vendors use the same word for different work. Constructor and Nosto lead with behavioral reranking - results reorder in real time based on what a shopper clicks during the session, with Constructor using reinforcement learning on full clickstream data. Dynamic Yield sits in segment-based personalization and A/B testing across the site. Klevu offers behavioral personalization, but only on its Expert tier. Nobi's lane is conversational and contextual: page-aware suggestion pills and semantic search that reads descriptive queries.

Pricing model matters as much as price. Constructor runs on revenue-share, so the bill scales with GMV. Nosto and Dynamic Yield are quote-only enterprise contracts. Klevu publishes three tiers but no dollar figures; third-party reporting puts the range at roughly $499 to $1,598 per month. Nobi is the only one with public per-unit pricing: $25 a month base, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message.

Implementation runs from a few hours on Nobi to several weeks or months on the enterprise platforms, which usually need data-team support to stand up.

## 1. Nobi

[Nobi](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-shopping-assistants-for-ecommerce) is an AI shopping assistant and site search platform. The personalization layer runs as page-aware suggestion pills, grounded Q&A with inline citation pills, and semantic search that reads long descriptive queries instead of exact-title strings. The same semantic layer runs the search bar, the collection filters, and the product discovery experience, so a homepage visitor and a PDP visitor see different starter prompts based on the page they're actually on. This covers two CVR leaks: shoppers whose natural-language queries keyword search can't match, and shoppers asking sizing and policy questions the FAQ buries. Behavioral reranking on individual click history isn't shipped yet, so if that's the headline requirement, the picks later in this list fit closer.

**Best for:** Teams whose personalization need is conversational and page-aware - suggestion pills tied to the homepage or PDP, grounded Q&A on materials and policies, semantic search that reads long descriptive queries - rather than behavioral reranking of product grids.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages). $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**
- The same semantic layer powers the search bar, collection filters, and [product discovery](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-top-ecommerce-platforms-for-native-ai-search-and-product-discovery). [Kilte](/customers/kilte) runs Nobi across all three.
- Inline citation pills and a sources sidebar on every answer let shoppers verify any claim against the source content, which directly addresses the "confident lies" fear that comes up in almost every AI-shopping evaluation.
- Connected knowledge sources refresh twice a day, so a pricing or policy update lands in shopper answers within hours instead of sitting stale until the next manual re-index.
- Transparent per-unit pricing with a published $25/mo entry point and clear per-search and per-message overages. No revenue-share, no surprise peak-season bills.

**Cons:**
- Behavioral reranking isn't shipped yet. Personalization today is personalized placeholder text and starter prompts, not in-session reordering based on individual click or purchase history. If behavioral reranking is the headline requirement, Constructor or Nosto is the more direct match.
- Nobi curates the search results page, not category or collection pages. Brands that need site-wide merchandising personalization across the entire storefront will still need a dedicated tool for that work.

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when conversational discovery, page-aware suggestion pills, and grounded shopper Q&A are the personalization features you actually need, and transparent per-unit pricing matters. Skip it when behavioral reranking across search and browse is the headline requirement, or when you need merchandising automation across category pages.

## 2. Constructor

[Constructor](https://constructor.io) pairs semantic search with real-time session-signal personalization, so products reorder as a shopper clicks, views, or adds items during a visit. The same ranking model runs across search, browse, category pages, and recommendations, not just the search bar. For a head of ecommerce whose personalization work has to move across the full storefront - category curation, collection ordering, browse, and search together - that breadth is the reason to look here. In exchange, you commit to revenue-share pricing with no published list, weeks-to-months of implementation, and an internal data team to keep the ranker tuned after launch.

**Best for:** Large-volume retailers with an internal data team where personalization has to move across category, collection, browse, and search together, not just the search bar.

**Pricing:** Revenue-share with no published list price; costs scale with GMV.

**Pros:**
- Session-signal personalization reorders results in real time based on what each shopper clicks, views, and adds during a visit, so the ranker keeps adapting without a merchandiser intervening
- Personalization covers the full site - category, collection, browse, recommendations - so the work isn't trapped inside the search bar
- Built-in A/B testing infrastructure lets the team measure which personalization change actually moved CVR or revenue per visit

**Cons:**
- Revenue-share contracts mean a successful CVR campaign costs more as GMV grows, and bills can scale in surprising ways during peak season
- Implementation runs weeks to months and needs data-team hours to keep tuning the ranker after launch
- Smaller brands that don't generate enough click-and-purchase signals give the ranker too little to learn from - without a steady stream of behavioral data, the model doesn't improve after launch

**Verdict:** Pick Constructor when behavioral personalization across search, browse, and category pages is the headline requirement and the data team is there to feed it. Skip it when you want transparent per-unit pricing or just the personalization layer without the full discovery platform attached.

## 3. Nosto

[Nosto](https://nosto.com) is an ecommerce personalization platform built around onsite recommendation widgets, segment-based targeting, and email-triggered campaigns. The core engine is recommendations - product grids, related items, recently viewed, and cross-sell blocks - tuned to each visitor and segment. For a head of ecommerce whose CVR work lives in widget placements across the PDP, cart, and category pages, plus segment-targeted personalization rules that marketers can run without engineering, this is what Nosto covers. Search sits inside the platform as a newer module rather than the headline play, so teams whose biggest gap is search-side personalization will likely find purpose-built search vendors more mature.

**Best for:** Brands that want recommendation widgets and segment-based personalization as the headline play, with search and other modules added as supporting capabilities.

**Pricing:** Quote-only; scales by store size, sessions, and the module bundle (recommendations, segmentation, search).

**Pros:**
- Mature recommendation engine with widget placements across PDP, cart, category, and email that have been in production with mid-market and enterprise brands for years
- Segmentation tooling lets marketers target personalization rules by behavior, source, geography, or custom attributes without engineering involvement
- One contract covers recommendations, segmentation, content personalization, email triggers, and search, so the personalization stack stays under a single vendor

**Cons:**
- Quote-only pricing that runs above mid-market comfort, which makes budgeting hard before a sales cycle
- Search is a newer add-on rather than the primary focus, so teams whose biggest gap is search-side personalization may find the search module less mature than purpose-built search vendors

**Verdict:** Pick Nosto when recommendation widgets and segmentation are the personalization lanes you actually need and you want them under one mature platform. Skip it when search-side personalization or conversational discovery is the headline gap, or when quote-only pricing is a non-starter.

## 4. Dynamic Yield

[Dynamic Yield](https://dynamicyield.com) is an enterprise personalization and experimentation platform owned by Mastercard. The product wraps recommendations, behavioral targeting, and a heavy A/B testing layer into one stack, with experimentation sitting on equal footing with the personalization engine itself. For a head of ecommerce whose program lives or dies on running real tests against personalization variants - not just shipping a recommendation widget and hoping it lifts AOV - that experimentation depth is the reason to look here. The trade is enterprise-grade pricing, multi-quarter implementation, and a stack that's lighter on conversational discovery than on segmentation and testing.

**Best for:** Enterprise teams that want experimentation tooling sitting next to the personalization layer, with the budget and headcount to run a real testing program against it.

**Pricing:** Quote-only enterprise contracts; multi-module bundles across recommendations, A/B testing, and behavioral targeting.

**Pros:**
- Deep experimentation tooling lets product teams A/B test personalization variants, content blocks, and recommendation algorithms with real statistical rigor
- Behavioral targeting and recommendation modules backed by a mature platform with enterprise references across retail, travel, and financial services
- Mastercard ownership brings the security, compliance, and procurement posture that some enterprise buyers explicitly require

**Cons:**
- Enterprise pricing and implementation overhead that puts it out of reach for most ecommerce teams, with multi-quarter rollouts standard
- Heavy on experimentation tooling and lighter on conversational discovery - there's no native shopping assistant or conversational search inside the platform

**Verdict:** Pick Dynamic Yield when enterprise-grade experimentation alongside personalization is the actual job and the team has the budget and headcount to run it. Skip it when conversational discovery is the gap or when you need a packaged install measured in days.

## 5. Klevu

[Klevu](https://klevu.com) is AI-powered Shopify search where personalization sits as a tier-gated feature rather than the headline play. The AI matching engine reads your catalog and figures out what shoppers actually mean, so a query like "wide-leg cropped trouser in navy" surfaces the right product even if your title says "cropped wide pant." That part runs on every tier. Behavioral personalization - reordering results based on what each shopper clicks, views, and adds during a visit - only ships in the Expert tier. For a head of ecommerce whose biggest miss reason is conversational query mismatch, with personalization as a secondary need you can fund by reaching for the top contract, Klevu fits.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose biggest miss reason is conversational query mismatch, with personalization as a secondary requirement they can budget for the Expert tier to include.

**Pricing:** Quote-only across three tiers (Essential, Advanced, Expert), priced by domain count, sessions, and SKU volume. Third-party reporting puts the range around $499 to $1,598 per month. Contact Athos Commerce for current rates.

**Pros:**
- AI matching catches long, descriptive queries and synonym pairs before they resolve to empty pages, so your team isn't writing synonym lists for every common phrasing
- The Smart Merchandising dashboard handles pinning, boosting, and category-page fallbacks without an engineering ticket
- "Did you mean" suggestions handle most typos and misspellings automatically
- Packaged Shopify install gets the team live in a few days, not months

**Cons:**
- Behavioral personalization is gated to the Expert tier; Essential and Advanced plans do not include it, so the headline personalization feature lives behind the highest contract
- Klevu is now a division of Athos Commerce alongside Searchspring and Intelligent Reach

**Verdict:** Pick Klevu when conversational query mismatch is your main miss reason on Shopify and the Expert-tier budget gets you the personalization layer you need. Skip it when personalization is the headline job and you don't want it gated to the top tier.

## How should a head of ecommerce pick between these AI personalization platforms?

Start with the personalization lane that matches the actual gap, then filter by pricing model and how fast you need to be live.

If behavioral reranking across search and browse is the headline job - results reordering in real time based on what each shopper clicks, views, and adds - [Constructor](https://constructor.io) is the most direct fit. The whole platform is built around that work, with reinforcement learning on full clickstream data feeding one ranker across search, browse, and category pages. [Klevu](https://klevu.com) is the Shopify-native alternative if you are already committed to that ecosystem, with the catch that behavioral personalization sits in the Expert tier only.

If conversational discovery, [page-aware suggestion pills](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-best-ai-assistants-for-pdps), and grounded shopper Q&A are the actual gap, Nobi is the closest match on this list and the only one that pairs that work with transparent per-unit pricing. Behavioral reranking on individual click history is not shipped yet, so that piece has to be a secondary requirement.

If onsite recommendation widgets and segment-based targeting are the primary play, [Nosto](https://nosto.com) is built around exactly that lane. If experimentation tooling matters as much as the personalization engine itself, [Dynamic Yield](https://dynamicyield.com) is the only platform here built around that combination.

Two downstream filters narrow the list further. Pricing model: revenue-share (Constructor) compounds with GMV, quote-only enterprise contracts (Nosto, Dynamic Yield, Klevu) take a sales cycle to size, and transparent per-unit pricing (Nobi) holds steady as traffic scales. Implementation calendar: packaged installs measured in hours for Nobi or a few days for Klevu, versus multi-quarter enterprise rollouts (Constructor, Nosto, Dynamic Yield).
