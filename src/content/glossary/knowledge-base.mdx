## What is Knowledge base?

A knowledge base centralizes information that would otherwise be scattered across emails, PDFs, or the heads of individual employees. It can be as simple as a folder of help articles or as complex as a tagged, versioned content library synced across multiple channels. Search tools, AI assistants, and customer service platforms connect to it to retrieve relevant answers on demand. The goal is consistency - every channel gives the same correct answer because they all read from the same source.

## How does knowledge base work?

- Content is authored and organized into articles, categories, or structured data records
- A search or AI layer indexes that content so it can match incoming questions to the right entries
- When a customer asks a question, the system retrieves the most relevant content and surfaces it as an answer or a link
- Operators update articles over time; the connected tools automatically reflect the latest version

## Why does it matter?

Accurate, up-to-date information reduces support volume because customers can self-serve common questions about shipping, returns, or vehicle specs without waiting for a human. For ecommerce operators, a well-maintained knowledge base also improves site search quality and decreases returns caused by misunderstandings about a product. For dealerships, it ensures that every digital touchpoint - chat, search, email - gives buyers consistent answers about inventory, financing, and service.

## Frequently asked questions

**What is the difference between a knowledge base and a FAQ page?**
A FAQ page is a single static page listing common questions and answers. A knowledge base is a full content system - multiple articles, categories, and search - that can power many channels at once, including site search, AI assistants, and help centers.

**How do AI assistants use a knowledge base?**
The AI indexes or retrieves content from the knowledge base and uses it to ground its answers. Instead of generating a response from general training data, it pulls the specific policies, product details, or procedures the business has written, which keeps answers accurate and on-brand.

**How often should a knowledge base be updated?**
Any time a policy, product detail, or process changes, the relevant articles should be updated before the change goes live. Stale content is the most common cause of incorrect answers from support tools and AI assistants.
