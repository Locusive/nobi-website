## What is a product identification tool?

A product identification tool helps a shopper land on the right SKU - through keyword search, a conversational question, an image, or an answer drawn from product copy and policies. In ecommerce, the job is narrowing thousands of SKUs down to the one that fits this shopper's question, then giving them enough confidence to buy. Where the question gets asked matters too: a search bar, a product page chat, an email reply, or a directory listing on the open web all call for different tools.

Nobi sits on the storefront: site search, [conversational product discovery](https://nobi.ai/blog/ecommerce-product-discovery-beyond-filters), and automatic shopper Q&A in one platform, from $25/month base. Strong when shoppers ask their question on your site; weaker when they reach you through SMS, WhatsApp, or voice.

[Yext](https://www.yext.com) builds a knowledge graph that organizes brand data for AI engines and open-web search results. Strong when shoppers find your brand off-site through directory listings and AI overviews; weaker as a storefront-side product finder.

[Intercom](https://www.intercom.com)'s Fin runs inside Intercom's Messenger on top of a helpdesk subscription, answering questions from connected articles and uploaded knowledge bases. Strong when product questions arrive through a support inbox; weaker as on-site search.

[Ada](https://www.ada.cx) deploys autonomous agents across web, app, email, and messaging to resolve requests end-to-end. Strong when one tool needs to cover several channels at once; weaker as ecommerce-specific discovery tooling.

## 2. Yext

[Yext](https://yext.com) is a knowledge graph and answers platform that organizes a brand's facts - locations, hours, product details, policies - into a single source of truth, then publishes them across search engines, voice assistants, and directory listings. For a head of ecommerce running a multi-location brand or a content-heavy site with store pages and location-specific catalogs, that means the same answer to "do you carry this in size 10 near me?" lands in Google, Maps, voice search, and your own pages without being maintained in five different places.

**Best for:** Multi-location brands and content-heavy sites with directories or location-specific catalogs that need consistent answers across the open web and owned surfaces.

**Pricing:** Not publicly listed; Yext quotes by directory size, location count, and channel mix, so finance can't model the bill before a sales conversation.

**Pros:**
- Knowledge graph publishes the same answer across SERPs, voice assistants, and on-site search - hard to assemble from separate point tools
- Strong fit for brands with hundreds of physical locations or franchise SKUs where one fact needs to land everywhere at once
- Mature integrations with major search engines and listing platforms

**Cons:**
- Multi-location and answers-focused, not purpose-built for product discovery on a single brand site
- No public entry pricing means a procurement cycle and a sales conversation before finance can model spend

**Verdict:** Pick Yext when consistent answers across many third-party surfaces - Maps, voice, syndicated directories - are the job; skip it if shoppers only need to identify products on your own site.

## 3. Intercom

[Intercom](https://intercom.com) is a customer messaging platform with the Fin AI Agent layered on top of an Intercom helpdesk. Fin sits inside the Intercom Messenger and answers questions from native Help Center articles plus synced external sources - public URLs (up to 10 sources, 3,000 pages each), PDFs, DOCX uploads, and direct syncs from Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, and Zendesk. For a head of ecommerce already running Intercom as the support backbone, Fin is the AI layer that handles product questions on the same surface where tickets and live-agent conversations already happen, with handoff to a human native rather than bolted on.

**Best for:** Teams who already run Intercom for ticketing and want product Q&A handled inside the same Messenger and inbox.

**Pricing:** Essential $29/seat/month annual or $39 monthly; Advanced $85 annual / $99 monthly; Expert $132 annual / $139 monthly. Fin AI Agent is $0.99 per resolution on top of seats. A 10-seat Advanced team running ~1,000 Fin resolutions a month lands around $1,980 ($990 seats monthly + $990 Fin), or about $1,840 on annual billing.

**Pros:**
- Fin pulls from native Help Center articles plus synced external sources (public URLs up to 10 sources / 3,000 pages each, PDFs, DOCX, and direct syncs from Notion, Confluence, Guru, Salesforce, and Zendesk)
- The AI sits inside the same Messenger and inbox the support team already lives in, so handoff to a human is native rather than bolted on
- Mature workflow tooling for tickets, automation, multibrand support, and reporting alongside the AI layer

**Cons:**
- External knowledge sources refresh weekly; only native Help Center articles update instantly, so a price or policy change on a synced URL can take days to reach answers
- Not ecommerce-specific - many SaaS-oriented support features go unused on a product-identification use case
- Fin only runs inside Intercom's Messenger, so adopting it as the AI Q&A layer means buying the full Intercom platform underneath

**Verdict:** Pick Intercom when you already run it as your helpdesk and want product Q&A on the same surface; skip if you don't need a full ticketing platform underneath the AI.

For a head of ecommerce whose shoppers don't all land on the storefront before they ask a question - some reply to an order-confirmation email, some DM on Instagram, some text for shipping updates - Ada puts one AI layer over all of those surfaces instead of stitching together a separate tool per channel.

**Best for:** Brands whose shoppers reach them across web, mobile app, email, SMS, and messaging channels and want one AI layer covering all of them.

**Pricing:** Quote-only, scaled by resolution volume and channel mix. Expect an enterprise procurement cycle - Ada doesn't publish public entry pricing.

**Pros:**
- Cross-channel autonomous resolution across web, app, email, SMS, and messaging out of the box
- Reporting on automated resolution rate is broken out by channel, so you can see which surface the AI is actually carrying
- Sits on the front line of support without locking you to a specific underlying helpdesk

**Cons:**
- Enterprise-leaning rollout with data integration and tuning measured in quarters, not days
- Less category-specific tooling than products purpose-built for on-site discovery and product Q&A
- No public entry pricing, so finance can't model the bill before a sales conversation

**Verdict:** Pick Ada when shoppers identify and ask about products across many channels and you want a single autonomous AI layer covering all of them; skip if on-site Q&A is the only job and a quarter-long rollout is more than the problem warrants.

## How should a head of ecommerce pick between these tools?

Match the tool to where the identification question actually gets asked. If shoppers ask on your site - in the search bar, on a product detail page, or in a chat widget - Nobi's combination of search, conversational Q&A, and citation-backed answers is the most direct fit. If your brand spans many physical locations or syndicated directories and answers need to land on Maps, voice, and the open-web SERP, Yext's knowledge graph is the right pick. If you already run Intercom as your helpdesk, Fin keeps product Q&A inside the same Messenger your team manages today. And if shoppers reach you across email, SMS, app, and messaging at once, Ada is built to put one autonomous AI layer over all of those channels. Rollout time and pricing transparency are the second filter, not the first.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do these tools support image-based product identification?** Coverage varies. Visual search - a shopper uploads or snaps a photo and the tool returns matching SKUs - is a separate capability from text search and conversational Q&A. Nobi focuses on keyword search, conversational product Q&A, and citation-backed answers from your connected sources; image-based identification isn't a Nobi feature today. If a shopper-uploaded photo is the primary entry point for product identification on your site, you'll want a tool whose product detail pages explicitly list visual search.

**What's the difference between site search and conversational product Q&A?** [Site search](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-vs-traditional-site-search) returns a ranked list of products to a keyword query ("running shoes size 10"). Conversational Q&A answers a full question in prose ("which of these is best for flat feet?") and grounds the answer in your catalog and policies. Most modern shoppers do both on the same site, which is why Nobi runs them on one platform.

**How do AI customer support tools avoid hallucinating product details?** By grounding answers in sources you connect and showing the receipts. Nobi pulls from web pages, FAQ and help-center articles, and PDFs you point at, refreshes those sources twice a day, and renders inline numbered citation pills on every answer - hover shows the source document, date, and exact excerpt.

**Do you need a helpdesk to add AI product Q&A to your site?** No. Nobi runs standalone on your storefront. Intercom Fin runs inside the Intercom Messenger on top of an Intercom subscription - so adopting Fin means buying the helpdesk underneath.
