## What is Octane AI, and when does the quiz model stop being enough?

[Octane AI](https://www.octaneai.com) is a quiz and AI assistant platform for Shopify, and the quiz is the product. A shopper answers five to ten questions about skin type, budget, or routine goals, and the platform maps those answers to product attributes to surface a specific SKU. For beauty brands running segmented email campaigns, that quiz-to-inbox connection is genuinely useful: completed quiz data flows into Klaviyo as segment attributes, so a shopper who flags sensitive skin lands in the right lifecycle sequence automatically. The $50/month Basic plan covers 400 quiz completions; Plus runs $200/month for 2,200, and Enterprise starts at $500/month.

The quiz works well when shoppers need a guided path. It runs out of road when they don't. A shopper who types "does this serum have fragrance?" into a chat input, arrives without a clear starting point, or wants to track an order isn't going to complete a decision tree - there's no branch for that. Open-ended ingredient questions, sizing edge cases, WISMO, and browsing-without-intent don't fit a scripted flow, and Octane AI isn't designed for them.

None of the alternatives in this article are quiz builders. If a guided question tree is still the right discovery path for your catalog, Octane AI remains the dedicated tool for that. But if your shoppers are asking questions the quiz can't route, the tools below - including Nobi, a conversational website assistant that combines product search, automated shopper Q&A, and first-party customer data capture in one product - cover what comes next.

## 2. Rep AI

[Rep AI](https://hellorep.ai) is a proactive-chat tool built for Shopify, and the most mature option for beauty marketing teams who need campaign-specific conversation paths without filing engineering tickets. Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder: non-engineers can design a deterministic flow for a product launch, a Black Friday promo, or an abandoned-cart rescue, and fire it based on shopper behavior. That matters for beauty brands running frequent drops and seasonal campaigns, where each launch needs its own conversation logic and waiting on dev capacity kills timing. Rep AI also handles post-purchase tasks inside the chat - a shopper can initiate a return, check tracking, or cancel an order without leaving the widget. Where it doesn't reach is beyond the chat itself: site search, browse-page ranking, and pre-chat product discovery stay unchanged.

**Best for:** Ecommerce marketing ops and CX teams who want to script campaign-specific conversation flows without engineering tickets, or who need a human specialist to drop into an active AI session mid-thread for higher-stakes questions.

**Pricing:** Public interactive calculator at hellorep.ai/pricing. Entry tier is "Pay As You Grow" at $12 per 1,000 visitors (unlimited sessions, 500-product catalog cap). Pro tiers priced by monthly sessions across three plans (AI Sales Agent, AI Support Agent, AI Concierge bundle). At 125K sessions, Pro tiers: Sales $616, Support $280, Bundle $740 per month. Enterprise custom above ~500K sessions. 30-day free trial.

**Pros:**
- Live-agent drop-in is a true hybrid: a human specialist can join an AI session while it's still running, rather than a binary handoff that pauses the conversation. This matters when a sensitive-skin or ingredient question escalates mid-thread and the AI alone isn't enough.
- Post-Order Tasks Agent lets shoppers self-serve cancellations, returns, and WISMO lookups inside the chat widget without routing to a helpdesk.
- Drop-off-reason analytics show why a shopper abandoned a session, not just that they did - a more actionable signal than standard CVR dashboards.

**Cons:**
- Chat widget only; no site search or catalog ranking outside the widget
- Limited personalization depth

**Verdict:** Pick Rep AI when scripted campaign flows, an AI-plus-human hybrid chat mode, or in-chat post-purchase task execution is the conversion lever you most need; skip it when your bottleneck is site search quality or open-ended pre-purchase Q&A rather than the chat conversation itself.

## 3. Gorgias

[Gorgias](https://gorgias.com) is an ecommerce helpdesk built around agent-side workflow: ticket routing, macros, returns processing, and refund handling, with Shopify order context sitting inside every conversation. When your inbox is flooded with WISMO requests, exchange asks, and return-over-refund situations, Gorgias is more purpose-built for that job than any quiz or chat-first tool. An agent can cancel, refund, or modify an order without switching tabs, and email, live chat, and social all land in the same ecommerce-aware inbox - useful for beauty brands where post-purchase questions arrive across every channel. It doesn't help a shopper find the right moisturizer or shade before they buy. Its scope starts after the purchase decision.

**Best for:** DTC and ecommerce teams on Shopify whose main bottleneck is post-purchase ticket volume - WISMO, returns, exchanges - and who want email, chat, and social support in one ecommerce-aware inbox.

**Pricing:** Tiered by ticket volume. Base starts low; AI add-on extra.

**Pros:**
- Mature automation: macros, routing rules, SLA tracking, and multi-brand tenant support for teams running multiple labels off one stack.

**Cons:**
- Helpdesk-first - requires committing to a ticketing system
- AI features available on higher tiers only
- No product discovery or search capability

**Verdict:** Pick Gorgias when post-purchase ticket volume - WISMO, returns, exchanges - is the core problem you're solving; skip it when you need a pre-purchase AI assistant driving product discovery and CVR.

## 4. Tidio

[Tidio](https://tidio.com) is a live-chat platform built for Shopify merchants, with Lyro AI layered on top to handle first-touch shopper questions when no agent is available. The product is built around a staffed team. The agent inbox and Shopify order context in the ticket view are designed for operators who live there all day. Lyro fills the gaps: after-hours coverage, standard WISMO queries, and first-touch questions that don't need a specialist. For a marketing manager running a lean support team, the structure is clear - AI backs humans up rather than replacing them. The free tier makes it easy to get the chat widget live and validate the setup before committing budget to Lyro's AI add-on.

**Best for:** Ecommerce brands whose priority is a staffed 24/7 chat widget with humans owning complex product and policy questions, and AI covering after-hours and standard first-touch volume.

**Pricing:** Free tier available for piloting the chat widget. Paid plans start at $24.17/mo (Starter, 100 conversations) and $49.17/mo (Growth, 250 conversations). Lyro AI is a separate add-on from $32.50/mo for 50 AI conversations. Billed annually; monthly pricing is slightly higher.

**Pros:**
- Strong Shopify integration with a fast install; teams can be live on product pages in a day without an engineering ticket.
- Lyro handles FAQ-style questions on shipping, returns, and standard product queries, reducing straightforward ticket volume without requiring a specialist on the line.

**Cons:**
- Lighter on product discovery and semantic search
- Lyro AI responder is first-touch oriented, not a deep conversational agent

**Verdict:** Pick Tidio when the job is a staffed 24/7 chat widget with AI covering after-hours and standard first-touch volume. Skip it if AI-led product discovery or catalog-grounded pre-purchase Q&A is the primary conversion lever you need to move.

## Frequently asked questions

Matching the tool to the use case leaves a few practical questions open. These are the ones ecommerce marketing teams ask most when evaluating Octane AI alternatives.

### Do any of these tools replace Octane AI's quiz builder?

None of them do. Octane AI's quiz maps shopper answers to SKUs and pushes segment attributes into Klaviyo - a structure none of the tools in this list replicate. Rep AI, Gorgias, Tidio, and Nobi each cover what quiz routing can't handle: open-ended Q&A, post-purchase support, and staffed chat. If a guided question tree is the right discovery path for your catalog, Octane AI is still the tool for that.

### Which tool is the cheapest to start?

Gorgias is tiered by ticket volume; check their pricing page for current rates. Nobi starts at $25/month (2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included), with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Rep AI's entry tier is $12 per 1,000 visitors. Tidio has a free plan, but Lyro AI conversations are capped at 50 total on the free tier; ongoing AI use requires a paid Lyro add-on.

### Can Gorgias answer pre-purchase product questions?

Gorgias is built for the post-purchase side: tickets, returns, refunds, and order changes inside an agent inbox with Shopify order context. It does not include an on-site assistant for ingredient questions or sizing queries before a shopper buys. For pre-purchase catalog Q&A, Nobi and Tidio's Lyro both cover that ground.

### Does Tidio work on platforms other than Shopify?

Tidio's deepest native integration is with Shopify. Teams on BigCommerce, Magento, WooCommerce, or a custom storefront should confirm platform support directly with Tidio before committing to a paid plan.
