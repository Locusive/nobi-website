## How did we evaluate Tidio AI and its alternatives?

We compared each tool on the specific job it does best: staffed chat coverage, conversational product Q&A, lead re-engagement, or SMS and reviews. We also weighed pricing transparency and how fast a marketing team can get it live without an engineering ticket. Nobi is one of the tools evaluated here, alongside Tidio, Conversica, and Podium.

Tidio's job is staffing a live chat widget. Lyro AI picks up first-touch questions when no agent is online, then hands the rest to a human. Its strength is coverage: a small team can keep the widget answering around the clock without hiring more agents. The weakness is depth. Lyro is built for triage, not for reading your full catalog and site content, so detailed questions still need a live agent. Pricing is public: a free tier for piloting, then $24.17/month (Starter) or $49.17/month (Growth), with Lyro AI priced separately from $32.50/month. A marketing team can have the widget live the same day, no engineering ticket required.

Conversica solves a different problem: chasing a lead through repeated follow-up until it responds or opts out. That makes it strong for re-engaging leads who went quiet, not for answering an active visitor's question in the moment. Pricing isn't public. Conversica quotes per-conversation enterprise deals, and buyers should expect a multi-week sales process before signing, which rules it out for a team that wants something running this week.

Podium fits businesses juggling reviews, SMS, and payments across multiple locations. It's strong at getting review requests out and following up by text, weaker as a source of grounded answers to specific questions since it isn't built to read your site content. Pricing is also quote-only, per location, with add-ons layered on top, so a marketing team can't model the real cost until they talk to sales.

Nobi answers customer questions directly from your connected content, with a citation back to the source document behind every answer. That's a different job than staffing a chat queue or chasing a lead by text: it's built to carry product and policy questions on its own, day and night. Pricing is public and usage-based, unlike Conversica's and Podium's quote-only enterprise deals, so a marketing team can model the cost before ever talking to sales. Installation is a small site-side change, live in hours rather than weeks, which matters if getting something running without an engineering queue is the point.

## 2. Tidio

[Tidio](https://tidio.com) is a live-chat platform built for Shopify sites, with Lyro AI layered on top to handle first-touch customer questions when no agent is available. The product centers on a staffed inbox: agents own the conversation, and Lyro steps in only when nobody's on shift. For a marketing manager sizing this up, the pitch is less "AI-first" and more "staffed chat with an AI backup." Lyro handles straightforward questions on shipping, returns, and product details, then hands off anything more complex to a person. That setup fits a team that wants a chat widget staffed around the clock without adding headcount, and where the AI's job is to catch overflow, not lead the conversation.

**Best for:** Businesses whose priority is a staffed 24/7 chat widget, with people owning complex product and policy questions and AI covering after-hours and routine first-touch volume.

**Pricing:** Free tier available for piloting the chat widget. Paid plans start at $24.17/mo (Starter, 100 conversations) and $49.17/mo (Growth, 250 conversations). Lyro AI is a separate add-on from $32.50/mo for 50 AI conversations. Billed annually; monthly pricing is slightly higher.

**Pros:**
- Strong Shopify integration with a fast install. Teams can be live on product pages in a day without an engineering ticket.

**Cons:**
- Lighter on product discovery and semantic search.
- Lyro AI responder is first-touch oriented, not a deep conversational agent.

**Verdict:** Pick Tidio when the job is a staffed 24/7 chat widget with AI covering after-hours and standard first-touch volume. Skip it if AI-led product discovery or catalog-grounded pre-purchase Q&A is the primary lever you need to move. If you're skipping it, [these Tidio chat alternatives](https://nobi.ai/blog/tidio-chat-alternatives) break down where Nobi and other options fit instead.

## 3. Conversica

[Conversica](https://conversica.com) builds AI agents that chase inbound leads by email and text until someone replies. It's a different job than answering questions on your own site: Conversica doesn't sit on a page waiting for a visitor, it goes after a lead who already asked something and then went quiet. For a marketing manager, that fits a specific gap. If your leads come in through a form or a demo request and then stall for days before anyone follows up, Conversica keeps working the lead in the background. If the problem is visitors leaving your site unanswered in the moment, that's a different tool's job.

**Best for:** Teams with high-intent inbound leads that go cold without follow-up - long sales-cycle products where a buyer asks a question and then disappears for days.

**Pricing:** Quote-only - per-conversation enterprise pricing model with no public starter tier published on conversica.com. Expect a multi-week sales cycle before signing.

**Pros:**
- Keeps following up automatically until the lead replies or opts out. Useful for any high-consideration purchase where a single outreach isn't enough.
- Email-first foundation with SMS now layered in, so teams can reach warm leads on the channel they actually reply on.

**Cons:**
- Email-first; later added SMS but channel breadth still narrower than newer multi-channel entrants.
- Enterprise sales cycle and per-seat pricing; expensive for small-location independent teams.
- Originally automotive-focused; now multi-vertical, so automotive-specific feature depth is shared with other verticals.

**Verdict:** Pick Conversica when you have qualified leads going cold and need a persistent agent that keeps following up; skip it if you need on-site product Q&A rather than post-visit lead re-engagement.

## 4. Podium

[Podium](https://podium.com) is a multi-channel customer messaging platform built around SMS, web chat, reviews, and payments. It got its start with local-services and SMB retail. Companies running multiple locations or an active texting program use it as their central inbox. It combines review collection, two-way SMS, and payment links in one place. For a marketing manager, the fit is narrow but real: if texting is how your customers actually reach you, and reviews are a real lever for winning new business, Podium ties both into one place across every location. That's a different job than answering detailed product or policy questions on your site. Podium is built for the messaging and reviews motion, not for reading your content and answering questions from it.

**Best for:** Multi-location retail and local-services teams whose customers prefer texting, and where reviews actively drive conversion.

**Pricing:** Quote-only - per-location SMB pricing with no published starter tier on podium.com/pricing. Modular add-ons layer on top (AI Employee, Bulk Messages credits, 10DLC fees). The pricing page directs prospects to "talk to our sales team for details".

**Pros:**
- Native SMS conversations plus reviews and payments in one tool. Useful when customers prefer texting over web chat.
- Strong reviews workflow that captures customer feedback, which compounds in any category where social proof drives conversion.
- Web chat that hands off to SMS, so the conversation continues after the visitor leaves the site.

**Cons:**
- Multi-vertical SMB tooling; not automotive-specific (no DMS integration, no automotive lifecycle workflows).
- Strongest in reviews, texting, and payments. Lighter on site-specific BDC or sales-floor tooling.
- Per-location pricing scales fast for multi-location team groups.

**Verdict:** Pick Podium when SMS, reviews, and payments are the channels driving your business; skip it if your core need is grounded on-site product or policy Q&A rather than multi-channel messaging.

## Which tool fits your marketing team's job - Lyro, Nobi, Conversica, or Podium?

Match the tool to the funnel problem, not the vendor's marketing pitch. Tidio fits when you're staffing a chat widget and want AI as after-hours backup. Nobi fits when on-site visitors bounce before getting an answer. Conversica fits when qualified leads go quiet after they've already asked a question. Podium fits when SMS and reviews are how your customers already reach you.

Nobi is the pick when the real leak is on your own pages: visitors land, have a question your site doesn't answer in the moment, and leave. That's a grounded Q&A and semantic search problem, not a staffing problem. Nobi answers straight from your connected content, with a citation behind every answer, and doesn't need a person on shift to do it.

Tidio fits a team that already has agents covering chat and just wants the widget staffed when nobody's online. Lyro AI catches first-touch questions after hours and hands off anything harder to a person the next shift. If your gap is coverage hours, not answer depth, Tidio solves it directly.

Conversica solves a different stage of the funnel: a lead who requested info, went quiet, and needs someone (or something) chasing them until they respond. It's built for persistent email and SMS follow-up over days, not for answering a visitor's question the moment they ask it.

Podium is the right call for a business with multiple locations where texting and reviews are how customers actually engage, not just search. It ties SMS conversations and review requests together across locations, a job none of the other three tools are built for.

## Frequently asked questions

Short answers to the questions marketing teams ask most when evaluating Tidio's Lyro AI against dedicated alternatives, building on the point that these tools aren't mutually exclusive.

**Is Lyro AI the same as an AI customer support?** No. Lyro is a first-touch responder for a staffed chat inbox. It catches routine questions when no agent is online and hands off anything harder to a person. A catalog-grounded conversational assistant like Nobi works differently: it reads your connected content and answers detailed product and policy questions on its own, without needing an agent behind it. For a broader look at the category, see [the best AI customer support chatbots](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-chatbot-for-customer-support) of 2026.

**Does Tidio's Lyro AI cite its sources?** No. Tidio hasn't published a sourcing feature for Lyro. It answers from the FAQ content you configure, but it doesn't show the visitor a citation back to a specific document the way a grounded assistant does.

**Can you run Tidio and Nobi together?** Yes. Tidio is a staffed live-chat widget where Lyro AI covers first-touch and after-hours questions, then hands off anything harder to an agent. That's a coverage job, distinct from a catalog-grounded assistant that reads connected content and answers detailed questions on its own. Running both means the chat inbox stays staffed around the clock while a separate assistant handles grounded product and policy Q&A. See a full [Nobi vs Tidio comparison](https://nobi.ai/blog/nobi-vs-tidio) for how the two divide the work in practice.

**Does Conversica or Podium replace an on-site AI assistant?** No. Conversica works over email and text, following up with a lead who's gone quiet until they reply or opt out. Podium works over SMS, reviews, and payments for businesses managing multiple locations. Both operate as outbound messaging tools rather than tools built to read site content and answer a visitor's question in the moment.
