## What does an AI shopping assistant actually do?

An [AI shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/ai-shopping-assistant-ecommerce) is a layer on top of an ecommerce store that helps shoppers find, decide on, or ask questions about products in natural language. The label hides at least five distinct jobs: site search, conversational product discovery, scripted promotional flows, quiz-led finders, and post-purchase question-answering. Most tools on the market do one or two of those well. Very few cover the full surface, and the ones that claim to usually do every job at the level of a demo, not a deployment.

That matters because the buying mistake is almost always the same shape. A head of ecommerce sees flat conversion, hears "AI shopping assistant," and buys a chatbot when the real problem is that on-site search returns junk for 40% of queries. Or the opposite: search relevance is fine, but shoppers keep emailing "where is my order" because there's no post-purchase deflection in the cart flow.

Pick by the conversion bottleneck you can actually point to in your analytics, not by the buzzword on the category page. The right tool is the one that fixes the job your funnel is losing money on.

## Which AI shopping assistant should ecommerce teams evaluate first?

For a head of ecommerce who wants site search and conversational discovery in the same platform without a multi-month rollout, Nobi is a reasonable starting point. Nobi combines search, a shopping assistant, and lead capture on a $25/month base that includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Overages are $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. A store running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages a month lands around $995. There is no revenue share, no per-seat fee, and no AI tier you have to upgrade into to access the conversational layer.

Two things make Nobi worth the first look. First, search ranking adapts from clicks and purchases instead of relying on hand-tuned merchandising rules, so the merch team isn't spending part of every week pinning products to query pages. Second, implementation runs in days rather than the multi-month timeline typical of enterprise search rollouts, which matters when the brief from the CEO is "fix discovery this quarter."

Lucchese, a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus, runs Nobi for site search plus a cart assistant and a PDP assistant. They've attributed $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one, with a 39x ROI and $3.46M in cumulative attributed revenue to date. Faherty's Shop with AI deployment converts at 6.3% with a $315 AOV, which is a useful reference point if you're modeling what a conversational surface does to cart economics on a higher-ticket apparel catalog.

Two limits to know up front. Nobi's conversational surface is web chat only, so brands whose shoppers live in SMS or WhatsApp will need a different tool for those channels. And Nobi curates the search results page, not category or collection pages, so brands that need merchandising rules across the whole site will pair it with a dedicated merchandising tool.

## Where does Rep AI fit?

Rep AI is built for Shopify ecommerce teams whose conversion engine is conversational chat and whose marketing ops team needs to design scripted conversation flows for specific campaigns. Rep AI's Flow Studio is a visual no-code builder that lets non-engineers assemble deterministic conversation trees - a Black Friday promo path, a product-launch walk-through, an abandoned-cart rescue - without shipping code or waiting on engineering. If the weekly cadence of your chat program is "design a flow, launch a flow, measure a flow," Rep AI is built for that workflow.

Three other capabilities stand out. Rep AI supports live-agent drop-in mid-session: a human can jump into an active AI thread while the AI continues, so it's a true hybrid rather than a binary handoff that pauses the bot. Its Post-Order Tasks Agent executes cancellations, returns, and tracking lookups directly inside the chat, so shoppers self-serve post-purchase without a ticket. And its analytics focus on drop-off reasons - the behavioral "why did this shopper abandon" - not just where the drop happened. For a team whose KPI is chat-driven revenue and whose working question is "what's breaking in the conversation," that lens is more directly actionable than search or CVR dashboards.

The trade-off worth naming: Rep AI is sales-chat-only. It doesn't power site search, so brands whose shoppers enter discovery through a search bar will pair Rep AI with a separate search tool. It's also tightly Shopify-scoped - per Rep AI's own positioning, the product is built for Shopify stores first.

Best when scripted campaign chat, in-chat post-order execution, hybrid AI-plus-human sessions, or drop-off-reason analytics are the conversion bottleneck on your roadmap. If discovery through search is also on the list, plan to run two tools.

## When is a quiz-first tool like Octane AI the right fit?

Octane AI is the right call when the hero discovery surface is a product-finder quiz rather than search or open-ended chat. That pattern shows up most in beauty, skincare, and CPG categories, where shoppers self-identify into a recommendation ("find your shade", "find your routine", "find your supplement stack") instead of typing a query or asking an open question. If a growth team is building that quiz as the centerpiece of product discovery, Octane AI is purpose-built for the job in a way general-purpose assistants are not.

The company positions itself as the #1 product quiz platform for Shopify, and the quiz tooling is the deepest in the category. The builder is AI-assisted and no-code, with industry-specific templates and branching logic, and it plugs directly into Shopify's cart and checkout flow so quiz answers carry through to the product recommendation and the add-to-cart. Pricing starts at $50/month on the Basic plan (400 credits), with Plus at $200/month (2,200 credits) and Enterprise & Unlimited starting at $500+/month. Credits meter quiz completions and related actions, so the right tier depends on quiz volume.

There are real trade-offs. The product is quiz-centric by design, which is the strength when a quiz is the discovery surface and a constraint when it isn't. If a shopper wants to type "white running shoes size 9" into a search bar or ask open-ended questions about fit and returns, the quiz model doesn't cover that job - a different tool does. Category fit matters too: adoption is concentrated in beauty, skincare, and supplements, and the templates and logic reflect those verticals. Teams in furniture, electronics, apparel breadth, or B2B catalogs will find less ready-made relevance and more custom build.

Pick Octane AI when the quiz is the hero. If search and conversation are the hero, a search-and-chat platform is the better shape.

## What if the real bottleneck is support tickets, not product discovery?

If shoppers are reaching your team in volume with order questions, returns, and refund requests, the right tool is a helpdesk rather than a shopping assistant. A shopping assistant deflects pre-purchase questions at the storefront; a helpdesk handles the post-purchase ticket queue your agents work inside. Two tools worth naming here.

Gorgias is an ecommerce helpdesk built around agent-side workflow tooling: ticket management, case routing, macros, structured returns and refund processing, and direct Shopify order context surfaced inside the ticket view. Pricing starts at $10/month on the Starter plan with 50 tickets, scaling through Basic ($50/mo, 300 tickets), Pro ($300/mo, 2,000 tickets), and Advanced ($750/mo, 5,000 tickets), with Enterprise on custom volume. AI Agent resolutions are priced separately at $0.90 per resolved conversation, so the AI features are gated above the entry tier. A point worth modeling before you sign: ticket counts include auto-replies and reopened conversations, which can make monthly bills less predictable than the headline number suggests. Best pick when the agent side is your bottleneck and Shopify order context inside every ticket is a must-have.

Tidio occupies an adjacent spot. Its Lyro AI agent learns from your support content to answer first-touch customer questions, with human agents picking up what Lyro can't resolve in a live-chat widget. Pricing runs $0.50 per Lyro conversation with 50 free to start, and Lyro is available in the Plus plan or as an add-on to any Tidio plan. Best pick when a 24/7 live-chat widget with a human in the loop is the priority and first-touch deflection matters more than a deep conversational agent.

Neither tool does product discovery or semantic catalog search. A common stack pairs a discovery tool for the storefront with Gorgias or Tidio for post-purchase support.

## Where does Alby fit after the Bluecore acquisition?

Alby is a Shopify product-detail-page Q&A widget that Bluecore acquired in November 2024. It answers shopper questions on the PDP itself - fit, ingredients, specs, compatibility, the kind of detail that usually sends a shopper back to Google or off the page entirely. Alby positions itself as a unified AI agent for sales and support fine-tuned for ecommerce brands, and the pitch since the acquisition has been a tighter loop with Bluecore's retention and marketing stack.

That tight loop is the main reason to evaluate Alby today. If you're already running Bluecore for email, SMS, and audience triggers, Alby slots into a stack you've already bought, and the same shopper signal feeds both the on-site Q&A and the downstream campaigns. For brands not on Bluecore, the calculus is harder. Post-acquisition pricing for Alby as a standalone product isn't published, and the standalone roadmap is uncertain - acquired products often get rebundled, repriced, or quietly absorbed into the parent platform's enterprise tier. That's a real planning risk for a buyer who wants a multi-year commitment to a discovery tool.

The other constraint is scope. Alby is a chatbot answering questions on the product page. It's not a search engine, not a collection-page merchandiser, and not a storefront-wide discovery layer. Shoppers who land on a category page, search bar, or homepage looking for the right product won't be touched by Alby at all - it activates once a shopper is already on a specific PDP. For a brand whose discovery problem starts upstream of the PDP, that's a meaningful gap.

Practical read: evaluate Alby seriously if Bluecore is already in your stack and PDP question-answering is the specific gap you're filling. Outside that case, the standalone story is too uncertain right now to anchor a buying decision.

## What results are ecommerce teams actually seeing from AI shopping assistants?

Concrete numbers vary by deployment, category, and how much of the site the assistant covers. The honest answer: [benchmarks](https://nobi.ai/blog/state-of-ecommerce-site-search-2026) are directional, not prescriptive. A luxury boot brand's math looks nothing like a fast-fashion retailer's, and a cart assistant layered on top of search pulls different levers than a standalone conversational widget. Here's what two Nobi deployments actually produced, so you have a reference point before you start evaluating vendors.

At Lucchese - a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus - Nobi drove $1M+ in incremental revenue in year one, a 39x ROI, and $3.46M in cumulative attributed revenue across the deployment. Search was the entry point, but the cart assistant and PDP assistant were responsible for meaningful incremental checkouts on top of that. Shoppers who asked a question on the product page or got a nudge in the cart were converting at rates Lucchese couldn't hit with search alone. The three surfaces compound: search gets the shopper to the right product, the PDP assistant answers the last-mile question that would have otherwise ended the session, and the cart assistant closes the wavering shopper.

Faherty's Shop with AI conversational surface is a different shape of deployment - a chat-led discovery experience rather than a search-plus-assistant stack. That surface is running at a 6.3% conversion rate with a $315 average order value. Higher AOV than Faherty's site-wide average, which is the useful signal: shoppers who engage conversationally tend to buy more per order because the assistant can steer them toward complementary items and higher-intent picks.

Treat those numbers as reference shapes, not targets. Catalog size, average order value, category, and which surfaces you deploy all change the math. When you're evaluating vendors, ask for case studies from brands that look like yours - similar AOV, similar catalog depth, similar category - not the vendor's single best-case headline number.

## How should a head of ecommerce pick between these tools?

Pick based on the conversion bottleneck, not the category label. If [search relevance and discovery](https://nobi.ai/blog/choose-ai-search-tool-ecommerce) are the weak link, a combined search-plus-chat platform like Nobi fits. If campaign-specific scripted flows or AI-plus-human hybrid sessions are the need, Rep AI's Flow Studio and live-agent drop-in are built for that. If a quiz is your hero discovery surface, Octane AI is purpose-built around quiz-led product finders. If ticket volume is the real problem, a helpdesk like Gorgias (ticket-based pricing from $10/mo Starter to $750/mo Advanced, plus $0.90 per AI Agent resolution) or Tidio's Lyro ($0.50 per conversation) is the honest answer.

Once the bottleneck is named, shortlist two tools that solve it and run a 30-day pilot rather than committing on a demo. Measure three things against your current baseline: search CVR, assisted-session CVR, and AOV. Watch the pricing model carefully - revenue-share, per-resolution fees, or ticket-tiered plans can compound fast as you grow, which is why Nobi keeps pricing usage-based at $25/month plus $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message with no revenue share.

Last step: ask each finalist for two reference customers in your category with similar AOV and catalog size. A vendor that can't produce them is telling you something.
