## Why are ecommerce teams looking for ChatGPT shopping assistant alternatives?

ChatGPT is a general-purpose chatbot, not an ecommerce product. It has no native awareness of your catalog, inventory, pricing, personalization signals, or checkout, which means shoppers get confident-sounding answers that can be wrong, stale, or off-catalog. A customer asking "is the navy crewneck in stock in medium?" might get a fluent reply that bears no relationship to your live PIM. That is a brand and refund problem, not a feature gap.

Custom GPTs and retrieval setups close some of the grounding gap, but they still ship without the things a head of ecommerce actually has to report on. There is no merchandising layer to pin promoted SKUs, no view of revenue per search, no CVR lift attribution, no lead-capture step when a shopper isn't ready to buy. You get chat transcripts, not a P&L line.

A production assistant also has to live where shoppers already are: in the search bar, on the PDP, in the cart drawer. A standalone chat window detached from those surfaces just adds another tab to ignore.

## What should a shopping assistant actually do on an ecommerce site?

A shopping assistant's job is narrow and measurable: help visitors find the right product, answer product questions from your live catalog, and move them toward checkout. The metrics that matter are conversion rate, average order value, and revenue per session. Everything else is decoration.

Four jobs sit underneath that. First, catalog-grounded responses. The assistant should only recommend products you actually sell, in the sizes and colors you have in stock right now. A general-purpose chatbot will happily suggest items from your competitors or describe a product you discontinued last season. Second, behavioral personalization. A returning shopper who browsed men's outerwear twice last week should not see the same homepage carousel as a first-time visitor from a paid search ad. Third, search and chat that share ranking signals. If your search bar and your chat assistant rank products differently, you end up with two merchandising systems that contradict each other and double the work. Fourth, attribution. Every chat session needs to tie back to an order so finance can see what the assistant is actually contributing in revenue, not just engagement.

Faherty's Shop with AI is a useful benchmark for what this looks like when it works. Their conversational shopping assistant hit a 6.3% conversion rate at a $315 average order value. That kind of result comes from a tool built around catalog data and shopper behavior, not a general LLM bolted onto a storefront.

[Nobi's search results learn from shopper behavior](https://nobi.ai/why-nobi/better-search), so the ranking improves without a merchandiser writing rules every week. Returning shoppers see results weighted toward what they've shown interest in, with no manual rule-tuning required. That feedback loop is the difference between a chatbot that talks and an assistant that sells.

## Which ChatGPT shopping assistant alternatives should ecommerce teams evaluate?

The practical shortlist for ecommerce brands comes down to six tools: Nobi, Alby, Rep AI, Octane AI, Gorgias, and Tidio. Each one does a different primary job, so the right pick depends on whether you're optimizing for product discovery, support deflection, or quiz-driven lead capture.

**Nobi** combines site search, conversational shopping, and [lead capture](https://nobi.ai/lead-capture) in one platform. Pricing is public and usage-based: $25/month gets you 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, with $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message after that. A brand running 50,000 searches and 5,000 messages monthly should do the math up front, since usage scales the bill, but there's no revenue share and no per-seat fee.

**Alby** is an AI shopping assistant for Shopify that answers product Q&A. Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024, so it's most relevant if you're already inside Bluecore's stack. Self-serve pricing is outcome-based on assisted conversions, with custom fixed-rate plans for enterprise.

**Rep AI** is a proactive sales chatbot. It's engineered to engage browsing visitors before they leave, with the explicit goal of reducing cart abandonment and turning conversations into revenue. Best fit if exit-intent recovery is your top problem.

**Octane AI** is quiz-led. It's the dominant product-quiz platform for Shopify, especially in beauty and CPG, with templates for product finders, routine builders, and personality quizzes. Plans run from $50/month (Basic, 400 credits) up through $500+/month at the Enterprise tier.

**Gorgias** is a helpdesk first, AI agent second. Tickets are the core unit, with plans from $10/month (Starter, 50 tickets) to $750/month (Advanced, 5,000 tickets). AI Agent resolutions are billed separately at $0.90 per resolved conversation. Right pick if your AI surface is a ticketing system.

**Tidio** is live chat with starter AI through its Lyro agent. A free trial covers 50 Lyro conversations, and it integrates with Shopify and WooCommerce. Affordable entry point for small-to-mid Shopify stores that want chat plus light automation.

Watch for revenue-share or GMV-based models in this category. They look cheap at low volume and become hard for a CFO to forecast as you grow.

## Which shopping assistant alternative fits which use case?

Match the tool to the primary job you're solving. There's no single winner across every ecommerce brand, because these products were built for different leaks in the funnel.

Nobi fits brands where discovery and conversational shopping are the same problem. If shoppers land on your site, search, browse, and ask questions before they buy, running search and chat on one platform means both surfaces rank products against the same behavioral signals. [Lucchese, a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus](https://nobi.ai/customers), combined Nobi's search with a PDP assistant and a cart assistant and drove over $1M in incremental revenue in year one at 39x ROI, with $3.46M cumulative revenue attributed. The assistants drove meaningful incremental checkouts alongside search, not instead of it.

Rep AI fits brands whose biggest leak is browsing visitors bouncing off PDPs. Rep's positioning centers on proactive engagement: anticipating intent and intervening before a shopper leaves. If your analytics show healthy PDP traffic and weak add-to-cart rates, that mechanic is the match.

Octane AI fits brands where a quiz is the hero discovery experience. Think beauty, skincare, supplements, pet: categories where shoppers need to be diagnosed into the right SKU. Octane's product finder, routine builder, and AI quiz generator are purpose-built for that funnel, with plans starting at $50/month.

Gorgias fits brands where ticket deflection is the main AI use case. It's a helpdesk first, with AI Agent resolutions priced at $0.90 per resolved conversation. If post-purchase support volume is the cost you're trying to cut, start there.

Tidio fits small-to-mid Shopify and WooCommerce stores that need affordable live chat with starter AI via Lyro. It's a sensible entry point when budgets are tight and the bar is responsiveness, not ranking intelligence.

Alby fits Shopify brands already committed to Bluecore's stack, since Alby was acquired by Bluecore in November 2024 and now sits inside that ecosystem.

## When is a different tool the better call than Nobi?

Nobi isn't the right pick for every ecommerce team, and it's worth naming the scenarios where a different tool wins. If your hero discovery surface is a quiz, if your assistant has to close the post-purchase loop, if your shoppers live on SMS, or if your brand hinges on a named chat character, look elsewhere first.

**Quiz-first product discovery.** If your conversion model is built around a product finder quiz (skin type, fit, routine, bundle), Octane AI is purpose-built for that flow. Their Basic plan starts at $50/month for 400 credits, with Plus at $200/month and Enterprise at $500+/month, and they're focused specifically on AI-generated quizzes with customizable logic and branching. Nobi's assistant can ask clarifying questions conversationally, but it isn't a dedicated quiz builder and won't replace Octane for brands whose funnel is the quiz.

**Returns, refunds, and order modifications inside chat.** Nobi helps shoppers find and decide; it does not process WISMO tickets, issue refunds, or modify orders from the chat window. If ticket deflection and agent augmentation are your core need, Gorgias is the better fit. Gorgias plans range from $10/month Starter up to $750/month Advanced plus Enterprise, with AI Agent resolutions billed at $0.90 per resolved conversation. Many brands end up running both: Gorgias for support tickets, Nobi for pre-purchase discovery.

**SMS or WhatsApp as the primary channel.** Nobi is web chat only today. If the majority of your shopper conversations happen on SMS or WhatsApp, neither Nobi nor ChatGPT's consumer app covers those surfaces natively, and you'll need a messaging-native platform.

**A heavily branded character chatbot.** If your concept is a named AI stylist with a fixed persona voice as the centerpiece of the brand, a bespoke build on top of an LLM API will beat any off-the-shelf tool, Nobi included.

**API-first custom ranking.** Engineering teams that want to own ranking logic end-to-end will prefer a developer-centric search platform like Algolia over Nobi's managed approach.
