## How do you show shoppers search results the instant they start typing on Shopify Plus?

A shopper lands on your Shopify Plus store, taps into the search bar, and starts typing. On the default Shopify Plus search, nothing happens until they finish the word and hit enter - every keystroke before that lands nowhere. That wait is worse on a phone, where typing is slower and more error-prone to begin with, and shoppers don't stick around for a blank box. They bounce, and you lose the sale before you even know they were looking. Nobi closes that gap: a typeahead dropdown, built into your Shopify Plus search bar, that surfaces matching products, collections, and content the moment a shopper starts typing. Once they actually submit the query, a second layer kicks in - Nobi's semantic search, which handles vocabulary mismatch and natural-language phrasing. The two work together, but they're solving different problems.

## What is typeahead search, and why do shoppers leave when it's missing?

Typeahead search, also called instant or predictive search, shows matching results in a dropdown as a shopper types, instead of making them finish the query and press enter to see anything. Shopify Plus stores that run the default storefront search don't do this: the search box waits for a submitted, complete query before returning a single result. Every keystroke before that point is a dead end.

That gap matters more than it looks. Shoppers judge a search box in the first second or two. A blank dropdown after three or four letters reads as broken, even if the underlying search engine works fine once the full query lands. Most won't wait around to find out.

Mobile shoppers feel this hardest. Typing a full query on a phone keyboard is slower and more error-prone than on a desktop, so a search box that only responds to a finished query asks mobile shoppers to do the most work for the least feedback. A large slice of on-site search abandonment happens right there, in the space between the first keystroke and the first result.

## What is Nobi, and how does it add typeahead search to Shopify Plus?

Nobi is an AI-powered search and shopping assistant that installs on Shopify Plus as a single component, replacing the default search box with an instant dropdown plus a [conversational shopping assistant](https://nobi.ai/blog/adding-ai-chatbot-to-ecommerce-site) behind it. That's the piece missing from the gap the last section described: instead of waiting for a shopper to finish typing and hit enter, Nobi indexes your live catalog and content so the dropdown can surface matching products, collections, and pages the moment a shopper starts typing. No rule-building or manual synonym lists required to get useful matches showing up fast.

Behind that dropdown, Nobi refreshes its index against your live catalog twice a day. A new product, a restock, or a price change shows up in typeahead within hours instead of waiting on the next full sync. That matters more than it sounds: a dropdown that surfaces a product you pulled last week, or misses one you added yesterday, breaks the same trust a blank dropdown does.

Pricing starts at $25 a month, which includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages. Usage past that runs at a flat per-unit rate, so there's no revenue-share cut and no surprise bill at the end of the month. You can see what a given volume costs before you commit to it.

Nobi's search results are also AI-powered and semantic, meaning they match on what a shopper means, not just the exact words in a product title, and they're ranked for relevance rather than pulled from a keyword index.

## How do you install Nobi's typeahead search on Shopify Plus?

Nobi's typeahead component installs on Shopify Plus with one line of code dropped into your theme - no separate app block wiring or backend integration required to get the instant dropdown live. The component reads your existing product, collection, and content data on connection, so there's no manual re-entry of your catalog to get started.

Add this to your theme layout file, just before the closing `</head>` tag, replacing `YOUR_MERCHANT_ID` with the ID from the Installation page of your Nobi dashboard:

html
<script async src="https://assistant-script.nobi.ai/nobi.bundle.js" onload="window.Nobi.initialize({ accountId: 'YOUR_MERCHANT_ID' });">
</script>
If your team doesn't have direct code access, your Shopify Plus theme editor can paste the same line into the theme's layout file without a deploy.

That single line loads Nobi across every page it's placed on, but it doesn't render anything by itself. Add the search bar element wherever the dropdown should appear, typically in place of the default Shopify Plus search box:

html
<nobi-search-bar
 default-mode="ai"
 size="regular"
 show-mode-toggle="false"
 show-hint-row="false">
</nobi-search-bar>
Once that's in place, Nobi connects to your existing catalog and content automatically.

Shopify Plus supports this kind of theme edit cleanly, without the plumbing that direct backend integrations usually need.

To confirm it worked, open your storefront, start typing in the search bar, and check that results appear before you finish the word. If your catalog synced correctly, you'll see real products in the dropdown, not just a blank box waiting for enter.

## What shows up in the typeahead dropdown as a shopper types?

Those real products in the dropdown come with more than a name. As a shopper types, Nobi surfaces matching products with a thumbnail, name, and price, along with relevant collections and matching content like FAQ or policy pages, all ranked and updated with each keystroke. A shopper gets a preview of what's in the catalog before they've finished a full query, so a partial word, or one they've half misspelled, still produces something worth clicking.

Products lead the dropdown. A shopper can click straight through to the product page without ever submitting the search.

Collections and category pages surface alongside products when the typed characters match a category name. A shopper who types "jack" might see the Jackets collection page next to individual jacket products, letting them jump to the browsing page instead of a single item if that's what they actually wanted.

The same dropdown can also carry matching content, like an FAQ answer or a policy page, when the typed characters line up with a question a shopper is starting to ask. A shopper typing "return" might see a link to your returns policy before they've finished the word, catching a pre-purchase question before it turns into a support ticket. Because the index refreshes against your live catalog and content twice a day, what shows up reflects what's actually in stock and published, not a stale snapshot from last week.

## Does Nobi's typeahead dropdown use semantic search?

No. Typeahead is instant character and prefix matching against your live catalog while a shopper is still typing, the same speed layer the last section walked through for products, collections, and content. Semantic matching, which resolves vocabulary mismatch and natural-language phrasing, only runs once a shopper submits a full query.

The split exists because the two jobs pull in different directions. Typeahead's only job is speed: showing something useful after every keystroke, before the shopper has finished typing or even decided on the right words. Semantic matching's job is understanding a finished thought, including one that doesn't share a single word with your product titles.

Typeahead won't "understand" a vague or oddly worded partial the way semantic search will once it's submitted. A shopper typing "wint jack" gets fast prefix matches on what's already in front of them, products with "wint" and "jack" somewhere in their name or tags. A shopper who finishes typing "something warm for cold mornings" and hits enter gets Nobi's semantic layer, which can connect that phrasing to a winter jacket even though the words never appear together in your catalog.

## How do you measure the lift from adding typeahead search?

Nobi's search analytics track click-through and conversion rate on typeahead-originated sessions against sessions where a shopper submitted a full query, part of a broader set of [search optimizations that lift ecommerce conversion rate](https://nobi.ai/blog/increase-ecommerce-conversion-rate-search), so you can isolate what the dropdown itself is doing instead of guessing from your overall site conversion rate. [UNTUCKit](/customers/untuckit) measured a 0.5 percentage point absolute conversion rate lift on their Shopify Plus store specifically from adding Nobi's typeahead dropdown, isolated from any other search changes they made at the same time.

The first number to watch is typeahead click-through rate: how often a shopper clicks a result in the dropdown before they finish typing and hit enter. A low click-through rate on a lot of impressions usually means the dropdown is showing up but not showing the right things, which points back to catalog indexing rather than the interface itself.

The second is a straight comparison: conversion rate on sessions that started with a typeahead click versus sessions where the shopper typed a full query and submitted it. If typeahead sessions convert at a meaningfully higher rate, that's your evidence the dropdown is doing real work, not just adding a visual flourish.

The third is the [zero-result rate](https://nobi.ai/blog/fix-zero-result-searches) on live-typed queries, tracked as a shopper types rather than only after they submit. A rising zero-result rate on partial queries is a leading indicator of a catalog gap or an indexing problem, and it'll show up here before it shows up in your submitted-query metrics. Catch it at this stage and you're fixing a data problem, not losing a sale that already happened.

## When would you need more than Nobi's typeahead search on Shopify Plus?

If your team wants to hand-build a fully custom typeahead UI or write ranking rules in code, a developer-first option like [Algolia](https://algolia.com) gives you that level of control, at the cost of an engineering project to build and maintain it - see this [comparison of AI search tools for Shopify Plus](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-search-shopify-plus) for how the trade-offs stack up. Nobi is built to get instant search live without an engineering build-out, which is the right trade-off for most marketing-led Shopify Plus teams, but not for teams that specifically want to own that code themselves.

Algolia suits a different kind of team: one with a dedicated search engineer who wants to write custom ranking strategies, build a bespoke frontend, and configure synonyms and merchandising through an API. That's real, granular control, and some teams genuinely need it, say, a catalog with ranking logic too specific for any off-the-shelf tool to model correctly. But it comes with an ongoing commitment. Someone owns that code, tests it, and maintains it as the catalog and business rules change.

Nobi's install is a search bar element plus a short snippet in your theme, with no engineering project behind it.
