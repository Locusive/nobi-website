## What should you look for in an AI chat tool for a Webflow site?

The right AI chat tool depends on what your Webflow site is actually doing - following up on inbound leads, serving local customers by SMS, or answering questions about your products and content on the page. Most tools install through Webflow's Custom Code settings or the embed block - both require a paid Webflow plan - so installation is rarely the deciding factor; the match between what the tool is built to do and what your site needs is what matters.

## How did we choose these AI chat tools for Webflow?

We focused on tools a Webflow site owner can install without writing custom code, with published or clearly described pricing and a job that maps to a real Webflow use case. Nobi is one of the tools in this list - we build it, and we're naming it alongside the alternatives because the honest comparison is more useful to you than a curated shortlist that hides the conflict.

Three filters drove the shortlist. First: does the tool install through a snippet or Webflow's Custom Code settings without a custom integration project? Second: is pricing clearly described - a published number where available, or a named structure (per-conversation, per-location) where not? Third: does the primary use case fit a real Webflow scenario - inbound lead follow-up, local customer messaging, or on-site Q&A.

Nobi passed all three. Drop in a snippet, point it at your pages and documents, and it answers visitor questions with a citation on every response. $25/month base with published overage rates. Pick it when on-site Q&A is the bottleneck. Skip it if your priority is following up with leads after they leave the page.

Conversica passed on install - it works through Webflow's Custom Code settings - but pricing is quote-only with no public figure to check upfront. The tool is built for persistent, multi-touch follow-up that keeps contacting a lead until they respond or opt out. Pick it when automated outbound follow-up is the specific thing you need. Skip it if your Webflow site is a content or product site with no local footprint.

## 2. Conversica

[Conversica](https://conversica.com) is built for a specific problem: a potential buyer fills out a form on your Webflow site, then goes quiet. Conversica's AI agent picks up that thread - sending follow-up emails, and now SMS, until the lead responds or opts out. Founded in 2013 with automotive roots, it now serves SaaS and other verticals where high-consideration purchases involve a long gap between first contact and an actual conversation. For a founder whose Webflow site generates demo requests or gated content downloads, Conversica handles the follow-up sequence without anyone on your team chasing leads by hand. It's a different tool from a live on-site assistant - it works on leads after they've left your page, not visitors while they're still on it.

**Best for:** Teams running Webflow sites that generate high-intent inbound leads - demo requests, form fills, gated content downloads - where buyers engage once and then go quiet for days.

**Pricing:** Quote-only; per-conversation enterprise pricing with no public starter tier and a multi-week sales cycle before signing.

**Pros:**
- Keeps following up automatically until the lead replies or opts out - useful for any high-consideration purchase where a single outreach isn't enough
- Email-first foundation with SMS now layered in, so teams can reach warm leads on the channel they actually reply on
- Mature conversational AI tuned for sales follow-up specifically, rather than a general-purpose assistant retrofitted into a nurture sequence

**Cons:**
- Per-conversation enterprise pricing with a multi-week sales cycle prices out smaller operators
- No native on-site web chat - Conversica handles post-visit follow-up by email, not live conversations with visitors who are currently on your page
- Originally automotive-rooted; depth in any one vertical is shared across the broader customer base

**Verdict:** Pick Conversica when your Webflow site generates qualified leads that go cold and you need a persistent follow-up agent; skip it if you want real-time on-site chat or can't absorb a multi-week enterprise sales process.

## 3. Podium

[Podium](https://podium.com) is a multi-channel customer messaging platform built around SMS, web chat, reviews, and payments. It started with local-services and SMB retail, and that origin shapes the whole product: review collection, two-way texting, and payment links all land in one inbox across your locations. For a founder whose Webflow site is the front door to a physical business - a service location, a retail shop, a multi-site operation - Podium is built exactly for that scenario. Customers text you, you collect reviews, and payments happen in the same tool where the conversation lives. Installation runs through Webflow's Custom Code settings, which requires a paid Webflow plan. The real fit question isn't installation - it's whether SMS and reviews are actually how your business runs.

**Best for:** Multi-location local businesses and service providers on Webflow whose customers prefer texting, and where reviews actively drive conversion.

**Pricing:** Quote-only - per-location SMB pricing with no published starter tier. Modular add-ons layer on top (AI Employee, Bulk Messages credits, 10DLC fees).

**Pros:**
- Native SMS conversations plus reviews and payments in one tool, useful when customers prefer texting over web chat
- Strong reviews workflow that captures customer feedback, which compounds in any category where social proof drives conversion
- Web chat that hands off to SMS, so the conversation continues after the visitor leaves the site

**Cons:**
- Built around messaging, reviews, and payments for SMB retail - [SaaS products](https://nobi.ai/blog/tidio-alternatives-saas), agency portfolios, and Webflow sites without a physical presence won't find much depth here
- Per-location pricing scales fast across multi-location operations

**Verdict:** Pick Podium when SMS, reviews, and payments are the channels driving your business; skip it if your Webflow site is a SaaS product, agency portfolio, or any operation with no physical locations or texting customers.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I add AI chat to a Webflow site without a developer?**

Yes. All three tools in this list install by pasting a code snippet into Webflow's Custom Code settings under Project Settings. No developer required. The one thing to check before you start: Webflow's custom code embedding requires a paid plan (CMS, Business, or Business+). Free plan sites can't use it.

**Does Nobi work on non-commerce Webflow sites?**

Yes. Nobi's Q&A capability works on any Webflow site with published content - pages, PDFs, help docs. If your site has a product catalog, you also get the full search and discovery features. SaaS sites and agency portfolios mainly benefit from the conversational Q&A side: visitors ask questions, Nobi pulls answers from your connected pages and documents, and every answer includes a citation the visitor can check. Product search is most useful when there's a catalog to search.

**How is AI chat different from a standard live chat widget?**

A standard live chat widget connects a visitor to a human agent. [AI chat](https://nobi.ai/blog/best-ai-chatbot-for-customer-support) answers questions automatically from your site content, around the clock, with no one on your team on the other end. Some tools combine both: Podium, for example, handles automated chat but is also built around two-way SMS conversations with real staff. Nobi is AI-only - there's no hybrid handoff to a human agent mid-session.

**What happens when Nobi doesn't know the answer?**

Nobi shows inline citations on every answer, so visitors can tap through to the source document and check it themselves. For questions where you need a specific, exact response every time - a return policy, a warranty term, a high-stakes FAQ - query overrides let you pin an approved answer to that question. When a visitor's question matches, Nobi returns that exact answer with no AI paraphrasing. Everything else routes through the standard grounded-answer process.
