## What does an 'AI shopping assistant' actually do, and which job do you need it for?

An AI shopping assistant is software that helps a visitor find and decide on a product using natural language, behavioral signals, or guided flows. In practice, the category splits into four distinct jobs: site search and product discovery, proactive sales chat, quiz-based recommendations, and post-purchase support deflection. Most vendors are strong at one, maybe two. Rep AI is built around proactive sales chat that triggers on browsing signals. Octane AI is the quiz specialist, pricing from $50/month for 400 credits up to $500+/month for enterprise. Gorgias is a support help desk first, with an AI Agent bolted on, and ticket-based pricing that can get unpredictable once auto-replies and reopens count against your limit. Alby (acquired by Bluecore in late 2024) pitches a unified sales-and-support agent, though post-acquisition direction is unclear.

Buying on brand alone gets you the wrong fit. Name the single job that moves your number. For mid-market ecommerce, search plus conversational discovery is usually where the CVR, AOV, and revenue-per-search math actually lives, not feature-count bingo.

## Which AI shopping assistants are worth shortlisting in 2026?

Six tools cover most of what mid-market ecommerce teams actually evaluate in this space: Nobi for search plus conversational discovery, Rep AI for proactive sales chat, Octane AI for quiz-driven recommendations, Alby for Shopify PDP Q&A, Tidio for entry-level chat, and Gorgias for AI-powered support. Each is strongest at a specific job, and the rest of this article goes deeper on when each one is the right call.

Nobi combines site search and conversational AI in one platform, with usage-based pricing that starts at $25/month for 2,500 searches and 250 messages (a brand pushing 50k searches a month lands closer to $500, so do the math against your traffic before you anchor on the sticker). It's the pick when you want search and chat running off the same product index, without a revenue-share agreement.

Rep AI focuses on proactive chat that engages shoppers mid-session, built primarily for Shopify stores. If your problem is that browsers leave before anyone has a chance to help them, Rep AI is built around that exact moment.

Octane AI is the quiz specialist. Plans start at $50/month (400 credits) and go up to $500+/month for Enterprise. Beauty and CPG brands gravitate here because a product-finder quiz fits how those shoppers decide.

Alby is a Shopify-native PDP assistant that answers product questions on the page. It was acquired by Bluecore in November 2024, so longer-term roadmap direction is worth asking about on a demo. Pricing is outcome-based for self-serve and custom for enterprise.

Tidio is the friendly entry point: live chat with Lyro, its AI agent, available as an add-on and free to test on up to 50 conversations. Good for SMBs getting started, thinner on discovery.

Gorgias is support-first. Pricing runs $10 to $900/month plus per-ticket overages, and the AI Agent deflects repetitive tickets well. Gorgias itself acknowledges product discovery isn't its strength, so pair it with a discovery tool if that's the gap you're filling.

## When should you pick Rep AI or Octane AI over Nobi?

Pick Rep AI when your bottleneck is intent capture, not query answering. Its proactive chat is built around behavioral triggers that open a conversation at a specific moment in a session, which is a harder problem than responding to someone who already typed something into a search bar. If you've watched session recordings and concluded that your shoppers are bouncing before they ever ask a question, Rep AI's whole product is pointed at that wound. Pick Octane AI when your merchandising model is quiz-first. Octane positions itself as the top product quiz platform on Shopify, with customizable formats like product finders, routine builders, and personality quizzes feeding an AI rec engine wired to Shopify product data. In categories where a guided quiz genuinely outperforms open-ended chat (think skincare regimens, supplement stacks, foundation shade matching), that format does work conversational tools struggle to match.

Both tools have honest trade-offs. Rep AI is chat-only, so it doesn't replace your site search, you'll still pay for that separately and reconcile two ranking systems. Octane's quiz-centric design is the inverse problem: a quiz funnel handles the shopper who's willing to answer six questions, but it's a poor fit for the shopper who just wants to type "waterproof hiking pants under $80" into a search box. And if you've already invested months building a quiz-led merchandising program on Octane, switching costs alone probably argue for staying put, retraining your team and rebuilding flows on a different platform isn't free.

Nobi handles conversational discovery and search in one place, which is the right call when you want fewer vendors and one ranking model. It doesn't try to out-quiz Octane or out-proactive Rep AI on the surfaces those tools were purpose-built for.

## What about Alby, Tidio, and Gorgias, where do they actually fit?

Alby fits when your highest-value conversations happen on product detail pages and you want an assistant that lives there specifically. Its strength is PDP-level Q&A with real Shopify depth, and its own site describes outcome-based pricing tied to assisted conversions for self-serve brands, with custom fixed-rate plans for enterprise. The caveat: Bluecore acquired Alby in November 2024, so the roadmap is in transition and post-acquisition pricing isn't something we can pin down from public sources. If you're signing a one-year deal, ask where the product sits in Bluecore's stack before you commit.

Tidio is the right call for Shopify merchants who mainly want live chat with a light AI layer on top. Lyro, Tidio's AI agent, learns from your support content and answers customer questions in a conversational way, and Tidio lets you test it free on up to 50 conversations. Setup is quick and the pricing is approachable for small to mid-sized stores. Where it's thinner is semantic product discovery: if shoppers are asking "show me a breathable rain jacket under $150," that's closer to Nobi's territory than Tidio's. Tidio wins on live-chat simplicity. Nobi wins when search-led discovery is doing the real selling.

Gorgias is a helpdesk first, a shopping assistant second. Third-party pricing coverage puts its plans between roughly $10 and $900 per month on ticket-based tiers, with overage charges in the $0.36 to $0.40 range per ticket once you exceed your limit, and its AI Agent handles repetitive support conversations well. The trade-off for this reader: no product discovery or site search. If you're choosing a ticketing platform with AI deflection bolted on, Gorgias makes sense. If you're choosing a shopping assistant, it doesn't.

## How should you compare pricing without getting burned by revenue-share surprises?

Compare tools on cost per search and cost per conversation at your actual traffic, not on the sticker price. Nobi publishes $25/month base for 2,500 searches and 250 messages, then $0.01 per extra search and $0.10 per extra message. A mid-market brand doing 100,000 searches a month lands around $1,000, which is predictable against a budget. Layer in messages at your real volume and you can model the bill before you sign.

The other vendors split into three pricing shapes, and each shape carries a different risk.

Tiered SaaS is the easiest to model. Octane AI publishes Basic at $50/month, Plus at $200/month, and Enterprise starting around $500/month, gated by a credit pool. Rep AI and Tidio also publish tiered plans. The catch is overage: when you exceed your tier mid-month, the next tier is usually a step function, not a marginal rate, so a busy week can push you into a plan you don't otherwise need.

Per-resolution pricing is where Gorgias goes once you turn on the AI Agent. You pay for every ticket the agent closes. That math works when volume is flat. It punishes you during a sale, a product launch, or a shipping incident, exactly the moments when you want the agent handling more, not less.

Outcome-based pricing is the one to read carefully. Alby ties self-serve pricing to assisted conversions, with custom plans for enterprise. Some teams accept that the better the assistant performs the bigger the bill; others find it eats the lift.

Opaque pricing is its own cost. Alby and most enterprise search vendors require a sales conversation before you can model a single dollar. Ask every shortlisted vendor for the bill at your real search and message volume, get it in writing, and stress-test it against a Black Friday week.

## What kind of conversion results should you actually expect?

Credible AI shopping assistants tend to lift conversion and AOV in the single-to-low-double-digit percentage range once they've had a few weeks to learn from real shopper behavior. Results vary a lot based on catalog size, traffic quality, and where the assistant shows up in the journey. Category averages are close to useless. What you want from any vendor is a number from a brand that looks like yours: similar GMV tier, similar vertical, similar traffic mix.

[A few real numbers worth anchoring against](https://nobi.ai/customers). Lucchese, a luxury Western boot brand on Shopify Plus, drove over $1M in incremental revenue in its first year on Nobi, at a 39x ROI, and has crossed $3.46M in cumulative attributed revenue. Search did the heavy lifting, but their cart assistant and PDP assistant added meaningful incremental checkouts on top, which is the part most vendors quietly leave out of their case studies. Faherty's Shop with AI assistant runs at a 6.3% conversion rate and a $315 AOV, well above what most apparel sites see on cold traffic.

Two things to take from this. First, ask every shortlisted vendor for comparable numbers from a brand in your GMV range and vertical, not their flagship logo. A $200M brand's case study tells you nothing about what a $5M brand will see. If they can't produce one, that's a signal in itself. Second, demand a pilot with a real holdout group. No holdout, no real number. Attribution on conversational surfaces is messy, and without a control you're buying the vendor's dashboard, not actual incrementality.

Nobi's behavioral learning sharpens results over the first 30 to 60 days as the system watches what shoppers click, ignore, and buy. Plan your pilot window with that ramp in mind, and judge the lift after it, not during it.

## Who should NOT pick Nobi, and what should they look at instead?

If you're running a sub-$1M GMV store, Nobi is overbuilt for your traffic and a lighter tool like Tidio will get you further for less money. Tidio's Lyro agent is built for small and mid-sized businesses, includes a free tier with 50 Lyro conversations to try, and pairs naturally with native Shopify search while you're still figuring out what shoppers actually ask for. You don't need usage-based search pricing when your monthly search volume is a rounding error.

If your team has already standardized on a ticketing-first support model, Gorgias plus its AI Agent is the more natural path. Gorgias is a help desk first, with an AI Agent bolted on for ecommerce support and light sales assistance. Bolting a second discovery tool onto that stack usually creates more handoff pain than it's worth.

If your merchandising runs through quizzes, Octane AI will out-perform open-ended chat for that specific job. It's purpose-built as a Shopify product quiz platform, with Basic at $50/month and Plus at $200/month. And if your real need is PDP-specific product Q&A, Alby was designed for exactly that surface, though post-Bluecore-acquisition direction is worth diligence before you sign.

Two honest Nobi trade-offs to weigh: less brand recognition than Algolia, and a smaller third-party integration ecosystem.
