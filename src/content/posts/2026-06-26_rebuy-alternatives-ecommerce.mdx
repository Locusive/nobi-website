The friction shows up in your data before you can name it: high zero-result rates, thin add-to-cart rates on collection pages, support tickets about questions your site should have answered. ## Is Rebuy still the right tool for your ecommerce site?

Rebuy built its reputation on Smart Cart and post-purchase recommendation flows - a configurable engine that lifts AOV by surfacing bundles, upsells, and cross-sells at the cart and checkout stage. It does that job well. But merchants who need semantic site search, pre-purchase Q&A, or personalization that runs across category and collection pages - not just the cart - find it runs out of road quickly.

The primary value in Rebuy is recommendation widgets: what to pair with the item in the cart, what to offer after checkout, how to structure a bundle. Site-wide product discovery and site search are out of scope. A shopper who types a query into your search bar, or lands on a collection page, gets nothing from Rebuy.

There's also no conversational assistant or grounded Q&A. Shoppers who ask about materials, dimensions, sizing, or compatibility before they add to cart get no answer. That's a real gap for any brand where pre-purchase questions drive purchase decisions - apparel, footwear, home goods, anything with fit or spec complexity.

Personalization in Rebuy is cart-stage and widget-focused. Behavioral reranking across search results, browse pages, and category listings requires a separate tool. And brands that need to reduce ticket volume on order status questions, return policy requests, or sizing guidance need a product built for that job.

If the gap is search quality or pre-purchase shopper Q&A rather than cart recommendations, the alternatives below each address a distinct part of that problem.

## How did we evaluate these Rebuy alternatives?

We evaluated tools ecommerce marketing teams commonly reach for when they need capabilities Rebuy doesn't cover: site search quality, pre-purchase conversational Q&A, personalization across all surfaces, or full-funnel A/B testing. Nobi is one of the tools in this list and we build it; we have been direct about where each option fits and where it does not.

Four criteria shaped the evaluation. Does the tool close the specific gap Rebuy leaves? Is pricing per-unit, revenue-share, or enterprise-contracted? How fast is implementation? And do AI answers come from live connected content or static training data?

**Nobi** is a conversational website assistant that pairs product search with automated shopper Q&A on one platform. Pricing starts at $25/month base, and a small theme edit has it live same day. Behavioral reranking based on individual shopper history isn't in Nobi yet; personalization today covers placeholder text and starter messages. If that's the main gap, other tools on this list address it better.

**Constructor** is an AI-powered product discovery platform. Behavioral reranking runs across search, browse, and recommendations simultaneously, drawing from clickstream signals. Pricing is revenue-share with no published rate. Pick it when full-site personalization is the priority and you have a data team to support it.

**LimeSpot** is a Shopify recommendation app built around AOV uplift through upsell, cross-sell, and bundle widgets. It doesn't address site search or pre-purchase Q&A.

**Dynamic Yield**, owned by Mastercard, is an enterprise personalization and A/B testing platform covering segmentation, journey orchestration, and experimentation across web and mobile. Pricing isn't published; it's enterprise-contracted.

**Klevu** is AI-powered ecommerce search, now a division of Athos Commerce. Third-party sources cite $499-$1,598/month depending on tier; Athos doesn't publish rates. Personalization features are Expert-tier only.

## 1. Nobi

Nobi combines semantic site search with a grounded conversational assistant in one product. A shopper who types a query gets fast, ranked results; a shopper who asks about sizing, materials, or return policy gets an answer drawn from your connected catalog and content, with an inline citation they can check. The semantic layer handles vocabulary mismatch automatically, so shoppers find the right products even when their search terms don't match your product titles exactly. [Kilte](/customers/kilte) uses Nobi across the search bar, collection filters, and product discovery - the same semantic layer working across three parts of the site rather than just the search box. Where Rebuy covers the cart stage, Nobi covers what comes before: search quality and product Q&A from the first query to the add-to-cart.

**Best for:** Merchants whose main CVR friction is search quality and pre-purchase Q&A - sizing guidance, spec questions, return policy - rather than cart-stage recommendation widgets.

**Pricing:** $25/month base (2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages included). $0.01 per additional search, $0.10 per additional message.

**Pros:**

- Every answer shows the source document, date, and exact excerpt, so shoppers can verify a product claim or policy against your official content
- Connected knowledge sources refresh twice a day, so a pricing update or policy change lands in shopper answers within hours

- No site-wide merchandising.
- Not an API-first developer platform.
- No shopper-facing in-chat post-purchase transactional execution.
- Website chat only - no email channel.
- Not a heavily persona-branded character AI assistant.
- No quiz-led product finder flows.

- No live agent drop-in on AI conversations.

- Not a ticket workflow platform.
- Smaller third-party integration marketplace than enterprise incumbents like Algolia.
- Less brand recognition than Algolia or Bloomreach.

**Verdict:** Pick Nobi when the gap is pre-purchase search quality and grounded product Q&A; skip it when behavioral reranking across search results, browse pages, and collection pages is the primary requirement.

## 2. Constructor

[Constructor](https://constructor.io) reorders search results, category pages, browse pages, and product recommendations in real time based on what each shopper clicks, views, and adds to cart during their visit. The same behavioral signals run across every surface - search, browse, category listings, and PDP recommendations - so a merch team can tune all of them from one place instead of managing separate projects for each. Built-in A/B testing lets the team measure whether a ranking change actually moved CVR on a specific query or category page before rolling it out sitewide. Constructor is a behavioral personalization engine first; it adapts rankings automatically without a merchandiser stepping in every time.

**Best for:** Large-volume retailers with an internal data team whose merchandising workload spans category, collection, browse, and search, and where real-time behavioral personalization is the headline requirement.

**Pricing:** Revenue-share model. Costs scale with GMV.

**Pros:**
- The "glassbox" AI model lets merchandisers see why a ranking decision was made and override it when needed

**Cons:**
- Revenue-share pricing model can create surprise costs
- Enterprise sales cycle

**Verdict:** Pick Constructor when full-site behavioral personalization and real-time reranking are the headline requirements and you have a data team to keep the ranker sharp; skip it when you need pricing you can model before signing or a grounded answer engine for pre-purchase shopper questions.

## 3. LimeSpot

[LimeSpot](https://limespot.com) is a Shopify recommendation app covering homepage, collection pages, product detail pages, and cart. Tiered pricing starts lower than most alternatives in this category. For teams whose core requirement is recommendation widgets on those surfaces, it covers the job without added complexity. The setup runs through the Shopify admin with no engineering involvement. What it doesn't cover is site search, pre-purchase Q&A, or any grounded answer engine for shoppers who ask about sizing or dimensions before they add to cart. The scope is deliberate and narrow: AOV lift through recommendation widgets on the Shopify surfaces where most cart-stage decisions happen, with nothing else layered on top.

**Best for:** Shopify brands that want to replace Rebuy's recommendation widgets with a lower-cost alternative running on the same surfaces, without adding search or conversational capability.

**Pricing:** Tiered Shopify app pricing.

**Pros:**
- Supports bundles, frequently bought together, cart upsell, checkout upsell, and post-purchase offers in one app

**Cons:**
- Widget-based recommendations only
- Not conversational or search-capable

**Verdict:** Pick LimeSpot when you want a direct Rebuy recommendation-widget swap at a lower price point; skip it when the actual gap is site search quality, pre-purchase shopper Q&A, or behavioral reranking across category and browse pages.

## 4. Dynamic Yield

[Dynamic Yield](https://dynamicyield.com), owned by Mastercard, goes further than recommendation widgets. It personalizes full-page layouts - homepage hero banners, email content, promotional offers, and product listings - using segment and behavioral rules that run across every surface at once. A marketing team can run a multivariate test on a homepage layout while also tuning what a returning customer sees in their next email, all inside one platform. That breadth is the appeal. It's also the cost: the implementation runs multiple quarters, the pricing is enterprise-contracted, and the resource requirements are real. For brands whose personalization work is campaign-scale and spans multiple channels, that investment can make sense. For most ecommerce teams whose gap is more narrow - search quality or pre-purchase Q&A - Dynamic Yield is more platform than the problem requires.

**Best for:** Enterprise brands running personalization across homepage, email, and campaign-level A/B testing with dedicated implementation resources and budget for a multi-quarter rollout.

**Pricing:** Enterprise pricing, custom quotes; not self-serve.

**Pros:**
- Built-in A/B and multivariate testing across every personalized surface, so teams can measure which layout or ranking variant moved CVR before a full rollout
- Mastercard's Element feature brings anonymized offline spending signals most SaaS tools can't access

**Cons:**
- Enterprise pricing and implementation overhead
- Heavy on experimentation tooling, lighter on conversational discovery
- No native shopping assistant or conversational search

**Verdict:** Pick Dynamic Yield when campaign-level personalization and A/B testing across homepage, email, and recommendations is your enterprise-scale requirement; skip it when conversational search or grounded pre-purchase Q&A is the specific conversion bottleneck you need to close.

## 5. Klevu

[Klevu](https://klevu.com) is AI-powered site search for Shopify with a no-code merchandising dashboard. The problem it solves best is vocabulary mismatch: a shopper who types "wide-leg cropped trouser in navy" still lands on the right product even when the catalog title says "cropped wide pant." The AI reads what shoppers mean, not just what they typed, so your team isn't building a synonym list for every alternate phrase a buyer might use. The dashboard handles seasonal pushes and priority pinning without an engineering ticket, and a Shopify install typically goes live in days. One thing worth flagging before you finalize your shortlist: Klevu is now a division of Athos Commerce, the same parent that owns Searchspring. If both are on your list, you're comparing two tools from the same company, not two independent vendors.

**Best for:** Shopify brands whose zero-result rate is driven by long, descriptive queries a keyword engine can't parse, and who need occasional pinning on top.

**Pricing:** Three tiers: Essential, Advanced, Expert. Quote-only - no published dollar figures on athoscommerce.com/pricing; all tiers show "Get a demo" CTA. Third-party sources cite roughly $499-$1,598/month range. Personalization features are included in Expert tier only, not lower tiers.

**Pros:**
- AI matching closes the vocabulary gap between how shoppers describe products and how catalog titles name them, with no manually maintained synonym list needed for every phrasing variation

**Cons:**
- Expensive relative to brand size
- Merchandising UI is dated
- Personalization features are only in the Expert tier; Essential and Advanced tiers do not include them
- Consolidated under Athos Commerce (alongside Searchspring and Intelligent Reach) - shortlisting Klevu + Searchspring means picking between two products of the same parent company

**Verdict:** Pick Klevu when vocabulary mismatch is the main CVR leak on your Shopify store and you need occasional pinning on top; skip it if personalization matters and Expert-tier pricing is out of range.
