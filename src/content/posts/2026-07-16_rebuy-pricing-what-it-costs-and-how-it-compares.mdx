## How much does Rebuy cost on Shopify?

Rebuy's published pricing runs from $99/month on the Grow plan to $499/month on the Pro plan. There are four named tiers: Grow ($99/mo), Scale ($249/mo), Pro ($499/mo), and Enterprise (quote-only for Shopify Plus merchants). There is no permanent free plan, though a trial period is available - check rebuyengine.com for current length and terms, as they change.

One detail that catches merchants off guard: your Shopify subscription level determines which Rebuy tier you can access, not your revenue or order volume. Grow is for Basic Shopify stores, Scale for Shopify, Pro for Advanced Shopify. If you're on a Basic plan and want Pro features, you'd need to upgrade your Shopify subscription first - there's no way to self-upgrade within Rebuy alone.

At the top end, Enterprise pricing is quote-only and requires a sales conversation. Shopify Plus merchants cannot self-serve into that tier, so the $499/mo Pro plan is effectively the ceiling for anyone who wants to sign up without talking to sales.

## What does each Rebuy plan actually include?

Each Rebuy tier unlocks more recommendation widget placements, deeper Smart Cart customization, and additional A/B testing capacity.

**Grow ($99/mo)** covers the basics: homepage and product detail page recommendation widgets, plus the foundational Smart Cart. It's enough to surface related products and add a personalized cart experience, but post-purchase upsell flows and checkout extensions aren't available here.

**Scale ($249/mo)** adds post-purchase upsell flows and expanded A/B testing. This is where merchants who want to test different recommendation placements or run timed offers after checkout will land.

**Pro ($499/mo)** opens full Shopify checkout extension support, advanced personalization rules, and priority support. One caveat: checkout extensions require Shopify Plus. If you're on Advanced Shopify without Plus, you can pay for Pro but won't get access to the checkout extension features.

**Enterprise** (quote-only) adds custom recommendation logic, a dedicated onboarding team, and SLA commitments. It's built for merchants with complex catalog or merchandising requirements that the standard tiers can't accommodate.

One thing Rebuy does differently from some competing tools: its behavioral recommendation engine - learning from click and purchase history to surface relevant products - is included at all paid tiers, not locked to the top plan. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com), by contrast, gates its personalization features to the Expert tier only, so merchants on Essential or Advanced get AI search without the behavioral ranking layer.

What Rebuy doesn't include at any tier: a site search bar, semantic query matching, or a [conversational assistant that answers pre-purchase questions](https://nobi.ai/blog/adding-ai-chatbot-to-ecommerce-site). It's a recommendation and cart optimization tool. Shoppers who want to ask "does this come in a size 10?" or "what's your return window?" get no AI answer from Rebuy - those questions fall back to your support team or go unanswered on the page.

## Does Rebuy charge based on revenue, usage, or a flat monthly fee?

Rebuy uses a flat monthly fee tied to your Shopify plan level - not a revenue-share or per-recommendation model. A strong sales month does not produce a larger Rebuy invoice. The $249/month Scale subscription stays $249 whether you generate $50,000 or $500,000 in revenue that month.

That predictability matters when you're modeling quarterly spend. Your cost ceiling is your plan tier, and your plan tier is fixed to your Shopify subscription. No usage overages for recommendation impressions, no variable charges if a campaign drives a spike in traffic, no surprise line items at month end.

That's a meaningful contrast to [Constructor](https://www.constructor.io), which runs on a revenue-share model. Constructor's costs scale in lockstep with GMV - a strong month for the store is also a strong month for the invoice. That model can work well for large merchants who want aligned incentives, but it makes planning harder. A well-executed Black Friday push could push a Constructor bill well past your budgeted run rate.

One exception to Rebuy's flat-fee clarity: Shopify Plus merchants on the Enterprise tier are on a negotiated contract, not the published rate card. If you're in that tier, confirm with Rebuy's sales team whether any usage-based components apply, because the terms are specific to your agreement and aren't public.

For the vast majority of merchants - anyone on Basic Shopify through Advanced Shopify - the pricing is exactly what the tier page shows. No revenue split, no per-recommendation charge, nothing that scales with your store's performance.

## How does Rebuy's price compare to Klevu, Fast Simon, and Nobi?

Whether it's the right spend is a separate question, and it comes down to which conversion problem your store actually has.

Rebuy starts at $99/month for recommendation widgets and Smart Cart. It doesn't include a search bar or any way to answer pre-purchase shopper questions. [Klevu](https://www.klevu.com) doesn't publish pricing on its Athos Commerce page; third-party sources put the range at roughly $499–$1,598/month for AI search and merchandising, with personalization features available only at the Expert tier. [Fast Simon](https://www.fastsimon.com) starts at $39.99/month for visual merchandising and search, scaling by monthly session volume; merchandising rules and A/B testing are gated to the $299.99/month top tier. [Findify](https://www.findify.io) is tiered (now under Maropost; contact for current rates) and bundles search, merchandising, and personalization together at every tier. [Boost Commerce](https://boostcommerce.net) has affordable tiered pricing via the Shopify App Store; it sits at the affordable end with strong faceted filtering, though semantic query matching is limited. Nobi starts at $25/month, which includes 2,500 searches and 250 conversational messages, then $0.01 per additional search and $0.10 per additional message. Nobi curates the search results page, not category or collection pages, so brands that need site-wide merchandising beyond search will still want a dedicated tool for that.

The price spread across these tools is real, but picking by price alone is the wrong frame. A store with high search abandonment, shoppers hitting [dead ends on zero-result pages](/blog/fix-zero-result-searches), needs a [search or Q&A tool](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-top-ecommerce-platforms-for-native-ai-search-and-product-discovery), not recommendation widgets. A store where search works but shoppers land on PDPs and don't add to cart has the opposite problem: Rebuy's recommendation engine and Smart Cart address that gap directly. Match the tool to the bottleneck, not the budget.

## When does Rebuy's price make sense, and when should you consider a lighter alternative?

Rebuy earns its price when the CVR gap you're solving lives in the cart and post-purchase path - low AOV from missed cross-sells, abandoned carts a Smart Cart upsell could recover, or checkout flows with no personalized product push. If that's your leak, the $99-$499/month range maps cleanly to the problem.

If your CVR problem sits earlier - shoppers hitting zero results, leaving because a sizing or compatibility question went unanswered, or bouncing on a PDP before they ever reach the cart - Rebuy doesn't address it. A tool that handles pre-purchase Q&A or search relevance will [improve pre-purchase CVR](https://nobi.ai/blog/the-best-ecommerce-search-solutions-to-improve-conversion-rates) at a lower price point.

When budget only allows one, pull two numbers from your analytics: your search abandonment rate and your add-to-cart rate. High search abandonment with a low add-to-cart rate points to a front-of-funnel problem - shoppers aren't finding what they want, so they leave before reaching the cart. Start with search or Q&A. If search abandonment is low but AOV is the drag, your shoppers are finding products but not buying as much as they could. Start with Rebuy.

One honest limit worth naming: Nobi doesn't include Smart Cart functionality or post-purchase upsell widgets. It's not a substitute for Rebuy if lifting cart-stage AOV is your primary goal. Pick the tool that fits the bottleneck you actually have.

## Frequently asked questions about Rebuy pricing

**Is there a free Rebuy plan?**

No permanent free plan exists. Rebuy offers a trial period - check rebuyengine.com for the current length before you budget, since the terms change.

**Can I use Rebuy on Basic Shopify?**

Yes. The Grow plan at $99/month is designed for Basic Shopify stores. It covers homepage and product page recommendation widgets plus the foundational Smart Cart, though it includes fewer widget placements and less A/B testing capacity than the Scale or Pro tiers.

**Does Rebuy replace a site search tool?**

No. Rebuy handles recommendation widgets and Smart Cart. It doesn't include a search bar, semantic query matching, or a conversational assistant that answers pre-purchase questions. Merchants dealing with search abandonment or shoppers hitting dead ends need a separate tool for that.

**Does Rebuy's price go up if my revenue grows?**

No. Rebuy charges a flat monthly fee tied to your Shopify plan tier, not a revenue-share model. A strong sales month doesn't raise your Rebuy invoice. Your cost only changes if you move to a higher Shopify plan.

**What Shopify plan is required for checkout extensions?**

Shopify Plus. Checkout extensions are part of the Enterprise tier and aren't available on standard Shopify plans - even if you're paying for the Pro tier. You can subscribe to Pro on Advanced Shopify and still not get access to checkout extension features without Plus.
